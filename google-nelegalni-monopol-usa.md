# USA: Court Declares Google an Illegal Monopolist
> 

American federal judge Mehta ruled that [Google violated antitrust laws](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/article/2024/aug/06/google-antitrust-monopoly-ruling) by entering into exclusive deals with device manufacturers like Apple and Samsung and paying billions of dollars to be their default search engine. [The court will determine specific remedies in a subsequent proceeding](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0k44x6mge3o), which could include breaking up Alphabet. Google plans to appeal the decision. [Alphabet's stock fell 4.5% following the announcement.](https://www.reuters.com/legal/us-judge-rules-google-broke-antitrust-law-search-case-2024-08-05/) The Google company plans to challenge the decision.

Marek Lecián notes that Google recently entered into an agreement with Reddit [to exclusively license its content](https://www.reddit.com/r/google/comments/1ax1nyh/reddit_has_struck_a_60_million_deal_with_google/) for training artificial intelligence. According to Marek, [the exclusivity of this agreement could further harm Google in court](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7226329939174666241/).

In the EU, the DSA requires service providers to allow users [to choose their default search engine](https://www.brusselstimes.com/955313/apple-google-meta-new-eu-rules-on-internet-gatekeepers-take-effect-today).
