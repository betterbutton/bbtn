At the first edition of the Brno AI festival, two films succeeded: the detective film [Lost](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0evg-1aUXXw) and the animated film [Troubled Water](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2ny3JoxYXA0). The festival presented [33 experimental short films](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL3wwZ4p9YhojuwhsBQHWUi6jW3QtWBitm) that use various AI tools. The main prize was awarded not only for technical innovations but primarily for creative stories, [writes Wired](https://www.wired.cz/clanky/brnensky-ai-festival-ma-dva-viteze).

> "Technology is developing terribly quickly, sets the brain to a new way of thinking and encourages creativity."\
> — **[David Spáčil](https://www.wired.cz/clanky/brnensky-ai-festival-ma-dva-viteze)**

The jury highlighted that even with artificial intelligence, good storytelling remains a key element. In addition to the main awards, other projects in genre categories were also awarded, including music videos and commercials. Part of the festival was also the conference [Rules Have Changed](https://www.pravidlasezmenila.cz/), which welcomed experts in the field, such as Tim Simmons and Olivia Sarikase.
