# Petra Dolejšová: Reviews, Discounts, Button Text and Guarantees vs. Consumer Protection
> 

Lawyer Petra Dolejšová, who focuses on the digital environment and e-commerce, discussed at a workshop the changes to the Consumer Protection Act, specifically focusing on the amendment concerning the transparency of reviews and discounts on e-shops, which has been in effect for a year and a half.

> "Since last year, if you use reviews on an e-shop, you should communicate transparently whether you verify those reviews or not."
> **— Petra Dolejšová, [Most Common Mistakes and Troubles of E-shop Operators After the Amendment to the Law](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WrhpO3MWHNQ)**

For reviews, it is necessary to clearly communicate whether they are verified or not, not only in the terms and conditions but also directly next to the reviews. For discounts, e-shops must adhere to the 30-day rule, which stipulates that the discount must be calculated from the lowest price of the product in the last 30 days before the first discount. The discount must be transparent and must not be misleading.
