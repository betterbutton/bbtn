# Google Penalty: A Story with a Happy Ending?
> 

Milan Pichlík describes in the article how he [returned his penalized website to search results](https://www.milanpichlik.cz/zachrana-webu/) after moving it to a new domain. He states that Google penalties apply more to domains than to content and are not visible in [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/).

In August 2023, Milan's site suffered a dramatic drop in traffic due to a Google core update. The previously successful project, with an average of 12,000 visitors per day, nearly vanished. Milan describes four attempts at recovery, including work on content and authority. Finally, he chose a radical cut—redirecting the site to a new domain. This step brought unexpected success, traffic quickly rose to 25,000 people per day via [Google Discover](https://www.ivokylian.cz/blog/clanky/58-co-byste-meli-vedet-o-sluzbe-google-discover). However, the author remains cautious and prepared for further challenges.

> "Penalties by various Penguins and Pandas are at the domain level and apply to domains, not their contents."\
> **— [Dušan Janovský](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7220099923336138752/), Linkedin**

Milan recommends moving content to a new domain as a solution. He mentions that in recent years, sites focused on [YMYL](https://www.seoprakticky.cz/co-je/ymyl/) areas have experienced random traffic fluctuations due to algorithm changes. Dušan Janovský on Linkedin [estimates in his reflection](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7220099923336138752/) that Pichlík's ad-heavy site may have faced a penalty precisely due to excessive ads or weak content.
