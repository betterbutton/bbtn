# Working remotely? You won't get promoted.
> 

On the Wall Street Journal website[1](#footnote-1) article [Remote Workers Are Losing Out on Promotions, New Data Shows](https://www.wsj.com/lifestyle/careers/remote-workers-are-losing-out-on-promotions-8219ec63): Employees working remotely are 31% less likely to be promoted than their office-based colleagues, according to an analysis by [Live Data Technologies](https://www.livedatatechnologies.com/).

The reason is a lack of mentoring opportunities and "proximity bias," i.e., discrimination based on physical absence. This gap is more pronounced for women. Although remote workers show higher productivity and satisfaction, their career growth is limited.

> "There's some proximity bias going on. I literally call it discrimination."\
> **— Nick Bloom, economist at Stanford University**

Research from Stanford University shows that hybrid workers have similar promotion chances as those who are in the office daily.
