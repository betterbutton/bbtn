# Linkodrome

**Canva [acquired Serif](https://affinity.serif.com/en-gb/press/newsroom/canva-press-release/).** Serif is a British company that develops tools for designers and photographers under the name Affinity. Their bitmap and vector editor and desktop publishing tool is a popular alternative to Adobe, mainly because you can buy it outright for a few hundred crowns. In contrast, Canva is a subscription-based tool. [Affinity users are worried](https://twitter.com/fczbkk/status/1772597779363217812) that future versions of their favorite software will no longer be available for a one-time purchase.

**Vzhůru dolů releases webinar recordings.** Martin Michálek 👋 made a generous move when he [made recordings available for free viewing on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLIdlKRR9lxIdE7CmSnbIad2xoPzG3zijg), which he recorded either alone or with colleagues under the brand [Vzhůru dolů](https://www.vzhurudolu.cz/blog/242-vd-videa-zdarma) from 2020 to 2023. The videos cover CSS, HTML, and web development in general, so they will be appreciated primarily by coders. Thank you!

**They are not digital talent.** Deputy Prime Minister for Digitalization [Ivan Bartoš confided in the Euro magazine](https://twitter.com/PiratIvanBartos/status/1772543986344837367) that he is trying to align salary conditions in the public sector so that it can keep pace with the private sector on the labor market.

**Every designer should have their own product.**  is the head of Q Designers, who design amazing things. If we had to name one, maybe [the 100 groups project](https://www.qdesigners.co/aktualni-projekty/sto-skupin). Jindra says: *"It's not possible to design a good product without understanding the business."* You learn the most about business when running your own venture. That's also why [every designer should have some project of their own](https://jindrichfialka.substack.com/p/kazdy-designer-by-mel-mit-vlastni).

**Style your Google Map so it matches your brand.** Did you know that for Google maps embedded on your website, you can specify which elements it displays? You can turn off points of interest so they don't distract from the pin with your location, but you can also [recolor the entire map](https://mapstyle.withgoogle.com/) to match your company's colors. Check out [examples on Snazzy Maps](https://snazzymaps.com/).

**Is SEO changing into UXO?** When we lectured [on the relationship between UX and SEO](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/142565604/zivot-s-technologiemi) at SEO Restart, we didn't yet know that Danny Sullivan from Google would take to Twitter a day later to write [about the cargo cult of SEO](https://twitter.com/searchliaison/status/1770867218059800685): *"The most important key to success in Google search is content that pleases people, not what you may have heard that 'Google wants'."* Some [SEO specialists](https://twitter.com/glenngabe/status/1770885180443561986) see this as a harbinger of UX Optimization, [others are skeptical](https://twitter.com/GregBernhardt4/status/1771133998577951129). Thanks for the tip to [Jarda Hlavinka](https://x.com/neologyc/status/1771525268068356414).

> Please, if you want to achieve success, stop doing all those things you've heard from second, third, or any hand that supposedly "prove something to Google," and instead provide your visitors with a great, satisfying experience. That's how you show Google's evaluation systems that you deserve to succeed.
> **— [Danny Sullivan on Twitter](https://twitter.com/searchliaison/status/1770867218059800685)**

**🇺🇦 IT specialists were counted in Odesa.** According to a study among 7,000 Odesa IT workers, [95% of them donate an average of 10,000 hryvnias](https://www.digest.pro/news/v-odesi-porahuvali-ajtivciv-skilki-voni-donatyat/) (about 6,000 CZK) monthly. In Odesa, about 15,000 IT workers are employed in 132 development firms, which is about one-fifth of the total number in Ukraine. IT in Ukraine is among the largest exporters. For every IT worker, three newly created jobs are created in other fields. Over the past year, the number of IT workers in Ukraine increased by 22,000. Martin won't let up on [Spark Mail](https://sparkmailapp.com/) and Jakub on [Grammarly](https://www.grammarly.com/) from the days when it wasn't cool. And what [Ukrainian software](https://chat.openai.com/share/e30d3a5a-22b3-423d-a751-dad9d264e456) do you use? [Share your tips](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/13/comments) in the comments.

DIA is taking over the administration of CzechPoints, the Citizen Portal, basic registries, and in addition, it is starting to create its first own services. The most visible of these is [eDokladovka](https://edoklady.gov.cz/), a mobile app for digital ID cards, [other documents](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/display/EUDIGITALIDENTITYWALLET/What+is+the+Wallet), and [hopefully more in time](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/display/EUDIGITALIDENTITYWALLET/EU+Digital+Identity+Wallet+Home), launched in January.

For designers working on public administration projects, DIA is also important as a competence center that should set good practices, and among other things, it has taken over care of the [public administration design system](https://designsystem.gov.cz/).

DIA is creating its [research panel of respondents](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLScq8qEv3k_KPXFSqTyulXtfBtBTpUboWSAMO7DK00bC9DU60w/viewform). It probably wouldn't be entirely appropriate for you to sign up for it yourself. Just by reading this newsletter, you are likely more interested in the digitalization of public administration than the average citizen. But you surely have someone in your surroundings who could test the state's new digital services.

We look forward to seeing how DIA moves digitalization forward, and we are keeping our fingers crossed!

# What we're currently working on

Martin spent the whole Tuesday interviewing customers who came into the [Lentiamo optician store in central Prague](https://www.lentiamo.cz/optika-kremencova-praha.html). The e-shop, which you might still remember as [Vaše čočky](https://vase-cocky.cz/), is gradually opening brick-and-mortar stores and looking for a way to best connect offline and online customer transactions to more easily measure return on investment. Quantitative information is now being supplemented with qualitative data. In addition to answers to what and how much they want to obtain, they also gain insight into why and how customers behave. As always, when Martin works on a project related to health, he notices all the symptoms in himself. Right now, it looks like he needs new sunglasses!
