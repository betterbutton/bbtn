# Writebook – free tool for writing and publishing e-books
> 

[37Signals](https://37signals.com/) recently announced they've had enough of subscription-based software. They miss the old good programs with a one-time license fee. Recently they supported their shift to one-time payment for software with the project [Once](https://once.com/), under which they released the chat application [Campfire](https://once.com/campfire).

Their new venture [Writebook](https://once.com/writebook) goes even further. It is completely free. What you can create with Writebook you can see in their [collection of classics](https://classics.37signals.com/) or [in the examples](https://books.37signals.com/). Books can be publicly available or password-protected. The tool serves for writing and editing books, which can then be published as web pages. Texts are stored as [markdown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown) and are suitable for conversion to other formats from ePub to PDF. Writebook is a web application and is distributed as [docker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Docker_(software)), so while you won't pay a subscription for the software, you need a web server.

37Signals have rich experience [with writing and publishing books](https://37signals.com/books/). Those who remember surely have their twenty-year-old yellow book [Defensive design for the web](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/104752.Defensive_Design_for_the_Web) in their library.

However, it must be said that it was precisely 37Signals who broke through with subscriptions, for example with the tool for communication between agency and client and project management [Bascamp](https://basecamp.com/), and also their email and calendar [Hey](https://www.hey.com/) is only available with a subscription.
