# Book of 75 Tips on How to Request a Website
> 

**The first book for clients in Czech.** The book is for anyone who needs to **commission a design for a website, an application, or a digital product in general**. You will learn how to write a good brief, what the roles are in website creation, how to request a mobile app, a corporate intranet, or how to commission a website as a public contract. **Fun illustrations** make the text easier to understand.

![Two-page spread from the book with tip 67: Start with the smallest achievable goal.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a8c7316f-b439-4534-96e0-d4e1e136f01b_1228x574.jpeg "728x340")

Practical book for everyone who commissions a website

- How to write a good brief

- How to request a website

- How to oversee the supplier

## For whom and why did we write the book?

On the Czech market, there was previously no publication dealing with website creation **from the client's perspective**. So we compiled **a set of tips** on how to request a website and other digital products. We wrote the book for those of you who need **help now and here**. We wrote it **clearly and understandably**, so it can be easily read and the individual pieces of advice logically follow on from each other, but also so you can open it on any two-page spread and use it in practice.

The book is primarily for:

- **small and medium-sized entrepreneurs** (in the book, they are represented by a hairdresser)

- **large, corporate clients** (represented by a manager from a corporation)

- **civil servants and service workers** in public administration (in the book, they are represented by a public office clerk)

![Two-page spread from the book with tip 53: Start with a mobile version](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ec910def-dcc2-4c65-bb8b-9f2a31f6d197_1228x574.jpeg "1228x574")

## Includes a glossary of 100 industry terms

**Design has its own terminology**, which is good to understand. It's a mix of English and Czech terms from marketing, software development, and psychology. It may happen that you **talk to a contractor who** speaks Czech with you but **uses most of the keywords in English**. To make communication with the contractor simpler and more understandable, we have prepared a glossary of terms.
