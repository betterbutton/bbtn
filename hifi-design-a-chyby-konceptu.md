# Polished Designs Cover Up Conceptual Flaws
> 

More and more designers are pointing out the negative impact of preparing concepts in high-fidelity design tools. Erika Hall points out that advanced designs hide conceptual flaws:

> “Polished artifacts with a lot of detail indicate ‘done,’ hinder collaboration, and often draw attention to the wrong things. Rough, imprecise diagrams invite participation and, above all, criticism. Bad concepts need to be caught at the conceptual level.”\
> **— [Erika Hall](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7215149012159537152/), Linkedin**

Dylan Wilbanks reminds us that stakeholders can make important decisions based on designs, and if they perceive a design as already finished, the consequences can be confusing.

> “Time and time again, I’ve watched stakeholders or developers think the original design a designer presents is the final version. And that leads to confusion, arguments, and a lot of ‘but it looked finished!’.”\
> **— [Dylan Wilbanks](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7215145831035781120/), Linkedin**
