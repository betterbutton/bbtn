# What the Disabling of 3rd-Party Cookies in Chrome Brought to Advertising Campaign Numbers
> 

Seznam's product homepage Marek Vacek on Twitter summarized [the three most important results of the experiment](https://x.com/VacekMarek/status/1815664277052661920) with the cancellation of third-party cookies in Chrome:

- Scale Preservation: 86%

- ROI: 95%

- Remarketing Recovery: 49%

**Scale Preservation** shows how much the original scope of advertising campaigns is preserved. In this case, 86% of the original scope of advertising campaigns is preserved compared to Chrome with third-party cookie support.

**ROI (Return on Investment)** measures the return on investment in advertising campaigns. In the experiment, the return on investment reaches 95%. This means that every dollar spent on a campaign represents a five-cent loss.

**Remarketing Recovery** shows the effectiveness of remarketing campaigns. These target users who have previously encountered the brand. In this case, 49% successfully re-targeted campaign users.

We are just designers and campaign metrics are outside our field; if you would like to consult the data source directly, then [read on Google's website](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/15192137). More about how the data was obtained and measured can be found [in the help article](https://support.google.com/admanager/answer/15189422). Our humble opinion is that [the results don't look very good](https://privacysandbox.com/news/learnings-from-privacy-sandbox-testing/) even for Chrome without third-party cookies, for the [privacy sandbox](https://privacysandbox.com/intl/en_us/news/privacy-sandbox-update/), which was supposed to replace them.
