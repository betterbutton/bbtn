**The European Artificial Intelligence Act** has a clear Czech [official info page](https://digitalnicesko.gov.cz/ai-akt/). You can compare it with Tomáš Kapler's [one-month-old summary](https://www.kapler.cz/evropsky-parlament-schvalil-novy-zakon-o-umele-inteligenci/).

**Google will not end third-party cookie support in Chrome this year.** From the British [Privacy Sandbox report](https://www.gov.uk/cma-cases/investigation-into-googles-privacy-sandbox-browser-changes#quarterly-reports) for Q1 2024 and from [Google's statement](https://privacysandbox.com/intl/en_us/news/update-on-the-plan-for-phase-out-of-third-party-cookies-on-chrome/) from last week, it follows that, considering the comment periods of the British antitrust authority, we should not expect the end of third-party cookies this year. Once the antitrust authority gives the green light, a 60-day deadline will run, which the authority can double. Google originally announced the end of third-party cookies in 2020 and has been postponing the deadline since 2022.

**Large services must offer free access without targeted advertising.** According to the [European Data Protection Board](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/news/news/2024/edpb-consent-or-pay-models-should-offer-real-choice_en) (EDPB), service operators are abusing their position when they put users before the *consent or pay* decision. The consent-or-pay approach is common among content providers in German-speaking countries, but the EDPB was prompted to comment by Meta's approach at the end of last year due to its extensive impact. [David Slížek on Lupě](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/velke-online-sluzby-by-mely-nabidnout-obsah-zdarma-a-bez-cilene-reklamy-rikaji-evropsti-ochranci-soukromi/) covers the topic in more detail.

![Meme from the movie Toy Story. Buzz Lightyear enthusiastically points into space while a sad Woody stands next to him: Imagine a world without social networks](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/78d5e31d-04e6-4a10-ade7-77c3c630c8ac.avif "480x327")

**Kids prefer private sharing and limit public interaction on social networks.** The British communications regulator Ofcom released a study on [how children consume media](https://www.ofcom.org.uk/__data/assets/pdf_file/0021/283044/childrens-media-lives-2024-summary-report.pdf) (PDF, 6.7 MB). According to the study, children's attention is shifting to temporary presentation formats on the internet, such as TikTok and Snapchat, and to private conversations in messengers and games. Children tend not to verify information received from influencers. The study questions what effect the sensationalism and attention pressure of creators have on children's mental well-being and emphasizes the need to increase media literacy. Read the [study summary](https://blog.nic.cz/2024/04/25/kratke-vlny-deti-online-edice-2024/) at CZ.NIC.

**How to get a job in UX.** When twins do something, it's always interesting. After college, Yunan and Yutong Xue decided they wanted to get hired at AirBnB, so they did a [case study with an unsolicited redesign](https://www.yutongyunan.com/resource-writing/airbnb-china-case-study). It didn't get them a job at AirBnB, but it worked out well for them and they landed at Google and Meta.

**Responsive typography with variables in Figma.** Christine Vallaure has already made a nice line of tutorials for working with Figma, but [using variables for responsive typography](https://youtu.be/h_S4jQrNi00) looks like magic.

**Create your visual library.** If, like us, you're [sketching live at conferences](https://www.evalotta.net/visualstarterkit) and don't understand how people like Eva Lotta-Lamm do it, you'll enjoy [Krisztina Szerovay's workbook with a daily sketching challenge](https://uxknowledgebase.com/the-100-day-long-ux-visual-library-challenge-book-b8c99ca607be). Thanks to it, you'll be able to record lectures and meetings by sketching and either become a star or at least have nice notes.

**Don't trust SEO checklists.** [Pavel Ungr](https://www.pavelungr.cz/) took [dubious SEO checklists](https://www.linkedin.com/events/7180977599957921794/comments/) to task and in a twenty-minute talk, he tears them apart on [fidloprcičky](https://cestina20.cz/slovnik/fidloprcicky/). Did you know that...? But no, just listen to Pavel directly.

**A brand manual tool from Jirka Chlebus.** Jirka is a graphic designer and has put his know-how into the [Visualbook.pro](https://visualbook.pro/) tool, where you upload logos, fonts, colors, and usage examples, and deliver an interactive styleguide available on the web to the client. And what's best, you pay only once. No subscription.

# Life with Technology

*„Every now and then comes such a moment when I stop and realize: It's already here. This is it,“* says Martin to Jakub over a ristretto just brewed from a Nespresso capsule, *„This is the future!“* Do you also sometimes feel like you're living the [Smolík Family](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodina_Smol%C3%ADkova) series, but in reverse? When we look back at the time when we were little children, it's as if we're connecting with our ancestors from the distant past. But it's us.

The profession of a designer is associated with a certain dose of techno-optimism. We believe that all the problems humanity faces will be solved by technology, science, and progress. We will solve them!

> The future is already here — it's just not very evenly distributed.
> — **[William Gibson](https://quoteinvestigator.com/2012/01/24/future-has-arrived/)**

Just think about what we considered future fantasies as children. Did you believe you would one day live in it? Mobile phones gave us long-distance communication like in Star Trek. We replicate things around us on 3D printers like The Visitors. We talk to our cars and watches like Knight Rider. All the world's knowledge and information is a few clicks away or we ask a virtual assistant. We pay with electromagnetic waves. Hot water flows in public restrooms and there's no smoking in pubs!

But there are still places where the future hasn't arrived yet.
