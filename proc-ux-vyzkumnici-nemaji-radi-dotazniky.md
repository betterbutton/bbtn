Tina Ličková in the podcast [UX Research Geeks](https://www.uxtweak.com/podcast/zsuzsa-kovacs-surveys/) talks with [Zsuzsa Kovács](https://www.linkedin.com/in/zsuzsakovacs/), a UX researcher whom you might [remember from last year's WebExpo](https://slideslive.com/38984412/the-problems-in-your-head-how-cognitive-biases-affect-your-research?ref=speaker-107190). Zsuzsa shares her journey from programming to UX research and explains why it's important to carefully consider the use of surveys.

> „People are overwhelmed by surveys. When they respond, they often just want to get to the end as quickly as possible.“\
> **— Zsuzsa Kovács [00:10:29]**

She recommends methods like user testing and interviews, because surveys, according to her, often only confirm assumptions without discovering new insights. She also mentions problems with survey data analysis, which can be misleading. According to her, UX research is not only about gathering data but also about building empathy for real users.
