# Social media are responsible for algorithmic recommendations
> 

Until now in the US, it was held that platforms have broad immunity against lawsuits concerning [user-generated content](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User-generated_content). Immunity is guaranteed by the [Communications Decency Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communications_Decency_Act) (CDA) of 1996, specifically [Section 230](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Section_230).

In the United States, a legal dispute is currently underway between TikTok (ByteDance) and the parents of a girl who died during the [Blackout Challenge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackout_challenge). The court acknowledges that TikTok is not responsible for user-generated content, but [acknowledged the argument](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca3/22-3061/22-3061-2024-08-27.html) that the content reached the girl via the recommendation algorithm, for which the platform is responsible. The dispute [Anderson v. TikTok Inc, No. 22-3061 (3d Cir. 2024)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anderson_v._TikTok) is thus back under consideration.
