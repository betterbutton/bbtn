# Would you pay for search? 
> 

In May, we [wrote about the URL parameter](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/21?utm_source=publication-search#:~:text=Parametr%20udm%3D14%20v%20URL%20zbav%C3%AD%20v%C3%BDsledky%20hled%C3%A1n%C3%AD%20na%20Googlu%20reklamy.) `udm=14`, which strips Google not only of all its own search result enhancements but also of ads. The search results are then based purely on full-text website crawling. The contrast against search with paid results, comparators, products, Wikipedia, and images is stunning. But is it sustainable for Google's business?

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/681c8a78-8c03-48b6-93cc-722f512c129d_1571x796.png "1456x738")

[Paid Kagi search engine](https://www.kagi.com/)

[Kagi search engine](https://www.kagi.com/) goes straight to the point, asking users for money right away. It offers results that are not influenced by advertisers' interests. And really, why not? If we've already gotten used to paying for AI chat, we can just add another twenty dollars for a family search subscription to the family budget alongside cloud storage, TVs, and music.

Or not?
