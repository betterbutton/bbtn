# AI Relume Creates a Sitemap and Wireframe
> 

![Relume interface with sitemap](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/7b506fb4-b7fa-45d6-b8f3-d87cddf7259f_1920x954.png "1920x954")

Relume is another addition to the collection of AI tools for web design. You create the brief using a text prompt, and [Relume generates a sitemap](https://relume.io/) with a content priority guide that you can edit. When you feel you're done with the rough content structure design, you switch to the wireframe. Relume designs the wireframe directly in desktop, tablet, and mobile variants.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/c1291d85-93d3-4424-9a52-8b6abc607081_1920x956.png "1920x956")

As seen from the examples, Relume can design Czech content, and you can specify how extensive a site you want to design. If you're not satisfied with the result, you can edit it, have it regenerated, or change the prompt.

The final design can be exported to Figma for visual design refinement or directly to WebFlow, where you can apply a template's look and publish it. The site structure can be exported as a table. You can also share the design itself, which others can edit or comment on.

The question is whether Relume is a tool more for designers or for their clients. We can imagine both possibilities. With real-time collaboration, Relume could also be an excellent co-creation tool.

The tool comes with a monthly subscription in the range of a few dozen dollars, but if it saves you at least an hour or two, it pays for itself.
