# European Commission has launched an official investigation into Temu
> 

The European Commission has launched [an official investigation into the Chinese marketplace Temu](https://x.com/EU_Commission/status/1851946069803884749) over possible violations of the Digital Services Act (DSA), [writes Lupa](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/evropska-komise-vysetruje-temu-vadi-ji-prodej-nevyhovujiciho-zbozi-ci-riziko-zavislosti/). The Commission's [investigation focuses on four main areas](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_5622): preventing the reappearance of non-compliant products, limiting addictive elements like time-limited offers, transparency in product recommendations, and making data available to researchers.

The investigation does not yet mean that Temu has breached the rules, but it now has the opportunity to respond to the Commission's objections. Alongside Temu, the EC is dealing with similar cases with other platforms including TikTok and X, as well as companies Google, Meta, and Amazon.
