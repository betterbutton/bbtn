# How UCD and GDD differ in product development
> 

[User-Centered Design](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/user-centered-design#:~:text=User%2Dcentered%20design%20(UCD),and%20accessible%20products%20for%20them.) (UCD) and [Goal-Driven Design](https://medium.com/purple-rock-scissors/goal-driven-design-in-3-steps-58fd15a25aba) (GDD) are [two approaches to product development](https://medium.com/@mhmdhilman710/pembahasan-tentang-ucd-user-centered-design-vs-gdd-goal-directed-design-a8170a2a3a46). Both focus on optimizing user experience. UCD emphasizes user needs and preferences and places them at the center of the entire process. In contrast, GDD focuses on achieving specific goals, whether user or business-related, with an emphasis on efficiency.

While UCD promotes a pleasant experience, GDD ensures that the product fulfills clearly defined goals. Combining both approaches can lead to products that are user-friendly and simultaneously successful from a business perspective.
