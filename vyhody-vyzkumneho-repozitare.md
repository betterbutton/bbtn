# Benefits of Using a Research Repository
> 

Research repositories serve as a central place for storing research data and outputs, facilitating access to information and preventing duplicate work. Benefits include quickly finding insights, tracking long-term trends, and sharing results across teams.

Repository management typically falls under [ResearchOps](https://researchops.community/about/), and its successful implementation can increase an organization's UX maturity. Repositories can contain reports, insights, study materials, recordings, transcripts, and other data. Choosing the right tool and structure is key for easy use and contribution to the repository.

Maria Rosala dedicates an article to repositories, [Research Repositories 101](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/research-repositories/). She compares document repositories with insight repositories, describes what a repository should contain and what functions it should fulfill, and offers a way to build a repository and ensure its adoption in a company.
