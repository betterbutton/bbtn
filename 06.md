# Ask About the Web #06
> Hello! Google is deleting cache. The EU is leveling the playing field in the digital environment. Figma has a keyboard for designers. Petra Kutálková cannot be discouraged. And what about us?

# Linkodrome

**We're about to lose the cache.** Back in the day, before the web ran in the cloud on scalable infrastructure, it used to happen that some website was overloaded or simply crashed. Then, all you had to do was search for the page on Google, click the dropdown arrow next to the result, and view the page from the cache. [It's no longer there.](https://www.seroundtable.com/google-search-drops-cache-link-from-search-results-36787.html) You can still copy the URL from the address bar and [search for it on Google using the](https://www.seo.fr/en/blog/bookmarklet-seo#:~:text=browser%20address%20bar.-,3.CACHE%20GOOGLE,-javascript%3Alocation.href) `cache:` operator, but Danny Sullivan from Google has indicated that even [the operator will soon disappear](https://twitter.com/searchliaison/status/1753156161509916873).

**The EU is tightening the screws on cookie banners.** We'll stay with Google for a bit. A little further down, you'll read about the European Digital Markets Act; related to this, Google is not allowed to [combine data from its individual services](https://www.google.com/about/company/user-consent-policy/) without user consent. If you use Google services on your website, [implement Consent Mode from March 6th.](https://support.google.com/tagmanager/answer/13695607?hl=cs). Lawyer Petra Dolejšová delves into this in detail in the article [The Cookies Saga Continues, Welcome 2024](https://www.petradolejsova.cz/blog/cookies-saga-pokracuje-vitej-2024). If you want to clarify the issue, sign up for the [free webinar on consent banners](https://www.dataworkshop.cz/), hosted by Petra Dolejšová on February 26th.

**Maybe better not to track.** We can't advise you to stop measuring and personalizing websites, but if you're still looking for an alternative for greater user privacy, there are already several tools that will free you from the worries of consent mode. For example, the Slovenian [alternative to Google Fonts](https://fonts.bunny.net/) or the Czech [NoLog](https://nolog.cz/services/).

**A keyboard for Figma.** Last week we wrote about how [Jakub uses an Elgato Stream Deck](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/i/141062941/zivot-s-technologiemi). There are more specialized keyboards. Figma offers one such keyboard to designers. The [Figma Creator Micro](https://store-eu.figma.com/) keyboard will cost $139 and, in addition to twelve keys, offers two rotary knobs. You can switch the keyboard between four modes, giving a total of 48 shortcuts and 8 rotary buttons.

**Don't let yourself be discouraged.** Petra Kutálková, as a UX researcher, focuses on working with vulnerable groups. She emphasizes the need for systemic changes and the importance of a trauma-informed approach in design and research. The [interview for Design KISK](https://medium.com/design-kisk/petra-kut%C3%A1lkov%C3%A1-ka%C5%BEd%C3%BD-z-n%C3%A1s-m%C5%AF%C5%BEe-n%C4%9Bco-zm%C4%9Bnit-kdy%C5%BE-se-nenech%C3%A1me-odradit-71ddc6cbe373) presents her views on the role of design in the non-profit and commercial sectors, emphasizing ethics and user-oriented approaches in design.

**Want them?** American IT companies have been laying off employees in bulk for a year now. Here, we occasionally see [news about Productboard](https://archiv.hn.cz/c1-67289370-cesky-jednorozec-se-zmensil-temer-o-polovinu-productboard-potichu-propustil-dalsi-desitky-lidi), Avast, or [Kiwi](https://www.novinky.cz/clanek/ekonomika-kiwi-com-propusti-pres-200-zamestnancu-40459036). HubSpot chose an interesting approach. In addition to stating the number of layoffs, [it also publishes their names and contact information](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1AzNRk1EflTBUtFI1MLqle9br27Oc0U5s7ioOhTpeKbM/edit#gid=75186931) with links to portfolios and other details. They let go of nine researchers. Nine! Most Czech IT companies are happy to have at least one.

**~~Bard~~Gemini still isn't worth it.** The old journalistic rule says that when there's a probing question in the article's headline, the answer is always "no." The article [New Google Gemini Advanced with the Ultra Model – Is It Worth It?](https://www.kapler.cz/google-gemini-advanced-ultra/), written by , we've read for you and the result is: No. [Wired thinks the same](https://www.wired.cz/clanky/google-spousti-pokrocilou-ai-gemini-stale-ale-nechape-jak-se-susi-pradlo) and [Michal Illich summed it up in one sentence on Twitter](https://twitter.com/michalillich/status/1755590845653565726) as well. So, wait a bit longer.

# What We're Currently Working On

At the Švejda Goldmann agency, we work with the [OOUX — object-oriented user experience design](https://www.ooux.com/) methodology. The object-oriented approach to UX has been with us for almost ten years. [It builds on foundations](https://alistapart.com/article/ooux-a-foundation-for-interaction-design/) laid by [Sophia Voychehovski Praterova](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sophiav/) and is practically complemented by the [methodological ORCA framework](https://www.ooux.com/resources/introducing-orca-the-third-diamond-in-your-ux-process) (Objects, Relations, Call to actions, Attributes). It's suitable for complex content websites. It's no coincidence that OOUX emerged during the creation of the website for CNN.

OOUX is a counterpoint to task-oriented UX, which works well in interactive applications or administrative information systems. [Resources on OOUX in Czech](https://digichef.cz/objektove-orientovane-ux-ooux) are almost non-existent, so we decided to describe the object-oriented approach in a longer article. But that's for another time.
