# Canva is targeting professional designers. Is it good enough yet?
> 

Professional designers often look down on Canva as a tool for teachers and social media managers. That is changing quickly. Since its founding in 2013 as an Australian woman-led company, it has become popular among amateurs, entrepreneurs, and children thanks to its extensive template library.

> "Canva isn't trying to compete, but to redefine."
> **— Johannes Ippen, [Is Canva Getting Good?](https://johannesippen.com/2024/canva-goes-professional/)**

New features announced in May 2024, however, target professional designers. Canva now allows collaborative creation of banners, social media, print documents, and video animations. With the [acquisition of Affinity](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/13?utm_source=publication-search#:~:text=Canva%20koupila%20Serif.), Canva shows it means business. It targets marketing, sales, and HR who need design tools for presentations and brochures.

Designer Johannes Ippen thinks [Canva could change workflows](https://johannesippen.com/2024/canva-goes-professional/) and become a popular tool, especially due to its simplicity and accessibility.
