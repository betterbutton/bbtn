# Dark patterns vs. ethical UX
> 

> By prioritizing transparency and respect for users, companies can build lasting relationships and strong customer loyalty.\
> — **[Manalee](https://medium.com/@manaliborgaonkar7/dark-patterns-vs-ethical-ux-building-trust-through-design-27aa068ec6d1)**

Manalee focuses on [the difference between dark patterns and ethical UX design](https://medium.com/@manaliborgaonkar7/dark-patterns-vs-ethical-ux-building-trust-through-design-27aa068ec6d1). Dark patterns are manipulative tactics that force users into unwanted decisions, which short-term increases profits but damages trust. Examples include hidden fees or hard-to-cancel services, like with Amazon Prime.

How to approach ethical design:

1.  Be transparent

2.  Respect user autonomy

3.  Focus on long-term trust

On the other hand, ethical UX, as shown by Zomato and Swiggy, is characterized by transparency, user autonomy, and long-term relationship building. The key to successful UX, according to the article, is prioritizing trust over manipulation, which leads to lasting customer loyalty.
