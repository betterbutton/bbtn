# Samsonov: Stop Worsening Products
> 

User stories, as formalized user stories commonly used in agile development, often slide into focusing on tasks instead of user value, says Pavel Samsonov in the article [As a user, I don’t want to](https://uxdesign.cc/as-a-user-i-dont-want-to-973990a30158).

The *“As a [user role] I want [to perform a task]”* format leads product teams to concentrate their efforts on features and functions, increasing costs for users without providing real benefits.

The German word *verschlimmbessern* means to make something worse by trying to improve it – as [says Honza Andersson](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7236637895959195649?commentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28activity%3A7236637895959195649%2C7237791292091498496%29&dashCommentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afsd_comment%3A%287237791292091498496%2Curn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7236637895959195649%29): to worsen, and it captures the approach where adding new features to a product disrupts its overall usability.

Product creators often forget the importance of the overall user journey and a successful outcome when they focus on individual features. Before we can focus on the whole, the discussion mainly centers on [adding new features](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feature_creep), which often leads to the product being worse and more complicated for users.

The solution is simple: invert the user stories and focus on removing costs, not on optimizing tasks the user has to perform. If you understood the previous sentence like a goat understands parsley, you’d better [read Pavel’s article](https://uxdesign.cc/as-a-user-i-dont-want-to-973990a30158).
