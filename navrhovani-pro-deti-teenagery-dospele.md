# Designing for children, teenagers, adults, and seniors
> 

Designing for different age groups requires understanding their unique needs and interactions with technology. Norman Nielsen Group on LinkedIn offered [a list of resources for designers](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7242183338029981696/) who want to focus on designing for specific age groups.

- Children (3-12 years) need simple layouts and intuitive controls.

- Teenagers (13-17 years) prefer visually attractive and fast websites, but may struggle with complex interfaces.

- Young adults (18-25 years) are confident in using technology but do not tolerate poor user experience.

- Seniors (65+) require accessible design that minimizes cognitive load.

- The key to inclusive design is respecting these age differences.
