# Figma celebrates twelfth anniversary
> 

Although [Wikipedia says](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Figma) that Figma was launched on September 27, 2016 and Figma's website [mentions the first version](https://www.figma.com/about/) in 2015, [Dylan Field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dylan_Field) and [Evan Wallace](https://www.madebyevan.com/) founded it as early as August 13, 2012. Dylan was twenty years old at the time. On Twitter, [he recalls what he was doing](https://x.com/zoink/status/1823411804682453289) when he was twelve, like Figma is now.

Figma conquered the web design tools market four years after its launch with the onset of lockdowns during the coronavirus pandemic. Its competitors [Sketch](https://www.sketch.com/) and [Adobe XD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adobe_XD) could not withstand the sudden shift to remote collaboration. Figma's success inspired the creation of tools that try to imitate it, whether they are commercial [Pixso](https://pixso.net/), [Mockitt](https://mockitt.wondershare.com/home.html), or [Lunacy](https://icons8.com/lunacy), or open-source [Penpot](https://penpot.app/).

Figma is now the market leader that defines how websites and applications are designed.
