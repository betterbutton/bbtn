# Better Button #03
> Hello! It's time to prepare for the European Accessibility Act. ChatGPT will write image alt texts for you. But you have to step on the scale yourself. How does it look a month after Christmas? Is your website faster than you are?

# Linkodrome

**What is beautiful is also usable.** Scientists repeatedly prove that [aesthetic quality correlates with perceived usability](https://www.hindawi.com/journals/ahci/2014/946239/). But how to design beautiful websites and applications? You don't need exceptional talent, just follow [the rules for designing pretty interfaces](https://uxdesign.cc/58-rules-for-stunning-and-effective-user-interface-design-ea4b93f931f6).

**Website loading speed matters!** What you track tends to magically improve on its own. Martin Michálek and colleagues from the [Page Speed project launched monitors for web page loading speed](https://www.pagespeed.cz/blog/tester-hlidac-notifikace), so you can get alerts whenever your website gets congested and slows down.

![Meme with a goose where a man says: Indeed, frontend is more complicated than backend. The goose chases the man around, shouting: Who made it so complicated? Huh? Who?!](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/fa13d5ff-e285-4cf1-956e-98b0968b4bc6_519x480.png "519x480")

**Do you describe images on your website?** Writing alternative texts for images is part of good etiquette for accessibility and easier findability. At the same time, it's quite a demanding discipline. You can, for example, [get inspired by recommendations](https://design102.blog.gov.uk/2022/01/14/whats-the-alternative-how-to-write-good-alt-text/) from the British government's design studio Design102. Vašek Vančura prepared a GPT agent called [Image ALT Describer](https://chat.openai.com/g/g-045n12ghj-image-alt-describer) for writing alternative texts.

![Twitter meme with black text, "click here" on a white background. A black arrow points to the bottom left corner where there is a button to display an alternative image description with a hidden message up to 1000 characters long.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d45a763b-ff6f-48dc-b3da-33c1cdfadca8_976x549.jpeg "976x549")

**TIP💡 Hidden messages on social networks.** While we're on image alt texts: Have you noticed how much [ALT is pushing social networks](https://later.com/blog/alt-text/) like Twitter, Facebook, or LinkedIn these days? Not only is it good for accessibility and SEO, but post authors use them to bypass text length limits. For example, on Twitter without a paid account, you can only write posts up to 280 characters, but an image alt can fit another thousand. And it pays off! Unless you don't mind that probably no one will see it. Only poor blind people then have to read everything.

**AI in MS Office for everyone and ChatGPT for companies and teams.** Microsoft already offers its Copilot in all business MS365 plans. The advantage of Copilot is that it directly helps with Office documents alongside chat and image generation, and it doesn't train its model on your data. ChatGPT for teams has a higher quota for prompts and also doesn't train on your data. [Tomáš Kapler writes excellently about AI.](https://www.kapler.cz/microsoft-copilot-pro-office-365/)

At CES2024, [Withings introduced](https://twitter.com/markgadala/status/1746704384900133001) a device that measures body temperature, oxygen saturation, and works as an ECG or stethoscope – [they call it BeamO](https://www.withings.com/cz/en/beam-o). It's a bit of a weird gadget, but still less obscure than the [home laboratory U-Scan](https://www.withings.com/cz/en/u-scan), which you're supposed to place in the toilet bowl.

In the scientific journal Nature, [an article was published in November](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-023-00958-w) claiming that AI built on ChatGPT can [answer medical questions indistinguishably from real doctors](https://www.forbes.com/sites/williamhaseltine/2023/12/15/medical-text-written-by-artificial-intelligence-outperforms-doctors/).

Martin likes such things. He participated in his lab in testing a [therapy device](https://mindpax.me/) for bipolar disorder and recently worked with doctors from the Olomouc university hospital [on a telemedicine project](https://ntmc.fnol.cz/) for chronic patients. Jakub, however, is skeptical about similar things. Maybe also because he spent a semester studying medicine. And what do you think about it?

# What we're currently working on

We are preparing with clients for Saturday, June 28, 2025. That is the deadline when the [law on accessibility requirements for certain products and services](https://odok.cz/portal/veklep/material/KORNC9XGVCVG/) – perhaps known to you as the [European Accessibility Act](https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=1202) – is to take effect. It will cover a wide range of hardware and software products and services, including mobile apps and websites, but will also include various self-service kiosks or vending machines.

It might seem like there's plenty of time, but knowing how project budget allocation works, how long decision-making, design, and development take, a year and a half is just about enough. People who consistently deal with accessibility are starting to have full calendars slowly, so don't procrastinate.
