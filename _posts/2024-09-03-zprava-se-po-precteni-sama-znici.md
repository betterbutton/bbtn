# Message self-destructs after reading…
> 

It's not a good idea to send confidential information unencrypted through third-party means, but when it's not sensitive, services that allow you to send a message and then automatically destroy it can be handy helpers.

There are a bunch of self-destructive services. You can use them to [send files](https://file.io/), [bits of text](https://1ty.me/), or conversely, you can one-time [receive SMS](https://anonymsms.com/number/4915215304893/) or [email](https://mailinator.com). You can also [chat](https://temp.chat/) or [organize a video conference](https://talk.nolog.cz/cs/).

You never know if the intermediary really doesn't have access to your data and doesn't store it, but with careful use, [these services](https://pinboard.in/u:garcon/t:disposable) can be useful, for fun, or when you need to exchange information with someone once and don't want to set up user accounts.
