# Lookback.com: platform for remote user research
> Loopback combines live observation, asynchronous testing, and team collaboration into one smooth process.

![Initial screen of the Loopback.io service.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/9f4bddda-b64d-4931-b313-61d0807e1639_1896x1061.png "1456x815")

[Loopback](https://www.Lookback.com) allows observers to watch and comment on a facilitator's meeting with a respondent.

[Lookback](https://www.Lookback.com) is a research platform that helps product teams observe how real people use their digital products—live and asynchronously—and gain actionable insights. It's designed to remove barriers to testing and research, making it a natural part of the development process.

The platform's biggest advantage is the ability to conduct moderated testing in real time. A researcher leads an interview with a participant while other team members watch and communicate silently among themselves. This allows for deeper questioning without disrupting the test flow.

For unmoderated tests, the system records the screen, facial expressions, and participant comments. Everything is saved and timestamped. The team can then return anytime, tag key moments, and share findings without losing context.

The platform also includes meeting scheduling, consent collection, and secure recording storage, helping maintain compliance and keep research running smoothly even remotely. There's also a searchable archive of past sessions that helps uncover behavioral patterns and improve designs over time.

For researchers, designers, and developers, Lookback.com is a tool that turns user feedback into a continuous dialogue. It helps bridge the gap between what a designer intended and how something actually works for people.
