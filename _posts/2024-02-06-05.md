# Better Button #05
> Hi! A lot is in store for web creators! We can now use the Avif format for images. Pages must respond to clicks within 200 ms. Newsletters must allow one-click unsubscribe. NIS2 will start applying in the EU.

Last time we wrote about [what will soon end](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/i/140920126/linkodrom). Let's see what will soon begin.

# Linkodrome

**Edge finally supports AVIF!** [Avif is a more efficient image format](https://convertio.co/jpg-avif/) than GIF, PNG, JPEG, and even WebP. [It is now safe to use.](https://caniuse.com/avif)

**Google wants pages to respond quickly.** Google offers web frontend developers [Core Web Vitals](https://web.dev/articles/vitals), key metrics for user experience quality. From March, the key metric becomes [Interaction to Next Paint (INP)](https://web.dev/articles/inp). It measures how much time 🏃 elapses before the user interface changes after a user action. Simply: before something happens after a click. ⏱️ [The acceptable threshold is 200 ms.](https://www.pagespeed.cz/blog/metrika-inp#Doporucene-hodnoty:~:text=Doporu%C4%8Den%C3%A1%20hodnota%20pro%C2%A0spln%C4%9Bn%C3%AD%20metriky%20je%C2%A0200%20ms.) When Google cares about something, it pays to pay attention.

**One-click newsletter unsubscribe.** And there's Google again! As [Michal Špaček warned us](https://x.com/spazef0rze/status/1748397506445111584), from June Gmail will require [one-click unsubscribe](https://apps.google.com/supportwidget/articlehome?article_url=https%3A%2F%2Fsupport.google.com%2Fa%2Fanswer%2F14229414&assistant_id=generic-unu&product_context=14229414&product_name=UnuFlow&trigger_context=a) for bulk emails 📩. This is served by the [List-Unsubscribe header according to RFC 8058](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc8058.txt). When that header is in the email, [even Seznam users will appreciate it.](https://fbl.seznam.cz/unsubscribe)

**Europe will be more cyber-secure.** Companies from a wide range of industries and, of course, public administration fall under the [Network and Information Systems Security (NIS2) directive](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/CS/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32022L2555&qid=1707001635271). [New obligations](https://osveta.nukib.cz/course/view.php?id=184) include risk analysis, implementation of a security policy, data protection, IT infrastructure security, and employee training. ☝️ NIS2 also emphasizes supply chain security and data localization. It will start applying in September.

**Learn to generate images with Midjourney.**  is already hosting a webinar 🎨 [Midjourney for Beginners](https://www.online21.cz/webinar-midjourney/) this Wednesday, February 7th, from 8 PM. If you've been respecting Midjourney from afar so far, this is your chance to catch up in time. [Tips for other events](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/p/konference-2024) can be found on our website.

**Generating images with the same character.** It's quite hard to get AI to generate images in the same style and [with the same characters](https://twitter.com/emmanuel_2m/status/1738358988193092085?s=46&t=4t_cDZLlZNY0xmndnQo4EA) or environment. It seems there is finally a good solution for that. In the Scenario service, you can [train a character](https://help.scenario.com/training-a-character-lora).

**AI will create a logo for you.** Do you constantly need some logo or icon for your projects that don't deserve the touch of a living designer? Then [LogoDiffusion](https://logodiffusion.com/) will come in handy.

**A sidewalk for virtual reality.** While we're on entertainment: Disney has created a surface that allows people to [walk in virtual reality](https://techcrunch.com/2024/01/22/disney-holotile/), while essentially not going anywhere. That could also be useful. Just, how to get it home...

# Living with Technologies

Jakub has an [Elgato Stream Deck](https://www.elgato.com/us/en/s/welcome-to-stream-deck) on his desk at work. It's an auxiliary keyboard with programmable buttons with a backlit LED display. It comes in various sizes and Jakub already has his second Stream Deck. It's an ideal helper for boosting productivity. The buttons can function similarly to function keys on your laptop (volume control and the like), but you can also load a complex macro and trigger it with a single key press.

![The El Gato Stream Deck keyboard with 15 programmable buttons.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/60e32b53-f2fa-434d-b159-4831cfe2c69f_800x584.webp "514x375")

It's a bit reminiscent of the hardware era of [Shortcuts in iOS and macOS](https://support.apple.com/cs-cz/guide/shortcuts/welcome/ios), and it can even work with them. When you hit the limit of button count, you can change some of them into page toggles. Each button has its own LCD display, so you'll immediately see how your menu has changed.

The Stream Deck has become Jakub's personal command center, from which he controls his work world.

# What We're Currently Working On

Martin has kicked off a new project. He spends time conducting stakeholder interviews. When people need to talk about something complex, we ask them to explain it to us like 🐶 [to our dog](https://noisydecentgraphics.typepad.com/design/2013/10/speak-as-you-would-a-young-child-or-a-golden-retriever.html) or like 👼 [a small child](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/explain-like-you-would-small-child-andrew-macphee-cp-apmp/). The point of this request is for people not to delve unnecessarily into complex details, not to use professional jargon and corporate abbreviations.

*"I actually stole it from Kuba,"* Martin recalls, *"when someone speaks too complexly, Kuba stops them and says: Explain it to me like my grandmother."* It works perfectly with respondents from the street, but in stakeholder interviews it often has the opposite effect. ❓ *"How small a child is that? What does it already understand?"* they ask, and then they start speaking in metaphors, which doesn't help understanding much. What else to expect from the Přemyslid nation 🧠 than [overthinking](https://www.centrumlocika.cz/novinky/fenomen-s-nazvem-overthinking).

*"Imagine that child is 44 years old,"* Martin says at such moments.
