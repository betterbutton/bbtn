# Dot voting
> A simple and understandable method that helps groups quickly determine what matters most.

Dot voting ([dot voting](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/dot-voting/)) is a quick and *fair* way for a team to decide which ideas make the most sense. Everyone gets a limited number of dots – stickers or digital tokens – and can assign them to the proposals they consider most important, most interesting, or most realistic. The result is immediately visible: where there are the most dots, that's where the ideas the team considers key are.

You can apply this method during design workshops, agile planning, or team retrospectives. It helps quickly select what's essential and move forward without lengthy debates. Everyone participates, everyone has equal voting weight – and the team can immediately start working on what's truly important.

However, it's important to mention what this method *cannot* handle. It works well only when everyone understands what the individual proposals are about and when they are clearly formulated. Otherwise, misunderstandings or oversimplification may occur. And bias can still arise here – if someone votes first or persuades others, they can influence the results.

Even so, dot voting remains a useful tool. It doesn't decide for you, but it will help you narrow down choices and quickly find agreement. It's suitable wherever there's little time, many ideas, and a desire to move forward.
