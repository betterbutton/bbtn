# Content is King. Context is Queen.
> In the digital world, information reigns—but only when delivered at the right time, in the right place, and in the right way.

> *You are opening the first window of the BBtn Advent Calendar for 2025. In this year's advent series, we'll look at popular sayings of UX designers. Today's is: Content is King.*

In the 1990s, *Bill Gates* wrote a famous essay with the phrase *[Content is King](https://medium.com/@HeathEvans/content-is-king-essay-by-bill-gates-1996-df74552f80d9)*. He predicted that the value of the internet would come from information and ideas. He was right—but today, in an era of web saturation, success is determined by something else: context.

Context determines whether content reaches the user at all and whether it makes sense to them. A perfect article or video loses value if it loads slowly, doesn't display correctly on mobile, or is inaccessible to people with disabilities. Search engines like Google also evaluate this technical side—page speed, response time, and stability. Without it, even the best content remains invisible.

Context is the foundation of the entire environment. Proper data markup, responsive layout, and clear navigation help both people and algorithms understand the meaning of the text.

From a user's perspective, it's a simple rule: people don't read all the content on a website; they scan most of it. Eyes follow the page according to a pattern derived from visual hierarchy. Therefore, text must be structured, concise, and read aloud fluently and naturally.

Good design adapts to the situation—it speaks differently to someone in the sun than to someone reading on a mobile at night.

And artificial intelligence is pushing context even further—it doesn't just understand words, but also the user's intent and circumstances. Success on the web today depends on the harmony of two rulers: content and context.
