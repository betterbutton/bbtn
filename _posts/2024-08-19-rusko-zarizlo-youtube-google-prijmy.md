# Russia has cut off YouTube, Google AdSense revenues
> 

Russia continues in [cutting off its users from internet access](https://www.rferl.org/a/youtube-video-blocked-russia/33070407.html). YouTube has also appeared among the sites that are no longer easily accessible. In response to the continuing increase in tensions, Google announced the immediate [termination of all Russian AdSense accounts](https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/news/2024/08/12/7470047/). AdSense is a Google service that allows website operators to earn [money from advertising space](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_AdSense). The decision will severely impact local content creators, including popular YouTubers.

In 2022, Google stopped monetizing state-funded media and later blocked Russian ads globally. The last payouts to Russian creators will occur in August 2024. This step also comes in response to a new Russian law banning advertising on websites of foreign agents, which likely forced Google to implement further restrictions.

In Russia, the use of VPNs, proxy servers, and Tor has been illegal since 2017. Last month, moreover, on the censor's command, [Apple removed VPN applications](https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2024/07/04/russia-forces-apple-to-remove-vpns-from-app-store-a85608) from the App Store.
