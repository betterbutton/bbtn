# Onboarding
> Onboarding – a design process that helps users understand and experience the product's value by guiding them through their first interactions.

[Onboarding](https://whatfix.com/blog/onboarding-ux/) in digital design helps users quickly understand what a product is for and how to use it. Well-designed onboarding reduces friction, increases engagement, and helps people achieve their first success. It's not just about the first impression, but about making the user realize early on what the product brings them.

There are several approaches. Sometimes it's a simple step-by-step guide. Other times, help appears only when the person needs it. Some products introduce new features gradually. Others leave users to discover everything on their own, but support them with smart design along the way.

The purpose of the onboarding process is to answer three fundamental questions: What does it do? What should I do next? And why is this important to me? If people don't get clear answers to these questions, they'll stop trying and leave the product.

Good onboarding guides but doesn't restrict. It gives them space. It doesn't impose itself. And above all, it constantly evolves. What worked yesterday might not work today.

Onboarding works best when people don't even notice it. They simply understand the product and start using it. And that's exactly what it's all about.
