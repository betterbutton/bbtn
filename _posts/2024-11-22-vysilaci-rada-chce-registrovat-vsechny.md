# Broadcasting Council Wants to Register Audiovisual Creators with a Trade License
> 

[Uproar](https://x.com/PetrosKecyaPoli/status/1859876090334019852) among online video and podcast creators was caused by a [press release](https://rrtv.gov.cz/cz/files/press/TZ_Vlogge%c5%99i_24.pdf), issued yesterday by the Council for Radio and Television Broadcasting. The regulatory authority reminds audiovisual creators that, according to [§ 5 of the Act on Audiovisual Media Services on Demand](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2010-132#p5), they are subject to a notification obligation.

The press release itself does not help creators much in understanding their obligations. It uses terms like vlogger, which haven't been used for twenty years, and refers to [provisions of the law](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2010-132/zneni-20220915#p2) that, however, [define obligated entities by recursion](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Definice_kruhem).

RRTV apparently has no idea who it will be regulating, because instead of a digital service, which one might expect for a newly emerging obligation targeting [digital natives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_native), it presents creators with a [PDF form](https://rrtv.gov.cz/formulare/AVMSNV.pdf) that even requires data the state already possesses in central registers, since the obligated entities here are those already previously registered for a trade. The language of the document also reflects this, where you learn, for example, that you must fill in the *Jurisdictional Criterion within the meaning of § 3(2) to (4)*. RRTV also apparently has no idea how many entities would be affected by the announced regulation.

Critics from among audiovisual creators emphasize that referencing [the European directive on audiovisual media services](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/CS/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:32010L0013) does not mean that the notification obligation stems from the provisions of that directive. In other European countries, only entities above a certain turnover are subject to registration. However, the state already has this information from tax returns and from data it requires from electronic marketplaces like YouTube. The state is thus transferring its obligations arising from the European directive onto citizens and other taxpayers.

[Further criticisms](https://x.com/bartkovskym/thread/1859891531437723833) point out that RRTV's press release does not distinguish between broadcasting and making recordings available. Making recordings available is governed by a twelve-year newer [Act on Video-Sharing Platform Services](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2022-242). After all, the 2010 Act could not possibly have anticipated the current situation, because, for example, YouTube only became mainstream after 2014, when the service was led by [Susan Wojcicki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_Wojcicki).

However, the whole affair will probably soon fade away into the eternal hunting grounds, as [Minister of Culture Martin Baxa](https://x.com/MartinBaxa2/status/1859968249833673172) has now intervened in the situation.
