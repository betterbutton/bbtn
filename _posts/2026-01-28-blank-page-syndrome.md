# Blank Page Syndrome
> Repeated failure to start work, in which excessive freedom, premature evaluation, and perceived importance prevent the first meaningful step.

[Blank Page Syndrome](https://www.renenekuda.cz/prazdna-stranka/) ([blank page syndrome](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/rethinking-mental-health/202104/5-ways-overcome-blank-page-syndrome)) refers to the moment when work should begin but doesn't. The document is open, the canvas blank, the system ready—and yet nothing happens. It's not a lack of ideas or ability. It's a structural failure at the very beginning of creative or analytical work.

The essence of the syndrome is a situation where creation and evaluation merge into one moment. Instead of exploring possibilities, judgment of outcomes arrives. The first sentence, layout, or code change is understood as a definitive decision bearing responsibility for the entire result. In such a situation, hesitation is logical. To begin equals to risk making a mistake.

Another source of the problem is the lack of constraints. Web work offers an almost unlimited number of choices. Layout, components, tone of communication, and technology. Psychological research, however, shows that too many options actually weaken motivation. Every decision excludes many others, and commitment then feels arbitrary and dangerous. The blank page shifts from an opportunity into a threat.

The situation is worsened by cognitive load. The initial phase often requires simultaneously thinking about structure, content, interaction, accessibility, and future maintenance. When there are too many of these requirements, working memory becomes overwhelmed and starting fails. Subjectively, this state is experienced as emptiness.

Blank Page Syndrome is primarily a design problem. Effective solutions resemble good interface design. They introduce constraints, separate creation from evaluation, reduce the first step to something intentionally small, and rely on systems like templates, components, or content models. The goal is not inspiration, but conditions where starting doesn't require courage.
