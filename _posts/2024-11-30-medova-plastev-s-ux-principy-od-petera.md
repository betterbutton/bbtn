# Honeycomb with UX Principles by Peter Morville
> 

> „If a product or feature isn’t useful, it has no purpose, and there’s no reason to build it in the first place.“\
> **— [UXPin](https://www.uxpin.com/studio/blog/ux-honeycomb-definition-and-use/)**

![Elements of the honeycomb: usefulness, usability, attractiveness, findability, accessibility, credibility, and value.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/081b21eb-ee7b-481b-a4d7-fbc5e937aed0_640x390.png "640x390")

[UX Honeycomb: The 7-Factor Design Framework for Great User Experience](https://medium.com/@MohanKumarS96/ux-honeycomb-the-7-factor-design-framework-for-great-user-experience-d1858bc3ca31)

UX Honeycomb (honeycomb) is a design framework by [Peter Morville](https://semanticstudios.com/about/) from 2004 that helps evaluate user experience through seven key aspects:

- usefulness,
- usability,
- attractiveness,
- findability,
- accessibility,
- credibility and
- value.

[The method is ideal for redesigns and evaluation of existing products.](https://semanticstudios.com/about/ Thanks to clear criteria, it can reveal shortcomings and propose improvements that enhance user satisfaction. The framework is also a great tool for training junior designers. When used correctly, UX Honeycomb ensures products that are not only functional but also user-friendly and attractive.
