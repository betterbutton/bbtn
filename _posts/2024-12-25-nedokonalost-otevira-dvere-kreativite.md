# Imperfection Opens the Door to Creativity
> 

Perfectionism sometimes suppresses creativity, while curiosity fosters it. Venkadakrishnan Paranjothi shares his experience of how a [project-oriented approach helped him learn to work with new tools and systems](https://medium.com/@vnkidesigner/perfection-kills-creativity-but-curiosity-kills-perfection-edd21a0b389c), such as variables in Figma. Instead of passively watching tutorials, he recommends active discovery, experimentation, and learning from mistakes. This approach is likened to learning to swim—start in a safe environment, but don't be afraid to dive in. The key to solving design problems is not only practice but also curiosity and the ability to ask why and seek improvement. Creativity requires action and a willingness to accept imperfection.
