## People want to waste time

The biggest mistake of the current AI wave is the notion that every interface must be as fast as possible. Sometimes that's true. When someone needs to book accommodation, buy a ticket, or sign up for a service, they want it done as soon as possible. In those moments, any unnecessary delay is a burden.

But a large part of the digital world doesn't function like a to-do list. People often don't want to complete anything quickly. They want to watch, browse, compare, follow, and wait until something catches their eye. They don't necessarily want to save time. They want to spend it pleasantly.

That's precisely why browsing-based interfaces remain so powerful. The distinction between searching and discovering is still useful. Searching works when someone knows what they want. Discovering works when they don't yet know. Purely conversational interfaces hit a weakness here because they demand a clear intent from the user right at the start. Feeds, galleries, lists, and recommendations, on the other hand, first show options.

So it's not that people love inefficiency. It's that they move between two modes. Sometimes they want to get something done. Other times they want to dwell in something. Good UX must recognize which of these modes it is currently serving. A design that shortens everything to immediate resolution can easily remove the very reason the person came.

## Linkodrome

**Elements of User Experience.** *S. S. Karthik* explains [the five-layer UX model](https://medium.com/@sskarthik/understanding-layers-of-ux-for-interaction-design-with-a-simple-exercise-31bba405ae93) by *[Jesse James Garrett](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jesse-james-garrett)*: Strategy, Scope, Structure, Skeleton, and Surface. Each layer gradually transforms intent into a functional product: from defining the "why," through selecting features and their organization, to the concrete interface and visual style.

**A practical guide to UX research for solo designers:** *Sanjana Agarwal* shows how to analyze support tickets, talk to customers, track analytics, map user journeys, and quickly test prototypes. Even a small amount of research yields crucial insights into user behavior and needs. It's important to [directly connect findings to design decisions](https://sanjana-agarwal.medium.com/the-solo-designers-guide-to-user-research-no-team-no-budget-no-problem-78fc7abd7923) and build a routine.

**Pencil.dev speeds up website design with AI.** *Jan Polzer* appreciates local execution, speed, and versioning in the text. He sees the greatest benefit in [rapid design iteration](https://www.maxiorel.cz/pencildev-vibekoderi-se-obejdou-bez-designera-brzy) and its immediate conversion to code.

**UX audit without data and analytics:** *Hopemidah Kendi* shows that a [UX audit](https://medium.com/@hopemidah/how-to-run-a-ux-audit-without-fancy-tools-lessons-from-my-saruk-redesign-8dc18f30f763) doesn't have to rely on expensive tools but on clear thinking. It starts with defining user goals, continues with simulating a new user's journey and identifying problems. These are prioritized by severity and verified using [Nielsen's heuristics](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/usability-heuristics).

**Voice User Interface** (VUI) allows controlling technology by voice and provides a more natural interaction than traditional interfaces. From the first experiments in the 1960s to today's Siri or Alexa, the technology has undergone significant development thanks to AI and NLP. *Mahima Gupta* [succinctly summarizes past developments](https://medium.com/@mahimagupta015/voice-user-interfaces-vui-the-future-of-human-computer-interaction-55e7396642d1) and outlines where VUI will move in the near future.

[Jef Raskin](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jef-raskin) (March 9, 1943 — February 26, 2005). Advocated for computer design that respects human thinking and conserves attention and effort. In his book *The Humane Interface*, he criticized complex interfaces and emphasized simplicity, consistency, and error minimization. He was ahead of his time with the term *cognetics*, exploring the relationship between technology and cognition.

He rejected modes that confuse users and supported a modeless approach. Contributed to the creation of the *Macintosh* computer with a vision of an accessible computer for everyone. His Archy experiment showed how an intuitive, human-centered interface can work in practice. He understood interface design as a fundamental responsibility of technologists.

[Confirmation bias](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/confirmation-bias) is the tendency to search for and interpret information in a way that confirms our beliefs, while disregarding contradictory evidence. In digital environments, algorithms amplify this effect.

[The Cynefin framework](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/cynefin) helps decide how to approach a problem even before seeking a solution. It's based on the idea that not all situations are the same and therefore require different approaches: from routine processes through expert analysis to experimentation or crisis intervention.

[Figma Console MCP](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/figma-console-mcp) moves work with Figma toward direct AI-assisted design. Thanks to the Model Context Protocol, AI agents not only read designs but can also modify them, including variables, components, and layout.
