# Mozilla's New Visual Identity: A Return to Early Internet Aesthetics
> 

![New Mozilla logo](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/e6cf5a58-05b2-4819-b78f-8b32d07d9534_1771x850.png "1771x850")

[Mozilla](https://www.mozilla.org/), known primarily for its [Firefox](https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/new/) browser, has undergone an extensive visual identity change designed by [agency JKR](https://www.jkrglobal.com/work/mozilla). The goal of the rebranding is to highlight the company's broader impact, beyond just its browser, and to connect a community of activists, technologists, and everyday users.

The new logo, inspired by pixel aesthetics and the iconic T-Rex, symbolizes Mozilla's strive to "reclaim the web" and support user rights for an open and secure internet. The visual style, combining nostalgic elements of early internet culture, reflects the company's mission as a pioneer of digital rights and innovation.
