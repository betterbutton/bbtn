# Privacy by design
> Privacy by design is a proactive framework developed by Canadian Ann Cavoukian for incorporating privacy protection into digital systems from the very beginning.

**Privacy by design** ([Privacy by Design](https://www.epravo.cz/top/clanky/privacy-by-design-jako-jedno-z-novych-pravidel-pro-zpracovani-osobnich-udaju-107367.html)) is an approach created by [Dr. Ann Cavoukian](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ann-cavoukian-ph-d-3a78809/?originalSubdomain=cz). Its goal is to incorporate privacy protection directly into the design of digital services – even before they are created. It's not about late fixes or excuses – privacy becomes the foundation from the very first step of development.

The entire approach is based on seven principles. The first is **proactivity, not reactivity** – risks should be prevented. The second is **privacy as the default setting**, where personal data is protected automatically, without the need for user intervention. The third principle states that **privacy must be included directly in the design**, not added later.

The fourth principle, **full functionality**, emphasizes that there is no need to choose between privacy and usability – both can work together. **End-to-end security** ensures data protection throughout its entire lifecycle. **Transparency** brings traceability and trust. And finally, **respect for user privacy** means a user-friendly, understandable, and fair approach to how people control their data.

The principles of the Ontario concept of Privacy by Design ([PDF](https://www.ipc.on.ca/sites/default/files/legacy/2018/01/pbd-1.pdf)) are now part of modern legislation, such as GDPR, and are becoming a common standard in system design. They help create services that are not only functional and secure but, above all, trustworthy.
