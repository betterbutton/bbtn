# Discovery
> The initial phase of digital product design that uncovers user needs, reduces risks, and aligns goals before development begins.

Discovery ([discovery](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/discovery-phase/)) is the fundamental stage of any digital initiative. Its goal is to understand the real needs of users and connect them with business objectives. This significantly reduces the risk of creating a product that nobody wants or knows how to use.

The essence of discovery is learning – gathering insights through research, interviews, analyses, and prototyping. Teams validate assumptions before starting development and refine ideas based on feedback. This helps them avoid unnecessary mistakes and focus on what truly adds value.

Structured approaches, such as the double diamond model or Design Sprints, help navigate the entire process – from problem framing to validated solutions. They enable teams to quickly identify what is worth pursuing further and where they would simply waste time.

Discovery also includes mapping user journeys, usability testing, prioritization, or creating rough prototypes. These activities reveal technical constraints as well as human behavior while fostering collaboration between designers, developers, and stakeholders.

Thus, discovery doesn't slow down development – it accelerates it. It prevents unnecessary work and ensures that the foundation is solid from the start. The result is a well-thought-out strategy that meets people's needs and delivers better digital products.
