## From The Designer

![Example for the designer's tip. On the left and incorrectly is text with low contrast against the background. On the right and correctly is text with high contrast.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/0ce7919b-82df-463a-978e-2b592eaf7b43_2160x1080.png "1456x728")

[Accessibility rules](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum) require a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for body text.

**Don't skimp on contrast.** Low color contrast makes reading and navigation difficult.

❌ Light gray text on a white background\
✅ Black or dark gray text, clear background (or vice versa)

👉 Better readability for everyone (even in bright sunlight).\
👉 [You'll meet accessibility guidelines.](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/contrast-minimum)

[Contrast is calculated](https://www.accessibility-developer-guide.com/knowledge/colours-and-contrast/how-to-calculate/) – literally.

## Linkodrome

**[Penpot supports design tokens natively.](https://penpot.app/collaboration/design-tokens)** Penpot – an open-source alternative to Figma – supports open [W3C DTCG](https://docs.tokens.studio/manage-settings/token-format) standards, allows import/export in JSON format, and connects the design language with code. Thanks to token themes, it's easy to create different modes and component variants – for example, for rough drafts or for separate brands.

**How to write error messages.** Error messages determine whether a user in trouble moves forward or gets stuck and leaves frustrated, [writes Amy Hupe](https://piccalil.li/blog/how-to-write-error-messages-that-actually-help-users-rather-than-frustrate-them/). Good [microcopy](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/microcopy) is clear, specific, uses active voice, and always offers the next step – without unnecessary condescension or infantile humor. It's crucial to distinguish between errors the user can fix themselves and those that require a different form of support.

[Design by Committee](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/design-by-committee) rarely comes together with [design simplicity](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kiss).

**Believe in the power of simple design.** Charlie Deets [reflects on the value of simple design](https://charliedeets.com/writings/simpledesign.html) in a complex world. According to him, simplicity enables focus, increases usability, and carries a certain aesthetic quality that can be crucial for some. At the same time, Charlie admits that simplicity can hinder product growth and become a disadvantage in a competitive environment. The ideal is therefore the ability to balance simplicity with feature expansion. Can you do it?

**[What you need to know about the European accessibility law?](https://abra.ai/blog/the-european-accessibility-act-what-businesses-and-app-developers-need-to-know)** It introduces new obligations for companies and developers of digital products and services. The law covers computers, terminals, TVs, e-readers, and services like e-commerce, banking, communication, or transport. It will be effective from June 28, 2025 for new services, older ones must comply by 2028. Exceptions apply to micro-enterprises. Comply with the requirements of the [EN 301 549](https://accessible.canada.ca/en-301-549-accessibility-requirements-ict-products-and-services-1) standard, which follows the principles of [WCAG](https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/).
