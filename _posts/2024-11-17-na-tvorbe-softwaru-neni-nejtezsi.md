# In software development, the hardest part is not programming, but preparing the specification.

> 

Software development is not just writing code, but primarily [properly defining requirements](https://stackoverflow.blog/2023/12/29/the-hardest-part-of-building-software-is-not-coding-its-requirements/). This is still the domain of humans. Jared Toporek points out how difficult it is to precisely determine what the software should do. Problems are often caused by unclear, missing, or conflicting requirements.

> „Artificial intelligence can do some extraordinary things, but it can’t read your mind or tell you what you should want.“\
> **— [Jared Toporek](https://stackoverflow.blog/2023/12/29/the-hardest-part-of-building-software-is-not-coding-its-requirements/)**

AI can excel in tasks with fixed rules, like chess, but fails in dynamic scenarios full of variables. Just like in software development or even when driving a car. Despite advances in AI, the biggest challenge remains [to understand and properly define what users really need](https://stackoverflow.blog/2023/12/29/the-hardest-part-of-building-software-is-not-coding-its-requirements/).
