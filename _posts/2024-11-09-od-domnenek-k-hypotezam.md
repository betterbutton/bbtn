# From Assumptions to Hypotheses
> 

Andrew Duckworth explains [the difference between assumptions and hypotheses](https://grillopress.github.io/2024/03/12/moving-from-an-assumption-to-a-hypothesis.html) in the workflow. While assumptions allow for quick action based on belief, hypotheses offer a structured approach to validation and risk reduction. Assumptions are useful for accelerating the start of work, but without factual grounding they are risky, especially when designing for users.

Transforming assumptions into hypotheses helps reduce uncertainty and creates space for learning and quick reaction to changes. Duckworth particularly recommends hypotheses in high-risk situations or with unclear outcomes, where the cost of error can be significant.
