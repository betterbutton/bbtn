# Five Principles of Chinese UX Design by Juliette Xing
> 

Juliette Xing worked on analyzing European and American websites to understand [what culturally distinguishes their design](https://ux-uxr-portfolio-juliette.webflow.io/work/cross-cultural-study). On that occasion, she realized that we in Europe and America similarly fail to understand the cultural differences in the design of Chinese websites and apps, and so she wrote up [five principles of Chinese UX design](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7221393678399897600/) into a 30-page slideshow, building on her previous [five examples of good practice for Chinese UI](https://www.linkedin.com/my-items/saved-posts/). Juliette has a very [interesting profile on Linkedin for us Europeans](https://www.linkedin.com/in/juliette-xing/recent-activity/all/), as she consistently writes about specific aspects of things like Chinese TikTok or mobile wallets.

1.  **Holistic Perspective**\
    East Asian cultures, including Chinese, perceive the world holistically, meaning they focus more on the overall picture than on individual elements.

2.  **Typography Defines Layout**\
    Chinese characters are dense and each block represents one sound. This affects not only the density of buttons but also the density of fields in the layout.

3.  **Flexible Layout**\
    Unlike Western apps, which have regular and modular layouts, Chinese apps like XiaoHongShu allow variable field sizes, which can facilitate smooth eye transitions when browsing content.

4.  **Providing Related Content**\
    Chinese apps allow filtering search results by related topics rather than by content format, providing users with more relevant results.

5.  **Original Designs for New Practices**\
    New practices in Chinese apps require new icons and design elements that are not common in Western UX design.
