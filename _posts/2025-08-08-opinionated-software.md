# Opinionated Software
> Software that guides users through tasks according to a predefined methodology.

Opinionated software ([opinionated software](https://basecamp.com/gettingreal/04.6-make-opinionated-software)) is the kind that doesn't shy away from making decisions for the user. It is designed with a clear vision of how it should be used and is built from the ground up accordingly. For teams building digital products, this can mean faster development and fewer bugs.

By reducing the number of choices users have to make, the software simplifies the process. Opinionated software is based on best practices and protects users from common mistakes. This is particularly useful when working under time pressure or for less experienced users.

However, such rigid rules have their downsides. When the needs of a project or company diverge from what the tool assumes, customization becomes difficult. What helps beginners stay on track can hinder experienced users. And the moment the software's opinion diverges from yours, it stops being a support and becomes a brake.

Success with opinionated software depends on how much its approach aligns with yours. When they match, you gain a powerful ally. When they don't, it will hold you back.

The goal isn't to avoid such software, but to choose it consciously. Use it where its rules will support you—and be prepared to walk away where they start to limit you.
