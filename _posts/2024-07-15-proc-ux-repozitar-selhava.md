# Why research repositories fail
> 

Looppanel investigated why efforts to implement research repositories in companies are failing. They found that the problems stem from four areas:

- **Insufficient problem clarification**: Researchers place great expectations on repositories, but forget that they are just tools and won't solve insufficient sharing of research results across the organization for us.

- **Complex taxonomy**: The repository requires constant attention and ongoing management. With the implementation of the repository, a curator should appear in the research team who will maintain the tag system and ensure that research outputs are easily searchable.

- **Faster analysis outside the repository**: Repositories usually also include tools for analyzing artifacts and impose some specific approach, most often [atomic research](https://blog.prototypr.io/what-is-atomic-research-e5d9fbc1285c). With a thick stack of sticky notes, you are often more efficient, not to mention that it's easier to involve non-researcher colleagues in the evaluation.

- **Integration with workflow**: Repositories usually fit well into the workflows of the researchers themselves, but it's worse with the involvement of designers and product managers who are used to different tools. Repositories rarely work well together with task managers, roadmap management tools, or design systems.
