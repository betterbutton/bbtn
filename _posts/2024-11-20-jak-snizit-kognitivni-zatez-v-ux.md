# How to reduce cognitive load in UX design and why it matters
> 

Cognitive load refers to [the mental effort required to understand, decide, and complete tasks](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/why-cognitive-load-matters-in-ux-design-and-how-to-reduce-it-f96791b6361e). In UX design, it's important to minimize excess load caused by clutter and support users in achieving their goals. Strategies such as limiting choices, information hierarchy, or using familiar patterns facilitate interaction and increase user satisfaction.

Real-world examples, such as Amazon's "1-Click Ordering", show that fewer steps lead to better results. Modern design must respect the limited capacity of the human mind, create intuitive experiences, and provide mental relief to users.
