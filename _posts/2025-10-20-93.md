## Linkodrome

**Apple prepared CSS Liquid Glass effects for iOS26,** but only for itself. A new private CSS property `‑apple­‑visual­‑effect` allows using materials like `‑apple­‑system­­‑glass­‑material`, which mimic native design. It doesn't work in a regular browser or standard webview because it depends on the private `use­System­­‑Appearance` flag. *Alastair Coote* nevertheless found a way to [make it work](https://alastair.is/apple-has-a-private-css-property-to-add-liquid-glass-effects-to-web-content/), supporting the theory that Apple's webview uses more than meets the eye and very subtly.

**Prepare traps for unsuitable respondents.** A [screening questionnaire](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/screener) can easily let through inattentive or insincere research participants. The solution is traps that help reveal those who just click through questions, guess, or deliberately pretend to be someone else. Traps should be credible, thematically relevant, and used sparingly. They work best as [false options among real answers](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/screener-foils/).

**Great products save the user effort.** *Lea Verou* builds on *Alan Kaye's* principle *“simple should be simple, complex possible.”* If small complexity leads to great effort, a usability pitfall arises – a frustrating gap in user experience. [Great interfaces gradually scale effort](https://lea.verou.me/blog/2025/user-effort/) with task complexity and remove unnecessary noise. Products that save user effort feel like a good deal – and these are the ones users fall in love with, trust, and remain loyal to.

**Age verification on the internet is gaining momentum** – originally it was just about pornography, but now social networks are also joining. After the UK's *porn pass* fiasco in 2018, it seemed pressure for regulation would ease. Instead, rules are tightening: The UK introduced the [Online Safety Act](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/online-safety-act-explainer/online-safety-act-explainer), the EU and Australia are testing similar efforts. In the United States, the Supreme Court allowed age verification even for adult content. The trend is clear: more and more countries are considering mandatory [age verification at the expense of user anonymity and privacy](https://www.theverge.com/column/798159/age-gating-internet).

**Productivity is an illusion and outputs have replaced value.** We confuse speed and volume of work with real value. The result is a flooded backlog, technical debt, and chaos in product development. *Barry Prendergast* criticizes the cult of productivity without a meaningful goal. He reminds us that [good design is the result of thinking, testing, and decision-making](https://renderghost.leaflet.pub/3m2qmhm7kcc2l) – not just production. Real benefit is recognized not by quantity but by impact on real users.

**When to use a modal and when a dialog?** Their misuse can disrupt workflow and hinder accessibility. A11Y Collective explains the differences: modals require immediate attention and block the background, while dialogs allow interaction with the main content. They offer tips for accessible implementation using HTML ``, including focus management, contrast, keyboard navigation, and testing with screen readers. Is it necessary to interrupt the user? If not, [choose a non-modal dialog](https://www.a11y-collective.com/blog/modal-vs-dialog/).

## Tim Berners-Lee: The Web Is For Everyone

![Tim Berners-Lee sitting in front of a keyboard and two CRT monitors, with text: This Is For Everyone. (AI-generated drawing based on a photo)](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/c160ada6-3f26-4981-a7d0-58b716518d59_1536x1024.webp "564x376")

He invented the web and gave it to the world so it would remain free, open, and for everyone.

In 1989, [Tim Berners­‑Lee](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/timbl) created the *World Wide Web* at the CERN research center. He developed the `URL` addressing system, the `HTTP` protocol, and the `HTML` language. Instead of monetizing his invention, he chose to offer it to the world for free, enabling its global spread. Later, he founded the *World Wide Web Consortium* (W3C), which oversees open web standards.

He was elevated to the peerage, received the *Turing Award*, and is considered one of the most influential figures of the 20th century. To this day, he fights for an ethical, decentralized, and human-centric web. His vision *“This is for everyone”* remains a challenge and a hope for a free digital world.

## So We Understand Each Other

[A/B testing](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/ab-test) compares two variants of a website or application and uses data to determine which works better – typically measuring click-through rates, conversions, or interest. The idea behind it is simple: testing helps replace guesswork with understanding and creates design that truly matches user behavior.

[Democratization of design](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/democratization) means opening the design process to the general public, not just professionals. With tools like Canva, technical barriers disappear and more people can participate in creating products or services. The change is not just technological but also cultural. The main shift, however, is the ethical transformation of design into a tool of collective creativity accessible to all.

[Atomic research](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/atomic-research) brings a modular way of working with user insights, allowing validated findings to be stored, shared, and reused across projects. Each insight is structured as a *nuget* – an experiment, a fact, an observation, and a recommendation. For effectiveness, it requires consistent tagging and maintaining context. The main benefit is a scalable knowledge base.

[CUPID](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/cupid) is a framework for designing user interfaces that emphasizes long-term ergonomics. It offers five principles – Clear, Unobstructed, Predictable, Indulgent, Digestible – as a tool to identify places where the interface burdens the user even after adopting the system. It is ideal for tools used repeatedly, such as professional applications or office software.
