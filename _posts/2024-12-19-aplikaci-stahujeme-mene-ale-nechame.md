# We download fewer apps but pay more for them
> 

Despite a global decline in app downloads by 2.3% to 110 billion in 2024, user spending on them reached a record $127 billion, a 15.7% increase, [writes TechCrunch.](https://techcrunch.com/2024/12/18/app-downloads-decline-2-3-in-2024-but-consumer-spending-grows-to-127b/) Growth was led primarily by the Apple App Store, while revenue on Google Play declined. A subscription model that offers only 5% of apps generated nearly half of the revenue.

The highest-grossing app was TikTok. Instagram dominated downloads. Google Play introduced stricter rules for developers, contributing to a 60% drop in the number of new apps. Users are focusing more on using existing apps, while innovation is shifting towards AI.
