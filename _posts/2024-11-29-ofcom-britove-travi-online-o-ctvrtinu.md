# Ofcom: Brits spend a quarter more time online than last year
> 

British adults spent an average of 4 hours and 20 minutes online per day in 2024, nearly an hour more than last year, shows an Ofcom report, which [TechCrunch writes about](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/27/time-spent-online-by-adults-in-the-uk-jumped-by-nearly-an-hour-in-2024-says-ofcom/). The biggest increase is visible among young people aged 18–24, who spend over six hours online daily, predominantly on TikTok and Instagram. Women spend more time on the internet than men, with Generation Z showing a one-hour difference.

> “Ofcom found that 20% of children aged 8–15 falsify their age to access social media.”\
> **— [Ingrid Lunden](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/27/time-spent-online-by-adults-in-the-uk-jumped-by-nearly-an-hour-in-2024-says-ofcom/)**

Meta and Alphabet platforms dominate, while Reddit and Bluesky are growing rapidly. Significant problems include the spread of disinformation and hateful content. Ofcom plans regulations to protect children online in 2025.
