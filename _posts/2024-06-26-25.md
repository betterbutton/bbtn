# Linkodrome

**Designer maturity.** Before a design studio starts working with a new client, it tries to diagnose their level of maturity. According to Nielsen Norman Group, [UX maturity has six stages](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-maturity-model/). But is there something like a maturity model for designers? Five years ago, Invision came up with one such model. [It divides designers into five stages](https://web.archive.org/web/20190212120217/https://www.invisionapp.com/design-better/design-maturity-model/): Creator, Connector, Architect, Scientist, Visionary. Each stage corresponds to a certain skillset and brings specific benefits. The maturity stages of companies should correspond to the maturity stages of designers. As companies mature, [their designers should mature with them](https://medium.com/design-leadership-notebook/the-designers-growth-model-8240dafb7137).

**Which browsers do you support?** The era when websites displayed icons saying Optimized for MSIE 3.5 is gone forever, but how should developers approach supporting different browsers? Clearleft chose an approach [based on the wide availability of specific technical features](https://clearleft.com/thinking/a-modern-approach-to-browser-support). If browsers have supported them for at least two and a half years, their use on the web is safe. The approaches of [progressive enhancement](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Glossary/Progressive_Enhancement) and [graceful degradation](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Glossary/Graceful_degradation) are coming back into play.

**Adjust the design during testing.**

The purpose of testing is to uncover errors and bottlenecks. It's enough that you've discovered the problem; you don't need statistics on its frequency. Take advantage of the fact that you already know about the problem and focus during further testing on another part of the tested design or the next step in the scenario.

Allow yourself space between tests to quickly summarize the findings. Give the designer time to implement the changes.

Adjust the design continuously during testing. This way you'll test more variants in a shorter time.
