# Browser monoculture ruins the web
> 

Matthias Endler [ponders the dominance of the Google Chrome browser](https://endler.dev/2024/the-dying-web/). From Edge, through Opera, Vivaldi, Brave, Arc, and many others, they all build on the Chromium engine. We live in a [browser monoculture](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uh6GlUNjp1I). Of other browser engines, only WebKit (Safari) remains, and the once-popular Gecko (Firefox) is on the fringe of user interest.

Matthias criticizes the fact that many websites are today optimized only for Chrome, complicating access for users of other browsers. It reminds him of the era of the [computer wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Browser_wars), when Internet Explorer held a privileged position and websites even featured badges reading *optimized for MSIE*. In conclusion, Matthias urges readers to try Firefox and thus support diversity on the web.

The topic of browser dominance resurfaced after an American [court labeled Google a monopolist](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/google-nelegalni-monopol-usa) and people began discussing whether it's okay for Google to control browsers, search, email, advertising…
