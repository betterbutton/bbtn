# Letter from the Future
> Letter from the Future presents a fictional message describing a state where a project or product is already successfully completed and running. You use it when formulating a project brief.

**Letter from the Future** ([Letter from the Future](https://uxdesign.cc/backcasting-in-design-10b5e53e863f), Future Letter) is a letter written as if from a time when you have already achieved your goal. The website or application is finished, running smoothly, and already bearing its first fruits. This method has proven effective for expressing a product vision even before development begins.

[Amazon uses the Letter from the Future](https://www.inc.com/carmine-gallo/jeff-bezos-requires-amazons-leaders-to-perform-this-powerful-ritual-before-launching-anything.html) to help teams clarify what the final product should look like, how it should impact customers, and what results it should deliver – you can read about this in John Rossman's book [Think like Amazon](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/42435545-think-like-amazon). Writing a letter from the future helps unite the team with a shared vision and ensures everyone is working toward the same outcome.

The method works by forcing you to imagine in detail how the product will look when you launch it. Describe what customer problems you have solved, how the daily user experience has improved, and how the product has become an integral part of their lives. The letter then becomes a guide for planning all the steps that need to be taken. It allows you to quickly verify if the idea is sufficiently thought through, while also establishing a clear picture of the result you are aiming for.

Write the Letter from the Future clearly and persuasively to inspire all stakeholders and facilitate communication between departments. If writing the letter is difficult for you, try as an alternative writing a [press release](https://www.reddit.com/r/EntrepreneurRideAlong/comments/15dytdl/amazon_uses_something_called_a_future_press/) and [sticking to the 5W+2H framework](https://www.heflo.com/blog/process-mapping/5w2h-method-examples/). During the product design and development process, refer back to this text and remind everyone of the commitments and direction of the entire initiative.
