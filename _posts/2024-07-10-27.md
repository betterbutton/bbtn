# Linkodrome

**Login on the web split into two steps.** What is it for? Do you also wonder why Google, Microsoft, or Apple have split logins? [Why don't they just let us fill in the username and password at once?](https://joshwayne.com/posts/two-page-login/) With the rise of social networks, it became popular to offer users login via a social network account. For example, on Instagram or Medium, we originally logged in via Twitter. Over time, login variants multiplied, and people started forgetting which service they chose for registration.

Microsoft allows users to log in via their work or school accounts. It uses the first login step to distinguish, based on the email address, whether you are logging in directly with a Microsoft account or using an organization's directory. Apple and Google use the split login steps so we can completely bypass passwords. If you have a [passkey](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/passkeys-failed) set up for your account, you [don't enter a password in the next step](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passwordless_authentication).

**[State of User Research Report 2024.](https://www.userinterviews.com/state-of-user-research-report)** Do you have ReOps in your company? That is, Research Operations? Concurrently with companies losing interest in specialized designers and favoring generalists, democratization is taking hold in user research. Research is no longer done by researchers, but by everyone. For this to work, they need support, and that's exactly what ReOps provides. It has gone so far that bosses consider ReOps more important than product people.

The layoffs of researchers have been signed in belief in the future of user research. Only 73% of researchers expect to remain in user research. The good news is that researchers now report not to designers, but directly to product owners. What may be surprising is that only just over half of researchers use AI for work.

Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://www.katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 tips on how to ask for a website](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

Designing and creating a website is just the beginning. Prepare for feedback and be ready to respond to it by improving your website. A website is not a beautiful picture that is exhibited once and then just collects likes. Think of the website as a long-term service, as a way you communicate with customers.

Website management and operation are big commitments. Operation can cost many times more than the initial investment in website creation. Consider future operation during design. Keep in mind the requirements for changing content, website development, ease of editing, quality hosting, and IT security.

Think not only about users but also about system administrators. Divide users into internal and external and consider their different needs.
