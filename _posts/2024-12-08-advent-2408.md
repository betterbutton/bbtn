# Follow the checklist when publishing new content
> 

Publishing new content on a website or in an application is not just inserting text and images. It is a process that requires a systematic approach to maintain quality and established standards. This is precisely what a checklist serves as—an overview of steps that must be completed before or shortly after publication.

# Basic elements of a content publication checklist

The first step is verifying factual information, internal and external links, and the overall structure of the article. This is followed by a grammatical and stylistic review of the text. Next, it is important to check correct formatting, the functionality of elements like buttons or forms, and the addition of a suitable preview image.

# Technical aspects

Among the technical requirements, testing display on various devices, optimizing loading speed, and verifying accessibility for users with disabilities should not be missing. Check that the content is valid in terms of HTML, URL structure, canonical links, and possibly structured data, which helps search engines correctly interpret your content.

# Search engine optimization

Pay careful attention to headings, meta tags, and keywords. Ensure that keywords are naturally integrated and meta descriptions are understandable. Make sure that H1, H2, and H3 headings respect the content hierarchy. Checking both internal and external linking will support better user orientation and improve chances of higher placement in search results.

# Formal and legal requirements

Do not forget to comply with copyright for the used material. Verify that you share only content to which you have rights, including images and videos. Clearly label sponsored or advertising content.

# Testing and approval process

Before publishing the content, test it in a staging environment. Reach out to colleagues or external collaborators to review your text and obtain feedback. The approval process should involve at least one other person who confirms that everything meets the agreed standards.

# Publishing is not the end

Publishing the pages does not mean the work is over. It might be a good idea to mention them on social media. If the publication includes distribution (for example: newsletters, feeds, podcasts), you will need to prepare the materials and check the correct distribution. Use automation wherever human work can be replaced by a machine.

# Regular checklist updates

Once you start using the checklist, continuously supplement and adjust it based on new experiences or technological developments. What worked a year ago may not be relevant today.

With a carefully prepared checklist, you gain confidence that your new content meets all qualitative, technical, and legal requirements. The time invested will return in the form of satisfied visitors, better search rankings, and fewer errors. Start with a simple list and gradually improve it as needed. 🎄08
