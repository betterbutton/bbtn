# Deep link
> Deep links guide users directly to a specific screen in an app or page on a website, simplifying navigation and increasing engagement and conversions.

[Deep link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_linking), also known as [deep link](https://www.strafelda.cz/deep-link), is a link that takes the user straight to a specific location within an app or website – for example, a product page, settings, or a special offer. They don't have to go through the home screen or homepage.

On the web, such linking is common because every page has its own address. But for mobile apps, it's more complicated. Systems like universal links in iOS or app links in Android allow developers to map standard web links to specific content in the app. This way, the app opens directly where it should – without extra steps.

We distinguish three main types:

- Basic deep links only work when the app is already installed.
- Deferred deep links guide the user to the correct location after app installation.
- Contextual deep links additionally transfer information – such as referral codes or data to customize the app onboarding.

Deep links are often used in advertising campaigns, push notifications, emails, or search. They help shorten the path, maintain attention, and increase conversion rates. But here too, proper setup is crucial. A poorly configured link can lead to an error or cause the user to leave.

If your app still sends all users to the home screen, you're missing an important opportunity. Deep links aren't just a technical detail – they're the foundation of a modern, seamless user experience.
