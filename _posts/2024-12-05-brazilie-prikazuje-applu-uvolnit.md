# Brazil orders Apple to free up in-app purchases
> 

Brazil's antitrust agency ordered Apple to [remove restrictions for in-app purchases](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/26/apple-ordered-to-open-up-in-app-purchases-in-brazil/) within 20 days. The company must allow developers to link to external websites or manage payments independently. If Apple does not comply, [it faces a fine of $43,000 per day](https://www.theverge.com/2024/11/26/24306367/brazil-gives-apple-20-days-to-open-in-app-purchases). The verdict stems from a 2022 complaint by Mercado Libre and reflects global pressures to loosen App Store rules.

Apple has already made changes in several countries, but regulators worldwide claim the measures are insufficient. Brazil thus joins a growing list of countries pushing back against Apple's 'walled garden'.
