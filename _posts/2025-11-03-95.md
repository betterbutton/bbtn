## Linkodrome

**When does animation help and when does it harm?** *Emil Kowalski* shows that [animations should serve functionality](https://emilkowal.ski/ui/you-dont-need-animations): improve understandability (e.g., for marketing elements), add liveliness to the interface, or reinforce spatial consistency. Conversely, for frequent and routine interactions like keyboard shortcuts or opening menus, animations slow down and distract. The key is to think about the context and user's goal. Sometimes the best animation is none at all.

**When to choose top vs. side navigation?** *Taras Bakusevych* [has it clear](https://uxdesign.cc/top-navigation-vs-side-navigation-wich-one-is-better-24aa5d835643): Side navigation supports better scalability, vertical scanning, and is suitable for complex information structures. It suits applications with many links, like admin panels or content management tools. Top navigation saves space, works better with hover menus, and suits simpler structures, typically e-shops or smaller websites.

**Designers can, but the system holds them back.** The [2025 State of UX/Design](https://www.petermerholz.com/newsletter/tma-the-2025-state-of-ux-design-organizational-health/) survey revealed that although designers feel they are doing quality work and team collaboration works, organizations are failing them in three key areas: support for career growth, quality of outputs, and sufficient staffing. The highest satisfaction is with their own abilities and the impact of their work on users. The biggest disappointment stems from the absence of a clear career path and lack of time and people for quality outputs.

**Why do some digital products become habits** while others quickly fall into oblivion? According to behavioral researcher B. J. Fogg, it's the convergence of three elements: [motivation, ability, and triggers](https://medium.com/@mrafikusuma/why-do-some-digital-products-become-habit-forming-while-others-fade-after-a-single-use-1976e9e22933) – all must occur simultaneously. This model (Fogg Behavior Model) proves universally valid and particularly useful in the context of mobile user behavior in Indonesia. A well-timed prompt can turn a short-term emotion into a concrete action and create habits that stick.

**Domains \*.wpenv.net or \*.wpenv.dev point to 127.0.0.1.** They are ideal for local development without needing to edit the `hosts` file. `.dev` variants require HTTPS. *Kaspars Dambis* [originally created them](https://github.com/kasparsd/wp-env-domains) for simpler development of websites built on the WordPress CMS, but they work just as well for any other purpose where you need a domain on a local machine.

**What if personalization isn't that important after all?** A [meta‑analysis shows](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1002/mar.70073) that its effectiveness is minimal. Personalized messages have almost no impact on behavior. The study points out that earlier optimistic results were skewed by weak research designs and calls for greater methodological rigor.

**Screen readers aren't just for the blind** – they are also used by people with ADHD, impaired vision, or in situations where it's impossible to look at the screen. Ela Gorla debunks [seven common myths about screen readers](https://tetralogical.com/blog/2025/10/14/common-misconceptions-about-screen-readers/): that only blind people use them, that all users control them the same way, or that it's necessary to create special versions of websites for them.

## Molly Holzschlag had an open and accessible web as her mission

![Molly Holzschlag – drawing generated from a photograph](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/59a95c57-ec7c-4ee9-b0d3-82577d02c09e_1536x1024.webp "558x372")

“Web standards are not a done deal.” — Molly Holzschlag

[Molly Holzschlag](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/molly-holzschlag) (1963—2023), nicknamed *The Good Fairy of the Web*, was a tireless advocate for web standards, accessibility, and human-centered design. She collaborated with W3C, worked for the WaSP initiative, and helped convince browser manufacturers to unify technical rules. She wrote over 30 books, inspired the community with her approach to semantic HTML, and believed technology should serve people. She lectured worldwide and was an open mentor. Her legacy lies in a simple vision: the web should be open, accessible, and human – for everyone.

## Toolbox

[Google Admin Toolbox](https://toolbox.googleapps.com/apps/main/) is a free online set of tools for Google Workspace administrators, enabling quick diagnostics of DNS, email, and browser compatibility issues – even outside the Google ecosystem. Useful tools include Check MX, which verifies mail records, or Messageheader which analyzes delayed messages. Browserinfo and User‑agent help with connection and compatibility debugging. Log Analyzer simplifies reading and interpreting logs. The toolbox also offers tools like Encode/Decode or screen recording. It's ideal for quick diagnostics without installing any software.

## To understand each other

[Floating action button](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/fab) (FAB) is a key design element that embodies simplicity and direct control. Originating from Google's Material Design, FAB serves to invoke one main, non-destructive action – typically in the bottom corner on mobile. Its distinctive look, shape, and position make it a natural target for interaction.

[Hedonic treadmill](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/hedonic-treadmill) is a psychological phenomenon where people quickly get used to new stimuli and return to their usual level of satisfaction. In UX and design, this means even the most entertaining features quickly become mundane. Great design accounts for this effect – it reveals novelties gradually, supports long-term goals, and offers variability through social interaction or gamification.

[Principle of closure](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/closure), one of the Gestalt principles, describes the brain's ability to complete incomplete visual information into meaningful wholes. In UI design, the principle is used by icons or card layouts without frames.

[Problem statement](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/problem-statement) is a short, research-backed expression of a real user need – what, who, and why needs it. It helps focus on a meaningful solution. Formulating the problem translates research insights into a design challenge and guides the entire design process.
