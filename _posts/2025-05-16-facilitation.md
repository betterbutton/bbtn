# Facilitation
> Facilitation helps groups think, make decisions, and act together, and allows every individual to apply themselves.

Facilitation ([facilitation](https://www.workshopper.com/post/a-ux-designers-guide-to-facilitation)) helps groups collaborate better. It's not about telling people what to do. It's about creating a space where people can understand each other, engage, and move forward. The facilitator does not manage the content, but the way the group collaborates.

Unlike a moderator, the facilitator does not just watch the time or the order of speakers. They don't just keep the discussion in check. Rather, they open it up and give space to various voices. They help people hear each other and find common understanding.

A guide has a different role. They show the direction, impart knowledge, or lead the group along a known path. The facilitator does not determine any direction. They help the group find its own path.

In companies that work digitally or agilely, facilitation is becoming increasingly important. Meetings are not just about exchanging information. They can be places where decisions, new ideas, or change emerge.

Well-run workshops save time and prevent confusion. People collaborate better and understand each other more. The facilitator remains in the background. They just hold the framework and occasionally intervene when something needs to be unblocked or attention needs to be brought back to the goal.

When you know when to use facilitation, when moderation, and when guidance, you have a powerful tool at hand. Facilitation is suitable when you want the group to find answers together.
