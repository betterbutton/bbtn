# Digital footprints burden the planet
> 

Every online step we take leaves a carbon footprint that impacts the environment, [writes Arthur Holland Michel](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2024/07/how-much-data-ai-use/678908/) for The Atlantic. Even a simple act, like posting a photo or saving a video, carries an energy burden. Often a permanent one, since disks must remain powered on and algorithms constantly display them to others on screens.

> "Every post or email is like turning on a small light bulb that we'll never turn off."\
> — **A. H. Michel**, [Every Time You Post to Instagram, You’re Turning on a Light Bulb Forever](https://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2024/07/how-much-data-ai-use/678908/)

Every image posted on social networks, every sent email creates a digital footprint that permanently burdens the planet. Technology and artificial intelligence increasingly contribute to emissions that already reach the level of the aviation industry. Although individual efforts to reduce digital footprint may seem like a futile fight, the question of personal responsibility in the digital age remains relevant.

Real changes, however, must come from the big players who have the power to redirect technological progress onto an environmentally sustainable path.
