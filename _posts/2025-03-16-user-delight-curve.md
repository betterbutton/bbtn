# User Delight Curve
> User Delight Curve – shows how user satisfaction changes over time and how satisfaction influences their engagement, retention, and loyalty.

The [User Delight Curve](https://uxmag.com/articles/building-delight-in-your-design-system) describes how users' emotional responses to a product change and how this affects their long-term experience. When the curve is [well-designed](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pillars-user-delight/), it helps maintain satisfaction and minimize user churn.

It starts with the **first impression**. Users react to visual design, onboarding, and usability. An intuitive and smooth introduction increases the chance of long-term engagement. Then comes **product learning**. If users encounter obstacles, their enthusiasm may wane. This can be prevented by well-designed UX elements—such as tooltips, progressive feature disclosure, and clear feedback.

In the phase of **long-term engagement**, the product becomes part of the user's routine. Personalization, automation, and effective tools help maintain their interest. Over time, however, satisfaction may decline as the product becomes routine. Regular updates, new content, and UX improvements help maintain satisfaction.

Some psychological factors influence user enthusiasm. The Aesthetic Usability Effect states that more attractive products seem more intuitive. The Peak-End Rule means users remember the most intense moment and the end of the experience. Hedonic Adaptation explains why novelty loses its charm over time—hence the need for innovation.

[Modeling the User Delight Curve](https://uxdesign.cc/the-different-delights-of-ux-295fd1712f34) is used, for example, in e-commerce, where personalization and a smooth purchasing process increase satisfaction. SaaS products benefit from intuitive onboarding and feature discovery. Mobile apps can leverage gamification and micro-interactions.

A properly designed User Delight Curve helps create not only functional but also emotionally engaging products that lead to higher loyalty and more frequent recommendations.
