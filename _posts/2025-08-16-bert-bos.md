# Bert Bos – co-creator of cascading styles and a visionary in web design
> A Dutch computer scientist who helped create CSS – the technology that makes the modern web clear, sustainable, and easy to modify.

![Bert Bos sitting on the ground with a laptop on his lap](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/5b4fa396-6436-41c7-ab3e-197d49cfea74_1536x1024.webp "1456x971")

Bert Bos – co-creator of cascading styles

[Bert Bos](https://www.w3.org/People/Bos/) (*November 10, 1963) is a Dutch computer scientist who made history as a co-creator of [cascading style sheets](https://www.w3.org/Style/CSS/) – commonly known as CSS. Together with Håkon Wium Lie in the mid-1990s, he worked on enabling developers to easily and uniformly control the appearance of web pages. The result was the CSS specification, which was [published by the W3C consortium in 1996](https://www.w3.org/TR/REC-CSS1-961217).

Their work made it possible to separate content from visual presentation – a fundamental principle on which the modern web still stands. Without CSS, HTML would still be mired in manually written tags for font size, color, or alignment.

Even before Bos joined the W3C, he designed his own browser named Argo in 1994, which had a built-in styling system. It was this experience that helped him collaborate with Lie and bring the CSS specification to a usable form. Argo was notable for its extensibility through a system of *applets*, which were essentially the first browser extensions.

Bos and Lie also established the rule that user style sheet settings take precedence over the style prescribed by a web page's author.

However, Bert Bos did not stop with the first version of CSS. Throughout his tenure at the W3C, he collaborated on subsequent versions of the styling language and also worked on topics such as web accessibility or multilingual support. His approach to standards is simple and clear – with an emphasis on understandability and long-term sustainability.

Today, every web page we visit uses CSS. And in every style sheet, there lives a tribute to Bert Bos.
