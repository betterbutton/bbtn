# Better Button #04
> Hello! Let's start from the end: Emails without DMARC are ending. (tomorrow) Third-party cookies are ending. (in six months) And support for IPv4 is ending. (or is it?) The school semester is ending, but we are just beginning!

# Linkodrome

**Unidentified email will no longer be delivered.** Companies [Google and Yahoo announced](https://dmarcian.com/yahoo-and-google-dmarc-required/) that from February they will stop delivering 📨 emails without [DMARC](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/DMARC). The new rule applies to senders who send more than five thousand emails on any given day. Setting up DNS records and reconfiguring email headers is a matter of moments, but [it's still better to check](https://mxtoolbox.com/dmarc.aspx) whether your IT team hasn't forgotten about DMARC.

**Third-party cookies are ending.** 🍪 It sounds like a revolutionary slogan, but in reality, it's a shame that we still have such a technology. Third-party cookies are information stored in your browser by servers that you don't directly visit, but that websites you visit send requests to – typically for advertising, measurement, or user login. Safari and Firefox already don't count third-party cookies. [Chrome is joining this year after years of hesitation](https://developers.google.com/privacy-sandbox/blog/cookie-countdown-2023oct#:~:text=To%20facilitate%20testing%2C%20Chrome%20has,UK%27s%20Competition%20and%20Markets%20Authority.).

> *I personally believe that third-party cookies should never have been created. In the early days of the web, however, every technical trick was useful, so they survived until today.*
>
> **Dušan Janovský**, search engine architect at Seznam.cz ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/janovsky/status/1738181961523368274))

**When will support for short IP addresses end?** Another such technology that has long been [on its way to Valhalla is IPv4](https://konecipv4.cz/cs/) – the original device addressing on the internet. Which state will be the first to stop using this standard? Given the level of [support for the new IPv6 protocol](https://test-ipv6.com/) among internet service providers, this could mean that a substantial portion of citizens will find state services technically inaccessible. And that's not the right approach. ☝️ Besides, [Seznam.cz is not preparing to end IPv4 support either](https://x.com/vpecinka/status/1749815073567359204). But then again – the government has set the deadline for 2032, so maybe it will resolve itself.

**The legend of Facebook.** From a certain moment, every company needs its own mythology. [The Facebook employee handbook](https://twitter.com/gregisenberg/status/1747711217890726051) is very good in this discipline.

**Accessibility; only bad solutions, please.** For every complicated problem, there is a very simple and fundamentally bad solution. In the case of web accessibility, it is [accessibility overlay](https://overlayfactsheet.com/). Just as someone thought that the privacy protection problem would be solved by a cookie banner, in countries where [legislators are lagging](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/p/03#%C2%A7na-cem-prave-pracujeme) in transposing the European accessibility law, a plague of JavaScript overlays is spreading, which are supposed to remove accessibility problems. [The EU has also spoken out about this](https://commission.europa.eu/resources-partners/europa-web-guide/design-content-and-development/accessibility/testing-early-and-regularly/accessibility-overlays_en), and that's saying something. Just like with cookie banners, there is already a [browser add-on](https://www.accessibyebye.org/) that removes accessibility overlays. Fortunately, we still have a year 🦾 to roll up our sleeves and show that we are serious about accessibility.

A good source of information [on accessibility in UX design](https://stephaniewalter.design/blog/accessibility-resources-tools-articles-books-for-designer/) is a hub curated by Stephanie Walter. For updates in Czech, you can [visit the podcast 🙈 Poslepu](https://poslepu.cz/), recorded by Radek Pavlíček and Ondřej Pohle.
