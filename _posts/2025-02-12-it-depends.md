# It depends! Trust me.
> It depends! Trust me. – A designer's response expressing the solution's dependence on context and the need to trust expert decision-making. A key phrase illustrating the complexity of the design process.

When clients ask for clear answers, designers often respond: **"It depends. Trust me."** These words capture the reality of design – complex decision-making that depends on context, constraints, and user needs.

**"It depends"** means there is no universal solution. Every project has different goals, technological limitations, budgets, or branding requirements. Design is about finding a balance between aesthetics, functionality, and feasibility.

**"Trust me"** emphasizes the need for trust between the client and designer. Clients may not see all the connections and might want quick solutions. Designers, however, know that the best result often requires testing and iterative work. Therefore, they rely on research, personas, prototyping, and user testing to verify what actually works.

Designers also commonly use other phrases: Less is more. Form follows function. That's an interesting hypothesis! All of them reflect the importance of empathy and understanding context. Design isn't just about visuals – it must work within technical possibilities, cultural differences, and user habits.

The phrase "It depends!" is not an excuse, but a reality. Great design doesn't come from predetermined answers, but from the ability to navigate complexity. Trust between designer and client, testing ideas, and understanding the user are key to success.
