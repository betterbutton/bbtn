# UX Design is Maturing: Between Influences, Cycles, and Stagnation
> 

A week ago we wrote [about Jakub Karlica's view](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/karlec-o-ux-v-cesku-klesa-zajem) on the Czech job market in UX design. The article sparked an interesting discussion, and [Ondřej Kratochvíl's observation](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:ugcPost:7264321485601742848?commentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28ugcPost%3A7264321485601742848%2C7264592684554629122%29&dashCommentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afsd_comment%3A%287264592684554629122%2Curn%3Ali%3AugcPost%3A7264321485601742848%29) deserves particular attention.

According to Ondřej, UX design has reached its peak and is now transitioning into a calmer phase similar to other older fields, like software development. Ondřej compares the evolution of design to waves after a splash in water—from exponential growth to stagnation. Design has gained respect and importance thanks to its ability to translate business requirements into development, which was especially crucial in the early days. Today, however, extreme appreciation awaits only exceptional talents or specific situations. Ondřej notes that similar cycles have been observed in other areas, such as during the mechanization of agriculture.
