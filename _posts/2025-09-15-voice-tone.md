# Voice and tone
> Voice is the product's personality, tone is how it adapts to the situation. Together, they determine how the user feels.

Voice and tone ([Voice and tone](https://uxdesign.cc/workshop-guide-define-the-tone-of-voice-of-your-product-e1a34f27af2c)) are key elements of product design. They influence how people perceive a brand, how safe they feel while using it, and whether they trust you—especially when something goes wrong. Voice is constant, tone changes.

Voice stays the same. It's the personality behind the content—it can be formal or friendly, positive or factual, playful or straightforward. It expresses the product's values and should feel familiar in every interaction.

Tone adapts to the situation. When a user successfully completes a task, the tone can be joyful. When an error occurs, it should be calm and supportive. Tone helps users feel understood, especially in challenging moments.

Designers and writers must work together to clearly define the voice and plan appropriate tones for different situations. Voice and tone guides—like those from companies such as [Slack](https://a.slack-edge.com/4d5bb/marketing/img/media-kit/slack_brand_guidelines_september2020.pdf), [Uber](https://base.uber.com/6d2425e9f/p/03856f-writing-for-users), or [Atlassian](https://atlassian.design/content/voice-and-tone-principles/)—help the team maintain a consistent style and avoid ambiguities that could undermine trust.

Why it matters: Good text isn't just clear. It's human. People want to feel like they're communicating with someone, not something. A thoughtful voice and an adaptable tone reduce uncertainty, support successful task completion, and strengthen the relationship with the brand.

Products that speak like people make users feel they are taken seriously as people, not as a problem.
