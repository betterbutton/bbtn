# How to become a UX researcher without a degree
> 

While the traditional path to UX research often goes through degrees in psychology or HCI, more and more professionals are entering this field from non-traditional backgrounds. Key skills, such as data analysis, communicating findings, and the ability to influence decisions, can be acquired outside of academia.

> „Just do. Practice. Fail. Grow.“\
> — **[Kaitlyn P.](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/breaking-into-ux-research-non-traditional-paths-to-success-2c340c06868d)**

[Stories like those of Kaitlyn, Aprill, and Areebah](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/breaking-into-ux-research-non-traditional-paths-to-success-2c340c06868d) show how important experience, mentorship support, and willingness to learn are. Diversity of thoughts and experiences improves research quality, which is also confirmed by teams with diverse researchers. Success in UX research lies in passion, skills, and determination, not in specific education.
