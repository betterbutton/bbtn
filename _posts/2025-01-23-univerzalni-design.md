# Universal Design
> Universal Design – an approach to creating products and services that are accessible and usable for the widest possible range of users without the need for additional adaptation.

**Universal Design**, also known as [design for all](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/design-for-all?srsltid=AfmBOooRwPLN5SQtnmWQcJCvzqMJxMYsy4IL8muOJCX3yxBQxqQHbBTj#what_is_the_difference_between_design_for_all_vs._universal_design_vs._inclusive_design) or [inclusive design](https://www.designprovsechny.cz/clanky/10-nejcastejsich-predsudku-o-inkluzivnim-designu), is not just about accessibility for people with disabilities. It's about a broader inclusion of all users. A website or application should be easy to operate for people of different ages, from diverse social and cultural backgrounds, and with varying technical skills. The goal is to create an environment where everyone feels welcome and can find what they are looking for without unnecessary complications.

Universal Design should not be perceived as a design constraint, but as an intentional goal and a value-based prioritization. At first glance, implementing Universal Design may seem challenging. However, the benefits outweigh the costs. You will improve user satisfaction, increase traffic, and meet [legal accessibility requirements](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424). Universal Design is not just a trend; it is a necessity in today's digital world with an aging population and a multicultural environment. Moreover, you are creating a product that is fair and accessible to everyone.
