# State of UX in Mobile Apps in 2024 and Their Most Common Errors
> 

In 2024, the majority of mobile e-commerce apps show average UX performance, as demonstrated by [Baymard's research on over 8,000 elements and 7,000 examples from 26 apps](https://baymard.com/blog/mobile-app-ux-trends). A full 88% of apps fall into the average or worse category.

> 56% of mobile apps do not provide a complete overview of categories on the main screen.

Researchers share the 10 most common errors, which include issues with navigation on the main page, unclear links, and excessive ad promotion. Recommended practices include clear descriptions of links on the homepage and limiting visually distracting ads. The study shows that none of the tested apps achieve high quality, indicating significant room for improvement.
