# Lazy registration
> Lazy registration is a UX design pattern that allows using a digital product before registration, thereby reducing entry barriers and increasing conversion and user satisfaction.

[Lazy registration](https://ui-patterns.com/patterns/LazyRegistration), also known as [deferred registration](https://www.slideshare.net/garcon/lazy-registration-design-pattern), is an approach where the user does not need to create an account right at the beginning. Instead, they can first try out how the service works. Only when they get used to it or create something with it does the right moment for registration come.

Deferred registration [works well](https://90percentofeverything.com/2009/03/16/signup-forms-must-die-heres-how-we-killed-ours/) in online stores, where the customer can purchase goods without logging in. Some mobile apps also allow browsing content or starting tasks without an account. Registration then appears only when it is necessary to save something or continue later.

From a technical perspective, the user first has an anonymous session. Their data is stored, but the account is created only later, upon registration. It is important [that everything works smoothly](https://guptadeepak.com/effortless-sign-up-with-lazy-registration/) and the user does not lose anything.

Of course, this also has its risks. If the user does not return, they might lose unsaved data. Deferred registration helps people start simply and without barriers. Timing is important. The best moments for registration are when the user has achieved something—when they have completed their first task, saved their progress, or want to share something. At such a moment, creating an account makes sense. And that is exactly what [lazy registration](https://www.slicedbreaddesign.com/blog/pattern-lazy-registration) does well.
