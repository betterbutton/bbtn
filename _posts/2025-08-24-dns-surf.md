# DNS.Surf: How servers around the world see your domain
> DNS.Surf is an online tool that allows you to monitor how DNS servers worldwide respond to queries for your domain.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/c2479372-b6e2-4096-a221-bb749b2ad98f_1898x1079.png "582x330")

[DNS.Surf](https://dns.surf/) shows how DNS servers around the world respond to requests.

[DNS.Surf](https://dns.surf/) is an online tool that helps you find out what users in different locations on the planet actually see when they enter your domain address. Unlike a standard DNS resolver, it doesn't track where your query is sent, but what is returned in response—and it does so from the perspective of dozens of global locations and various DNS server networks.

It works simply. You enter a domain name and select a record type, and DNS.Surf shows which record is visible to different DNS servers in the Americas, Europe, Africa, Asia, or Australia. You can easily verify whether DNS changes have already propagated, whether visitors are being directed to the correct server, or if geo-routing might be causing issues.

For developers, CDN operators, e-shop administrators, or operators of multilingual websites, it provides a useful look under the hood of the internet. Especially if you need to be sure your users always reach the correct version of your site—whether they are in Tokyo, Paris, or Brno.

[DNS.Surf](https://dns.surf/) saves time and uncertainty. It allows you to see the network from a perspective you normally don't have—through the eyes of your actual visitors around the world.
