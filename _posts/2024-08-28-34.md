# Linkodrome

**Seznam.cz plans to introduce paid content.** Matěj Hušek, member of Seznam's board, gave an interview to Médiáři on the topic of paid content. According to him, [Seznam will start with paid content on the blogging platform Seznam Medium](https://www.mediar.cz/matej-husek-seznam-cz-musi-vyzkouset-i-placeny-obsah/). Seznam wants to attract established creators to its platform, offering payment processing and traffic as advantages over other platforms.

Seznam owner [Ivo Lukačovič once had a blog](https://web.archive.org/web/20050324201730/http://blog.lide.cz/ilblog/2005/01/12/1). He is now experimenting with a subscription on Patreon, where [he has over 2,200 paying subscribers](https://www.patreon.com/ilblog). [Seznam Medium](https://medium.seznam.cz/) currently shares advertising revenue with authors. They benefit from driving traffic from the algorithmic feed on Seznam's homepage. According to Netmonitor measurements, [Medium had 56 million views and 3.5 million users in July](https://e-public.gemius.com/cz/rankings/10279/977327). It's not widely known, but [Seznam also distributes podcasts](https://podcasty.seznam.cz/), into which it inserts ads.

Martin Procházka, one of the authors publishing on Seznam Medium, recently [summarized his experience](https://medium.seznam.cz/clanek/martin-prochazka-rok-a-ctvrt-se-seznam-medium-65834) and estimated he receives about 50 CZK per thousand views with a 50% share of ad revenue. His articles have over a hundred thousand views. However, getting an article on Seznam's homepage is subject to editor approval, so not all Media authors have similar earnings. According to year-old data, there are [around five hundred regularly publishing authors on Medium](https://blog.seznam.cz/2023/09/cesi-maji-na-vsechno-nazor-a-seznam-medium-jim-dava-prostor-rika-pavla-veverkova/#:~:text=500%20z%20nich%20publikuje%20na%20platform%C4%9B%20pravideln%C4%9B.), producing about a hundred articles daily.

If nothing changes about how Seznam Media operates, content creators face a significant trap: Media users now belong to Seznam, and creators have no direct contact with them. Therefore, if they ever decide to change platforms in the future, they will have to build their subscriber base from scratch. On platforms like the aforementioned Patreon or the Substack we use, subscribers belong to the creators. They can thus export their list and contacts at any time.

**Are we in for optimization for human memory?** The new Chrome will be able to [open a page from browsing history based on a text prompt](https://www.zive.cz/clanky/ai-ve-chromu-vyhleda-predmety-vyresi-rovnice-a-porovna-produkty-v-otevrenych-kartach/sc-3-a-229514/default.aspx). With the advent of features like [Microsoft's Recall](https://www.zive.cz/clanky/microsoft-meni-recall-fotografickou-pamet-ve-windows-11-lepe-zasifruje-a-aktivaci-necha-na-uzivatelich/sc-3-a-228543/default.aspx) or the ability to open a page from Chrome's history based on what the user remembers, a new discipline opens up for web designers.

It's no longer enough to have just search engine optimized content—pages must also be memorable in some way. Original design, engaging text, or unique interactions can decide whether users remember a page and return to it.

The advertising industry has been playing this game for a long time. You surely know that furniture store—you know, the one with the big chair. The question arises: Isn't this the next big thing that could overshadow even full-text search as we know it today?

Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://www.katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 tips on how to ask about the web](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

Look at your website as a story. Each page of the website is like a panel in a comic. For individual pages, note how they help you get closer to a specific goal.

Do you have pages on your sitemap to which you haven't assigned any goals? Feel free to remove them. A sitemap will help you get an idea of what types of pages you'll use on the website and how much and what content you need to prepare. Just like in a road atlas—on a sitemap too, you should distinguish main routes from side ones at a glance, important places, intersections.
