# Geyser of Ideas
> A turbulent phase of idea generation in the design process, where ideas gush forth with uncontrolled intensity and without immediate evaluation.

**Geyser of Ideas** refers to an intensive phase of idea generation during the design process. It's the moment when a team switches from problem analysis to solution-seeking—and ideas begin to gush like a geyser. In this phase, there is no judgment, no criticism, only creation. The goal is the largest possible quantity of diverse proposals, regardless of their feasibility.

This creative burst occurs in the *Create* phase, the third of the four steps in the [Double Diamond](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/double-diamond) framework. Unlike free-form brainstorming, a geyser of ideas can be [structured and facilitated](https://workpluscollective.org/media/219/download). Using methods like SCAMPER, the product team stays within the thematic context and is encouraged to cross usual thinking patterns. Even ideas that sound crazy can later pave the way for innovation.

The purpose of a geyser of ideas is not to find the final solution, but to expand the space of possibilities. Only then does the *Deliver* phase follow, in which ideas are evaluated, prototyped, and tested.

For teams working on designing digital services or products, the geyser of ideas is a moment of freedom and the joy of creation. It generates not only designs but also team bonding, energy, and confidence. If this phase is led correctly, the team leaves with a head full of ideas and a feeling that they've come up with something.
