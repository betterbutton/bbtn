# How to Discover User Needs
> 

Jan Dittrich, while studying design at Bauhaus-Universität, compiled a guide for user research. And because Jan is a strong advocate of open-source, you can also find the entire text online: [A Beginner's Guide to Finding User Needs](https://jdittrich.github.io/userNeedResearchBook/) focuses on qualitative research methods to understand user motivations, activities, and problems. It presents various approaches, from interviews through observation to creating diagrams and maps.

Research can be used in the early design phase, during redesign, or to discover new opportunities. It emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and ethics, including fair compensation for participants. Preparation includes defining goals, recruiting participants, and creating a 'cheat-sheet'. The book emphasizes that research is not a linear process and prepares readers for uncertainties and adjustments during the project.
