# WIIFM: What's in it for me?
> The question every user, visitor, and customer asks. And you should know the answer before they do.

What's in it for me? [WIIFM](https://boagworld.com/emails/wiifm-the-five-letters-you-need-to-influence-stakeholders/). ([What's in it for me?](https://uxdesign.cc/whats-in-it-for-me-and-other-user-questions-you-must-answer-db52cfb9cc98)) This is the question people ask before they even finish reading your slogan. If you don't answer it right away, they'll leave. Not because they're lazy. But because the world of digital products is full of other options.

A clear answer is essential. Why should someone click, sign up, or download your app? It's not enough to say what you do. You need to show what the user will get. And quickly, simply, and without unnecessary chatter.

Every detail on a website or in an app should answer exactly this. The headline, the button, the text, and the app's behavior. Not "Export data." But "Download your results to Excel." Not "Registration." But "Get free access to all content."

WIIFM is like a litmus test. If you can't clearly state what the user gains, it probably isn't worth launching yet.
