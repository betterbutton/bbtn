# What No One Teaches You About User Research
> 

Your first user research project can be daunting, especially in an environment without established processes. Yashika Chhabaria advises [collaborating with customer-facing departments](https://chhabariayashika.medium.com/what-nobody-tells-you-about-conducting-your-first-user-research-edadb2531e60) to recruit participants. A key lesson was adapting the interview script—shifting to a flexible style allowed for a deeper understanding of user needs. Another important insight: users want to share feedback, which is an opportunity to strengthen relationships and ensure future collaboration. The author recommends thanking them, sharing the findings, and demonstrating how their input influences product development.
