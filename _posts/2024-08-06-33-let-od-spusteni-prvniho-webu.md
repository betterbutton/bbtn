# 33 Years Since Launching the First Website
> 

![Screenshot from the first web pages](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/94d2161c-751c-4a98-a9dc-1e350f139bb0_1915x1079.png "1456x820")

[The first website put on the internet](https://line-mode.cern.ch/www/hypertext/WWW/TheProject.html)

On Tuesday, August 6, 1991, Tim Berners-Lee put the [first web pages](https://info.cern.ch/) on the internet. Thanks to CERN, we can open them again.

Thirty-three years. A third of a century. For most of us in the Czech Republic, this period overlaps with life in a free world, although we likely discovered the internet and, with it, the web only several years later. In Martin's case, it took another six years before he opened and designed the first web pages.

Perhaps we expected a utopia of unlimited possibilities, but we got more of a Rorschach test on a planetary scale. The web became a mirror that mercilessly reflects humanity's nature.

We promised the democratization of knowledge, but often ended up in echo chambers of our own convictions. We wanted a global village, but we also created digital ghettos. We desired freedom of expression, only to discover how thin the line is between freedom and chaos.

Nevertheless, it would be a mistake to demonize the web. It is a tool of immense power that amplifies the best and the worst in us. It has allowed us to see the world through others' eyes, to organize against injustice, to share beauty and pain across continents.

The web is not just technology; it is a living, pulsating entity shaped by billions of interactions. It is a collective dream and a collective nightmare, a digital Gaia and a technological Leviathan.

After thirty-three years, we still stand on the threshold of a new era: Web 3.0, artificial intelligence, virtual reality – tools that can either deepen our connections or deepen our divides. The choice is ours.

Perhaps the web's true gift is the realization that technology itself is neither good nor bad. It is a mirror. And what we see in it depends on us.

And what does the web mean for you? What has it brought into your life?
