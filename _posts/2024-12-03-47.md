**Persuasive design, conversion optimization, or dark patterns?** The website [Growth.Design](https://growth.design/) collects UX laws and cognitive biases and supplements theoretical definitions with interactive case studies that illustrate the use of psychological influence on customers, for example, [at self-service ordering kiosks in McDonald’s](https://growth.design/case-studies/mcdonalds-self-serve-ux).

**Typography as the key to perfect UX.** Jeremiah Shoaf is a typography enthusiast and has been offering [inspiration, tools, and tutorials on the website Typewolf](https://www.typewolf.com/) since 2013. Among his masterpieces are font recommendations, pairing guides, and examples from real projects. Thanks to Typewolf, he teaches designers to improve readability, visual hierarchy, and the aesthetics of designs. Shoaf draws from his two decades of experience. He emphasizes the practical use of type in design. The project attracts over 350 thousand visitors monthly, and as of today, you can be one of them too.

**The UX Association investigated** what the design community expects from it. The main priorities turned out to be support for professional development and creating networking opportunities. Furthermore, AUX should focus on raising awareness of UX outside professional circles, including cooperation with non-profit organizations and public administration. According to board member Klára Scholleová, the association [plans to open grants](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7269390598321704961/) and expand member involvement options so that activities lead to sustainable results. Designers suggest greater involvement of business people to enrich know-how and collaboration.

**Perceive the web as an investment with a return, not as a cost**

Creating a larger website is often financially demanding. However, it is important that these expenses yield measurable results. Change your perspective on the web: not as a simple expense, but as an investment whose benefits can be objectively assessed. For e-shops, return is measurable through increased sales. However, if your goals are not purely financial, define other key performance criteria and regularly monitor their development.

At the same time, consider whether your problem can be solved in a way other than investing in the web – sometimes a completely different approach can be more effective.
