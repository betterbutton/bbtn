# Breadcrumbs
> A UI navigation element that indicates users' current position within the website structure. It enhances orientation, boosts conversions, and supports both accessibility and search.

**Breadcrumbs** ([breadcrumbs](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/breadcrumbs/)) are a strip of links on a page that shows the path from the home level to the currently displayed content and allows users to easily return to higher levels of the website hierarchy.

Also describe the page hierarchy using structured data according to the [Schema.org](https://schema.org/BreadcrumbList) standard. This will facilitate the work of search engine robots, voice assistants, and screen readers.

The visual design must also suit mobile devices. If the path is too long, shorten it into a dropdown menu. On touchscreens, ensure a sufficiently large active link area so that users can comfortably tap it with their finger.

Inspiration comes from the fairy tale about two children [Hänsel and Gretel](https://www.nasepohadky.cz/jenicek-a-marenka/c729), who marked their path through the forest with breadcrumbs. Similarly, today we guide visitors through the maze of articles, products, and settings. When we offer them a clear and consistent trail, they won't get lost and will be happy to return.
