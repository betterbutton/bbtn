# Design Challenge
> A concise and actionable question that transforms vague project goals into a clear creative direction.

[Design Challenge](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/designova-vyzva) ([design challenge](https://medium.com/sap-design/a-practical-guide-to-designing-a-design-challenge-c3c31422b67a)) is the most precise tool a website owner or client can use to focus creative work. It turns a business problem into a question designers can explore: *"How might we help visitors choose the right plan faster?"* Unlike a feature list, the challenge defines what the design should achieve—not how to do it.

At the start of any digital project, clarity is more important than code or colors. Many redesigns fail because the problem isn't framed correctly. A design brief sets goals and constraints, a problem statement describes what's wrong, and a user perspective adds a human dimension. The design challenge ties it all together into a direction—it shows where to focus attention and how to evaluate results.

Other tools have narrower purposes. A [user story](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/user-story) helps with implementation (*"As a visitor, I want to reset my password..."*), a project statement sets deadlines and deliverables, and [jobs to be done](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jtbd) describes user motivation. The design challenge sits between them: it connects strategic intent with human need.

Writing a good challenge means framing success as a curious question: *"How might we make our help text instantly useful?"* It keeps designers creative, developers aligned, and everyone else focused on what truly matters.

A well-crafted design challenge saves time and money. It replaces assumptions with clear purpose and turns design from decoration into a problem-solving tool. In an online world where attention and trust are scarce, that single question can decide whether a design merely looks good or actually works.
