# UX Office Hours: A Key to Improving Design Influence in Large Teams
> 

UX designers in large organizations with extensive product portfolios face a whole range of problems stemming from the size of the organization and the design teams themselves. In these companies, consistent design is often lacking because designers work in isolated teams and are significantly outnumbered by other specialists.

> “Office hours are a way to eliminate guesswork about when and how to contact the team, while also actively inviting people into your design system.”\
> — **[Jules Mahe](https://zeroheight.com/help/guides/how-to-conduct-office-hours-for-your-design-system-and-help-with-adoption/)**

[UX Office Hours are informal](https://linnk.ai/insight/software-development/ux-office-hours-a-solution-for-scaling-design-influence-and-consistency-across-large-product-portfolios-WQZd6V1I/), but structured weekly meetings where designers can extend their influence beyond their immediate teams.

UX Office Hours are presented as an effective solution—regular informal meetings where designers can [share knowledge and align their practices](https://zeroheight.com/help/guides/how-to-conduct-office-hours-for-your-design-system-and-help-with-adoption/) across products. The author recommends implementing UX Office Hours, especially in organizations with limited design resources and for teams that still lack sufficient awareness of design thinking.
