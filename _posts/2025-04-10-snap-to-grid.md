# Snap to Grid
> Snap to grid is a design principle and tool for aligning elements to an invisible system, ensuring consistency, precision, and rhythm in digital interfaces.

Imagine you are designing something—a website, an app, or a game. You have elements that need to be aligned so that everything looks clean and functions as it should. And that’s exactly when the grid comes into play. And an even more important feature: snap to grid.

It’s like when you’re assembling a puzzle and all the pieces click perfectly into place. It helps maintain order. Buttons aren’t shifted by a millimeter, spaces are uniform, and the entire interface feels more confident. Even if users might not consciously notice it, it just *feels* right. It feels trustworthy.

In games, it’s about more than just aesthetics. Game elements often need to hold together according to precise rules. When you’re building a city or moving characters across a map, the grid ensures everything works as intended. Without it, objects would collide where they shouldn’t, and the game would start to fall apart.

But even with a grid, you need to be careful. Relying on it too much can make the result feel overly mechanical. Too polished. The best designs combine structure with a touch of freedom. Like having a steady rhythm but occasionally skipping a beat to make it feel more alive.

Ultimately, snapping to the grid is more than just a function. It’s a way of thinking. We try to organize things so they make sense. So they are understandable. And even though we can’t see the grid, we feel its effect with every click.
