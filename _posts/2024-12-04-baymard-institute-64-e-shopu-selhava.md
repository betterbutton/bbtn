# Baymard Institute: 64% of E-shops Fail at Product Sorting
> 

Research by the Baymard Institute revealed that [most e-shops do not provide all four key types of sorting](https://baymard.com/blog/essential-sort-types):

- by price,
- user ratings,
- popularity, and
- new arrivals.

This makes it difficult for customers to orient themselves and select products. Sorting by price is the most common and should necessarily be bidirectional. User ratings, although crucial for trust, are missing from 36% of websites.

> "Alphabetical sorting is mostly unnecessary and unhelpful."\
> **— [Mark Crowley](https://baymard.com/blog/essential-sort-types)**

Best-selling products help less knowledgeable customers, but half of the websites do not offer this option. New arrivals are preferred by returning customers, yet 16% of websites lack it. On the other hand, alphabetical sorting or vague "Sort By" options often confuse users and unnecessarily complicate the shopping process.
