# July 17
> July 17 in the emoji calendar sheet on iOS and other environments refers to the day the iCal app was released in 2002.

When you look at the emoji calendar on an iPhone, you notice one detail. It shows July 17. You might wonder – why exactly this date?

![Emoji calendar from iOS 18.4 shows the date Jul 17.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/be3df8b1-3d7c-4d88-b765-27fce8f647b3_160x160.png "160x160")

Emoji calendar from iOS 18.4

It's a quiet reference to Apple. It was precisely [July 17, 2002](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2002/07/17Apple-Introduces-iCal/) that the company introduced the iCal app. It long displayed this day on its icon. Apple then decided to use the same motif in the emoji.

Emojis don't look the same everywhere. There is indeed a list of which emojis exist, but each company decides for itself how they will be drawn. Therefore, they can differ in colors, shapes, and small details, such as the date on the calendar.

For developers and designers, this is important. It means that even the most common symbols are not completely universal. What looks familiar might have a slightly different meaning on another platform.

July 17 can thus be considered [Emoji Day](https://worldemojiday.com/). Unofficial, perhaps a bit funny, but it shows how even a tiny graphic element can tell a story.
