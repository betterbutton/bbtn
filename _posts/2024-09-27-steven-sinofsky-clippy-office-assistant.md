# Steven Sinofsky: How Mr. Clippy paved the way for today's AI
> 

Steven Sinofsky is the person who was responsible at Microsoft for introducing the ribbon and Mr. Clippy. It probably won't surprise you that [Kevan Attebery](https://www.kevanatteberry.com/), who created the character of Mr. Clippy, was working on Macintoshes at the time and did not include this creation in his portfolio. Mr. Clippy, the iconic assistant from Microsoft Office, was a pioneer on the path to today's AI assistants. He is something of a forefather not only for today's AI assistants but also for Siri, Alexa, or Cortana.

But back to Steven Sinofsky. The Notion team with Steven did [an interview on the occasion of the launch of their assistant](https://www.notion.so/blog/clippy-walked-so-notion-ai-could-run) Notion AI. Despite the failure in the 1990s, Mr. Clippy was key to the development of artificial intelligence. Steven Sinofsky, then head of the Office team, describes Mr. Clippy as a premature and bad idea that suffered from technological limitations. Sinofsky emphasizes that the risks Microsoft took at the time were necessary for further innovation in AI. Today's assistants build on the experience with Mr. Clippy and have significantly advanced his legacy.
