# Awkward Silence
> Intentional pause after response that helps uncover deeper, authentic user needs during UX interviews.

Awkward Silence ([Awkward Silence](https://uxdesign.cc/how-to-talk-to-your-users-like-a-pro-dc77ef19d2e3#:~:text=we%20talked%20about-,working%20with%20silence,-during%20my%20psychology)) during a conversation with a user can lead to the most important things we would otherwise never learn. When we remain silent for a moment after the response, we give the other person a chance to say something extra – something that occurs to them only at that moment. And that's often where the most valuable insights are hidden.

Imagine a common situation. You ask someone how they use your product. They answer quickly. Everything is fine. And you want to move on. But instead, you stay silent for a moment. And in that pause, something changes. The person reflects, maybe looks aside, and then says something they wouldn't have said otherwise. Something they truly feel.

Silence feels uncomfortable mainly to us, researchers. We tend to interrupt it quickly. But that silence is important. It creates space. It allows honesty. The user stops answering as they think they should – and starts speaking as they really feel.

Using silence isn't a trick. It's a way to show respect to the other person. It means we're truly listening. That we're not just following a script of questions, but letting the conversation flow for a moment.

It takes practice. It takes calmness. And maybe a little courage. But when we learn to endure that brief moment of silence, we often hear something that would otherwise remain unspoken. And that can change how we think about what we're designing.
