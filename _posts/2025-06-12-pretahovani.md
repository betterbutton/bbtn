# Drag and Drop
> Drag and drop is a natural conceptual model for user interface control. It is used for moving objects between different locations or for applying functionality to an object.

Drag and drop ([drag and drop](https://www.pencilandpaper.io/articles/ux-pattern-drag-and-drop), [táhni a pusť](https://theses.cz/id/15vnu6/?isshlret=Manipulace%3B;zpet=%2Fvyhledavani%2F%3Fsearch%3DManipulace%26start%3D35)) is a commonly used control method in user interfaces today. It offers users a natural way to manipulate digital elements. When designed correctly, it increases clarity, speeds up work, and gives users a sense of direct control.

However, beneath its simplicity, drag and drop hides a range of design and technical challenges. From determining which elements can be dragged, to correctly marking drop targets, to ensuring accessibility and synchronization between devices—drag and drop is definitely not just about dragging and dropping.

Not every situation is suitable for drag and drop. It can reduce comprehensibility, especially on touch devices, and if feedback or visual guidance is missing, it may confuse users rather than help. To prevent this, every action should be achievable in another way—for example, via a menu, a button, or a keyboard shortcut.

Accessibility for all users, including those with visual impairments, must be a given. This means supporting screen readers, proper keyboard behavior, and appropriate element labeling.

[The origin of this interaction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drag_and_drop) dates back to 1984, when Apple introduced the first Macintosh. It was the first to allow users to drag a file into the trash—mirroring real-world behavior. This principle brought computer work closer to ordinary people and became the foundation of graphical interfaces.

As digital tools grow in complexity, drag and drop has become a common part of many applications. Trello boards, Figma canvases, email clients, or file uploads—it serves for smooth control everywhere. If you are designing websites or mobile apps, it is important to know not only the benefits of this model but also its pitfalls. Well-designed drag and drop is natural. Poorly designed drag and drop is just another obstacle.
