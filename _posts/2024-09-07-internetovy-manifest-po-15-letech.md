# Internet Manifest Still Relevant After 15 Years
> 

Fifteen years ago, on Monday, September 7, 2009, a group of fifteen German journalists published the [Internet Manifest](https://www.internet-manifest.cz/). The document presents seventeen principles for the functioning of modern journalism in the digital age. Even with the passage of time, the manifesto remains relevant—it's hard to say whether this is a good reflection of the state of journalism or if the original authors simply managed to capture the core of the problem.

The authors emphasize that the internet has changed the media landscape and requires new approaches to publishing, funding, and interaction with the public. Content quality remains key, while traditional business models are giving way to new forms of monetization. The manifesto also defends internet freedom and supports open dialogue between journalists and users. The goal is to create journalism that utilizes all the possibilities the internet offers and ensures access to information for everyone.

The Internet Manifest was released two years after the iPhone was introduced to the market and before the mass spread of Facebook. Social networks like Instagram or TikTok did not yet exist. YouTube was under Google's ownership for three years at that time.

The German original and original location of the Internet Manifest can today only be found via the Wayback Machine of the Internet Archive.
