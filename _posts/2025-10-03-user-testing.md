# User Testing
> Observing real people use your product reveals hidden flaws before it reaches the world.

[User Testing](https://www.proofreason.com/blog/metody-uzivatelskeho-testovani) ([user testing](https://userpilot.com/blog/what-is-user-testing/)) is one of the fastest and cheapest ways to uncover design flaws. Even short testing with just five people can reveal fundamental usability issues. What users actually do often surprises you more than what they say they will do. Timely and repeated observation of real user behavior helps uncover blind spots and validate what truly works.

Why do we avoid testing? Because it's not pleasant. You watch someone struggle with what you designed. But it's not a critique of your work. It's an opportunity to improve it. If you skip testing, you'll get that feedback later—and it will be more expensive.

As designers, we often assume the interface is clear. But if a user can't find or understand something, it's a system problem, not a human problem. Testing reveals these hidden spots—moments when reality falls short of our assumptions.

Don't wait for an analyst to show you people are leaving. By then it's usually too late. Test early. Test often. It's the most honest feedback you can get.
