# That User Research Is Easy Is a Myth
> 

Lesley Crane already [has enough of the trivialization of user research complexity](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/why-the-idea-that-anyone-can-do-user-research-is-a-bad-one-8b259d4d67bb). The democratization of user research, which was supposed to bring better collaboration across the team, backfires on researchers when managers get the impression that anyone can do user research. The shift to AI is slowly but surely leading to the layoff of researchers and the notion that research with real users is essentially unnecessary.

Leslie reminds us of the garbage in—garbage out rule. *"Valid and reliable research isn't about a few questions — it's a science that requires training and discipline,"* she says, warning that if we let things continue this way, sooner or later the entire field of human-centered design and UX will be contaminated and undermined by the same muddled thinking.

![Six layers of user research from philosophical foundations to data processing.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/e33e7e15-feb8-4d85-ae8f-bdf33be5fcbb_1091x916.png "1091x916")

[Onion layers model for user research](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/why-the-idea-that-anyone-can-do-user-research-is-a-bad-one-8b259d4d67bb)

Truly valuable research is based on rigorous methodology and the ability to apply the right tools, which requires experience and training. Research isn't just asking questions, but a complex scientific process that cannot be simplified.

An example of such methodology is the *onion layers model*, a systematic framework for planning and conducting research. This model guides researchers from basic philosophical principles through strategic planning to specific methods of data collection and analysis. An adapted version of this model for user research emphasizes that quality research is a scientific process that cannot be reduced to a few questions or a simple procedure.

[The onion layers model, originally developed by Mark Saunders](https://jfsdigital.org/articles-and-essays/2018-2/towards-an-explicit-research-methodology-adapting-research-onion-model-for-futures-studies/), helps systematically plan and conduct research through individual layers – from the philosophical foundations of research through strategy to specific methods and techniques.

The main benefits of the onion layers model for user research:

- **Systematic approach**: Provides a clear framework for planning and conducting research.
- **Rigor**: Helps maintain scientific precision even in a practical environment.
- **Flexibility**: An experienced researcher can apply the model quickly and effectively.

The author argues that only through such structures can user research achieve reliable results.

Leslie [presents an adapted version of the model](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/why-the-idea-that-anyone-can-do-user-research-is-a-bad-one-8b259d4d67bb) tailored to the needs of user research. She shows that quality research is not haphazard but requires a thoughtful process that an experienced researcher can apply quickly thanks to their training and practice. The model is also a defense against the amateurish approach, as it emphasizes the importance of methodological precision and expertise that amateurs often lack.
