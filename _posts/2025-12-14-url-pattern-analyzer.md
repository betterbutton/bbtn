# URL Pattern Analyzer Shows the Structure of Website Addresses
> A simple online utility that extracts patterns from URLs and helps reveal a site's structure.

![Screenshot of the URL Pattern Analyzer tool](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/c60de22e-37aa-4965-a349-a5945b91dff2_1238x627.png "1238x627")

Just enter a domain and the [URL Pattern Analyzer](https://www.prokopsw.cz/app/url-patterns/) will tell you what the address structure looks like.

The [URL Pattern Analyzer](https://www.prokopsw.cz/app/url-patterns/) by *[Mark Prokop](https://www.prokopsw.cz/cs)* is a lightweight and fast tool that runs directly in your browser. It's designed to help you understand a website's structure – for example during SEO audits, redirect planning, or content migration.

Using it is simple. Either paste a list of URLs or enter a domain from which the tool will automatically load the site map. Then just click the button and in an instant you'll get a clearly grouped overview of URL patterns. You'll see how many addresses fall under which part of the site and how the pages are organized.

Everything happens exclusively in your browser – no data is sent anywhere. The tool can process up to fifty site maps and a maximum of three hundred thousand URLs. It can also handle sitemaps that are split into multiple files.

There's also an option to deploy the tool on your own server. The [source code](https://github.com/MarekProkop/url-pattern-analyzer) is freely available on GitHub and you can even contribute to its improvement. Although it has certain limitations, for example when working with enormous sites, it does its job quickly, clearly, and with an emphasis on privacy. [You can try it out for free.](https://www.prokopsw.cz/app/url-patterns/)
