# See–Think–Do–Care
> A digital strategy framework that segments the audience by intent. It serves to design and improve websites and digital products not just by conversions, but by people's real needs.

*[See–Think–Do–Care](https://www.kaushik.net/avinash/see-think-do-care-win-content-marketing-measurement/)*, often abbreviated as *[STDC](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/see-think-do-care-model)*, is a framework for designing and evaluating websites based on user intent, not just traffic or conversion rates. In the 2010s, it was popularized by *[Avinash Kaushik](https://www.kaushik.net/avinash/)* as a response to the practice of treating every visitor as if they are ready to buy immediately. STDC helps align content, UX, and measurement with what people actually want at a given moment.

At the core of the framework is the division of the audience into four concurrent groups based on intent. *See* includes people who are just discovering a topic and have no purchase intent. *Think* represents users who are comparing options and forming preferences. *Do* denotes those ready to act—to buy, register, or submit a form. *Care* focuses on existing customers, their success, support, and retention. These are not linear stages but overlapping modes of behavior.

STDC is often compared to the classic *[AIDA](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/aida)* model: attention, interest, desire, action. AIDA remains useful for structuring messaging at the page or campaign level. STDC, however, operates at the level of the entire website or product and explicitly includes the post-purchase phase, which is often neglected in digital design.

For UX design, STDC is an organizational framework. It reminds us that different intents require different interactions, structures, and success metrics. Discovery needs clarity, consideration needs decision support, action needs efficiency, and care needs long-term usability. Used judiciously, STDC helps create calmer, more human digital environments—and measure them by the right metrics.
