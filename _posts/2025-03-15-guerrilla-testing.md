# Guerrilla testing
> Guerrilla testing is a simple testing method where we gather feedback from users in their natural environment, such as a café or on the street.

[Guerrilla testing](<<<URL_0_0ac7a>>>) is a quick and inexpensive way to find out how people use websites and applications. Unlike classical testing, it doesn't require complex preparation or a special laboratory. You simply approach people in public places, such as a café or library, and ask them to try a simple task in the app or on the website.

The guerrilla testing method is ideal for quickly uncovering problems in the early stages of development. It allows developers and designers to get direct feedback and understand what users find unclear or complicated. Although testing in an uncontrolled environment can be influenced by distractions, it provides valuable insights that help improve the user experience.

For guerrilla testing to work best, it's important to prepare a few simple tasks that the tester wants to test. Each test should last a maximum of 15 minutes. It's best to observe how users interact with the application and ask about their impressions. Open-ended questions, such as "What do you think this feature does?" or "Was anything confusing for you?" help uncover key issues.

Although guerrilla testing cannot replace detailed user research, it's an excellent way to quickly gain useful insights. If this method is used regularly, it can significantly improve the usability of websites and applications and ensure they are truly intuitive and easy to use.
