# Designers' Frog Wars
> 

Design is hard not only because of its complexity, but also because of disagreements within the community. Specializations like UX, UI, or graphic design often focus on differences instead of common challenges, leading to the "narcissism of small differences".

> "We get stuck trying to win small battles and don't realize that we're losing the war."\
> **— [Scott Berkun](https://whydesignishard.substack.com/p/why-designers-fight-with-each-other)**

These disputes can weaken our ability to solve bigger problems, such as promoting design to key people or increasing the influence of the field. Although specialization is beneficial, a unified community would better contribute to achieving the common goal: improving the world through design. By the way, Scott Berkun, author of [reflections on designers' frog wars](https://whydesignishard.substack.com/p/why-designers-fight-with-each-other), has released a new book [Why Design is Hard](https://scottberkun.com/why-design-is-hard/.
