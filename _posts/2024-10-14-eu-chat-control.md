# EU Chat Control: Protecting Children vs. User Privacy
> 

The European Commission's proposal, known as [Chat Control](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=COM%3A2022%3A209%3AFIN), would require messaging apps to scan users' private conversations for child sexual abuse material (CSAM), [writes TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/12/chat-control-the-eus-controversial-csam-scanning-legal-proposal-explained/). The European Parliament proposes changes that would limit the scanning to suspicious individuals, thereby minimizing mass user monitoring.

The law would threaten encrypted apps (E2EE) by forcing platforms to deploy message scanning technology, which can lead to false positives and mass surveillance. Critics argue that this move undermines user privacy and security without effectively preventing abuse. The proposal still faces significant opposition and it is uncertain whether it will pass.
