# Linkodrome
> A Czech slang word that perfectly captures the fine line between clever improvement and unnecessary complexity.

[Linkodrome](https://cestina20.cz/slovnik/vyfikundace/) is a Czech term that perfectly describes a situation where a good idea in design backfires on its author. It means both "clever improvement" and "overly clever nonsense."

For web designers and site administrators, it's the precise name for those small tweaks that start with good intentions and end in frustration—a shiny element, an unnecessary script, or a feature no one asked for.

The word comes from German, from the expression *pfiffig*, which means clever, sly, or inventive. It passed into Czech already in the last century. "Linkodrome" sounds a bit cheeky, but it's used more playfully than vulgarly, although a sharp ear might hear the obscene word starting with 'k'. Similarly, in the word, you can hear *wi-fi* and it refers to technical features.

In everyday speech, it can mean both a brilliant idea and a ridiculous, over-engineered gimmick. And that's exactly what fits into today's digital world, where creativity and overdoing it so often get mixed together.

When trying to translate it into English, you'll sweat a little. It could just as well be [gizmo](https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=gizmo) (with a hint of genitalia), [contraption](https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=contraption), or [gimmick](https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=Gimmick), or perhaps a fancy add-on.

In web design, "linkodrome" denotes a common malady: an unnecessary enhancement that complicates navigation, slows loading, or breaks the interface's purpose. It deepens what *Don Norman* calls the "[gulf of execution and evaluation](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/gulf-of-execution-and-evaluation)"—users find it harder to orient themselves and even harder to understand what's happening. With physical products, deploying linkodromes sometimes also leads to [unnecessary waste](https://medium.com/@eduardomagrani/welcome-to-internet-of-useless-things-7730b71ae42).

At the same time, this word can also appreciate genuine inventiveness. A good linkodrome is a small, thoughtful trick born from observing real usage. It's just a matter of recognizing the moment when an idea helps users and when it merely embellishes a problem.

Before adding another brilliant feature or effect to your website, try to stop and ask: Does this help or hinder? It's precisely at that moment that the difference is made between a good linkodrome and a bad one, between a useful idea and digital self-satisfaction.
