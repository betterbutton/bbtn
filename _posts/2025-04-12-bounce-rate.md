# Bounce Rate
> Bounce rate refers to the percentage of visitors who enter a webpage and leave it without any further interaction.

**Bounce Rate** ([bounce rate](https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/12195621?hl=cs)) shows how many people come to a page and leave immediately. They don't click further, they don't react in any way. It's a simple number, but its meaning can be complex. Sometimes it indicates a problem. For example, if a page loads slowly, is confusing, or doesn't meet expectations. Other times, it's fine. A visitor arrives, finds an answer, and leaves satisfied.

It depends on what kind of page it is. For articles or help pages, it's common for people to leave right away. For a product landing page, it might mean the content didn't deliver on its promise. If a visitor doesn't react in any way, it's possible they didn't find what they were looking for.

Website administrators often just look at the number. But it's more important to ask why. Did people leave because something discouraged them? Or because they didn't need anything more? Is the page clear? Does it offer a clear direction to continue?

Bounce rate makes sense in connection with other data. How long did visitors stay? How many of them did something? Not every quick exit means failure. In fact, bounce rate is a quiet message from visitors. Maybe they didn't click, but they told you something. You just need to listen.
