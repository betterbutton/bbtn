## E

[Engelbart, Douglas](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/douglas-engelbart) – American engineer who didn't seek to invent individual devices, but to systematically enhance human ability to think, collaborate, and solve complex problems with computers.

[Eich, Brendan](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/brendan-eich) – Created JavaScript, turning web pages from static documents into interactive applications.

## F

[Feinler, Elizabeth](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/elizabeth-feinler) – Instrumental in the creation of the WHOIS system and top‑level domains.

[Fitts, Paul](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/paul-fitts) – Changed the understanding of movement, response, and control in user interfaces.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## G

[Garrett, Jesse James](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jesse-james-garrett) – User experience pioneer who gave designers a framework, a vocabulary, and a name for the web revolution.

[Goldberg, Adele](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/adele-goldberg) – Co‑created the modern graphical user interface with windows, icons, menus, and the mouse cursor.

[Goldwasser, Shafi](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/shafi-goldwasser) – Pioneer whose theories enable digital communication to be private, secure, and trustworthy.

[Goodwin, Kim](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kim-goodwin) – UX pioneer who reshapes design leadership through organizational ethics, human values, and strategic influence.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## K

[Kare, Susan](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/susan-kare) – Designer who transformed computer interfaces with icons like Happy Mac or the Command key symbol, making technology more understandable for everyone.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## L

[Laurel, Brenda](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/brenda-laurel) – Moved HCI from interface mechanics to lived experience and fundamentally influenced how UX understands agency, engagement, and meaning.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## M

[Mayer, Marissa](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/marissa-mayer) – Engineer and manager at Google, director at Yahoo, who shaped web usability, global search design, and everyday tools.

[Miller, George Armitage](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/george-miller) – American cognitive psychologist who showed that human limitations are not a barrier to design, but a key to understanding and respecting human thinking.

[Molich, Rolf](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/rolf-molich) – Danish engineer, co‑author of heuristic evaluation, and leader of comparative usability studies who has shaped UX practice since the 1980s.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## N

[Norman, Donald Arthur](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/don-norman) – Shifted responsibility for errors, usability, and ethics from users to design itself. Defined the intellectual foundations of user experience and opened the question of moral responsibility in design.

## R

[Raskin, Jef](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jef-raskin) – Held that a humane interface means designing computers based on human thinking. The computer adapts to the person, not the person to the computer.

[Resig, John](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/john-resig) – Creator of jQuery, which changed JavaScript, championed open source, and enabled millions to build for the web.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)

## S

[Shneiderman, Ben](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/ben-shneiderman) – Connected computer science with design and influenced how we interact with technology today.

[Sinclair, Clive](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/clive-sinclair) – British inventor who changed the world of technology by making computers accessible to the general public.

[Spool, Jared](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jared-spool) – pioneer of usability testing and advocate for evidence‑based design who shaped modern UX.

[Suchman, Lucy](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/lucy-suchman) – Researcher who fundamentally transformed the field of human‑computer interaction by showing that human action is always situated – and that technology redistributes responsibility.

[Swartz, Aaron](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/aaron-swartz) – Saw the web as civic infrastructure – a space where technical design, legal frameworks, and ethical responsibility cannot be separated.

[▲ up](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/168273133/)
