# UX Maturity Model
> UX Maturity Model – A structured framework for assessing how well an organization integrates user experience into product development and decision-making processes.

The **UX Maturity Model** ([UX maturity model](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/ux-maturity)) helps companies understand how well they handle user experience. It shows whether UX is addressed at all, or if it is part of every decision. This allows teams to better understand what they are doing well and what needs improvement.

There are several types of models. [Nielsen's model](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-maturity-model/) describes six levels—from the very beginning to a state where UX is user-driven. Each level reflects what the team looks like, how much support it has, whether it conducts research, and whether UX is actually used in decision-making.

Companies with low maturity often have no UX at all. Or they perceive it only as something that is done at the end. In better cases, they already conduct testing and have processes, but UX is separate from the rest of the company. The most mature teams have UX integrated with everything. Everyone collaborates, leadership supports the team, and outputs are regularly measured and improved.

When a team understands where it stands, it more easily recognizes why something isn't working. For example, why people don't understand the product, why design changes frequently, or why no one wants to engage in testing. The model shows how to improve. Not just by acquiring new tools, but primarily by collaborating better and listening to users.

For people who create websites or applications, this is an important step. It helps ensure that the final product will be not only functional but also pleasant and understandable for those who use it.
