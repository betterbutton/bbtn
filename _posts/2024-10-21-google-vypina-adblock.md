# Google disables adblock
> 

[Google is disabling support for extensions](https://www.theverge.com/2024/5/30/24168057/google-chrome-extension-change-manifest-v3-ad-blockers?fbclid=IwY2xjawGDKxlleHRuA2FlbQIxMAABHSk37aDsgjYgSo2yz1vQY_H0WQqG9KEXHXvrCuZNDLprWM_YJ-GilMStLA_aem_bkKQ3S0b5IaI-g9L4Lhm4w), which are based on Manifest V2 in the Chrome browser. This means the end of the popular adblocker [uBlock Origin](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ublock-origin/cjpalhdlnbpafiamejdnhcphjbkeiagm)'s functionality. Users can switch to the less effective version [uBlock Origin Lite](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ublock-origin-lite/ddkjiahejlhfcafbddmgiahcphecmpfh?hl=en), or [change the browser](https://alternativeto.net/category/browsers/chromium-based/), to continue blocking ads. Alternative browsers include Brave, Opera, or Vivaldi, which still support the older system. Google defends itself by saying that [most extensions have already moved to the safer Manifest V3](https://www.theverge.com/2024/5/30/24168057/google-chrome-extension-change-manifest-v3-ad-blockers?fbclid=IwY2xjawGDKxlleHRuA2FlbQIxMAABHSk37aDsgjYgSo2yz1vQY_H0WQqG9KEXHXvrCuZNDLprWM_YJ-GilMStLA_aem_bkKQ3S0b5IaI-g9L4Lhm4w).
