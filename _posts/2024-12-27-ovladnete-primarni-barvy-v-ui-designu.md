# Master Primary Colors in UI Design
> 

Primary colors are the foundation of visual consistency in UI design. They are used to create tones and shades that [define the hierarchy and states of components](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/everything-you-want-to-know-about-primary-colors-in-ui-design-9de311c49398). Lighter tones (e.g., 100—300 in Material Design) are suitable for backgrounds and subtle accents, while vibrant shades (400—600) emphasize important elements like buttons.

Harmony is ensured by a consistent base color from which all tones derive. When working with colors, it is crucial to ensure natural transitions between states (default, hover, active) and correctly distinguish shades to improve usability and visual hierarchy.
