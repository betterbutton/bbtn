# Passwordless Sign-in
> Passwordless sign-in represents identity verification without traditional passwords, with an emphasis on higher security and a simpler user experience.

Passwordless sign-in ([passwordless authentication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passwordless_authentication)) means the user does not need to enter a password to verify their identity. They confirm it in another way – for example, by clicking a link sent by email, entering a one-time authentication code, confirming on their device, using a fingerprint, scanning their face, or iris scan. Passwordless sign-in is often faster, more convenient, and frequently more secure.

The technology for passwordless sign-in is widely available today. Implementing passwordless sign-in in the form of [passkeys](https://fidoalliance.org/passkeys/) works in all modern browsers and on most platforms. Developers can choose which verification method to use. Each has its advantages and limitations.

The highest security is offered by methods that do not transmit any password or code. For example, signing in via a device the user physically has with them. Such a solution is highly resistant to attacks and fraud. Better-known methods, such as codes sent via SMS or email, are conversely more vulnerable to misuse and [social engineering](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soci%C3%A1ln%C3%AD_in%C5%BEen%C3%BDrstv%C3%AD_(bezpe%C4%8Dnost).

Introducing passwordless sign-in also means considering what happens if the user loses their phone or uses multiple devices. This too needs to be appropriately addressed.

The benefits are clear – fewer forgotten passwords, less burden on technical support, and primarily sign-in that is fast, intuitive, and secure.

If you are creating a new application or modernizing an existing system, passwordless sign-in should be part of your strategy.
