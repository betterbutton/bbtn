# You are not the user
> You are not the user – a slogan referring to the mistaken assumption that other people share our needs and expectations. When designing websites and applications, we avoid flawed decisions through user research.

Websites and applications are not made for their creators, but for real users. Yet developers and designers often unconsciously rely on their own experiences. This leads to mistaken assumptions about how users will behave. This phenomenon is known as the [false consensus effect](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efekt_fale%C5%A1n%C3%A9ho_konsenzu) – the mistaken belief that other people think the same way we do. The result can be a product that feels intuitive to the creator but that users struggle with.

To avoid this problem, it is necessary to systematically verify our assumptions. Established methods include user testing, in-depth interviews, A/B testing, and analysis of actual user behavior. It is not enough to rely on personal judgment or feedback from colleagues – it is important to observe how people actually use the product and adapt it accordingly.

One effective practice is to list assumptions about user behavior and then verify them. For example, if we assume that a certain feature is easily found by everyone, we should confirm this through testing. If it turns out that users have trouble finding it, that is a clear signal to adjust the design.

Companies that regularly conduct user research and analyze feedback create products that are better received and effectively fulfill their purpose. Developing a website or application is not a matter of personal preference, but of the real needs of the target users.
