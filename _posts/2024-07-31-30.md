# Linkodrome

**Universal address form.** In elementary school, we learned to write an address on an envelope. But then one day, a request comes to design an address form, ideally in a way that handles addresses from different countries. That's when it turns out that not all addresses fit the format: street, house number, town, and postal code. Besides addresses in what are for us exotic countries like India or Japan, we might be surprised by something like [Luxembourg being not just a country but also a city](https://x.com/WalterStephanie/status/1817112720040182005).

In Shopify, they concluded that for some countries they have a localized form including structure and element order, and for others they attempted a [universal address structure](https://ux.shopify.com/designing-address-forms-for-everyone-everywhere-f481f6baf513). Etsy took it a step further by having a [specific address form](https://www.etsy.com/codeascraft/how-etsy-localizes-addresses/) for two hundred countries. And then there's the option to let users type the address into a single textarea. It looks like an [urban legend about astronauts using pencils instead of space pens](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/fact-or-fiction-nasa-spen/), but it turns out people then omit some part of the address.

![Drawing of an astronaut with a red star, writing something with a pencil on paper in free space.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/331bf974-37e0-4a23-9a05-55e507a83eb5_1792x1024.webp "1456x832")

The urban legend says that while Americans developed the space pen for millions, the Soviets sent astronauts into space with a pencil.

**Web Vitals are measurable UX signals for SEO.** [Core Web Vitals](https://web.dev/articles/vitals) represent key metrics Google uses to evaluate user experience on the web. They focus on three aspects: loading speed ([Largest Contentful Paint](https://docs.pagespeed.cz/docs/lcp) – LCP), interactivity ([Interaction to Next Paint – INP](https://docs.pagespeed.cz/docs/inp)), and visual stability ([Cumulative Layout Shift – CLS](https://docs.pagespeed.cz/docs/cls). These factors influence SEO, and improving their scores can lead to better search rankings, lower bounce rates, and higher conversion rates.

Luke Stahl reminds that besides improved user experience and search engine optimization, Web Vitals [also impact lower bounce rates and higher conversions](https://dev.to/builderio/an-seo-aha-moment-understanding-core-web-vitals-42pe). A website that renders quickly and responds snappily isn't abandoned prematurely by people.

![On an operating table lies a cassette tape with the tape spooled out, and around it are other cassettes like doctors and nurses. The cassette-doctor asks the cassette-nurse for a surgical tool: "Pencil!!!"](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/99c9f1a8-fae4-46ee-a4ba-5e8b479322a4_1816x1268.jpeg "1456x1017")

Pencils aren't appreciated only by astronauts; they also played a non-negligible role in rescuing tangled cassette tapes.

**Pencil and paper – popular tools for UX design even in 2024.** We have increasingly sophisticated tools for designing websites and apps, yet pencil and paper repeatedly [appear among designers' favorite tools](https://www.secretstache.com/blog/importance-sketching-web-design/). Why? Pencil and paper are the shortest way to get an idea out of your head.

When we recently met former BoB boss Marvan [on the premises of the Government Office](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/28#%C2%A7na-cem-prave-pracujeme), he had a hand-drawn presentation ready. Writing and drawing by hand is incredibly fast. The [speed, flexibility, and simplicity of expressing an idea](https://uxmisfit.com/2019/09/02/pen-paper-why-sketching-is-the-ultimate-design-tool/) with a pencil on paper is irreplaceable in the conceptual part of the design. You have them at hand anywhere and [are not limited by internet access or electricity](https://www.hongkiat.com/blog/advantages-pen-paper-computer-design/), which some designers deliberately use to cut off from distracting stimuli.

And as Martin likes to remind: once you have a finished design system, you only need to describe the interface in the form of sketches and diagrams. Using artificial intelligence tools, you can then convert your drawings into interactive digital form, but if you have a process set up with developers and product people, you often don't need any other visualization.

Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://www.katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 tips on how to ask for a website](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

Designers typically draw quickly. Sketches on paper are an opportunity to create designs and their versions and variants without blowing the budget right at the start of the project. A sketch will serve as a basis for both visual and functional design without details.

Sketching and drawing awaken creativity and stimulate the subconscious.

But beware! Just because someone can draw on paper doesn't necessarily mean they are also a good web page designer. After all, drawing by hand is quite distant from a digital product. For you, such a presentation requires a large portion of abstraction to imagine your website. After the first presentation of the electronic version, you might be unpleasantly surprised.
