# Reframing the Problem
> Reframing – the practice of redefining a problem or perspective in order to find a better solution, greater clarity, and alignment with user needs.

Reframing ([reframing](https://uxplanet.org/a-guide-to-problem-framing-ae58713364ec)) is an important skill in digital product design. It allows you to look at problems differently and thus create a better user experience. It can be a new perspective on a feature, a need, or even a bug. Re-evaluation opens the path to greater clarity and usability.

In practice, this means questioning what we take for granted. For example, we might think the problem is that users don't understand the instructions. But maybe the problem is that they don't understand why they should even start. Such a change in perspective leads to a completely different solution.

It also helps during testing. If a user fails a task, the error might not be in them. Perhaps the interface didn't explain the task clearly enough. Or it wasn't clear what the actual goal was. This brings us to fixable things. It's not about shifting blame, but about better understanding what is really happening.

Reframing itself doesn't solve problems, but often it is precisely when the real solution begins to emerge. Because without re-evaluation, a team might design a beautiful solution to the wrong question.
