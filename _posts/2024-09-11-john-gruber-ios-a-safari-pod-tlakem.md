# John Gruber: iOS and Safari under DMA pressure
> 

On Monday, Apple announced new features, and customers in the EU saw for the first time the consequences of the new Digital Markets Act and Digital Services Act legislation. Last week, [long-time digital world commentator John Gruber shared his observations on a similar note](https://daringfireball.net/2024/09/the_ios_continental_drift_widens) on his Daring Fireball blog: Apple must implement changes due to the EU's Digital Markets Act, which requires a mandatory browser choice.

> "In the end, we, the normal users, will suffer because of the idiots who decided to save us (…), even though we certainly didn't ask for it."\
> **— Apple fan [David Hořínek on Twitter](https://x.com/granatovyjablko/status/1832136695665279453)**

All Safari users in the EU will see the browser choice screen again after updating iOS, including when migrating to a new device. Furthermore, they will have to go through the entire list of browsers, which, according to Gruber, could lead to user confusion.

The new rules will mainly affect Safari users and will likely strengthen the position of Google Chrome on Apple's platform, which goes against the original intention to break up monopolies. The European Commission is focused on breaking Safari's dominance and Apple's integration within iOS, but this solution may lead to unintended consequences for users.
