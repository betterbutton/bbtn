**Robots are already walking.** According to the [2025 Bad Bot Report](https://www.imperva.com/resources/resource-library/reports/2025-bad-bot-report/), automated traffic on the internet has surpassed human activity. Cloudflare statistics say that on the web, compared to the entire internet, [the situation is still in favor of people](https://radar.cloudflare.com/bots). On the web, nearly twenty percent of robotic traffic comes from AI bots. Search Engine Land notes that [two-fifths of AI bots still do not respect restrictions](https://searchengineland.com/ai-scraping-ai-search-traffic-report-453160) in robots.txt (We're looking at you, Perplexity!).

While AI bot activity has doubled over the past year, [AI traffic to the web remains relatively sparse](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-search-traffic-conversions-ahrefs/) – according to Ahrefs, it's about half a percent. Ahrefs tracked a twenty-three times higher conversion rate for visitors from AI compared to organic search, and Semrush attributes [four times the value](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-search-seo-traffic-study/) to AI users compared to search engine visitors. But with mass adoption, this will surely change for the worse.

**LLMs.txt will help AI understand website content.** With the growing popularity of [vibe coding](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/vibe-coding), it's becoming important for creators of development technologies how well AI can utilize their tools. They could [tell you all about it in Supabase](https://x.com/kiwicopple/status/1934750289590128763). That's precisely why the `/llms.txt` file format was created. It provides robots with [a clear and concise overview in Markdown format](https://llmstxt.org/) that is easy for LLMs to process. The entire content of the llms.txt file should fit into the AI's context window, which should limit hallucinations, especially in documentation and educational materials.

If llms.txt can work for code documentation, SEO optimizers ask, why couldn't this format also make other content accessible to AI? Martin Malý [plans to use llms.txt in media](https://adent.substack.com/p/jak-udelat-web-citelny-pro-ai-agenty) at the Economia publishing house. When it's no longer about clicks but brand awareness, does it make sense to create two versions of websites? One for people and another for machines? Isn't it a step aside, like when we created a version of the website for mobile phones?

However interesting llms.txt is, it would be far better if there remained only one website. When content is understandable to both people and machines, it usually has a positive impact on the comprehensibility of the content for people as well. When you look at the structure of the llms.txt document, you might be thinking: Why couldn't documents for people be written just as clearly?

**Cloudflare builds a firewall against AI agents.** Last week, Cloudflare CEO Matthew Prince stirred the pot when [he said in an interview with Axios](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H5C9EL3C82Y) that in ten years, the ratio of pages Google serves to visitors dropped from 2:1 to 6:1, and in the last six months even to 18:1, because AI overviews answer directly on the page. For OpenAI, the ratio is 1500:1, for Anthropic 60,000:1.

Without clicks from search engines, website operators lose money from advertising, subscriptions, and prestige. Matthew Prince warns that a web without rewards for authors will collapse. According to him, the solution is to create an artificial scarcity of data and collectively block bots until AI companies start paying. Cloudflare launched the [Pay per Crawl](https://blog.cloudflare.com/introducing-pay-per-crawl/) service, which allows publishers to close content to bots and enforce fair agreements. Prince sees hope in a model where LLMs and AI agents pay for data.

For payment purposes, the reserved [HTTP status code 402](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTTP/Reference/Status/402) exists in the hypertext transfer protocol. It offers the possibility to revive the concept of micropayments. Only this time, it wouldn't be people paying for content, but their AI agents. This is also counted on by the [L402 protocol](https://www.l402.org/). PayPal is already [sharpening its teeth for the new paywall](https://x.com/positiveblue2/status/1938754335317598684).

**User research only makes sense if it leads to real changes.** Paul Boag shows why research often ends without impact – from poor timing to organizational silos – and how to prevent it. He advises involving stakeholders already in planning, aligning research with business goals, and presenting results understandably. [Paul offers a practical plan](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2025/07/turning-user-research-into-organizational-change/) on how to make research a driving force for long-term improvement.

**Quality is a trap, or Why the debate about quality in design is pointless.** Quality is a variable term. It depends on context and time. Many simplify it to aesthetics or technical details, but [quality design is more than that](https://ericwbailey.website/published/quality-is-a-trap/). Sometimes quality is even perceived as something not relevant at all, thinks Eric W. Baily. Instead of focusing on quality, we should invest in performance, security, accessibility, and usability, which have measurable and concrete values. Simply into quality quality.

**Are you thinkers, doers, or connectors?** [Each type brings a unique approach:](https://medium.com/@sarda.vaish/where-do-you-fall-as-a-designer-a38f4bdc70ab) The Thinker is focused on research and strategy. The Doer excels in implementing design and technology. The Connector *wait-for-it* connects both worlds. The Thinker invents how to solve problems and why they exist. The Doer translates ideas into concrete form. And the Connector maintains balance between both. Understand your role and direction in the team and you'll live better.

## So We Understand Each Other

The term [idea geyser](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/geyser-of-ideas) was probably coined by Martin. He uses it to fluster design students in the ideation phase when, during a practice assignment, he wants them to come up with a hundred variations of an app icon in fifteen minutes. The ideation phase is visualized in the [double diamond](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/double-diamond) by unfolding, and the word "geyser" further amplifies the idea of its divergent nature.

The acronym [OHIO](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/ohio) – Only Handle It Once – is easy to remember. In interface design, it leads to the adoption of [atomic design](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/atomic-design). But it also serves well as a general project management principle.

The word [accessibility](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/accessibility) we decline often, but what is it actually? Accessibility of digital products is no longer just a recommendation but, since last June's Saturday, also an obligation required by European legislation. Websites and applications must be accessible even for users with disabilities, but it improves the experience for everyone.

[Semantic web](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/semantic-web) offers a vision of the internet where data is interconnected and has a clearly defined meaning, enabling better interaction between machines and humans. This contributes to better search and connection with smart devices.

[Research repository](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/research-repository) is a central place for storing research outputs: data, interviews, artifacts. It allows building on previous insights and prevents duplicate work. It also helps with employee turnover, supports an evidence-based approach, and connects specialists across disciplines.
