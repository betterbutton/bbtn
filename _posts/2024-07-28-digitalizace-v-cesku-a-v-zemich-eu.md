# Digitalization in Czechia and EU Countries
> 

The Statistical Office released an [overview of digitalization in Czechia and EU countries](https://csu.gov.cz/produkty/digitalizace-v-cesku-a-v-zemich-eu-2024). The statistics track internet and digital technology usage in households, businesses, and public administration in Czechia and the EU. The data is relevant for monitoring the goals of the [Digital Decade 2030](https://vlada.gov.cz/cz/evropske-zalezitosti/umela-inteligence/digitalni_dekada_2030/digitalni-dekada-2030-192759/) and contributes to the European Commission's [Digital Economy and Society Index](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/desi) (DESI).

- In 2023, 87% of Czech households had internet access, which is one million more than ten years ago.
- Mobile phones are present in 99.6% of households.
- In 2022, 84% of fixed internet connections in Czechia were ultrafast (100 Mbit/s and more).
- Almost 80% of the population uses online banking, which is above the EU average.
- However, internet usage is lower among older generations – only 35% of people over 65 use the internet on a mobile phone.

The publication includes data on voice and data services, ICT equipment and usage, as well as information on investments and human resources in the digital field.
