# Wired Elements – a library of web components in the style of hand-drawn wireframes
> 

[Wired Elements](https://wiredjs.com/) is a library of UI elements that look hand-drawn. It is available as pure [HTML, CSS and JS](https://codesandbox.io/s/wired-elements-vanilla-4bpny) or for use with frameworks [Vue](https://codesandbox.io/s/vj389y9375), [React](https://codesandbox.io/s/react-wrapper-for-wired-elements-vid1j), or [Svelte](https://codesandbox.io/s/wired-elements-svelte-4hfkb). You might, like us, slap your forehead and wonder what such a thing is good for. Surely the advantage of hand-drawn wireframes is the speed with which we can design and the low level of detail we can consider during design. This is in contrast to designing in code.

![Example of components from the Wired Elements library.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/90de3b3b-36fd-43ea-a195-bbbaa9a84c0d_480x317.avif "480x317")

If you are designing for existing services, you might find that you are faster in code. You can connect your components directly to data, because you are no longer designing UI components, just using them. However, it still holds that when you then present early designs to stakeholders or users, they will assume hi-fi functionality and usability from a hi-fi output. Advanced fidelity of the presentation layer will mask problems in the concept, and they will surface at a time when it will be difficult to fix them.

In such cases, it pays to degrade the presentation layer. Then you might reach for a library like [Wired Elements](https://wiredjs.com/).
