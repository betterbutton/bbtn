# 8 Fundamental Principles of Information Architecture
> 

[Dan Brown](https://eightshapes.com/dan-brown/), a UX expert, proposed eight key principles of information architecture that simplify website navigation. Jack O’Donoghue diligently studied and [shared his notes with us](https://makeiterate.com/8-useful-principles-for-a-better-information-architecture/):

1.  **The Principle of Objects** – Content is treated as a living entity with its own lifecycle, behaviors, and attributes.

2.  **The Principle of Choices** – Provides users with meaningful options focused on a specific task.

3.  **The Principle of Disclosure** – Shows only as much information as is necessary to understand what they will find upon further exploration.

4.  **The Principle of Examples** – Represents content categories using representative examples.

5.  **The Principle of Front Doors** – Assumes that users often arrive at a website from various subpages, not just the homepage.

6.  **The Principle of Multiple Classification** – Offers different ways to classify content for diverse user preferences.

7.  **The Principle of Focused Navigation** – Content is not grouped unless it is mutually related.

8.  **The Principle of Growth** – Anticipates that the volume of content will continually expand.

Good information architecture requires thorough user research, conducting a content audit, and testing via tree testing. The foundation for success lies in repeated iteration and continuous optimization based on user feedback.

> “It’s important not to confuse information architecture with UX design – they are related but distinct disciplines.”\
> — **[Jack O’Donoghue](https://makeiterate.com/8-useful-principles-for-a-better-information-architecture/)**
