# Wireframes might be unnecessary, but lo-fi designs aren't
> 

Wireframing, once an essential part of UX design, is losing its significance today, [thinks Jason Barrons](https://uxdesign.cc/wireframes-are-not-low-fidelity-1e3e12b5cff0), although we don't quite agree with him. With modern tools like Figma and design systems, wireframing is neither faster nor more efficient than directly creating finished designs.

Critics argue that it often unnecessarily duplicates work and complicates communication with stakeholders. Instead of traditional wireframing, Jason in his book [UX Magic](https://medium.com/the-interaction-design-foundation/the-magic-of-semantic-interaction-design-1864ccafdc51) recommends methods like designing with placeholders, implementing [UX grammar](https://rcdoux.com/ux-magic/), or object-oriented UX ([OOUX](https://www.ooux.com/)), which are faster, more flexible, and better support iteration. The key is to abandon thinking of fidelity as a binary phase and view it as a continuous process.
