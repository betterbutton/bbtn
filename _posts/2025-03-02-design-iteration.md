# Iteration
> Iteration is a repeated process of gradual improvement of a product or system based on testing, feedback, and analysis. It is used in product management, design, and programming.

**Iteration** ([design iteration](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/design-iteration-brings-powerful-results-so-do-it-again-designer)) means repeating a certain process to achieve gradual improvement. It is used in many fields, especially in programming, digital product design, project management, and user experience optimization. Each cycle of the process brings new insights, on the basis of which adjustments and improvements are made. This makes the product or process more effective and higher quality.

In UX design, the iterative approach is used to optimize user-friendly interfaces. Continuous user feedback, A/B testing, and analysis of user behavior allow for the optimization of usability, visual style, and overall effectiveness of digital products. The cycle of testing and improvement leads to higher user satisfaction and better conversion.

Similarly, iterations are applied in the development of websites and mobile applications. Instead of a one-time product completion, cyclic development allows features to be constantly tested, evaluated, and adjusted. The iterative approach is at the heart of [agile development](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agiln%C3%AD_metodiky). Methods like Scrum or Kanban allow the team to work in short, repeating cycles called sprints.

Product management builds on iteration as a tool for managing digital products. Based on user feedback and market analysis, the product evolves continuously. New features are prioritized and strategies are adapted. Thanks to the iterative approach, it is possible to respond more effectively to changing customer needs. The product is thus optimized to have the greatest possible impact.
