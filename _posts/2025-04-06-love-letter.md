# Love Letter
> A Love Letter is a qualitative UX research technique where users express their emotional relationship with digital products through letters full of affection or disappointment.

Love letters to apps and websites ([UX love story](https://www.makingscience.com/blog/an-insightful-ux-love-story/)). It sounds a bit strange, but in reality, it's a powerful method to discover what people truly feel about digital services. In UX research, we usually ask what doesn't work. Here, we ask differently: what do you love? What would you miss if the service disappeared?

Just ask the user to write a letter. Some will vent. Others will open up. Some will thank. Others will say goodbye. And it's in these words that things otherwise hidden emerge: trust, disappointment, habit, relief, joy.

The letter can be about small things. That the app starts quickly. Or that it helps you fall asleep. But precisely these are the moments that often decide whether we grow to love a service or are quietly irritated by it.

For developers and designers, it means one thing: listen. Not to tables and numbers, but to real voices. This way, the user tells you what they love, what tires them, and what they no longer want.

This method, of course, isn't for everyone. It needs time, space, and trust. But when used correctly, it shows you what people really think. Not just about the design, but about the relationship they've built with the app.

A Love Letter isn't just a message. It's a relationship summarized in a few sentences. And it's precisely in that relationship that what truly matters is hidden.
