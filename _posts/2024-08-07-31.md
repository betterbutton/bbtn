# Linkodrome

**[Maintaining third-party cookies in Chrome](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/google-3rd-party-cookies-zmena) won't save the advertising business,** argues David Voráček on Lupě. The [problem of obtaining user consent](https://www.lupa.cz/clanky/cookies-apokalypsa-nekonci/) still remains: *"If you don't have consent, it doesn't matter whether the browser blocks third-party cookies or not. You still can't collect the data."*

Debates about the end of third-party cookies are already tiresome. [As Dušan Janovský says](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/04?utm_source=publication-search#:~:text=Osobn%C4%9B%20se%20domn%C3%ADv%C3%A1m%2C%20%C5%BEe%20cookies%203.%C2%A0strany%20nem%C4%9Bly%20nikdy%20vzniknout.): that technology should never have been created. The solution of privacy protection via browser settings is a consequence of browser makers not giving users a tool to control access to their personal data. EU lawmakers expressed their intention with the GDPR as early as 2012. Browser makers had enough time.

It will turn out that users will eventually turn off third-party cookies in Chrome themselves. The proportion of people who refuse to consent to data usage will gradually grow. Only large platforms with logged-in users will have the data. Others will have to find a different business model.

**Hardware on subscription?** Logitech is considering [introducing a subscription for a mouse](https://www.zive.cz/clanky/logitech-nekonecna-mys-predplatne/sc-3-a-229419/default.aspx). This time it's not about a mouse on installments or rental. It's about buying a mouse and paying for access to software features. Logitech's new CEO, Hanneke Faber, [presented this mouse](https://open.spotify.com/episode/78o9OVUAZGYSebEqp1noWt) in the Decoder podcast. The mouse would be bought only once, but users would regularly pay for software enhancements.

Logitech is already experimenting with integrating artificial intelligence into its mice, enabling features like AI Prompt Builder. The subscription mouse is still just a concept. The prompt builder probably won't be the killer feature, because you'll find such a function for free everywhere in a moment. But perhaps eventually there will be something specifically aimed at professional mouse usage under subscription.

We are already used to paying subscription for software. But it's always software for professional use. Software with functionality that is specific. The subscription must be beneficial to the user in that for a fraction of the price of a one-time license, they get access to functionality that allows them to earn enough to cover the subscription for the next months. That will be difficult with a mouse.
