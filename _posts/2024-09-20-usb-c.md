# One hundred days since the unification of connectors to USB-C
> 

One hundred days have passed since the moment the European legislation [on a common charging solution](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/electrical-and-electronic-engineering-industries-eei/radio-equipment-directive-red/one-common-charging-solution-all_en) for portable electronic devices came into effect. USB-C has become the universal port for most devices. The EU expects this to improve user convenience, reduce electronic waste by 980 tonnes per year, and save consumers up to 250 million euros.

The law also establishes rules for fast charging and the possibility of buying electronics without a new charger. The initiative is also considering future harmonization of wireless charging.
