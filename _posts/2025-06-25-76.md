## From The Designer

**Avoid overly long forms.** People don't like exhausting filling.\
\
❌ 20-field form\
✅ Shorter form or splitting into steps\
\
👉 Greater motivation to complete the action.\
👉 Fewer errors and frustration.\
\
Short forms → more successful completions.

## Linkodrome

**Brand and credibility now mean more than clicks.** AI Overviews in the [Google search engine fundamentally change how search works](https://www.lupa.cz/clanky/jak-ai-ve-vyhledavaci-googlu-zmeni-vyhledavani-a-seo/) and SEO rules, writes Pavel Ungr on Lupa. Old SEO is dead – now relevance, authority, and real value matter. Instead of measuring clicks, we track impressions and user behavior. The future favors those who understand SEO as a trust strategy, not a collection of tricks. Success in the AI era depends on credibility ([E-E-A-T](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/eeat)), technical website quality, and the ability to offer direct, useful answers.

> “Pursuing purpose in your product works out a lot better\
> than pursuing features for your product.”\
> **— [Luke Wroblewski](https://x.com/LukeW/status/1937502940102819882)**

**Collaboration between product people and UXers is often tense,** especially during prototyping and product feature decisions. In the era of [vibe coding](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/vibe-coding), when prototyping is becoming increasingly accessible, these tensions could intensify, thinks Christina Wodtke. She suggests designers use vibe coding for prototyping, which [allows product people to focus](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7340170089972867073/) on business problems like monetization and user engagement.

**You ignored accessibility. And now what?** The accessibility law takes effect this Saturday. If your website or app doesn't meet the requirements, don't despair. [Start by reading Eric Eggert's article.](https://yatil.net/blog/so-you-screwed-up-your-eaa-compliance-what-now) Many businesses, especially small and medium-sized ones, struggle with insufficient accessibility and missing internal knowledge. The first step is collaborating with accessibility experts, regular testing, and transparent documentation. Focus on your website's main features, improve them gradually. Accessibility is a long-term process.

**Most lost conversions leak in small gaps inside the website.** A Value Leak Map helps identify where [user interest slowly drains away](https://medium.com/@trendalchemy/the-value-leak-map-identifying-and-fixing-your-websites-silent-conversion-killers-0cf1fc265f41) – from unclear CTAs, through slow loading, to overcomplicated forms. You'll learn how to use heatmaps, funnel diagnostics, intent and obstacle mapping, micro-surveys, and how to address the most effective changes based on ROI. The goal isn't to overhaul the website, but to eliminate silent value leaks and maximize conversions.

Last week we brushed up on terms [MVP](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mvp), [Double Diamond](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/double-diamond), [loadding time illusion](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/loading-time), [fake door testing](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/fake-door-test), and [emotional design](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/emotional-design).

Double Diamond is our favorite design framework. We work with it primarily for its clarity and didactic value. No other visualization of the design process so clearly separates the problem definition phase from the solution search. No other framework so clearly shows that we must first explore options and go broad, and only then narrow the selection.
