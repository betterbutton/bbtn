# Zero-click search
> Search results that answer the user directly on the page – changing how we drive visitors to websites.

[Zero-click search results](https://www.strafelda.cz/zero-click-searches) ([zero-click search](https://neilpatel.com/blog/zero-click-searches/)), or situations where a user finds an answer directly on the search results page, are becoming increasingly common. Need to check the weather forecast, convert currency, or find a word definition? Google can handle all of this itself – without the need to visit any website. With AI overviews in search results, the search engine is becoming an answer engine.

For website creators and managers, this represents a fundamental shift. Your content may be visible and referenced – but without anyone actually visiting your site. Google keeps users on its own page by providing a quick answer before they even consider clicking.

This changes the rules of the game in search engine optimization. Today, it's more important to be seen than necessarily to get clicks. Earning a so-called *featured snippet* or appearing in an information panel can build brand awareness, but doesn't always drive traffic. The key is to structure data correctly and use schemas so search engines can find and display your content properly.

At the same time, it raises questions: What happens when platforms display content without a website visit? How do we then measure success?

Yet, not all is lost. Content that invites deeper exploration – such as opinions, tools, or interactive elements – won't fit into a snippet. And when a user truly wants to dive in, they still have to click.

Zero-click results are here to stay. And it's up to us to think differently: to be visible, to be understandable – and to create content worth opening.
