# Mental model
> A mental model is people's conception of how things work. They vary and evolve based on individual experience and context. Designers explore mental models to create usable products.

[Mental model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental_model) represents the way people think things work. The actual way things work is called the conceptual model or system image. If the conceptual model matches the mental model, we consider the thing to be usable.

For designers, understanding users' mental models is key. They use ethnographic observation methods to build empathy. Designers test early designs and rough prototypes to see how their concept aligns with users' mental models.

People form mental models based on personal experience. Therefore, they often differ significantly between individuals. Context plays a significant role—even the same person may apply a different mental model in different situations. Mental models form subconsciously and automatically. Moreover, they constantly evolve and change with new experiences. Therefore, in design, it is advisable to consider the learning curve, which can respond to changing mental models.
