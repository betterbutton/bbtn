# Google gives smart glasses a new chance
> 

You might recall the [Google Glass project](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Glass) launched on the market eleven years ago. Back then, smart glasses didn't catch on, but this time Google believes it will break through with the project. Google is slowly unveiling its [ambitious project of AR glasses with artificial intelligence](https://techcrunch.com/2024/12/12/google-wants-to-sell-those-project-astra-ar-glasses-some-day-but-it-wont-be-today/) under the Project Astra program.

These glasses, equipped with the [Android XR operating system](https://blog.google/products/android/android-xr/), should provide users with multimodal AI capabilities, such as text translation, navigation, or news overview without needing to reach for the phone. However, they are still in the early development phase and available only to selected testers.

Google sees AI glasses as the "future generation of computing technology," but the final product and release date remain unclear. Competition, like Meta and Snap, also share similar visions, but Google has a head start thanks to its advanced AI system Astra.
