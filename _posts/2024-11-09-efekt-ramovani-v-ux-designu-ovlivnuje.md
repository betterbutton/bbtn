# The Framing Effect in UX Design Influences User Decisions
> 

[The Framing Effect in UX design](https://uxknowledgebase.com/the-framing-effect-in-ux-design-06c017697a2c) influences how users react to different options depending on whether they are framed as gains or losses. The study by Tversky and Kahneman shows that people tend to be conservative when framed as gains (e.g., saving lives) and risky when framed as losses (e.g., deaths).

This effect is used by apps like MyFitnessPal or Flighty, which through framing highlight benefits and support user decisions. Besides [framing](https://www.scribbr.com/research-bias/framing-effect/), UX is also influenced by other [cognitive biases](https://www.scribbr.com/research-bias/cognitive-bias/), such as [anchoring effect](https://www.scribbr.com/research-bias/anchoring-bias/) or [social proof](https://www.ilincev.com/social-proof).
