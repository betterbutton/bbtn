# Personas and Archetypes in Your Project
> 

In the UX world, there is often confusion around the term "persona." Some UX professionals are sensitive when you use this term as a general label for a user model. [Personas](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/personas-why-and-how-you-should-use-them) require careful and resource-intensive qualitative and quantitative research. In contrast, an archetype is simply a label for a user model. Researcher Odette Jansen elaborated [on the similarities and differences between personas and archetypes](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7221765446729940992/) in a LinkedIn post.

[Both personas and archetypes present data about users](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/personas-archetypes/), their behavior, goals, and pain points. The main difference lies in the presentation style: personas have names and photographs, which foster empathy, while archetypes manage with an abstract label. Archetypes can be useful where there is resistance to personas or where quality research is lacking. However, both concepts are essentially the same, and their choice depends on the team's needs and the project context.
