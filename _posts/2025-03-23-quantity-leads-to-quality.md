# Quantity leads to quality
> Quantity leads to quality is a product development principle that prioritizes rapid iteration and volume of outputs over premature perfection to generate feedback, insight, and a better user experience.

When developing a digital product, be it a website or a mobile app, striving for perfection from the very beginning is often the biggest obstacle. Winning teams don't wait until everything is perfect. They deploy quickly, test frequently, and learn from the volume of feedback – [quantity leads to quality](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/onlife/temata/planovani-vyzkumu/kvalita-nebo-kvantita)[1](#footnote-1) ([quantity leads to quality](https://austinkleon.com/2020/12/10/quantity-leads-to-quality-the-origin-of-a-parable/)).

In design, content, and development, quantity isn't about lowering standards. It's about tempo, momentum, and the ability to respond. Sketch ten designs instead of one. Create several versions of a message and see what works. Deploy smaller features more often and gather data from real people.

When you create more things, you get more feedback. Every version teaches you something. Conversely, quality without actual usage is just guesswork. Even the most beautiful product is useless if it never gets out there.

That doesn't mean quality doesn't matter. It just needs to be built in the right order. First knowledge, then refinement. The more you put into the world, the more you learn. And the more you know, the better thing you can create.

In the digital world, done is better than perfect. And more experiments usually lead to better results.

[1](#footnote-anchor-1)

Quantity leads to quality is translated into Czech in various formulations. We chose the variant *kvantita plodí kvalitu*, which we know from [Lukáš Pittner](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lukaspitter/).
