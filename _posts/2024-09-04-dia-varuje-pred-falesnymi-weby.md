[The Digital and Information Agency (DIA) warns](https://www.dia.gov.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/TZ-DIA_varuje_pr%CC%8Ced_fales%CC%8Cny%CC%81mi_weby_.pdf) about the growing number of fake websites that pretend to be official state portals, such as [Citizen Identity](https://www.identitaobcana.cz/), [Citizen Portal](https://obcan.portal.gov.cz/), and [Public Administration Portal](https://portal.gov.cz/).

Fraudulent pages try to obtain sensitive information, including login credentials for online banking, and threaten citizens' funds. DIA recommends checking the correct web addresses and warns about the risks of clicking on links in emails and SMS from unknown sources.

In case of suspicion, it is possible to contact the DIA info line during working hours at +420 227 023 444.

The problem with this recommendation is that not all state administration websites are yet on the `*.gov.cz` domain, so that users could easily verify if they are in the right place. One such website is, for example, the aforementioned Citizen Identity, which is a shame.
