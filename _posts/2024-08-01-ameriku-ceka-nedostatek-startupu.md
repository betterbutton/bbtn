# America faces a shortage of startups
> 

The startup economy is suffering, mainly due to a decline in early-stage investments and a smaller number of new founders. Data from [analyses for Q3 2023](https://carta.com/blog/state-of-private-markets-q3-2023/) show that funding in Seed and Series A rounds fell by 50% compared to Q3 2021. At the same time, the number of new founders decreased by 47% compared to the average of the previous five years.

> “The number of new founder roles in 2023 is down 47% versus the previous 5-year average.”\
> **— [Jason Salzman](https://humancapitalist.substack.com/p/cause-or-consequence), Human Capitalist**

This negative trend is a result of investor caution, unfavorable macroeconomic conditions, layoffs, and an increased number of failed startups. Founders and investors are mutually dependent, and both need to take risks for the ecosystem to recover.
