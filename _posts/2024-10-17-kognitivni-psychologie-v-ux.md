# Using Cognitive Psychology for Effective UX Strategies
> 

Psychology is a key tool in creating user experience. It considers how people process information and respond to products. Cognitive psychology deals with perception, memory, and decision-making. It allows designers to improve the usability and efficiency of their designs.

Understanding human cognitive processes, such as the tendency to least effort, reducing cognitive load, and recognizing cognitive biases, leads to creating intuitive interfaces and increasing customer satisfaction and conversions.

Deborah Magalhães offers an overview of psychological approaches that will help utilize knowledge of mental processes, reduce sensory load, avoid bias, support conversion, or prevent errors. The article [Psychology as a Strategy in UX](https://brasil.uxdesign.cc/psicologia-como-estrat%C3%A9gia-ux-e620629fe73a) is in Portuguese, but it's worth having it translated.
