# Google faces a lawsuit due to monopoly. Again. This time on the advertising market.
> 

After an American court found Google a monopolist in search in August, it faces a second antitrust lawsuit this week. The U.S. Department of Justice is seeking to break up its advertising business.

The ministry claims that Google controls 87% of the U.S. market for ad-selling tools and [uses its dominant position to suppress competition](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/09/09/technology/google-ads-antitrust-trial.html) and raise prices for advertisers.

The dispute focuses on the acquisition of DoubleClick, which the ministry considers [a key element of Google's monopolistic practices](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckg2dpkpmv1o). Google, which earned $230 billion in advertising last year, defends itself by claiming that its [success stems from the efficiency of its services](https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/public-policy/google-ad-tech-sept-2024/) and that there are many competitors on the market, such as Apple and Amazon.

According to the lawsuit, however, Google [abuses its dominant position](https://www.seznamzpravy.cz/clanek/ekonomika-firmy-lid-versus-google-usa-zaluje-google-kvuli-monopolizaci-reklamniho-trhu-259226) and brokers advertising under terms that allow it to take 36% of every dollar. The trial, led by Judge Leonie Brinkema, could have far-reaching consequences for digital advertising.
