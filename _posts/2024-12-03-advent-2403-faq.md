# Prepare an FAQ for the Web
> 

You're probably scratching your head right now: what are we recommending now? [Yesterday pop-ups](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/advent-2402-popupy-a-souhlasy) and today an FAQ. Among UX designers, it's said that if a website needs a frequently asked questions section, it means the content and information architecture are poorly designed. After all, all important information should be readable in the regular website content, which one should reach using standard navigation.

If you add an FAQ section to a website, it can also mean that all efforts for clear content and navigation are in vain, because the gravity of FAQs is stronger and people will start looking for information prominently in the list of questions and answers.

# Creating an FAQ as an Opportunity to Rethink Website Content

Creating an FAQ itself doesn't necessarily mean you'll actually place this section on the website. Go through common questions from website visitors and clients, and instead of creating an FAQ section, work the answers back into the website as content and navigation adjustments.

It doesn't have to mean a complete overhaul of the entire website right away. For example, you could place a structured summary of the most important points on each page. You can use the FAQ to structure text and create subheadings. Or you can split the FAQ and place parts contextually next to the relevant page content. FAQs can thus function as an outline for writing website texts.

# FAQ as Structured Data for SEO

FAQ – frequently asked questions – are very valuable food for search engines (and probably for AI training today). The [FAQ](https://schema.org/FAQPage) section and [Q&A](https://schema.org/QAPage) page (questions and answers) have standardized their structured data using schema.org. You can embed them on the website either as linked data in [JSON-LD](https://json-ld.org/) format, or they can be built directly into the HTML as a semantic [microformat](https://microformats.io/).

# How to Create a Useful and Effective FAQ?

1.  **Identify real questions**\
    The FAQ should answer real questions from your customers. Review customer emails, reviews, social media comments, or form inquiries and select the most frequent ones. Avoid adding generic or barely relevant questions – they just bloat the FAQ and reduce its clarity.

2.  **Organize content structurally**\
    Divide questions into categories if there are many. For example: payments, delivery, technical support. This helps visitors quickly find the answers they're looking for.

3.  **Use simple language and formatting**\
    Write concisely, clearly, and to the point. Use bullet points or numbers if it improves clarity. Break long answers into paragraphs.

4.  **Optimize for search engines**\
    If you add an FAQ to the website, use structured data [in FAQ schema.org format](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/faqpage#json-ld). This technical step allows search engines to display your questions and answers directly in search results. This can increase your website's visibility and attract new visitors.

5.  **Test structured data**

    Test the result of embedding the FAQ on the website in the form of structured data for a rich snippet, so you know [how it will be seen, for example, by Google](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results).

A well-designed FAQ should be more of a supplement, not a replacement for quality content. However, if you use it smartly – with real queries, a clear structure, and an emphasis on SEO – it can significantly help both your customers and you.

An FAQ isn't just an emergency patch, but a tool that can improve the user experience while increasing your website's value in the eyes of search engines. Ideally, you won't use the abbreviation FAQ or the phrase "frequently asked questions" in the text at all. 🎄03
