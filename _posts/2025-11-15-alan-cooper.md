# Alan Cooper: From Code to Responsible Design
> Father of Visual Basic and pioneer of ethical design, Alan Cooper transformed the world of technology—first through software, later through strategic thinking.

![Alan Cooper drawing generated from a photograph](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a9a9650d-7813-41c3-8f2f-9583f405645f_1536x1024.webp "618x412")

“Usability’s strength is in identifying problems, while design’s strength is in identifying solutions.” ― Alan Cooper, [About Face](https://www.goodreads.com/work/quotes/280443)

[Alan Cooper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cooper_(software_designer)) (June 3, 1952) is best known as the creator of *[Visual Basic](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_Basic)*, a revolutionary *Microsoft* tool that made programming accessible to millions. His greatest contribution, however, lies in the field of user experience.

> “I like to refer to personas as the bright light under which we do surgery.”\
> **— [Alan Cooper](https://uxpod.com/episodes/personas-and-outrageous-software-an-interview-with-alan-cooper.html#:~:text=I%20like%20to%20refer%20to%20personas%20as%20the%20bright%20light%20under%20which%20we%20do%20surgery.)**

In the 1990s, he founded *[Cooper Software](https://web.archive.org/web/20170318121650/https://www.cooper.com/)* and introduced the [goal-directed design](https://visible.org/site/cornish/readings/files/ddo_article_cooper%202.pdf) method, which uses [personas](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/persony) – fictional characters based on real users – to ensure technology truly meets human needs. His 1999 book *[The Inmates Are Running the Asylum](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6644061-the-inmates-are-running-the-asylum)* became a UX design classic. In it, he critically points out that user interfaces should not be designed by developers.

As a frequent speaker and instructor, Cooper influenced design approaches at IBM, SAP, and Google. His subsequent book *[About Face](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/137333683-about-face)* remains essential reading for UX designers. He defined the designer's role as strategic long before UX became a standard part of product development.

Cooper's career mirrors the transformation of the technological world – from lines of code to culture and values. His ideas are more relevant today, in the age of AI and automation, than ever before. The personas he helped champion are now an integral part of design processes worldwide.
