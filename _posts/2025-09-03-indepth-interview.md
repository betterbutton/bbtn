# In-depth interviews
> A research method that helps understand user behavior through deep exploration of their motivations, context, and problems.

[In-depth interviews](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/hloubkovy-rozhovor) ([in-depth interviews](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/345737833_In-Depth_Interviews), sometimes referred to as [deep dive](https://blog.yotako.io/user-interviews-a-deep-dive-guide-for-designers/) interviews, are an effective tool for qualitative research. Unlike analytics or surveys that show what users do, these interviews reveal why they do it. They help understand their needs, frustrations, and ways of thinking. That's why they are invaluable when designing or improving digital products.

Interviews [have a freer structure](https://www.simonjun.cz/blog/guide-hloubkove-rozhovory) – instead of a fixed script, open-ended questions are used. This allows the conversation to flow naturally and lead to topics that might otherwise remain hidden.

For creators and managers of websites and applications, these interviews are useful at moments when something isn't working but it's not clear why. When a user describes their own experience – in their own words and without interruption – hidden usability problems, misunderstood features, or emotional barriers often emerge.

However, such an interview requires experience. A poorly phrased question or unintended leading can distort the results. A good interviewer remains neutral, patient, and open. Even silence or digressions from the topic can lead to the most valuable insights.

After the interview, careful evaluation is key. It's best to involve the rest of the team – joint listening and analysis brings new perspectives. Just five interviews can fundamentally change the direction of development.

In-depth interviews are not about quantity, but about depth. And it is in that depth that their greatest strength lies.
