Access to information is a human right, and typography plays a key role in making it accessible. In his talk [Typographer vs. Accessibility](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XQkCBdEEWCE) from the [beyond tellerrand](https://beyondtellerrand.com/) conference, [Oliver Schöndorfer](https://pimpmytype.com/bio/) emphasizes that accessibility is not just a matter of technology, but also of design—especially for the 1.1 billion people with various visual impairments.

He explains that good design must consider both functionality and aesthetics, debunking the myth that accessible text has to be boring or ugly. The focus is on proper font selection, contrast, and readability, all of which contribute to better accessibility and user experience.
