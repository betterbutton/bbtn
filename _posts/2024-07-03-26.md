# Linkodrome

**Google is driving only about a third of search traffic to third-party websites.** In [the previous newsletter edition](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/25#:~:text=P%C5%99es%2080%20%25%20dotaz%C5%AF%20na%20webu%20je%20informa%C4%8Dn%C3%AD%20povahy), we pondered what the deployment of AI on informational queries might do to website traffic. And yet, [only a bit over a third of queries currently result in a click-through to a website via search](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/google-opousti-tretina-prokliku). According to the [Zero-Click Search Study](https://sparktoro.com/blog/2024-zero-click-search-study-for-every-1000-us-google-searches-only-374-clicks-go-to-the-open-web-in-the-eu-its-360/), in 37% of cases, a search is not followed by a click on a website, and in 22% of cases, it leads to another search. That leaves about 40% of searches after which the user clicks a link in the results. Of those, one percent goes to an ad, and a quarter continues to some other Google service.

**What should a website for the deaf look like?** When it comes to accessibility, we usually think primarily of people who cannot see. But what about the deaf? People who communicate in sign language consider Czech a foreign language. Not all of them understand it perfectly. Sometimes it's better to create a dedicated [section of the website for the deaf](https://www.deaffriendly.cz/blog/jak-ma-vypadat-webova-stranka-pristupna-neslysicim) and offer videos with information in sign language there.

**Name the flaws of your website**

Think about what your current website fails to deliver according to your expectations. Do you know what clients complain about on your website? Ask your clients what they miss or dislike on your website. Realize that you are also a client of your website, just from the other side. What do you dislike about managing the current website? Ask your website administrators (internal or external) how they work with the website as it is: easily or with difficulty?

What bothers you might not bother your clients. Try to figure out why you disagree. However much minimalism in the user interface is a trend, most enterprise applications look completely different from Google's homepage or an Apple product for good reasons.
