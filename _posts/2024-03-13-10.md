# Better Button #10
> Hi! Google wants to remove AI-generated pages from search results. The EU wants us to know when we're interacting with AI. AI is in children's toys. AI is simply everywhere. Fortunately we can still read books.

# Linkodrome

**Google's search algorithms will focus on low-quality content.** SEO specialists say the [Core Update](https://www.digitalinformationworld.com/2024/03/googles-big-update-in-march-2024.html), happening now in March, aims to remove AI-generated content, copied content, and low-quality content created by website users themselves from search results. Come discuss it at [SEO Restart](https://www.seorestart.cz/).

**EU users must know when they're interacting with AI.** Last year, the EU decided to regulate AI with the regulation known as the [AI Act](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/topics/en/article/20230601STO93804/eu-ai-act-first-regulation-on-artificial-intelligence). It is expected to come into effect at the turn of 2025 and 2026. It distinguishes information systems using AI by risk level and [sets graduated rules](https://www.kapler.cz/ai-act-dohoda/) for each level.

**Can't click the map in Google search results?** Apparently, Google [addressed new regulations](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/06) to level the playing field this way. Fortunately, you can [turn the service connections back on](https://myactivity.google.com/linked-services). [Thanks, Mark!](https://twitter.com/VacekMarek/status/1766097588745306600?s=20)

![ ](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/6f878e7f-1c00-4d46-807d-cbdc745b5ebc_480x493.webp "480x493")

**Sliders are a terrible control element.** They are poorly controlled on touch devices. Basically, you never hit the desired value precisely with them. For some strange reason, they have become particularly popular with financial institutions. Adam Silver tried to design a way to [bypass these slider problems](https://adamsilver.io/blog/sliders-degrade-ux-so-do-this-instead/).

**Biometrics are coming to Google.** [Last time we complained](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/09) that Google, with the login screen redesign, is pushing [passkeys instead of passwords](https://www.irozhlas.cz/veda-technologie/technologie/passkeys-prihlasovani-bez-hesla-kyberneticka-bezpecnost_2305061503_ara) more. And hey, if you have Google Workspace, your administrator can [enable passwordless login](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/13548313). Then you just need to [activate passwordless login for your account](https://myaccount.google.com/signinoptions/passkeys). If you only use iOS and macOS, once is enough. If you alternate between Windows and Android, separately for each platform.

**Safe cars will have physical buttons.** Recently we [wrote about](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/08) Škoda also having rotary controllers on the touchscreen. Euro NCAP, an independent consortium that tests new car safety, in its 2026 protocol [specifies specific actions](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7170429367108448256?updateEntityUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_updateV2%3A%28urn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7170429367108448256%2CFEED_DETAIL%2CEMPTY%2CDEFAULT%2Cfalse%29) for which cars must have physical controls. The new car interface designs were also noticed by **Vitaly Friedman**, who added links to other [car interface style guides](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7171887899062321156/?updateEntityUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_updateV2%3A%28urn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7171887899062321156%2CFEED_DETAIL%2CEMPTY%2CDEFAULT%2Cfalse%29).

**AI is increasing demand for designers.** The question isn't whether AI will affect our work, but how. Henley Wing Chiu [analyzed five million job listings](https://bloomberry.com/i-analyzed-5m-freelancing-jobs-to-see-what-jobs-are-being-replaced-by-ai/) to find out which professions have lower demand after AI's arrival and where demand is growing instead. You probably don't want to make a living writing, translating, or in customer support, but it seems designers and developers are actually benefiting from AI so far. And video editing is experiencing an incredible boom!

# Living with Technologies

Jakub and Martin could talk for hours and hours about their love for [markdown](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown). You know the drill: you have to make a presentation, you open ~~PowerPoint~~ Keynote and after two hours you're staring at a blank slide, but at least you've probably set colors, fonts, and chosen a few images. You don't experience that when writing in markdown. It completely lacks the ability to set appearance. You just have structured text and you write.

A few days ago, they met in a cafe to discuss further plans. Jakub asked Martin what he was working on. And that shouldn't have happened! Martin opened [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) and showed Jakub how, [with a GraphViz plugin](https://github.com/QAMichaelPeng/obsidian-graphviz), he directly writes relationships between nodes and edges in a diagram right in the text:

    ```graphviz
    graph domenovy_model {
        label="Doménový model přihlášky"
        Poskytovatel -- Program [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];
        Administrátor -- Program [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];
        Program -- Cíl [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];
        Program -- Soutěž [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];
        Cíl -- Soutěž [ taillabel="N" headlabel="1" ];
        Soutěž -- Projekt [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];
        Projekt -- Uchazeč [ taillabel="1" headlabel="N" ];    
    } 
    ```

Martin is currently working on information architecture. He needs to generate a diagram with a site map from a table created [in a programmable notebook](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/141557352/zivot-s-technologiemi). The table will change and the diagram needs to be kept up to date. GraphViz is perfect for this. It's text-based and can easily be generated automatically from a table. The resulting image can be styled quite well with a few attributes.

They didn't get to discussing the plans. For the rest of the time, Jakub and Martin talked about [GraphViz](https://graphviz.org/), [PlantUML](https://plantuml.com/), [Mermaid](https://mermaid.js.org/), [writing notes](https://abcnotation.com/learn) and [tabs](https://hackmd.io/@docs/fretboard-syntax) in plaintext, and [plugins for Obsidian](https://help.obsidian.md/Extending+Obsidian/Community+plugins), and about [HackMD](https://hackmd.io/s/features#UML-Diagrams) and tools that can be installed via command line using [~~apt-get~~](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AptGet/Howto) [~~choco~~](https://chocolatey.org/) [brew](https://brew.sh/).

# What We're Currently Working On

Martin is teaching **Human-Centered Design** in the summer semester. He enjoys that for the first time he doesn't have to prepare the curriculum himself – he's working with materials from **[Filip Dědic](https://www.linkedin.com/in/filipdedic/)**. Mike and Erika are typical San Francisco intellectuals, so it's no surprise that when their publishing licenses expired, he published them at his own expense in magazine format and at a magazine price. Postage to Czechia spoils it a bit, but really only a little. Plus, you can always buy the e-book.
