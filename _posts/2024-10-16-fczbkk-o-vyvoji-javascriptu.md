# 

S10E03 - Riki Fridrich - JavaScript Development Over the Last 20 Years

## 

·PodVocasem

He focuses primarily on the technology [Signals](https://dev.to/this-is-learning/the-evolution-of-signals-in-javascript-8ob), which could cause a revolution in how JavaScript handles reactivity. The discussion covers how Signals might replace existing frameworks like React, and why JavaScript is gradually abandoning some old practices. He emphasizes that frontend reactivity is gradually moving toward native solutions, potentially making frameworks unnecessary.

> "In my opinion, Signals will be a big thing. \[…\] I personally think we are indeed on the brink of React ending up in a similar position as jQuery. \[…\] All modern frameworks that use reactivity will have to reflect this new change. Reactivity will move to the native level."​\
> — **[Riki Fridrich](https://fczbkk.com/)**

But an hour and a half is a long episode, and Riki also covers other topics. He speaks wisely about his career and the evolution of JavaScript, from the early days with jQuery to modern frameworks like Vue.js and React. Riki emphasizes how JavaScript has gone through a puberty period and is now maturing into a productive tool. Although React currently dominates, new technologies may reconsider its role and bring it closer to the fate that befell jQuery.

> "It's a sine wave and it's quite funny to watch how these young developers, they only see a piece of that sine wave and think, OK, now it's rising, so it will go to infinity…"​\
> — **[Riki Fridrich](https://fczbkk.com/)**
