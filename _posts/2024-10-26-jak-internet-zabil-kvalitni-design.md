# How the internet killed quality design
> 

Lance Evans [put on a gloomy note](https://www.creativebloq.com/design/graphic-design/the-internet-killed-design). He first looks back at the peaks of graphic design, and then begins to sink deeper and deeper into sorrow over how the pressure of technology transforms what we consider quality design.

Since the 20th century, when design ruled the world, through its digital explosion in the 90s to the demise of classic web design. The internet first promised endless possibilities for graphics thanks to new technologies like DTP, but web design [limited creativity and replaced it with technical requirements](https://www.creativebloq.com/design/graphic-design/the-internet-killed-design).

The boom of HTML, responsive design, and *Mobile First* brought a significant decline in aesthetic quality, because creators must design for various screen sizes. The result are pages built more on functionality than aesthetics, leading to stagnation in web design innovation.

Every technology shapes aesthetics. Technological innovations always go against established habits. What may seem like a decline to older designers is often just clinging to the old world and its orders. Web design as applied creation must primarily subordinate itself to usability, technical requirements, and business needs. And so websites, instead of an explosion of creativity, cater to simplicity, variability, and easy traceability. And that's no small thing!
