# AI companies are quietly ignoring the new California legislation
> 

[California law AB-2013](https://digitaldemocracy.calmatters.org/bills/ca_202320240ab2013) introduces an obligation for generative AI developers to disclose information about the data used to train their systems. By January 2026, developers will have to provide overviews on their websites about data sources, their purpose, quantity, and types of data points, including data protected by copyright or personal information.

The law does not apply to AI developed for national security or operating aircraft. This step aims to increase transparency and public trust in AI technologies.

Although companies like Stability, Runway, and OpenAI have agreed to comply, most tech firms, including Microsoft and Google, have refused [to TechCrunch's inquiry](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/04/many-companies-wont-say-if-theyll-comply-with-californias-ai-training-transparency-law/) to answer or comment on the situation. The reason for the silence may be fear of legal issues, as training data often includes protected content. Companies may face copyright-related troubles.
