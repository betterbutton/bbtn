# The State Fails to Operate Digitally and Delays the Deadline for Citizens' Right to Digital Service
> 

Lupa writes that the state is failing to meet the deadline set by the [Digital Services Act](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2020-12) from January 2020. [The government proposes to delay the deadline by another two years.](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/vlada-chce-odlozit-ucinnost-digitalni-ustavy-o-dva-roky-nektere-agendy-se-digitalizovat-nemaji/) The amendments to the Digital Services Act also include the possibility of exceptions for agencies that cannot be effectively digitized.

According to ministers, the delay should be used to expand functionalities and connect with data from private companies, such as insurers. The justification seems like an excuse – the state could fulfill the law with the feature set expanded later.

By 2025, 114 new digital services have been made available. Digitization has been delayed due to the slow start of preparations and the unwillingness of state employees to fulfill their duties on time, which now threatens their ability to meet the original deadlines.
