# Linkodrome

**Microsoft changed the keyboard shortcut for bold in Word.** No, it's not April. If you have MS 365, it's quite likely that the keyboard shortcut for bold text has stopped working in Word. Microsoft decided to change the shortcut to Ctrl-N – yes, exactly the one we used to use for opening new files. The new file now has no keyboard shortcut. Don't ask us about the product reasoning behind it. We don't know. Fortunately, we can [customize keyboard shortcuts](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/answers/questions/2120253/keyboard-shortcut-for-bold-text-changed-in-czech-v).

**CUPID Cheatsheet.** Clear, Unobstructed, Predictable, Indulgent, Digestible – or in Czech: Jasný, Bez překážek, Předvídatelný, Shovívavý a Stravitelný – should be the design. Hans van de Bruggen accompanied the book [Learnability Isn’t Enough](https://payhip.com/bookshansvcom) with a comprehensive [downloadable cheat sheet](https://hansv.com/book/CUPID-Cheat-Sheet.pdf). CUPID presents a system of principles focused on minimizing user effort. For each keyword, it offers practical tactics, such as quick feedback, removing unnecessary delays, or enhancing aesthetics. The cheat sheet serves as a checklist to help identify problems and optimize interfaces.

**Frame0 competes with Balsamiq.** [Balsamiq](https://balsamiq.com/), with its shaky lines and Comic Sans font, once ruled the aesthetics of wireframes. Today, you'd probably reach for the [Fisamiq UI kit](https://www.figma.com/community/file/880190575678066152/fisamiq-low-fidelity-ui-kit), which steals the aesthetic. A few weeks ago, we noticed a mention of the app [Frame0](https://frame0.app/) on [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41517312). It's quite similar to Balsamiq. It can be downloaded for Windows, macOS, and Linux. And best of all, it's still in beta, so its authors don't want anything for it. If we send you a lo-fi prototype in PDF soon, you'll know where the wind is blowing from.

**Don't underestimate the moral aging of websites**

Even the best website eventually stops fitting. It becomes morally and technically obsolete. New devices with new capabilities emerge that the old website doesn't utilize, and the risk of attacks and errors grows. Simultaneously, the original goals shift, and people need different solutions over time. Business also moves forward. There's no point in fixating on timeless design if it doesn't keep up with new requirements. Therefore, maintain the website continuously, or completely rebuild it once every three years. This ensures it responds to new conditions and technologies.
