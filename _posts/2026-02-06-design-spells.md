# Design Spells: a live archive of interface spells
> Design Spells is a collection of micro-interactions and user interface details that prioritize delight over metrics – and offer designers a rare source of inspiration based on emotional design.

![Screenshot from the Design Spells collection.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d72fcb6c-b7ad-423b-b354-957af4b6acdc_3840x2400.webp "1456x910")

[Design Spells](https://www.designspells.com/) – details in design that feel like magic.

*[Design Spells](https://www.designspells.com/)* is a carefully maintained archive of moments when digital interfaces feel almost like magic. These are subtle animations, playful interactions, and unexpected details that aren't created to optimize conversion, but to make digital products feel human. The project doesn't offer examples of best practice or scalable patterns – it focuses on small, yet memorable moments.

The power of Design Spells lies primarily in its curatorial approach. Each spell captures a specific situation where design meets development and together they create joy. These are moments that are short, easily overlooked, but crucial for the long-term memorability of a product. Design Spells thus cultivates the ability to notice quality on the margins – in cursor behavior, transitions, feedback, or small easter eggs that typically don't make it into strategic presentations.

The project's rhythm also plays an important role. New posts appear continuously on the website and are distributed via RSS and a newsletter that has been published regularly since 2023. This gradually creates a living reference, not a static gallery.

The project is the work of Singaporean creators *[Chester How](https://chester.how/)* and *[Duncan Leo](https://www.duncanleo.me/)*. Their collaboration itself illustrates the project's main idea: emotional design emerges most naturally where a passion for detail meets technical precision.

At a time when digital design is increasingly driven by numbers and efficiency, [Design Spells](https://www.designspells.com/) quietly reminds us that delight has its own value – and that the most important details often cannot be measured.
