# Linkodrome

**A four-point grid for consistent and effective interface design.** UI designers call it a [4-pixel grid](https://medium.com/@aratidube12lns/4-point-grid-system-for-more-consistent-interface-design-efea81dea3f3). The four-point grid aligns all interface elements to multiples of four. It offers greater granularity than the previously often used eight-point grid. It is ideal for [denser interfaces and various resolutions](https://uxdesign.cc/the-4px-baseline-grid-89485012dea6). It facilitates work for designers and developers thanks to easy alignment and predefined rules. The grid supports visual balance and [quick interface scaling](https://www.thedesignership.com/blog/the-ultimate-spacing-guide-for-ui-designers).

**Tailwind version 4 has been released.** Tailwind is a CSS framework. Frontend developers praise it because it speeds up web style development thanks to ready-made classes. [Version 4.0 brings](https://tailwindcss.com/blog/tailwindcss-v4) faster building, uses modern technologies like cascade layers and [container queries](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/23?utm_source=publication-search#:~:text=Kod%C3%A9%C5%99i%20je%20cht%C4%9Bli%2C%20ale%20vyu%C5%BE%C3%ADvaj%C3%AD%20je%3F), and allows easy configuration directly in CSS. In the new version, Tailwind has revamped its color scale and comes with more vibrant shades, with 3D transformations and advanced gradients. Installation is significantly simpler, saving developers' time and facilitating work on modern web projects. [Lubomír Blažek talked about the news at Frontkon](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RRmC5TG5VNw).

**At KISK, they are again opening a [distance bachelor's study program in service design](https://www.phil.muni.cz/pro-uchazece/bakalarske-studium/26780-design-informacnich-sluzeb).** Last time they accepted 14% of applicants. For admission, 54.5 points out of 100 were enough. The application deadline is midnight on February 28. Graduates work as designers, researchers, and product managers in online and offline services. Distance study takes place completely online and largely asynchronously, so it suits those of us who didn't manage to graduate in our twenties. However, it's not exactly a breeze and you'll sweat while studying alongside work and family care.
