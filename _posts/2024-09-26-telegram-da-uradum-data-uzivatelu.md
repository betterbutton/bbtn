# End of anonymity: Telegram will share data with authorities
> 

Pavel Durov, head of the communication platform Telegram, announced that [will begin providing authorities with IP addresses and phone numbers of suspicious users](https://www.wired.cz/clanky/sef-telegramu-nechce-do-basy-jeho-sit-zacne-uradum-poskytovat-uzivatelska-data). Durov faces charges of aiding illegal activities on Telegram, including drug sales and distribution of child pornography.

> "These measures should deter perpetrators of criminal activities. Search on Telegram is intended for finding friends and discovering news, not for promoting the sale of illegal goods."\
> **— [Pavel Durov](https://www.wired.cz/clanky/sef-telegramu-nechce-do-basy-jeho-sit-zacne-uradum-poskytovat-uzivatelska-data)**

The company has already introduced a moderation team that uses artificial intelligence to block harmful content. Durov blamed outdated EU laws for holding platform heads responsible for user content, but acknowledged the need for better moderation. Durov's arrest sparked a debate about platform responsibility, while critics claim that Telegram facilitated illegal activities.
