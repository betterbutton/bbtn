# Peter Merholz: UX Design at the Crossroads
> 

Design teams face changes depending on the maturity of the organizations they work for. [Peter Merholz](https://www.petermerholz.com/about-peter/) [in a little less than ten-minute video](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7265430103839391746/) compares the adoption of design to the technology adoption curve and uses the [maturity model](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-maturity-model/) from Nielsen Norman Group.

> "Design teams in some companies have shrunk from 20 to 6 people. They see design only as a production function."\
> **— [Peter Merholz](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7265430103839391746/)**

While mature companies fully integrate design and perceive it as a competitive advantage, less mature organizations often cut back on investments. These companies adopted design due to a trend, not a true understanding of its value, which led to failure. The key factor for moving forward is strong design leadership that can demonstrate impact on business.
