# EU forces Meta to reduce ad personalization
> 

Meta (Facebook, Instagram, Threads, WhatsApp…) is changing its approach to advertising in the EU, responding to [new pressure from the DMA regulatory framework](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/12/europes-dma-forces-meta-towards-less-personalized-ads/) ([Digital Markets Act](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/index_en)), which restricts ad personalization without user consent. European users who do not pay for a subscription will be offered less personalized ads, which include only general data such as age, location, and gender.

At the same time, Meta is reducing prices for ad-free versions of Facebook and Instagram. Although the company claims it still considers personalized ads the best option, European regulations are pushing it to make concessions to avoid fines for non-compliance with GDPR and DMA.
