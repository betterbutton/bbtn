# Linkodrome

**Don't build user profiles based solely on sociodemographics.** You're not providing designers with the information they need for design. Sociodemographic data tells you *who* your users are, but doesn't reveal *how* they behave or *why* they make certain decisions. This is where [behavioral data and qualitative research](https://medium.com/@taaminsights/do-you-now-your-users-introducing-behavioral-personas-8668f56b97d8) come into play.

[Behavioral data reveals actual user interactions](https://supademo.com/blog/how-to-create-and-use-behavioral-personas-to-drive-growth/#:~:text=Behavioral%20personas%20are%20semi%2Dfictional,lifestyle%20preferences%2C%20and%20psychological%20attributes.) with your website. You'll find out what they click on, where they spend the most time, or what makes them leave the page. Tools like Google Analytics or Hotjar give you an overview of what actually works on your site. But to delve deeper into their motivations, it's essential to involve *qualitative research* – user interviews, observing their behavior in natural environments, or mapping the user journey. These methods reveal *[why](https://userpilot.com/blog/behavioral-personas/)* [users act the way they do](https://userpilot.com/blog/behavioral-personas/).

[Behavioral data is a goldmine for designers.](https://buildux.com/blog/decoding-user-behavior-the-power-of-behavioral-personas) It allows them to create websites that not only look good but, more importantly, serve users effectively. They can tailor content and features to meet real needs and expectations. The result is a better user experience, higher conversion rates, and more satisfied customers.

**Creating a website is not an irreversible process; everything can be adjusted**

One of the greatest advantages of websites, applications, and the internet in general is the ability to fix errors at any time. Keep the webpage in a state where modifications are technically and logically easy. If you focus on continuous measurement and improvement, it will help you achieve better results. However, avoid building a website like a fragile system where changing one part could unexpectedly affect other elements. The web isn't heart surgery. It doesn't have to succeed on the first try.
