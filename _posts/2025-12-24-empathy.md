# Empathy
> A systematic effort to understand users' situations, constraints, and goals well enough to design interfaces that reduce friction and errors.

*[Empathy](https://www.manakmichal.cz/blog/user-experience/empatie-nejlepsi-nastroj-designera/)* ([empathy](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/empathy-in-ux-design-a1241eb47315)) in UX design is not a personality trait or a moral stance. It is a practical skill that helps designers, website managers, and their owners make better decisions about systems used by other people. At its core, it recognizes that users come to a website with different knowledge, pressures, and expectations than those who build it.

For people responsible for websites and applications, empathy is most crucial where failure is costly. Users are often distracted, stressed, or under time pressure. They don't know the system's internal logic or its terminology. Empathetic UX design accounts for this imbalance and reduces it through understandable structure, predictable behavior, clear language, and forgiving interactions.

Fundamentally, empathy in UX is not based on guesses. It relies on evidence. User interviews, usability testing, analytics data, and customer support records show what people actually do and where they encounter problems. Synthesis tools can help teams, but only when they stem from real research, not assumptions.

Empathy also has its limits. Designers cannot fully live users' lives, and trying to immerse oneself can lead to false certainty. Quality UX work therefore balances closeness and distance. It first learns from users and then consciously returns to design decisions within technical, organizational, and legal constraints.

In daily practice, empathy manifests in small choices. In error messages that explain what happened and how to proceed. In forms that tolerate mistakes. In navigation that matches users' mental models. In accessibility as a foundation, not an add-on. Empathy in UX is not an abstract concept. It is visible in how a system behaves when things go wrong.
