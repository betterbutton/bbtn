# Three Levels of Evaluation by Google: Document, Domain, and Authorities
> 

SEO expert Olaf Kopp formulated the theory that Google has gradually expanded [SEO evaluation from the level of individual documents to also include domains and authoritative entities](https://www.kopp-online-marketing.com/the-dimensions-of-the-google-ranking), such as authors or organizations. This evolution includes key updates like the Vince Update (2009), Panda (2011), and E-E-A-T (2014), which shifted content quality assessment to a macro level.

In SEO, it is crucial to understand three dimensions of evaluation:

- document level (content relevance to the query),
- domain level (quality of the website as a whole), and
- entity level (credibility of the author or source).

[E-E-A-T](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-e-e-a-t-how-to-demonstrate-first-hand-experience/474446/) (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) helps Google filter low-quality content, which is especially necessary with the rise of AI-generated text.
