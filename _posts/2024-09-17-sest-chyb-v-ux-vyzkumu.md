# Six UX Research Mistakes You Can Easily Avoid
> 

Even though UX research is an essential part of product development, many researchers make recurring mistakes. These errors can influence results and lead to incorrect conclusions. [Johanna Jagow has summarized the most common mistakes into a concise overview:](https://greatquestion.co/blog/ux-research-mistakes-to-avoid)

1.  **Insufficiently Prepared Ground for Research\**
    Conducting research and delivering results is not enough. Use every opportunity to present the value and benefits of research. Insist on sharing research at company events, write for the internal company blog or newsletter, and put up posters around the office with the most important insights.

2.  **Weak Connection with Stakeholders**

    Don't settle for just presenting research results to product managers and designers. Approach stakeholders not only as interview subjects but actively try to understand their motivations and obstacles, and offer help using research.

3.  **Falling in Love with Beautiful Presentations That Interest No One**

    Results of research are often awaited for a long time, and time is running out. Better than polishing a research report is to immediately after the research make an interactive board with notes available, clip important moments from videos, and share them with everyone who might benefit from the research in their decision-making.

4.  **Skipping Follow-up Actions and Not Defining Action Points**

    The presentation of results doesn't end the work. Arrange a meeting in a day or two to share proposals on how to use the research results. Don't forget to turn results into tasks with deadlines and responsible persons.

5.  **Not Addressing Skepticism and Establishing Your Credibility**

    Deal with critics of the research. Be transparent about the methods used. Share not only interpretations but also the underlying data. Invite colleagues to comment on the chosen approach even before the research begins.

6.  **Insufficient Participation and Building Empathy**

    Try to involve stakeholders in the actual data collection and evaluation so they have an unfiltered insight. Engage executive management with the research so they understand the purpose and value of research for the company's future.

For successful research, it is crucial to recognize and avoid these mistakes, which allows for a better understanding of user needs and creating products that truly work.
