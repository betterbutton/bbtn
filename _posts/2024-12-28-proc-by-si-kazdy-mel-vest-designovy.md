# Why everyone should keep a design diary

> 

Design diary, originally intended for sketches and ideas, has become for [Uchchita Joshi](https://www.linkedin.com/in/uchchita/details/experience/) an indispensable [tool for creativity and self-expression](https://medium.com/@uchchita/the-decade-of-the-design-diary-e11fd39d4ed1). The habit, formed during architecture studies, develops her observational skills, improves hand-eye coordination, and reduces anxiety.

The key to success is not to set strict rules and to be authentic. Diaries can contain anything from sketches and poems to lecture notes. Consistency lies in finding your own rhythm – whether in the regularity of writing or in the diary's aesthetics. Uchchita advises always to have the diary at hand, document everything inspiring, and enjoy the free process of creation.
