# Will Europe finally know the typographic rules? Typofix!
> 

[Typofix](https://www.typofix.org/) is an international project led by Filip Blažek and Radek Sidun and focuses on preserving the typographic rules of the 24 official EU languages.

The goal is to create a publicly accessible database that will preserve cultural diversity and facilitate the correct use of typography.

Part of the initiative is also the future development of a web application for the general public and institutions.

The project is funded by the Technology Agency of the Czech Republic and supported by other European institutions and experts.

The result will be not only the preservation of tradition but also the promotion of typographic accuracy in a globalized world.

If it turns out as expected, a set of algorithmic rules will be created that can be easily implemented into editing and publishing software.

In an ideal case, based on language information, the software will be able to automatically replace with correct quotes, handle single-character prepositions at line ends, typeset numbers properly, and so on.
