# Microinteractions
> Small moments in the interface that determine how intuitive, pleasant, and memorable your product will be.

Microinteractions ([microinteractions](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/microinteractions/)) are the silent heroes of digital design. These small, single-use actions—like tapping a button, pulling to refresh, or flipping a switch—often decide whether users experience joy or frustration. When done right, they feel natural and human. When missing or poorly executed, they leave the user feeling confused, even if they don’t consciously realize it.

Every microinteraction has four basic parts:

- **trigger** (what initiates it),
- **rules** (how it behaves),
- **feedback** (what the user sees, hears, or feels) and
- **loops or modes** (how it changes over time).

These elements together create a smooth flow of action and reaction.

Although small, microinteractions have a huge impact. A subtle animation, a brief vibration, or a visual cue can significantly improve clarity, usability, and overall impression. They are precisely what makes some apps feel natural and correct, even if the user can’t pinpoint exactly why.

Ignoring microinteractions means overlooking what users actually interact with most often. In an era where features are easily copied, it’s the details that create a sense of care and personality that determine success.

Designing these small moments isn’t about effects, but about clarity, understandability, and attention to the user. It’s these almost invisible moments that people remember the most.
