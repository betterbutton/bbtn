# Linkodrome

**Traveling the EU with just a phone.** [DIA writes](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7191114946615681026/) that the [revision of the eIDAS regulation](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/CS/TXT/?uri=OJ:L_202401183), which adjusts the recognition of documents in electronic form, has been published in the EU Official Journal. This means that anywhere in the EU, we should soon only need the [eDocuments](https://edoklady.gov.cz/) app with an ID card, and perhaps a driver's license and insurance card. The app already handles ID cards, so now we need the other documents, please.

And how does voice cloning work? Each of us recorded a few minutes of our voice. We cleaned the recordings of background noise and echo with a randomly found online tool from the internet. We then uploaded the samples to the ElevenLabs interface and had them create clones. We then assign these synthesized voices to parts of the text in the project editor in ElevenLabs.

Voice synthesis works quite well even without special text adjustments. Here and there we throw out a part that doesn't make sense without formatting or hyperlinks. Sometimes we adjust punctuation for better phrasing, and in completely exceptional cases, we add pronunciation to the dictionary. If you don't want to bother with creating a dictionary, you can of course simply write the word in the text directly in the way you want it to be pronounced, instead of the spelling form.

You might be thinking: Why don't we just narrate the text ourselves? We usually prepare the newsletter late at night or early in the morning. Usually, one of us isn't around. Or we're in an environment where it's not appropriate to speak out loud, or it's quite noisy around us. We already have some experience with recording and know that it takes quite a lot of time to get anywhere. The option to generate audio from text seems more practical to us. Last but not least, we would like to venture into English speech, and let's face it, our clones speak English much better than we do. We'll still be tinkering with the voiceover for the newsletter, but we'd soon like to start publishing a full-fledged podcast and webinars as well.

We'll be glad if you [write to us](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/18/comments), how you like the voiceover from our robot voices and how we could adjust the audio version of the newsletter to make it more pleasant for you to listen to.
