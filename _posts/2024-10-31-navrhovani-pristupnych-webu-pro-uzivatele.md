# Designing Accessible Websites for Users with Cognitive Limitations
> 

[Websites adapted for users with cognitive limitations](https://www.a11y-collective.com/blog/designing-for-cognitive-disabilities/) require specific design that reduces cognitive load and facilitates orientation. Recommended practices include clear labels, consistent layout, and the use of simple language.

And who are the people with cognitive limitations we're talking about? Users with ADHD, autism, or dyslexia face issues with attention, reading, or navigating complex interfaces, for example.

Testing website accessibility not only with automated tools but also with users who have specific needs is crucial. Web accessibility is an ongoing process requiring regular improvement and education.

Accessibility is becoming more urgent with the approaching effective date of the [European Accessibility Act](https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=1202) and the Czech transposition into [Act 424/2023 Coll.](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424?text=o+po%C5%BEadavc%C3%ADch+na+p%C5%99%C3%ADstupnost+n%C4%9Bkter%C3%BDch+v%C3%BDrobk%C5%AF+a+slu%C5%BEeb), which falls on Saturday, June 28, 2025.
