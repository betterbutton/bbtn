## Linkodrome

**How to handle accessibility in data visualization.** According to [Sarah Fosheim](https://fossheim.io/), [accessibility of data visualization](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z6jdEqz7qf8) doesn't end with adding ALT text to an infographic. She points out issues like insufficient contrast, incorrect color use, and poor keyboard or screen reader support. Sarah recommends getting inspired by well-designed solutions, such as the [Highcharts](https://www.highcharts.com/) tools or Apple's visualizations. It's important to understand that accessible data is not just an ethical necessity but can fundamentally influence decision-making.

Does [Zero UI](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/zero-ui) represent the future of interfaces?

**Product strategy needs more than just a workshop.** Using workshops and Miro templates as a substitute for real [product strategy leads into a sticky trap](https://newsletter.herbig.co/posts/351), says Tim Herbig. Product teams replace informed decision-making with various frameworks. Real strategy connects goals with measurable outcomes and should be a continuous process, not a one-off exercise. For strategy to make sense, it must be evidence-based, not intuition-driven. Templates can help synthesize decisions but must not govern them. The key to effective strategy is the ability to say "no" with a clear justification.

**Applications will die, the era of agents is coming.** Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella claims that [traditional business applications will die in the agent era](https://youtubetranscript.com/?v=uGOLYz2pgr8). According to him, SaaS applications are just a thin UI layer over databases, and AI agents will soon take over all business logic and data interaction. Nadella suggests that Excel, CRM, and other software will become just tools for AI that will directly manipulate data and perform actions based on user requests. (Thanks for the tip [to Machal](https://x.com/machal/status/1896283732707016874).)

**Banana test.** User testing doesn't have to be dry. Vitaly Friedman demonstrates a technique for testing interface intuitiveness where [labels for key actions are replaced with the word "banana"](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-test-measure-content-ux-vitaly-friedman-23kue/). Respondents then try to figure out which action is masked by the banana.

**Pong in browser tabs.** What to do with dozens of unclosed tabs?  supplies us with its [playful little games](https://eieio.games/). This time, in 240 Chrome browser tabs, he created an 8×30 grid and synchronized the movement of the ball and paddles through favicons. After experimenting with various games (including Snake), he finally chose Pong because it best utilizes the blending effect between the game window and the tabs. The result is [a fascinating demonstration of playful programming magic](https://eieio.games/blog/running-pong-in-240-browser-tabs/).

## So We Understand Each Other

A [retrospective](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/retrospective) has landed in our glossary of design terms – a team ritual that helps with relational hygiene and, when practiced correctly, step-by-step improves collaboration among team members.

At the Berlin [UXcamp Europe](https://www.uxcampeurope.org/), people were running in t-shirts saying *Not The User*. They belonged to a local research agency. This slogan refers to the lesson that [products should stem from user research](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/you-are-not-the-user) among real or potential users and not from the assumptions and desires of product people, designers, and developers.

[People remember unfinished processes well.](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/zeigarnik-effect) As if open loops didn't allow us to forget. They saved Scheherazade's life in A Thousand and One Nights, and they increase engagement and draw users back into applications.

Another design maxim says that things rarely succeed on the first try. And so [we design in iterations](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/design-iteration). First we come up with a concept, then a rough draft, and only then do we design down to the smallest detail.

    Imagine if we had to write social media posts in HTML. Mark up and wrap 
    text using tags. The internet would probably remain a privilege for nerds and geeks.

Fortunately, we have editors that display the final look directly instead of the source code. This approach is called [WYSIWYG](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/wysiwyg) and is common even outside the web, for example in office applications, graphics tools, and the like. Today this approach is so natural that we've stopped using the term.

Over fifty years, personal computers have made an enormous leap forward in user interfaces. From the command line through visual metaphors of the graphical user interface and direct object manipulation via touch control, we are reaching a point where we can completely skip the interface and task computers directly. Interface without interface, that's [Zero UI](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/zero-ui). But as skeptics say: there will always be some interface between the computer and the human.
