# Backlink
> A link from another website to your pages – it remains one of the factors for ranking search results, but its influence is gradually receding in favor of content quality and relevance.

A backlink ([backlink](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backlink)) once determined rankings in search engines. Google's original [PageRank](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/PageRank) algorithm treated every link as a vote of trust, and the volume of links thus determined a site's authority. Every link passed on strength, and links from the strongest sites passed on the most strength. Whoever had the most strong links won – regardless of their quality. SEO turned into a chase for links – through directories, exchanges, and paid publications. [The metric became the goal](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/goodharts-law) and ceased to be effective.

This model ended after the [Penguin update in 2012](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Penguin), which targeted manipulative link schemes and favored natural, editorially placed links. Since then, Google has built its algorithm on hundreds of signals – from user intent satisfaction to overall site trustworthiness. In 2023, Google representatives, including *[Gary Illyes](https://www.linkedin.com/in/garyillyes/)* and *[John Mueller](https://www.linkedin.com/in/johnmu/)*, said that backlinks are no longer among the most important ranking factors.

Today, links primarily serve to discover new pages and as a signal of trustworthiness. AI-driven systems, such as *[SpamBrain](https://spambrain.com/)*, can detect and neutralize the purchase or automated exchange of links without necessarily penalizing sites. Research from *[FirstPageSage](https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/the-google-algorithm-ranking-factors/)* and *[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/blog/what-are-backlinks/)* shows that a few relevant links from quality sources still help, but each additional one provides diminishing returns.

The best strategy is clear: create content that others will naturally want to link to – studies, tools, or guides – and carefully interlink your own pages. Mass guest posting, link markets, and automated systems are becoming pointless. An acquired backlink is thus usually just a byproduct of building high-quality, authoritative content.

The year 2025 confirms: backlinks still carry weight, but more as a correlation than a currency. Search engines see them as a mark of trust, not the primary measure of value.
