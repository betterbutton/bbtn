# Is Firefox tracking users or protecting them?
> 

Firefox has been testing [Privacy Preserving Attribution](https://support.mozilla.org/cs/kb/privacy-preserving-attribution) since July, an alternative to Google's [Privacy Sandbox](https://privacysandbox.com/), designed to enable ad targeting and measurement after third-party cookie support is removed.

[Live blog](https://www.zive.cz/clanky/firefox-mel-soukromi-uzivatelu-chranit-porusuje-vsak-gdpr-rika-organizace-ktera-sla-i-po-facebooku/sc-3-a-230398/default.aspx) reports that the technology is disliked by the Austrian non-profit noyb, which focuses on internet privacy protection in relation to GDPR and ePrivacy. While Mozilla claims PPA enhances user privacy, critics argue the feature tracks users without consent, contradicting GDPR rules.

> “Users should have a choice and the feature should be opt-in by default.”\
> — **Felix Mikolasch**, data protection lawyer, noyb

PPA is enabled by default and users are not informed about it. Legal experts from noyb warn that [this is another way to track users](https://noyb.eu/en/firefox-tracks-you-privacy-preserving-feature) and demand that Mozilla switch to an opt-in system and delete unlawfully collected data.
