# Good Design Is Invisible
> Good design is invisible – when it works, people notice the result, not the design itself.

> *You are opening the seventh window of the BBtn Advent Calendar for 2025. In this year's advent series, we'll look at popular UX designer proverbs. Today's is: Good design is invisible.*

Good design is the kind you don't notice. It recedes into the background and lets the user smoothly achieve their goal. When design draws attention to itself—through confusion, delay, or distraction—it loses its purpose.

*[Donald Norman](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/don-norman)* writes about this in his book *[The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/840.The_Design_of_Everyday_Things)*: good design fulfills needs so precisely that it becomes invisible. *Dieter Rams* expressed this with the principle *"as little design as possible"*—objects should serve, not show off. And *[Jared Spool](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jared-spool)* added: *"Good design, when it’s done well, becomes invisible. We notice it only when it’s done poorly."*

> “Good design, when it’s done well, becomes invisible.
> It’s only when it’s done poorly that we notice it.”
> **— [Jared Spool](https://talks.ui-patterns.com/videos/the-curious-properties-of-intuitive-web-pages-jared-spool)**

On the web, invisible design means seamless usability. Pages load instantly, navigation is intuitive, and forms guide the user without thought. Invisible design works with instinct, not patience. Every unnecessary step, unclear label, or delay yanks the user out of focus and reminds them they’re battling an interface.

But invisibility isn’t the same as minimalism. Removing ornaments isn’t enough if clarity disappears too. Truly invisible design stands on empathy, consistency, and care—in typography, in responsiveness, in speed. And its ultimate test is accessibility. If someone can’t use the page, the magic vanishes.

Behind every flawless experience lies thoughtful craft—testing, tweaking, and respect for the user’s time. Invisible design isn’t the absence of design, but its perfection. When a visitor leaves a page remembering what they did, not how they did it, then the design has truly disappeared.
