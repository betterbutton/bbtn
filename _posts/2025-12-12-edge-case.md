# Edge Case
> A rare or extreme user situation that reveals design limits and tests its resilience in the real world.

When a product fails, it's usually not because of a common scenario. More often, it's due to an [edge case](https://www.easyproject.cz/dokumentace/clanek/co-jsou-okrajove-pripady) ([edge case](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/edge-cases/)) – an unusual situation or extreme input that designers didn't consider. These moments expose system weaknesses and test user trust. Ignoring them means ignoring real people.

An edge case is any unusual but valid user situation: a name that's too long and breaks page layout, a slow connection that causes a form to fail, or a stressful moment when someone tries to cancel a subscription. These boundary situations most often affect users outside the mainstream – people with disabilities, older devices, or unreliable internet.

Experts like *Luke Wroblewski* and *Alan Cooper* argue that [good design must account for extremes](https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?554) without complicating common use. *Eric Meyer* and *Sara Wachter­‑Boettcher* introduced the term [stress case](https://abookdeparts-dfrl.netlify.app/) – not a rare bug, but a moment when the user is vulnerable.

The principle of inclusive design states: *[“Design for one, and you’ll help many.”](https://accessibility.education.gov.uk/training/accessibility-inclusion/page-15)* By addressing extreme situations, we improve the experience for everyone. A page accessible to a blind user is also easier to read on a mobile phone in bright sunlight.

Test products outside ideal conditions: try slower networks, assistive technologies, break forms. Design for failure scenarios – clear messages, fallback states, and preserving user data. Prioritize situations where harm or exclusion could occur; note the rest and return to them gradually.

Edge cases are not exceptions. They are part of daily life with imperfect devices, divided attention, and unpredictable circumstances. When we consider them during design, we create products that are not only reliable but also humane.
