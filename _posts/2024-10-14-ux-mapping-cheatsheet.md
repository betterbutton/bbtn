# Cheat Sheet for UX Mapping Techniques
> 

Sarah Gibbons clearly compares [four main UX mapping methods](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-mapping-cheat-sheet/): empathy map, customer journey map, experience map, and service blueprint. Each method has its specific use – from understanding user emotions to identifying weak points in business processes.

The correctly chosen method helps create shared understanding among teams, improve design, and optimize services. Sarah also offers a three-step framework for selecting the type of mapping and emphasizes that combining methods leads to a deeper understanding of both users and organizations.

Anna Vasyukova also returns to mapping techniques in the article [An overview of mapping techniques used in UX Design Process](https://medium.com/@a.vasyukova/an-overview-of-mapping-techniques-used-in-ux-design-process-756bd3d245bf) or Aaron Christopher in the article [16 UX mapping techniques to improve your Product development process](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/15-ux-mapping-techniques-to-improve-your-product-development-process-31daa493587f). If you've already read enough about mapping techniques in UX, hang the [UX mapping techniques cheat sheet](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7249403203908235264/) by Samaneh Dehghanpour next to your monitor.
