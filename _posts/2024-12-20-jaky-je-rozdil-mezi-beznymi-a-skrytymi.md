# Do you know the difference between regular and closed captions for video?
> 

Subtitles and closed captions serve different purposes: subtitles translate dialogue into another language, while closed captions include not only a transcript of spoken words but also other audio elements in the video. [Closed captions serve people with hearing impairments.](https://design102.blog.gov.uk/2024/11/01/subtitles-and-captions-whats-the-difference/)

Closed captions can be customized and turned on/off, unlike movie subtitles which are usually rendered directly into the video. [Closed captions improve accessibility](https://www.ceskatelevize.cz/vse-o-ct/caste-dotazy/pristupnost/#:~:text=Co%20jsou%20skryt%C3%A9%20titulky%20a%20jak%20je%20navolit%3F), help with understanding complex content, and are also used by people without disabilities in quiet environments where playing sound aloud is not appropriate. They are also popular when studying foreign languages.

Netflix states that [40% of their viewers watch content with closed captions](https://idealinsight.co.uk/infographics/netflix-captions#:~:text=85%25%20of%20Netflix%20users%20opt,two%20services%20for%20subtitle%20use.). When creating closed captions, it is important to ensure accuracy, appropriate formatting, and consideration for various devices.
