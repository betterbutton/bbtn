# What You See Is What You Get
> WYSIWYG tools enable visual website creation without coding knowledge. We track their rise, decline, and new future with no-code platforms.

**WYSIWYG** – [What You See Is What You Get](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WYSIWYG) ([what you see is what you get](https://www.strafelda.cz/wysiwyg)) editors brought a revolution to web design by allowing users to create pages visually without writing code. Tools like [Adobe GoLive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adobe_GoLive), [Microsoft FrontPage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_FrontPage), and [Macromedia Dreamweaver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adobe_Dreamweaver) enabled designers and companies to easily build websites in the 1990s and early 2000s. With the arrival of CMS platforms like [WordPress](https://wordpress.org/) and no-code tools, however, traditional editors began to decline. Today, dominant alternatives are [Webflow](https://webflow.com/), [Webnode](https://www.webnode.com/), and [Solidpixels](https://www.solidpixels.com/cs), which offer greater flexibility, cleaner code, and automation.

Many older WYSIWYG tools produced inefficient and inconsistent HTML code, causing compatibility issues across browsers. Once CSS and responsive design became standards, these editors couldn't keep up. Microsoft discontinued FrontPage, Adobe ended GoLive and focused on Dreamweaver. While Dreamweaver survived, it was gradually pushed out by cloud-based editors and modern drag-and-drop platforms.

Although both WYSIWYG and no-code simplify web creation, no-code goes further—enabling automation, database work, and interactive app development. While older editors were limited to simple layouts, platforms like Webflow and [Bubble](https://bubble.io/) allow building complex applications without programming. This renders traditional WYSIWYG editors obsolete, as they lack scalability, efficiency, and integration with modern technologies.

No-code tools, headless CMS systems, and AI now shape the future of web design. Modern tools like Webflow or Solidpixels let designers work visually while generating clean, structured code. AI automates processes and helps companies manage digital content without technical knowledge. As technology evolves, WYSIWYG editors are fading, but their contribution to accessible web design remains an important part of web creation history.
