# Evidence-based design
> Evidence-based design uses research and data to design products and services. It reduces risks and improves control over development. It is often combined with other approaches, such as lean or design thinking.

[Evidence-based design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evidence-based_design) – design based on evidence. An approach to design that relies on research and data. This approach has proven effective especially in areas where lives are at stake. Typically in healthcare, transportation, or when developing work tools. Thanks to pioneers like [Donald A. Norman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Norman), [Susan Weinschenk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_Weinschenk), or [Brenda Laurel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenda_Laurel), evidence-based design has become a common part of user interface design.

This approach allows for creating new products and services based on actual user needs. It provides innovators with information and reliable data, so they don't have to rely solely on random invention.

Evidence-based design focuses on creating and testing prototypes. This reduces the risk of unnecessary work in later development phases and improves control over business risks. For the final product or service, it also sets measurable goals. These serve to evaluate success and to gather feedback that aids further development.

However, this approach is not the only one. [Lean design](https://medium.com/@nizektech/what-is-lean-design-78193ddf08a) focuses more on experimentation and design adjustments during iterations. Design thinking emphasizes the work of diverse teams bringing expert knowledge. Participatory design directly involves users in the product development process.

In architecture, [speculative design](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/pristupy/spekulativni-a-kriticky-design) is popular, which often ignores practical or feasible aspects of designs. Instead, it tries to provoke discussion and change approaches to problems. In founder-led companies, we often encounter an approach based on vision. Each of these approaches has its advantages and disadvantages. It is often advisable to combine them to achieve an optimal result.
