# Tim Berners-Lee: Creator of the World Wide Web
> British computer scientist who invented the World Wide Web in 1989 and still defends its ethical principles.

![Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee in a short-sleeved striped shirt sitting in front of two CRT monitors and a keyboard. Generated drawing based on a photograph.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/1db5fdd8-2af4-4cb6-a719-bca27a1a08a5_1536x1024.webp "1456x971")

Sir Tim released a memoir book in September, [This is for everyone](https://thisisforeveryone.timbl.com/)

**[Timothy John Berners-Lee](https://www.timbl.com)** (*June 8, 1955*) invented the [World Wide Web](https://home.cern/science/computing/birth-web) in 1989 while working at CERN. He introduced URLs, the HTTP protocol, and the HTML language, which enabled global information sharing. Instead of patenting his invention, he chose to provide it for free, ensuring the Web remains open and accessible to all.

> “It was simply that had the technology been proprietary, and in my total control, it would probably not have taken off. You can’t propose that something be a universal space and at the same time keep control of it.”\
> **— [Tim Berners-Lee](https://www.w3.org/People/Berners-Lee/FAQ.html#:~:text=It%20was%20simply%20that%20had%20the%20technology%20been%20proprietary%2C%20and%20in%20my%20total%20control%2C%20it%20would%20probably%20not%20have%20taken%20off.%20The%20decision%20to%20make%20the%20Web%20an%20open%20system%20was%20necessary%20for%20it%20to%20be%20universal.%20You%20can%27t%20propose%20that%20something%20be%20a%20universal%20space%20and%20at%20the%20same%20time%20keep%20control%20of%20it.)**

His work fundamentally influenced society. It changed the way we communicate, learn, and collaborate. Sir Tim was awarded the [Turing Award](https://amturing.acm.org/award_winners/berners-lee_8087960.cfm), knighted, and named among [Time's 100 most influential people of the 20th century](https://content.time.com/time/subscriber/article/0,33009,991227-1,00.html).

> “I believed that giving users such a simple way to navigate the internet would unlock creativity and collaboration on a global scale. If you could put anything on it, then after a while, it would have everything on it.”\
> **— [Tim Berners-Lee](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/sep/28/why-i-gave-the-world-wide-web-away-for-free#:~:text=I%20believed%20that%20giving%20users%20such%20a%20simple%20way%20to%20navigate%20the%20internet%20would%20unlock%20creativity%20and%20collaboration%20on%20a%20global%20scale.%20If%20you%20could%20put%20anything%20on%20it%2C%20then%20after%20a%20while%2C%20it%20would%20have%20everything%20on%20it.)**

In 1994, he founded the [World Wide Web Consortium](https://www.w3.org/) (W3C), which manages open web standards. With growing threats like misinformation, data abuse, and the monopolization of digital space, Sir Tim began advocating for a [Contract for the Web](https://contractfortheweb.org/) and ethics in the digital world. He remains optimistic and believes the Web still has the potential to serve the public good.

> “The spirit there was very decentralized. The individual was incredibly empowered. It was all based on there being no central authority that you had to go to to ask permission. That feeling of individual control, that empowerment, is something we’ve lost.”\
> **— [Tim Berners-Lee](https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2018/07/the-man-who-created-the-world-wide-web-has-some-regrets?srsltid=AfmBOoqeVg9nZGTZzuSkbhNUcpEr8nff2mOLheWj-Rgc6ytZhzFZXyeb&utm_source=chatgpt.com#:~:text=%E2%80%9CThe%20spirit%20there%20was%20very%20decentralized.%20The%20individual%20was%20incredibly%20empowered.%20It%20was%20all%20based%20on%20there%20being%20no%20central%20authority%20that%20you%20had%20to%20go%20to%20to%20ask%20permission%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said.%20%E2%80%9CThat%20feeling%20of%20individual%20control%2C%20that%20empowerment%2C%20is%20something%20we%E2%80%99ve%20lost.%E2%80%9D)**

Sir Tim is not just a technical pioneer. He is an advocate for an open and fair digital world. His vision—that the Web must remain "for everyone"—continues to inspire both technological development and political decisions.

> “In order to succeed, therefore, it would have to be free.”\
> **— [Tim Berners-Lee](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/sep/28/why-i-gave-the-world-wide-web-away-for-free)**
