## From The Designer

![Example of centered text (wrong) and left-aligned text on a pennant (correct)](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/18daeb46-3e41-47a2-b530-2ac3a295369a_2160x1080.png "1456x728")

**Don't center everything.** Centered text may look *nicely symmetric*, but it's harder to read. The eye finds it challenging to locate the beginnings of subsequent lines when each starts in a different place. For long texts, center alignment is tiring.\
\
❌ Everything centered\
✅ An important heading is fine – but regular text is better left-aligned\
\
👉 The reader's eye loves left-aligned text.\
👉 Lines are held better and less is lost.\
\
Design isn't just about appearance – readability is also important.

## Linkodrome

**AI is changing how we build products.** Julie Zhuo describes how [AI is changing the process of digital product development](https://lg.substack.com/p/the-death-of-product-development). The traditional three-legged model (designer, PM, engineer) is giving way to small teams with blurred roles, where AI enables rapid prototyping and data-driven decision-making. Instead of planning, testing and iteration are preferred. It's no longer managers at the center, but individuals with good taste and a high degree of autonomy. AI becomes a tool for rapid idea validation and dynamic learning from real-time results.

**CSS is learning to program:** CSS is gaining more powerful tools thanks to [functions](https://css-tricks.com/functions-in-css/), which work similarly to those in programming languages – with parameters, return values, and typing. [The new specification](https://drafts.csswg.org/css-mixins-1/) allows defining custom functions using `@function`, utilizing default values, type checks, and calculations. Currently, the functionality is available in experimental mode in the [Chrome Canary](https://www.google.com/chrome/canary/) browser. Functions currently do not allow recursion, full work with local variables, or cascade layers, but they already open the door to more elegant and flexible solutions in CSS.

**Replace the four-pixel grid with the Fibonacci sequence.** Luis Ouriach questions the established [4px-spacing system](https://blog.designary.com/p/layout-basics-grid-systems-and-the-4px-grid) in digital design and proposes an alternative inspired by the Fibonacci sequence ([PDF, 812 KB](https://kdfls5.troja.mff.cuni.cz/tabor/images/2022-Fibonacciho-posloupnost.pdf)). He claims the 4px grid is monotonous and visually watery. Luis [experiments with the Fibonacci sequence](https://medium.com/@disco_lu/saying-bye-to-4px-spacing-and-hello-to-fibonacci-58477e3ecca3), which he says brings a better visual rhythm.

Run the [original Mac's System](https://infinitemac.org/) in your browser.

Have you ever wondered what the **operating system of the first Apple computer** looked like? The [Infinite Mac](https://infinitemac.org/) project offers the ability to run a clean installation of any historical Apple computer operating system in your browser, and as a bonus, also the NextStep operating system developed by the company Steve Jobs left Apple for before his return.

Another term applies mainly when designing tools that require deep concentration – [flow](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/flow). Designers and developers also enter flow, but that's another story. In tools, we often cannot avoid creating a user account. [Lazy registration](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/lazy-registration) is an approach where we link the user account to authentication with a delay, so registration doesn't disrupt the user's intent.

AI generates a large amount of content on the web, and search engines are facing this by adjusting their algorithms.
