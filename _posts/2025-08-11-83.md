## Linkodrome

**Shadows in the user interface are not just decoration.** [Properly used shadows add depth, hierarchy, and an interactive touch to elements.](https://medium.com/@dhirajkothalkar/everything-about-shadow-ui-guide-5f3fb94894c4) Light comes from above: raised elements have a lighter top edge and a shadow below, while inset elements conversely have a lighter bottom edge and a shadow inside. The size and blur of the shadow determine how close the element appears to the user.

**Designers often sound negative. Why is that a good thing?** Designers think in a mode of defensive pessimism – they [anticipate potential problems to prevent them](https://www.andybudd.com/archives/2025/07/why-designers-sound-negative-and-why-that-s-a-good-thing). This approach is common among pilots or surgeons and protects projects from failure. In an environment where strategic optimism reigns and confidence is mistakenly equated with competence, caution is perceived as resistance, writes Andy Budd.

**Usability testing** is a way to verify the functionality and understandability of a design before it hits the market. It's not a tool for experts, but a simple process where real people from the target group complete tasks and you observe their reactions. The goal is to uncover points where users get stuck, what delights them, and what frustrates them. Testing can be done in person or online via specialized platforms. The result? Clear priorities for adjustments and certainty that [your product is not only beautiful but also usable](https://medium.com/@saniaa.taimour/usability-testing-made-simple-how-real-people-can-make-your-design-better-1077a3df6675).

**The role of design in the product triad.** The triad: designer, product manager, and programmer is a collaboration model that ensures the product will be desirable, viable, and feasible. Instead of dividing "ownership" of individual aspects among roles, the product triad functions as a single team that makes decisions and solves problems collectively, says Pavel Samsonov. [The designer brings knowledge of user needs, shapes the vision, and connects it with business goals and technical possibilities.](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/the-product-triad-designs-role/)

**A practical guide to creating accessible image descriptions.** Alt text and image description are the foundation for [making visual content accessible to people with visual impairments](https://veroniiiica.com/how-to-write-alt-text-image-descriptions-visually-impaired/) while also improving SEO. Alt text is a short, hidden description embedded in metadata, while image description tends to be longer, visible, and more detailed. Veronica Lewis, who has low vision herself, advises on how to include the image type, text, people or animals, environment, colors, expressions, object positions, and interesting details.

**10 tricks that improve UI design.** These include visual hierarchy, working with real content, carefully designed loading and error states, typography and use of white space, purposeful use of color, optimization for quick content scanning, and testing designs with real users. [Small, systematic adjustments add up](https://medium.com/@reviewraccoon/10-little-ui-design-tips-that-make-a-big-impact-001fde84a662) and lead to a smoother, more intuitive, and more attractive user experience.

**Can you tell ambition from initiative?** Marty Cagan will teach you: Ambition is the desire to be the best, initiative means taking responsibility and acting. [Initiative is a condition for success, but without ambition it won't awaken.](https://www.svpg.com/agency-vs-ambition/) Even in top product companies, not all teams are strong, but it's precisely the best ones that never stop striving for improvement. **User Journey Map** is a visual overview of the steps, emotions, and obstacles a user goes through to achieve their goal. Breakthrough Fatoki shows us [how to map user journeys](https://medium.com/@breakthroughdeborah/user-journey-map-94765a2608b3). It helps designers eliminate their own biases, uncover *pain points*, and seek opportunities for improvement. The process involves defining tasks, detailing smaller steps, and mapping user emotions.

## Brendan Eich – the creator of JavaScript who turned web pages into interactive applications

**Brendan Eich** created the JavaScript language reportedly in just ten days in 1995. He wanted to make the beta release of the Netscape Navigator 2.0 browser. His work transformed the web from passive pages into interactive applications, and thanks to the ECMAScript standard, JavaScript became the universal language of the web.

Eich co-founded Mozilla, where he launched Firefox, breaking Internet Explorer's dominance and promoting open standards. He had to leave Mozilla when it burst that he financially supported the opposition movement to same-sex marriage. Later he founded Brave Software – a browser focused on privacy and fair compensation for creators.

## To Understand Each Other

Now it's in to skip [design outputs](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/deliverables) and jump straight into code. While AI can quickly generate interfaces, without human guidance it often just copies established patterns. Well-prepared materials from personas and user flows to prototypes speed up feedback, facilitate communication, and protect the quality of the outcome.

[Law of common region](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/common-region), one of the foundations of [Gestalt psychology](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/gestalt), states that people perceive elements within a single visually bounded area as a group. In UI design, it's used to organize related elements so users can orient themselves and understand their connections faster.

[The Mechanical Turk](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mechanical-turk) was a trick by Bratislava native Ján Vlk Kempelen who pretended the machine played chess, though a person was hidden inside it. That's precisely how Amazon's Mechanical Turk service works too.

[Opinionated software](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/opinionated-software) is designed to guide users according to a predefined methodology, thereby speeding up work and reducing the risk of errors. It relies on proven practices and is particularly useful for beginners or when working under pressure. The disadvantage is difficult customization if needs deviate from the original concept.
