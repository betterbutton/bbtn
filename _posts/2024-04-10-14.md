# Linkodrome

**[The .cz domain administrator is launching an auction.](https://blog.nic.cz/2024/04/08/otestujte-s-nami-aukce-domen/)** The Czech domain registrar, the CZ.NIC association, will launch an [auction of domains](https://aukce.nic.cz/) on the holiday of the lovestruck. The auctions will concern [expired](https://dvojka.rozhlas.cz/expirace-nebo-exspirace-7548901) domains, and to participate in the auction, CZ.NIC requires a [verified MojeID account](https://www.mojeid.cz/) or an [eIDAS identifier from another country](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/cs/policies/discover-eidas).

**Are you clicking away consent on the cookie bar?** Wired calculated that some popular [websites share data about users](https://www.wired.com/story/cookie-pop-up-ad-tech-partner-top-websites/) with up to 1500 entities.

**The Czech government is writing to the European Commission about its digital priorities.** We read the [shortened version of the document](https://digitalnicesko.gov.cz/media/files/FIN_Priority_CR_pro_evropske_digitalni_politiky_2page.pdf) twice and decided that we like the priorities and support them. Mainly because the government supports the educational framework of [micro-credentials](https://education.ec.europa.eu/education-levels/higher-education/micro-credentials). We don't know how it relates to [digital priorities](https://digitalnicesko.gov.cz/aktuality/cesko-predstavilo-priority-evropske-digitalni-poli/), but we're for it.

**Experiments with AI in the interface.** UI designers are rediscovering the term [semantic zoom](https://twitter.com/hturan/status/1776225700954939608). With a pinch gesture, the user can request text condensation, and conversely, a spread gesture triggers details. Kids will love this in mandatory reading on Kindles and in textbooks on tablets!

**Amazon has canceled [the cashierless self-checkout project](https://gizmodo.com/amazon-reportedly-ditches-just-walk-out-grocery-stores-1851381116#:~:text=Amazon%20Ditches%20%27-,Just%20Walk%20Out,-%27%20Checkouts%20at%20Its). For now?** *„It's of course funny that the AI service was actually 1000 people in India, but that's how AI things are usually made. First, you give the task to people and collect input and output data. Then you give some of the data to the dumb AI and a person corrects it. And in the end, the AI can do it all.“* comments on Amazon's move [Petr Soukup on Twitter](https://twitter.com/petrsoukup/status/1775489838680080738). Erika Hall has [a slightly different view](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/erikahall_if-your-technology-doesnt-work-unless-you-activity-7180958830694993923-fjFa) on it. Where is the truth?

In ten quarter-hour episodes, we will look back at the development of the Czech gaming scene from its beginnings behind the Iron Curtain in the 1980s to the present day. We will travel forty years from the aesthetics of 8-bit computers to today's hyper-realistic images.

You can reminisce with us about the first Czech computer game you played. You very likely still have some old issues of Score or Level hidden away somewhere.

# What We're Working On

Martin is furious about the design systems of multinational corporations that require pages to start with a [hero section](https://blog.logrocket.com/ux-design/what-is-website-hero-section/#:~:text=A%20website%20hero%20section%20is,and%20a%20call%20to%20action.). Statistics show that only a fraction of users scroll [past the fold](https://www.optimizely.com/optimization-glossary/above-the-fold/#:~:text=Above%20the%20fold%20content%20is,but%20the%20content%20continues%20underneath.) through the hero section. Also, the fondness for using large [feathered illustrations](https://www.vydavatelstvi.upol.cz/pro-autory/jak-vydat-knihu-old/slovnicek-zakladnich-terminu/#:~:text=P%C3%A9rovka%20(lineart%2C%20black%26white) (Typical example: a marker drawing.) as icons and massive components with huge fonts and generous margins and indentation leads to pages stretching unnecessarily, and not even a single [product card](https://m3.material.io/components/cards/specs) fits into the [browser viewport](https://whatismyviewport.com/) on a standard laptop.
