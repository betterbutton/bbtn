# Better Button #08
> Hello! Bing has only 3% of the market. Meanwhile, PHP runs on 76.5% of servers. UX professionals are jumping into HR. Škoda is getting smart knobs. AI is already generating frontends for websites. Did you manage to go skiing this year?

# Linkodrome

**What will the end of third-party cookies bring?** We wrote about 3rd party cookies in the [fourth newsletter](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/i/140920126/linkodrom). You can read about [what it means for web browsing](https://www.computerworld.com/article/3712705/what-a-future-without-browser-cookies-will-look-like.html) in Computerworld.

**Bing has only a 3% market share.** When Microsoft began arguing that the [EU should not apply the DMA to it](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/p/06#:~:text=Share-,EU%20narovn%C3%A1v%C3%A1%20podm%C3%ADnky%20v%C2%A0digit%C3%A1ln%C3%ADm%20prost%C5%99ed%C3%AD.,-Do%206.%C2%A0b%C5%99ezna), it was revealed that [Bing has a very small share of the search engine market](https://www.computerworld.com/article/3712560/microsoft-bing-edge-may-be-exempted-from-eu-s-digital-market-act-report.html). But perhaps Copilot, which is starting to permeate Windows, will move the needle.

**PHP remains popular.** Although articles on developer sites and conference talks might make it seem like Python and JavaScript are conquering web development, the truth is that [PHP is still the most popular language](https://w3techs.com/technologies/overview/programming_language) for web servers. The Next Web ponders the [sources of PHP's popularity](https://thenextweb.com/news/why-php-continues-to-be-a-popular-but-divisive-programming-language).

![A laughing man with glasses in a light suit and blue shirt with a tie holds a banana in his hand and says: "I changed all my passwords to 'incorrect'. So now, whenever I type my password wrong, it says: 'Your password is incorrect.'"](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/e5e49432-167d-4729-87d3-91a0ee462845_400x400.jpeg "400x400")

**Škoda will get new infotainment with smart knobs.** In car multimedia systems, touchscreens are displacing the old reliable rotary knobs and buttons with a proper haptic response. Škoda's designers are going against the grain. The new touchscreen will be complemented by [smart rotary controllers](https://www.skoda-storyboard.com/en/skoda-world/new-controls-for-skoda-cars-a-single-system-but-customisable/), whose function will change contextually, and since they have their own small LED display, their appearance will also change. And as if that weren't enough, the voice assistant [Laura will also get ChatGPT](https://www.auto.cz/skoda-octavia-ma-po-faceliftu-az-265-koni-konec-trivalce-a-umela-inteligence-151540#:~:text=Inteligentn%C3%AD%20par%C5%A5%C3%A1k%20na%20cest%C3%A1ch).

**UX boosts employee productivity.** [UX design principles also apply to the field of employee satisfaction.](https://uxpsychology.substack.com/p/enhancing-employee-experience-through) A happy employee = a happy customer. And that's what it's all about. Not long ago, people were moving from HR to UX. Now they might start returning to their original professions, but newly as EX – Exployee Experience designers.

**Twilio is ending desktop support for Authy.** If you, like Martin, have grown fond of Authy for generating one-time authentication codes, you'll need to find a replacement for desktop use by March 19th at the latest. [Michal Bláha](https://twitter.com/michalblaha/status/1759874147931332633) recommends [Bitwarden](https://bitwarden.com/).

**Less is more.** No, don't worry, no growth spurt. Also, when you want to improve something, your first instinct is to add something new, right? Colleague Ondra Ilinčev described [7 sources of inefficiency](https://www.ilincev.com/mene-je-lepe) according to Toyota and recommends the book [Subtract](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/53138204-subtract). Saint-Exupéry would be pleased!

Kuba went so far as to write an entire system in the terminal on his MacBook that tracks project status and maintains a to-do list, which he prefers to have printed on a printer.

Lately, Martin has been spending a lot of time with his iPad and [uses programmable notebooks instead of a terminal](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/i/141557352/zivot-s-technologiemi), but recently he's taken a liking to Ubuntu running as Windows Subsystem for Linux and enjoys every opportunity to analyze some logs or change the structure of data files.
