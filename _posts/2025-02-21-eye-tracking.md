# Eye-Tracking
> An eye camera tracks eye movement during interaction with a user interface. It helps discover where people look, what they overlook, and how to optimize the design for better usability.

**[An eye-tracker](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sledov%C3%A1n%C3%AD_pohybu_o%C4%8D%C3%AD)** records where the gaze is directed and tracks the eye movements of users as they interact with a user interface. It helps determine where people look, in what order, and for how long they observe individual elements, and what they might overlook.

Research shows that users often scan a screen [in an "F"-shaped pattern](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/f-shaped-pattern-reading-web-content/) when reading content. They ignore elements that resemble ads and make very quick decisions about whether to stay on a page. Testing with an eye-tracker helps improve the design to be more comprehensible and clearer.

Previously, eye-tracking devices were expensive and only available in laboratories. The cheaper ones cost around ten thousand dollars. Today, there are [affordable desktop eye-trackers](https://imotions.com/blog/learning/product-news/eye-tracker-prices/) for under a thousand dollars and even [software solutions](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/beam-gaming-head-eye-tracker/id1538790472) that track eye movement [using a standard webcam](https://gazerecorder.com/). Eye-tracking is thus becoming more accessible.

During [eye-tracking tests](https://www.nngroup.com/reports/how-to-conduct-eyetracking-studies/), unlike standard user testing, a moderator is usually not present, and we do not ask respondents to verbalize their thought processes. As an alternative to eye-tracking, analysis of user clicks on a page and mouse cursor movement is sometimes used. Nevertheless, eye-tracking offers deeper insight into how people perceive visual hierarchy and why they ignore certain elements.

In the near future, the integration of eye-tracking with artificial intelligence is expected, enabling faster and automated analysis of results and predictive personalization of the user interface. The technology may also become a standard component of mobile devices, gaming consoles, or virtual reality. It will also find application as an assistive technology [for users with limited mobility](https://www.unicef.org/ukraine/en/stories/eye-tracking-tech-helps-children-with-disabilities).
