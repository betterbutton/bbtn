# Designing for High Complexity
> 

When designing for complex systems, it is crucial to first understand their individual parts. Krisztina Szerovay [uses a train analogy](https://uxknowledgebase.com/designing-for-complexity-start-with-these-questions-5476acf184c5) to demonstrate an approach to understanding new domains. Explaining what a train is can be done through questions that help you understand not only the basic principles but also the more complex interactions within the system.

[The same questions](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/complex-application-design-framework/) can be used when first encountering any complex problem—from zooming into details to examining related processes. The goal is not to create an artifact, but to [understand the complexity](https://cwodtke.medium.com/five-models-for-making-sense-of-complex-systems-134be897b6b3) for effective design.
