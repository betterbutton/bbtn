![On the left (incorrectly), text form fields have labels in the form of placeholders that disappear when you start typing. On the right (correctly), the form text fields have labels separately and remain visible even when the field is already filled.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/e2f28667-83dc-432c-9caa-efd62ecbcd29_2160x1080.png "626x313")

**Don't forget form labels.** Forms must be clear and without surprises.\
\
❌ Only placeholders inside fields\
✅ Visible labels for every field\
\
👉 Clear instructions even after filling.\
👉 Higher success rate for completed forms.\
\
Labels save nerves.

## Linkodrome

**Google Material 3 Expressive** brings vibrant colors and [expressive typography](https://medium.com/@sicario.2077/typography-is-googles-new-weapon-for-material-design-expressive-b425d8472e67). Google updated its design system [Material You](https://m3.material.io/blog/announcing-material-you to [Material 3 Expressive](https://m3.material.io/blog/building-with-m3-expressive). The update brings, besides vibrant colors, also animations and new components aimed at increasing user emotional engagement. It focuses on the generation of youngest adults who prefer expressive design over the minimalist style of iOS. Material 3 Expressive will debut in the beta version of Android 16 and will later be available on Pixel devices.

**How to reduce website bounce rate through UX design.** A high [website bounce rate](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/bounce-rate) signals design problems. Loop11 offers [specific strategies](https://loop11.medium.com/reducing-bounce-rate-with-intuitive-ux-design-845476ef2ae5): fast page loading, clear navigation, quality content, mobile adaptation, and clear [visual hierarchy](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/visual-hierarchy). Practical examples show that by improving UX, bounce rate can be significantly reduced and conversions increased.

**Almost a quarter of e-shop owners don't use AI.** [They don't have time to learn it](https://www.gopay.com/blog/jak-cesti-a-slovensti-e-shopari-pouzivaji-ai-prohlednete-si-vysledky-unikatniho-gopay-pruzkumu/), a GoPay survey found. If you also don't have time to learn to use AI, Google has prepared [a prompting guide for you](https://services.google.com/fh/files/misc/gemini-for-google-workspace-prompting-guide-101.pdf). That it's 68 pages long needn't scare you. Only pages 2, 3, and 67 bring new information – the rest are usage examples. Claude can go into it more thoroughly. Even if you already prompt like a pro, [their guide](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/overview) will show you how different approaches work and what each is good for.

**SEO specialists expect a significant drop in traffic from search engines** as a result of deploying AI summaries in search results. Vojtěch Fiala points out that [the biggest drops can be expected for informational queries](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7327576012127367168/), which make up almost ninety percent. It will be worse, thinks Pavel Ungr, because [Google is testing to replace search with chat](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7330164630889107457/) and it's only a matter of time before [AI chats incorporate paid advertising into their responses](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2025/05/google-is-quietly-testing-ads-in-ai-chatbots/).

**UX researchers shouldn't leave the synthesis of generative research to AI.** According to Jon Kolko, [synthesis is not just a formal intermediate step to create an output](https://www.jonkolko.com/writing/notes/synthesis-and-chat-gpt). It's a process of understanding and connecting new insights with what we already know. It is precisely active reflection that gives research meaning and value – for both individuals and the entire team. Handing over synthesis to AI reduces the benefit of research and deprives us of the most important part of knowledge: our own understanding and interpretation.
