# Google app brings page annotations to iOS
> 

Google has added the [Page Annotation](https://9to5google.com/2024/11/25/google-ios-app-link-annotations-search/) feature to its iOS app, which [highlights interesting entities](https://www.theverge.com/2024/11/25/24305922/google-app-ios-inserting-search-links-websites) on web pages directly in the app's browser. After clicking on the highlight, the user is redirected to Google search results.

In other words: on your website without your knowledge [links appear in people's browsers](https://www.seroundtable.com/google-ios-app-page-annotation-38451.html?fbclid=IwY2xjawG2iFFleHRuA2FlbQIxMAABHTlTGB4D54_GFiit_02CX0-1mP2-WRFNgXHkjN44m9Z90n3AJwOyAfnsFg_aem_WIWJumttr-Jn92FRwUYtPQ) that lead to Google pages.

This change has raised concerns among website operators because it can divert visitors from their content. However, Google claims that the feature offers quick access to additional information without visitors having to leave the original page. Web administrators can deactivate the feature using an opt-out form, but deactivation takes up to 30 days.
