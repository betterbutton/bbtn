# Rolf Molich – pioneer of usability
> Rolf Molich is a Danish engineer, co-author of heuristic evaluation, and leader of comparative usability studies, who has shaped UX practice since the 1980s.

![Rolf Molich](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/5cbbcd2f-aa8e-4ca4-a58f-c982414d533f_1536x1024.webp "1456x971")

Pioneer of usability [Rolf Molich](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rolf-molich-b585493/)

[Rolf Molich](https://www.dialogdesign.dk/about-rolf-molich/) (\*1949) is a Danish pioneer of usability, known primarily as the co-author of [heuristic evaluation](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jak-vznikly-nielsenovy-heuristiky) with *Jakob Nielsen* in 1990. This method became a cornerstone for interface evaluation and expert inspections.

Rolf's later [comparative usability studies (CUE)](https://www.dialogdesign.dk/cue-studies/) revealed fundamental differences in testing among professional teams. The so-called [evaluator effect](https://www.dialogdesign.dk/cue-9/) changed how professionals approach testing, moderation, and result evaluation.

For over three decades, Rolf combined scientific rigor with practical recommendations. He is the [author of books](https://www.dialogdesign.dk/books/) *Brugervenlige edb-systemer* (1986) and *Brugervenligt webdesign* (2001), which were also published in English and Swedish. Together with Jakob Nielsen and colleagues, he also prepared the publication *[E-Commerce User Experience](https://www.amazon.com/Commerce-User-Experience-Jakob-Nielsen/dp/0970607202)* with over two hundred rules for designing online shops.

As co-founder and vice-chairman of [UXQB](https://www.uxqb.org/), he contributed to creating the international [CPUX certification](https://www.uxqb.org/ux-training-and-certification/), which unifies terminology and skills in UX.

For his contributions, he received the [UXPA Lifetime Achievement Award](https://uxpa.org/history-of-uxpa-award-recipients/#:~:text=students%20and%20colleagues.-,2014%20%E2%80%93%20Rolf%20Molich,-In%20recognition%20of) in 2014 and the [German UPA award](https://germanupa.de/mediathek/ein-abend-mit-rolf-rolf-molich-im-kamingespraech) for lifelong work in UX in 2022. Even today, he [publishes essays](https://dl.acm.org/profile/81100082422), shares experiences on the DialogDesign website, and [speaks at conferences](https://www.dialogdesign.dk/meet-rolf-molich/), where he serves as both a critic and an inspiration.

For web and application creators, Rolf's legacy is not only a set of practical tools but also a candid reminder that usability has its limits. It requires careful testing, involvement of real users, and continuous improvement of practices.
