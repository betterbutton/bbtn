# Above the fold
> Above the fold is the top area of a website or app visible without scrolling on the first screen. It influences the first impression and motivates users to continue reading, or to leave the page immediately.

**Above the fold** [comes from newspapers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Above_the_fold), where it refers to the upper part of the front page visible when the newspaper is folded in half. This is precisely the part of the front page that customers usually see on a newsstand. On the web or in an app, it refers to the area the user sees without scrolling right on the first screen. [Above the fold](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/page-fold-manifesto/) decides whether they continue further or leave immediately.

[A well-set first screen:](https://mailchimp.com/resources/what-is-above-the-fold/)

- **Motivates action.** It's clear to the user what they can do next – go to a specific category, book an appointment, subscribe to a newsletter.

- **Shows the key message.** It delivers the main information about what you offer or solve (a service, product, guides, inspiration).

- **Is clear and uncluttered.** Too many elements confuse visitors rather than inspire them.

- **Loads quickly.** A slowly loading page above the fold can deter users even before they see anything.

- **Respects different screens.** Above the fold is different on every device – you need to test desktop, mobile, and tablet.

The first screen should clearly express what the website offers and why the user will appreciate it. It's advisable to emphasize a call to action and indicate that the page continues on the next screen. If the main idea gets lost, the visitor may leave within a few seconds.

The speed of page rendering above the top edge affects the first impression. Slow loading often leads to bounces. Mobile interfaces require even greater emphasis on concise messaging because they occupy less space. Therefore, in practice, we use lazy loading for images below the fold to avoid cluttering the connection and slowing down the loading of the visible part of the site.

Above the fold is the first thing a user sees. Its content contributes to the overall impression and shapes the decision to stay or leave. Some people look for a clear message, others appreciate a simpler visual. Clarity is always fundamental.
