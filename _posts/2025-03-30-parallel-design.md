# Parallel design
> Parallel design is a creative process in which multiple designers simultaneously create different solutions to a single problem to find the best possible direction.

Parallel design ([parallel design](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/parallel-design/)) is a way of working where several people or teams design various options for solving the same task. It is most commonly used at the beginning of product design. It helps prevent designers from fixating on a single variant too early.

By comparing different approaches, stronger ideas are more easily revealed. The result is often a more understandable and user-friendly product. Web and app creators can thus save time and create a better outcome.

Designers don't focus on just one path from the start. Instead, multiple variants emerge. Each offers a slightly different perspective. This allows the design to develop faster and with greater confidence.
