# GDS reveals accessibility shortcomings after three years of monitoring
> 

The Government Digital Service (GDS) monitors [the accessibility of public sector websites and applications](https://accessibility.blog.gov.uk/2024/12/17/what-gds-has-found-from-public-sector-accessibility-monitoring/) to ensure they are available for the 16.1 million people with disabilities in the UK. Over three years, they checked 1203 websites and 21 applications, identifying key issues such as insufficient text contrast, invisible keyboard focus, and responsiveness problems. 85% of organizations published an accessibility statement, but many are not up to date. GDS commends the efforts of public teams to address the findings and stresses the importance of regularly updating both websites and statements.
