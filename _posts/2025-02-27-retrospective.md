# Retrospective
> A retrospective is a meeting of the project team to reflect on past work. Collaborators try to identify successes and look for opportunities for improvement to increase efficiency and collaboration.

**[Retrospective](https://retrospectivewiki.org/)** comes from agile development methodologies. It is a regular meeting at the end of a development iteration that helps teams look back on past work, evaluate what went well, and discover what could be improved. The team undertakes the retrospective with the goal of continuously improving team collaboration and work processes. When conducted correctly, a retrospective increases efficiency, removes obstacles, and strengthens team trust.

Teams use various methods during retrospectives, such as [Start-Stop-Continue](https://www.scrumgenius.com/blog/start-stop-continue-restrospective), [4L](https://www.funretrospectives.com/the-4-ls-liked-learned-lacked-longed-for/), or [Mad-Sad-Glad](https://soch.cz/blog/management/agile/scrum-management/retrospektiva-5-mad-sad-glad/). An important role is played by the facilitator—the person who leads the meeting and ensures it is constructive and doesn't devolve into mere complaining. At the end of the retrospective, the team should always come up with concrete steps to implement in the next period.

A retrospective typically consists of several phases. First, rules and the atmosphere are set, then the team collects feedback, jointly analyzes key insights, and defines specific actions for improvement. For a retrospective to be successful, it is crucial that team members are open, honest, and willing to implement changes in practice.

If a retrospective doesn't yield any concrete steps or repeats the same patterns without real changes, it loses its meaning. But when done well and when the team actually acts on it, it leads to improvements in productivity, work quality, and overall team member satisfaction.
