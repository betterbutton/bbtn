**Don't underestimate the power of gradual changes**

Much more effective than one-off, dramatic website overhauls is regular, gradual evolution. Small changes made continuously allow users to naturally get used to novelties without shocking or discouraging them.

If a website remains unchanged for a long time, its users develop strong habits that any sudden change disrupts. Such a situation is perceived negatively – people have to abandon familiar ways of navigation and orient themselves in a new environment. Wherever possible, consider gradual development over radical revolution. And even better – support this development with data and testing.

You've certainly experienced the confusion brought by a renovation of your favorite store. You enter knowing where to find pasta, but instead you're greeted by shelves with jam or cooling boxes with meat. You don't want to cause this kind of confusion for your users on the web.
