# SCAMPER
> A structured technique that uses seven filters to help generate a new idea during the ideation phase of projects.

[SCAMPER](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/learn-how-to-use-the-best-ideation-methods-scamper) is a practical tool that helps product teams generate new ideas by systematically reevaluating existing features. The name SCAMPER is formed from the initial letters of seven prompts:

- Substitute – replace,
- Combine – combine,
- Adapt – adapt,
- Modify – modify,
- Put to another use – put to another use,
- Eliminate – eliminate,
- Reverse – reverse.

The foundation of this method is a change in perspective. Instead of free-form thinking, the team goes through individual questions step by step: *What if we replaced this element? Or combined it with another? What if we removed something entirely?* Take, for example, a file upload feature – replacing a button with a QR code, combining upload with automatic tagging, or completely removing a status bar.

Unlike classic brainstorming, SCAMPER offers structured, targeted thinking. It's useful when you need to improve a known element or find a new approach to a known problem. It's undemanding – you don't need any workshops or complex tools – and you can use it alone or in a team.

While SCAMPER doesn't replace research or technical analysis, it helps uncover blind spots and new possibilities. It breaks habitual thinking patterns and gives product creators a simple but effective way to move forward.

It originally comes from pedagogy, but due to its clarity and simplicity, it has found its place in design and development. When you're running out of ideas or the roadmap is repeating itself, SCAMPER can help you restart your creativity.

A little focus is all it takes – and even small questions can lead to big changes.
