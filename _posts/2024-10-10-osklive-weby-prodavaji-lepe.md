# Ugly websites sell better
> 

Modern websites full of animations, videos, and 3D elements may look impressive, but they often lose the most important thing—speed and functionality. [Every additional second of website loading increases the abandonment rate](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ugly-websites-sell-better-noor-fatima-aiwhf/), which can lead to lost sales. Aesthetics usually come not with minimalism, but with decoration.

> „Users will abandon your shiny, over-designed site for something faster and more functional.“\
> — **[Noor Fatima](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ugly-websites-sell-better-noor-fatima-aiwhf/)**

[Ugly websites often convert better](https://www.crazyegg.com/blog/ugly-website-design/ than visually attractive pages thanks to their simplicity and functionality. The key to success is the clear value the website provides and the absence of unnecessary visual elements that could distract visitors.

Sites like [DMOZ](https://dmoztools.net/) with minimal design and clear organization succeed due to their speed and understandability. Nice websites can also work well, but any redesign should always consider whether it truly improves conversions or just adds unnecessary visual complexity.

Unnecessary animations and videos divert attention from the main goal—conversion. The example of websites like [Craigslist](https://prague.craigslist.org/) shows that simplicity and speed win over design stunts. Beauty may win awards, but functionality and speed bring customers.

Amazon calculated ten years ago that every second of delay in page loading [costs it $1.6 billion annually](https://www.fastcompany.com/1825005/how-one-second-could-cost-amazon-16-billion-sales in lost sales.
