# Conversion Rate
> The percentage of users who complete a desired action on a website or in an app –⁠ proof that the digital environment makes sense and works correctly.

Conversion rate, also known as [conversion rate](https://www.strafelda.cz/konverzni-pomer) ([conversion rate](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/2684489?hl=en), CR) expresses how many users perform a specific desired action on your website or in your app. This could be a purchase, registration, or even submitting a form. It is a crucial indicator of how well your digital space supports the user's intent.

A high conversion rate means users proceed smoothly, understand the offer, and react without hesitation. A low rate typically signals issues –⁠ unclear messaging, a confusing interface, or insufficient trust in the service.

Don't try to boost the conversion rate with superficial changes –⁠ like altering a button's color or adding a sense of urgency. These tricks might help short-term, but they don't address the core problem: whether the environment truly makes sense to the person using it.

Genuine improvement requires removing unnecessary obstacles. Find out where people hesitate, why they leave, and what raises doubts in their minds. And most importantly –⁠ adjust everything so the user knows what will happen when they click onward.

The conversion rate isn't just a number at the end of the journey. It's an indicator that the entire journey was understandable and trustworthy. If a user converts, it means everything before it made sense to them.

For both designers and developers, the conversion rate should be a natural consequence of well-designed user experience –⁠ not its sole objective.
