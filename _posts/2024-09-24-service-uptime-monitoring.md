# Is your website down, or is it just me?
> 

Do you keep an eye on whether your website is running? Hosting services often advertise a high uptime rate – the time during which they guarantee that servers are operational. But when you convert percentages to time per year, for example, 99.9% uptime means that your website is down for 8 hours and 45 minutes per year. Most outages are planned and spread throughout the year during regular maintenance.

Quality hosting services guarantee uptime an order of magnitude better; usually over 99.97%. And thus, the most common cause of website failure is an application problem, a code error, or abnormal load.

Hosting services typically offer server monitoring. But it's also useful to implement monitoring for website uptime, email server or API, SSL certificate expiration, and other features on which your business relies. That's where [services like Uptime Robot](https://alternativeto.net/browse/search/?q=uptime+robot) come in. Most such services have a fairly usable free plan, but if you can calculate what you earn on, it shouldn't be hard to justify the expense of preventing problems.
