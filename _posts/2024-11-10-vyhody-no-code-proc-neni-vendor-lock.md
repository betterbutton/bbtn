# Advantages of no-code: Why vendor lock-in isn't a threat
> 

Concerns about so-called vendor lock-in, or dependency on a specific provider, are a common argument against using no-code platforms. Nevertheless, it turns out that these concerns are often exaggerated. [Most no-code platforms offer some flexibility in terms of integrations](https://www.nocode.tech/article/mythbusting-06-getting-locked-in-with-a-no-code-vendor-is-a-big-problem), data export, or compatibility with other systems, making it easier to switch to another platform. Moreover, there are open alternatives that prevent vendor lock-in, and distributing functionality across multiple tools reduces the risk. In conclusion, the benefits of no-code for productivity and time savings outweigh the potential disadvantages.
