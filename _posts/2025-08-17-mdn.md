# MDN – the number one stop for web developers
> A proven and understandable resource for web development that developers have trusted across generations. 

![Screenshot of MDN's home screen.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/676cfbb1-97db-4334-b37e-bc81ad586512_1896x1056.png "656x365")

[MDN](https://developer.mozilla.org/) has been a reliable reference for web technologies for twenty years

[MDN](https://developer.mozilla.org/), formerly known as Mozilla Developer Network, is the most reliable and most comprehensive [documentation platform](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web) for HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and web APIs. Developers of all levels – from beginners to experts – rely on it daily as an accurate, up-to-date, and clear resource that offers practical explanations without unnecessary fluff. [For twenty years!](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/blog/mdn-turns-20/)

MDN's strong point is primarily how clearly it describes technology behavior across different browsers. It offers detailed compatibility tables and concrete examples that explain things simply and directly, without unnecessary tricks or complicated formulations. And all of this without ads and without articles written just for search engines.

MDN originally started under the Mozilla umbrella. Today it operates thanks to the Open Web Docs initiative, supported by the developer community from around the world. Everyone can contribute improvements – and that is precisely why MDN evolves continuously alongside the web itself.

But MDN is not just technical documentation. It is a model of how clear and understandable technical communication should look. Thanks to its well-structured content, precise language, and practical examples, it is equally useful when training new colleagues as when developing production features.

If you design, develop, or document for the web, MDN is a tool that stays within reach – once you bookmark it, you'll never remove it.
