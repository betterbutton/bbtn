# Typographic Contrast: How to Use It for Perfect Design
> 

Typographic contrast helps in graphic design to highlight key elements and guide the user's attention. Canadian designer Carl Dair defined [seven basic methods for creating contrast](https://www.creativebloq.com/design/fonts-typography/what-is-typographical-contrast):

1.  size,

2.  weight,

3.  form,

4.  structure,

5.  texture,

6.  color and

7.  direction.

> "Font weight indicates emphasis, but care must be taken not to impair readability."\
> — **[Matt Smith](https://www.creativebloq.com/design/fonts-typography/what-is-typographical-contrast)**

Every element influences visual hierarchy, emotion, and readability. The correct combination of these elements ensures a balanced design where important information is easily visible. To achieve maximum effect, a balance between visual impact and readability must always be maintained.
