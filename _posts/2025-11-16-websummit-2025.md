# Such Was Web Summit 2025 in Lisbon 
> Web Summit 2025 confirmed the shift towards vertical, practically focused AI and the connection of investments and geopolitical priorities. Lisbon became the nervous center of technological and strategic innovations for three days.

Lisbon woke up on November 10th flooded with badges, queues, and dozens of languages. The tenth edition of the conference *[Web Summit 2025](https://websummit.com/)* attracted 71,368 participants from 157 countries and confirmed that it is not just a conference but a global barometer of innovations. The air was charged with energy, but beneath the surface buzz about artificial intelligence, something much more substantial was happening. This year's edition was not about promises. It was about evidence.

The feeling that permeated the conference spaces was clear: the world stopped talking about the future and started realizing it. The era of theoretical discussions has ended and the era of execution has begun. Deep, often unexpected shifts have been revealed, which are already rewriting the global map of power, finance, and even what it means to be human in the digital age.

Forget vague visions. Here are five key insights from Web Summit 2025 – five pieces of the puzzle that together paint a picture of a new reality where technology undergoes a single test: the test of its human impact.

## Endgame: The End of Western Technological Dominance

Right at the beginning of the conference, a sentence rang out that made those present take notice. Web Summit founder *Paddy Cosgrave* on the main stage bluntly declared that the era of Western dominance is over.

> “This year, more than any year before, it’s clear that the era of Western tech dominance is fading.”\
> **— [Paddy Cosgrave](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/technology/western-tech-dominance-fading-at-lisbons-web-summit/articleshow/125240543.cms), opening keynote**

His claim was not just provocation. It was a statement of fact based on hard data. It's not just a shift in innovation, but a strategic battle for technological independence. As Paddy emphasized, China is accelerating the development of its own semiconductors and sovereign artificial intelligence, while the West is restricting exports. It's not a competition in innovation, but a fight for self-sufficiency.

But this shift is not only happening on the global East-West axis. It's also happening within Europe. Paddy called Poland the *'golden girl of the new Europe'*, signaling a migration of capital and talent that challenges old economic hierarchies. The world of technology is becoming multipolar and the old rules are ceasing to apply.

## The Payment Revolution Wasn't Started by a Bank. Brazil Started It.

Paddy's thesis about the end of Western dominance was not just theory. He immediately added the best proof: the Brazilian payment system *[Pix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pix_(payment_system)*. It has become a symbol of a new world order where the greatest innovations don't come from Silicon Valley, but from those who can actually solve the problems of millions of people.

Pix is a free, instant payment system used today by over 80% of Brazilians. It's tangible proof of how a country outside traditional centers of power managed to implement a solution that Western banks and fintechs only talked about.

*"The payment revolution does not come from an American or European bank. It comes from the Central Bank of Brazil."* These words clearly show that breakthrough, inclusive financial innovation is now emerging in places where we wouldn't have looked for it before. It's execution in practice – fast, efficient, and human-centered, which challenges established financial institutions and confirms that global power is indeed shifting.

## Artificial Intelligence is Moving from Answering to Acting

The discussion about artificial intelligence has shifted to a completely new level. While we have so far been talking about generative AI that responds to our commands, at Web Summit, agentic AI became the main topic.

The Portuguese startup *[Granter](https://www.theportugalnews.com/news/2025-11-18/portuguese-company-wins-big-at-web-summit/919464)* won the PITCH competition with an AI tool for automating the grant process. The victory well reflects the clearly visible trend of investors moving from general models to vertically integrated solutions.

*Cristiano Amon*, CEO of *Qualcomm*, presented [a vision of AI agents](https://websummit.com/sessions/lis25/077af7ae-fdc7-4f48-9698-787238a5d7e0/hybrid-ai-from-cloud-to-edge-intelligence-hosted-by-qualcomm/) as replacements for operating systems and redefined [6G as a sensory network](https://websummit.com/sessions/lis25/0b50211c-3fa0-4a1b-ad6f-80581bced0af/developing-for-edge-ai-bring-intelligence-closer-hosted-by-qualcomm/) for security and health applications.

> “We’re more and more dependent, especially on the American hyperscalers.”\
> **— [Maya Noël](https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20251107-global-tech-tensions-overshadow-web-summit-s-ai-and-robots), France Digitale**

*Chris Ballance* from *IonQ* emphasized [the importance of quantum computing](https://quantumzeitgeist.com/ionq-quantum-computing-quantum-applications/) for complex AI systems. *Siemens* and *NVIDIA* [demonstrated the use of digital twins](https://www.facebook.com/Siemens/posts/were-shaping-the-future-of-industry-with-nvidia-at-the-web-summit-in-lisbon-our-/999371175992318/) to dramatically shorten factory construction from years to months.

> “Half the world’s computer scientists and computer engineers who are on the frontier of these technologies are in China.”\
> **— [Rev Lebaredian](https://uk.finance.yahoo.com/news/western-tech-dominance-fading-lisbons-025343217.html), NVIDIA**

The *Pan-European Dual-Use Investor Network* launched investments in technologies for use in both the army and civilian sector – including space systems and *defense platforms. Commissioner *Des Hogan* from DPC presented [the European model of regulatory pre-certification](https://www.euronews.com/next/2025/11/14/irelands-data-protection-chief-says-we-cant-be-doing-enough-to-protect-children-online), which shifts the responsibility for compliance to tech companies already in the development phase.

The conference showed that the next big challenge is not just to perfect machine intelligence, but to teach it to understand the unseen, such as empathy. Why is this so important? Statistics are relentless: 83% of fraud victims say the emotional impact is worse than the financial one. Trust and empathy are not technical problems, but deeply human needs. The debate thus shifted from the question *'What all can AI do?'* to the much more important question: *'How can we ensure that it acts for our good?'*

## Authenticity is the New Currency

The rise of agentic AI creates a fascinating economic paradox. In a world that will soon be flooded with AI-generated content – a resource that is practically infinite and almost free – the value of the only resource that is demonstrably scarce is dramatically increasing: genuine human authenticity.

No one embodies this principle better than *Khaby Lame*, the most followed creator on TikTok, who built a global empire without uttering a single word. His message on the main stage was simple but incredibly powerful. His smile, as one reporter noted, lit up the arena more than all the lights on the main stage.

> “The more AI advances, the more authentic we must become.”\
> **— [Khaby Lame](https://deadline.com/2025/11/khaby-lame-maria-sharapova-lovable-web-summit-lisbon-1236613514/)**

In this simple message lies the key market dynamic of the future. Technology can scale products and streamline processes, but loyalty and genuine connection with people are built by the essence of humanity – something that cannot be replicated. In the age of machines, the greatest competitive advantage becomes precisely what makes us human.

## The Future is Focused on Humans

If we were to summarize the main message of Web Summit 2025 into a single idea, it would be this: the era of technology for technology's sake is definitively over. The conference was not just a showcase of trends, but a verdict on what actually works. And the verdict is that the winners are those who masterfully execute rooted in human needs.

From geopolitical shifts of power, evidenced by Brazilian Pix, to the economy of authenticity in the AI era – everything ultimately revolves around people. Real progress is not measured by computing power, but by the connections and opportunities that technology creates. From Lisbon, we take away the knowledge that the greatest revolution is not made by algorithms, but by the relationships we are able to build.

> “Web Summit 2025 confirmed that momentum is building for those who create, lead, and shape technology that serves real human progress.”\
> **— [Elise Quevedo](https://tomorrowsaffairs.com/courage-curiosity-collaboration-what-web-summit-2025-revealed)**

The future will not be about who has the most powerful computers, but about who can use technology to strengthen the most human in us: the ability to create and collaborate. And that is the vision that matters most.
