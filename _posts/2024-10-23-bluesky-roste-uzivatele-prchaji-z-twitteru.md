# Bluesky is Soaring: Users Flee X After Controversial Changes
> 

Bluesky, a relatively new social network, is experiencing rapid user growth and [entered the top 5 apps in the US App Store](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/18/bluesky-surges-into-the-top-5-as-x-changes-blocks-permits-ai-training-on-its-data/), becoming the second most popular in the Social Networking category. It is achieving similar results [on Google Play](https://bsky.app/profile/robpegoraro.com/post/3l6vyi3xzj62l).

This growth is entirely organic, fueled by controversial changes on Twitter, where users are reacting to modifications of the blocking feature and new terms allowing AI training on their data.

Elon Musk [plans to change the blocking feature](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/18/why-changes-to-the-block-on-elon-musks-x-are-driving-users-away/) on Twitter, which would allow blocked users to see the public posts of the user who blocked them. This change has raised concerns among many users, including anti-harassment advocates. Critics, such as Tracy Chou and experts on cyber harassment, warn that removing the blocking feature reduces user safety, especially for victims of stalking and harassment.

Furthermore, Bluesky is expanding beyond the US, with significant download growth in Japan, Thailand, and other countries.
