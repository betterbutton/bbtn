# Penpot will replace Figma in web design
> An open-source UI design tool that connects design and development through standardized tokens and a fair licensing policy.

![Screenshot of Penpot app's homepage.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/5ae5b554-9a79-4e3d-9b83-c262ecd9845a_1896x1022.png "516x278")

[Penpot](https://penpot.app/) is no longer the poor cousin of Figma.

[Penpot](https://penpot.app/) is becoming a full-fledged player among user interface design tools. It has open-source code, supports modern standards, and doesn't cost a fortune. A team license costs a maximum of one hundred and seventy-five dollars per month. Regardless of how many of you there are.

The editor works with design tokens. Thanks to them, you can easily switch themes, modes, or brands. This means one design adapts to different conditions or clients without complex rewriting. Everyone works with a single system that is also easy to read and export.

Penpot is no longer just a copy of Figma. It stands on its own feet and offers more than just a price advantage. It is designed not to restrict. You get eight editors for free. For a larger team, you still pay the same. No extra fees. No complexities.

Technically, it reminds you of a familiar environment. But under the hood, everything is different. Openness is the basic rule here. You can version designs, link them with development, and incorporate them into regular deployment.

Thus, Penpot is changing from an alternative to a first choice. For those who want to keep both design and development under control. Simply, clearly, and according to their own rules.
