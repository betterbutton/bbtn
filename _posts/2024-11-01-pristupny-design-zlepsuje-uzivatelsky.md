# Accessible Design Improves User Experience for Everyone
> 

Anton Sten shares how his view on accessible design shifted from a limiting to an inclusive "for anyone" approach. He realizes it's not just about rules for people with disabilities, but about considering various situations—from a parent with a child to a user in bright sunlight or noisy environments.

> „Accessible design isn’t designing for everyone, it’s designing for anyone.“\
> **— [Anton Sten](https://www.antonsten.com/articles/designing-for-anyone/)**

This "design for anyone" enables more effective and intuitive products for a wide spectrum of users, as demonstrated by practical examples and resources like Microsoft's guide. The key is to [view accessibility as an extension of user-friendliness](https://www.antonsten.com/articles/designing-for-anyone/), not as a restriction.
