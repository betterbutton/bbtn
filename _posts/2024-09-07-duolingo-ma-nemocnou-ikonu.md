# Duolingo faces criticism over sick mascot icon
> 

![Sick and tired Duo the owl mascot, sweating on its forehead and with a runny beak. Next to it is an expanded context menu on an iOS home screen. The first item is "Remove from Home Screen" and below it says: You can't hide from Duo.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/078029fe-6997-4861-a818-8adfd473dbe7_633x815.jpeg "633x815")

You can't hide from Duo?!

Duolingo released a new app featuring an [icon that shows the mascot, Duo the owl, as sick](https://www.outlookindia.com/international/us/duolingos-new-sick-app-icon-why-users-are-concerned-and-how-to-change-it-explained) – with red eyes, sweat on its forehead, and a dripping beak – if you haven't used the app for a while. Subscribers can change the icon.

The design was meant to encourage user interaction, but it [sparked negative reactions](https://www.reddit.com/r/duolingo/comments/1f2deam/im_sick_right_now_and_this_is_the_app_icon/). Some [users complained about the icon's disgusting appearance](https://x.com/search?q=duolingo+app+sick&src=typed_query) and even uninstalled the app or at least removed the icon from their phone's home screen because of it.

This isn't the first time Duolingo has tried to win back users who haven't opened the app for a long time. It [tried a similar icon trick last October](https://www.digitaltrends.com/mobile/why-is-duolingo-melting-app-icon-explained/) and [again this April](https://gizmodo.com.au/2024/04/duolingo-bird-app-icon-changes-sad-old-face/). It remains to be seen whether this [free PR](https://x.com/AdelaVod/status/1829764612008419535) yields results.

Designer *Sam Talbot* suggests on LinkedIn [charging emotional design with positive energy](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7237533979778629634/). Motivate people for regular learning perhaps by having the icon improve. At the same time, the icon should also alert users when their frequency or intensity of app usage exceeds a healthy level.
