# Foursquare will end in December
> 

The Foursquare service, which was popular for its user reviews of places, will officially [end on December 15](https://foursquare.com/city-guide-sunset/#accordion_v2-f05c2227-8a8d-4de1-8576-72bf5301045c) after fifteen years of existence. The product team decided to discontinue its operation and focus their efforts on developing the spin-off [Swarm app](https://www.swarmapp.com/). While Foursquare specialized in providing user recommendations and reviews of places, Swarm is focused on a geolocation game and social interactions related to visiting various places.

For Foursquare users, it's important that their data will not be lost. All data from Foursquare, including visit history and reviews, will continue to be available through the Swarm app. Moreover, if users wish to back up or transfer their data to other services, it will be possible to export data from Foursquare until the service is discontinued.

Both Foursquare and Swarm have already had their best years behind them. Today, their function has been taken over by maps and business profiles of search engines. Foursquare does have an official [spin-off](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C5%99%C3%ADstavek) called City Guide, but in reality, it never gained traction in travel like Tripadvisor or in services like Yelp. The gamification element, which Foursquare later pushed into Swarm, led to the app's playfulness being transferred to points of interest and their reviews and tips. Swarm and Foursquare users today form a small, distinctive subculture.

The closure of Foursquare reflects a broader trend in the tech sector, where companies are moving towards simplifying their products and greater focus on user experience. The transition to Swarm can thus offer users a new way to connect with friends and discover new places in a fun way, while older reviews will remain available.
