# Electronic Signature from Bank-iD
> 

Bankovní identita offers a [free service](https://podepis.bankid.cz/) for signing documents. However, Czech legislation recognizes several levels of trustworthiness, and Bank-iD provides only an electronic signature at the level of [Guaranteed Electronic Signature](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elektronick%C3%BD_podpis#Zaru%C4%8Den%C3%BD_elektronick%C3%BD_podpis) – it therefore does not meet the [eIDAS](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/EIDAS) regulation for electronic transactions within the European internal market and cannot be considered an alternative to a handwritten signature.

A document signed with a Bankovní identita signature will suffice for a large portion of submissions to the Czech state administration, for invoices, and for common documents. On the other hand, for cross-border electronic communication or when dealing with a court, you will most likely need a stronger qualified electronic signature (meets eIDAS requirements) or at least a recognized electronic signature (with a qualified certificate issued by an authorized provider).

Many documents, however, can be signed simply with an email signature or by attaching a first and last name. For communication with state administration, delivery via a data mailbox or verification through NIA will work well.

Thanks to Bankovní identita, however, when signing documents with people who have an account at a Czech bank, you will save money on commercial services like DocuSign, SignNow, and similar.
