# The goo.gl URL shortener has one year left
> Starting August 24, the [last year of operation of the goo.gl URL shortener](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/google-url-shortener-links-will-no-longer-be-available/) will begin. Google stopped shortening new URLs in 2019 and since then has only been redirecting the existing ones. Starting next month, it will begin displaying an interstitial page before the redirect with information about the planned service termination, which will occur in a year on August 25, 2025.

> "Every such decision adds arguments that it's better to do things yourself and not use someone else's services."\
> **— [Michal Bláha](https://x.com/michalblaha/status/1814271563367071843), Twitter**

[Wikipedia says](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_URL_Shortener) that the goo.gl URL shortener appeared in December 2009 as a functionality for the Google Toolbar browser bar and a monetization tool for web feeds Feedburner. Both mentioned services have already been discontinued.

The first known URL shortener is [TinyURL](https://tinyurl.com/) and it still works. It was created as a solution for broken links that occurred during email transmission encoding and line breaks of long links at the end of a line. This mainly happened in discussion groups.
