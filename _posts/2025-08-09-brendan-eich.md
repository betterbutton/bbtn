# Brendan Eich – creator of JavaScript
> In 1995, Brendan Eich created JavaScript, thereby transforming websites from static documents into interactive applications.

![Brendan Eich – creator of JavaScript](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/808ab2c3-595e-4581-ba64-f226130b3088_1536x1024.png "1456x971")

[Brendan Eich](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brendan_Eich) created a scripting language for the web

[Brendan Eich](https://brendaneich.com/) (*July 4, 1961) primarily influenced the shape of the world wide web by creating the programming language JavaScript. In 1995, Brendan designed JavaScript in just ten days to meet the release date of the beta version of the Netscape Navigator 2.0 browser.

This language transformed the web from static pages to interactive applications and later, thanks to the [ECMAScript](https://ecma-international.org/publications-and-standards/standards/ecma-262/) standard from 1997, which Eich helped promote, it became compatible across browsers. Today, JavaScript runs on virtually every connected device.

However, Eich did not only influence programming. He co-founded the Mozilla project and later the Mozilla Foundation, where he helped launch the [Firefox](https://www.firefox.com/cs/) browser. In 2004, it broke Internet Explorer's monopoly. Firefox promoted open standards, security, and cross-platform support. This accelerated the adoption of HTML5, CSS3, and modern APIs and restarted innovation in the browser space.

He also played a major role in improving web performance. He was instrumental in the birth of [WebAssembly](https://webassembly.org/), which brought browser performance close to that of native applications and enabled complex games or graphics directly in the browser.

After the [scandal](https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-26868536) involving support for the movement against same-sex marriage and his departure from Mozilla in 2014, he focused on privacy protection and new models of web funding. In 2015, he co-founded [Brave Software](https://brave.com/) and introduced a browser that by default blocks ads and tracking scripts. Brave also introduced the Basic Attention Token system – a cryptocurrency-based way to reward users and content creators directly, without intermediaries.

From the inventor of a scripting language to a privacy advocate – Eich set the direction not only for technology but also for the principles on which today's internet stands, and helped protect the World Wide Web from monopolization.
