# VideoAnt helps with video annotation
> A free web tool for attaching notes to videos that supports collaboration and enables secure sharing.

![Screenshot from the VideoAnt video tutorial showing the interface for adding annotations at specific times in the recording.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d6a3fcc7-51b2-4241-a255-ac783b1952e0_1919x997.png "500x259")

[VideoAnt](https://ant.umn.edu/) enables group video annotation.

[VideoAnt](https://ant.umn.edu/) is a web platform for inserting comments into videos, created by the College of Education and Human Development at the University of Minnesota. It allows you to add comments directly to videos published on the web. Each comment, so-called "Ant" (ant), is tied to a specific time in the video and enables a clear discussion. These notes can be exported, embedded into other websites, or shared with specific people, which is useful for teaching, product development, and user research.

Getting started is easy. You log in with a Google account, select a video, and can start annotating. All your notes are neatly stored in your personal interface called "Ant Farm" (ant farm). The environment works on both computers and mobile devices.

Notes in VideoAnt are not just passive comments. Each can be the start of a discussion tied to a specific moment in the video. Sharing is secure and you have full control over who has access to the notes. Additionally, you can embed them into a school system or websites.

The power of VideoAnt lies in its simplicity and openness to collaboration. Instead of just watching a video, it allows you to truly work with the content. This leads to better discussions, analysis, and planning.

And because everything runs in the browser without installation, VideoAnt is easily accessible and usable in various environments—from the classroom to corporate teams.
