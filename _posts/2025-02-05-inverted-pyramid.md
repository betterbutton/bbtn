# Inverted Pyramid
> The Inverted Pyramid is a writing style where key information is presented right at the beginning, followed by details. It facilitates reading, improves user experience, and aids search engine optimization.

The Inverted Pyramid, sometimes also translated as the reversed pyramid, is a proven method for structuring text. It first conveys the most important information, only then adding details. This makes the text clear and easy to read.

**Readers immediately get the main information**, which is important because online people often just [skim text](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/lide-na-webu-nectou). It improves user experience and aids search engine optimization. Important information is at the very beginning, so it appears [on the first screen](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/above-the-fold) even without scrolling.

How does it work? First, you state the main message, for example: *"The new law will reduce the tax burden for small businesses."* Then **details and context follow** – why it's important and who it affects. Finally, you add less essential information, such as background, expert opinions, and related facts.

Journalists use the Inverted Pyramid when writing news. On websites **it helps present content clearly**. In corporate communication, it ensures that important messages are understandable at first glance – for example in emails, press releases, or on social media.
