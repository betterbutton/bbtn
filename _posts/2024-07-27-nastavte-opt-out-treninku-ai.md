# Why to set up AI training opt-out in Figma
> 

Artiom Dashinsky had a [legal analysis of Figma's terms for using new AI tools](https://x.com/hvost/status/1813632462704296263) done and concluded that all designers who habitually create moodboards in Figma with examples collected from the internet should set up to exclude their files from artificial intelligence training.

Because if Figma used their moodboards to train its AI tools and somehow then infringed on the copyrights of the works from the examples on the moodboard, according to his legal opinion, it would be precisely the creator of those moodboards who would bear responsibility.
