**AI can already generate websites.** [Tomáš Kapler asks](https://x.com/tkapler/status/1836447290929680646) if you've ever tried to create a website with AI. It's no-code, but with a text prompt. Tomáš [tries the Dora service](https://www.dora.run/ai?utm_campaign=867112_867513&signature=ba7640f7f90d8d3e8515026e373cd1fc), which offers AI website generation relatively recently, but previously allowed importing a design from Figma or remixing templates. With a prompt, Tomáš created [a website about a fictional air quality meter](https://conspiro.dora.run/). Dora also arranged the content and images for the site.

Besides Dora, Tomáš tried building a website with [Claude AI](https://claude.ai). In this case too, the result is [a website about air quality measurement sensors](https://claude.site/artifacts/90852101-fde8-42f1-a4f9-202a586f61de), albeit significantly less spectacular. The integration with Claude looks promising, especially if the resulting website doesn't just consist of static pages but also involves AI in the application logic.

We recently [showcased the Relume service](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/relume-ai-wireframe?utm_source=publication-search), which doesn't build websites directly but prepares a site map, content priority guide, and wireframe that can then be exported to Figma or WebFlow.

Artificial intelligence can help programmers and designers, or replace them completely. However, it won't replace the person with the idea, with the brief. Martin Michálek notices this in his essay and concludes that [makers with AI have a powerful tool in their hands](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7241810067568619521/).

**Design tokens make it easier to maintain design consistency.** Jakub recently shared on LinkedIn his [insights on design tokens](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7239923997092577280/). He recommended the Figma plugin [Tokens Studio](https://tokens.studio/), which can directly commit design tokens to GitHub, where developers can work with them immediately. If you'd appreciate a bit more information about tokens, what they're used for, and how to work with them, you'll find the [playbook on The Design System Guide website](https://thedesignsystem.guide/what-are-design-tokens) helpful.

**How to create readable text in Chinese typography?** Chinese typography is specific due to the logographic writing system, which emphasizes individual characters forming words and sentences. French UX designer [Juliette Xing](https://www.linkedin.com/in/juliette-xing/overlay/about-this-profile/) reminds us that a key part of the Chinese typographic school is balancing readability and aesthetics.

[Juliette recommends](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7241672238255476736/) left-aligned text, automatic letter spacing, and a line height of around 150–160%. For body text, you need to choose a font size at least half larger than for Latin text – 24 px, and for mobile headings approximately 44 px. Common Chinese fonts include 宋体 (Songti) for serif and 黑体 (Heiti) for sans-serif. For emphasis, use bold font, size, and colors, but avoid italics and underlining, as they reduce the readability of Chinese characters.

**Is it centered?** We've played many games together that trained us in colors, fonts, or composition, but we haven't yet tried a test for centering elements. That's exactly what today's game is about. Test your skills and judge whether the dots are truly in the center of the shapes. Ten shapes are prepared for you to choose from, but to win, you need to get all ten correct. [Can you do it?](https://avark.agency/designers-eye/)

**What you measure tends to improve on its own**

Choose a regular time interval during which you will assess your website's performance.

Different metrics may require different intervals for their evaluation. Consider which indicators you can manage to measure and analyze yourself, and when it's appropriate to consult an expert. Agree on regular reporting. Also consider whether a given metric is better suited for a live dashboard in a tool like Google Data Studio, Power BI, or another BI solution, or whether a regular report will suffice.
