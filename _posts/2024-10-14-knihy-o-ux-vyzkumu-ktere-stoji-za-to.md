# Books on UX Research Worth Reading
> 

Krisztina Szerovay [shares her favorite books on UX research](https://uxplanet.org/my-favorite-ux-research-books-why-i-love-them-3a516bb4192b), which helped her improve her skills as a researcher and product designer. She primarily focuses on qualitative research, although she notes that the quantitative approach is equally important. Based on her experience in both academic and business environments, she also recommends reading from fields like behavioral economics or cognitive psychology. She encourages readers to discuss their own favorite resources.

Here they are:

- [Just Enough Research](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/208928492-just-enough-research) (Erika Hall)

- [Designing for the Digital Age](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/3880458-designing-for-the-digital-age) (Kim Goodwin)

- [Interviewing users](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/198340339-interviewing-users) (Steve Portigal)

- [The Moderator’s Survival Guide](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/17938561-the-moderator-s-survival-guide) (Donna Tedesco and Fiona Tranquada)

- [Don’t Make Me Think](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/159897875-dont-make-me-think-by-steve-krug) (Steve Krug)

- [User Story Mapping](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/22221112-user-story-mapping) (Jeff Patton)

- [This is Service Design Doing](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/37935807-this-is-service-design-doing) (Marc Stickdorn, Markus Edgar Hormess, Adam Lawrence, Jakob Schneider) & [library of methods](https://www.thisisservicedesigndoing.com/methods)

- [Mapping Experiences](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/26762484-mapping-experiences) (James Kalbach)

  - [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11468377-thinking-fast-and-slow) (Daniel Kahneman)

- [Predictably Irrational](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1713426.Predictably_Irrational) (Dan Ariely)

…and six more, which you’ll have to [find in the article](https://uxplanet.org/my-favorite-ux-research-books-why-i-love-them-3a516bb4192b) yourself.
