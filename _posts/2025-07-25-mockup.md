# Mockup
> A user interface mockup is a static, detailed design that shows what a digital product will look like before development begins.

A mockup ([UI mockup](https://uizard.io/blog/guide-to-designing-ui-mockups/)) is a static, detailed model of a user interface design that shows what a digital product will look like before development begins.

A UI mockup serves to give all stakeholders a clear idea of the final product's appearance. It shows the layout of elements, colors, typography, and graphics—essentially everything related to the [look and feel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Look_and_feel), not functionality.

In digital teams, a mockup plays a key role. It helps uncover design flaws early, facilitates feedback, and provides developers with a clear foundation for their work. This saves time, reduces the risk of unnecessary revisions, and ensures the final product aligns with both the vision and the brand.

Mockups are typically created in tools like Figma, Sketch, or Adobe XD. These tools allow for precise visual design and offer collaboration features—such as comments, sharing assets, and exporting graphics. However, they can also be built as prototypes directly in production technologies like HTML/CSS.

From a business perspective, a mockup minimizes the risk of costly errors. Clients and stakeholders can easily review and approve the design before any programming begins. For web and application administrators, knowing how to properly read and comment on a mockup is essential—it leads to better decisions and more satisfied users.

A mockup is not just the output of the visual design phase. It is a communication tool that helps align designers, developers, and stakeholders before the actual building begins.
