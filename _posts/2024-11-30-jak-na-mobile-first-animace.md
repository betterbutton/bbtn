# How to Approach Mobile-First Animations
> 

Animations on mobile devices play a crucial role in improving the user experience, but they must be thoughtful and efficient. The "mobile-first" approach focuses on optimizing animations for small screens, touch gestures, and performance constraints. Aleksei tackled this in the article [Mobile-First Animation: Finding the Sweet Spot](https://medium.com/@Alekseidesign/mobile-first-animation-finding-the-sweet-spot-0ca99999b1e1) in a truly systematic way.

First, it focuses on the principles of mobile animations:

1.  Purpose over decoration

2.  Prioritize performance

3.  Design for touch and gestures

4.  Be concise and pleasant

5.  Use animation to clarify, not to distract

Then it continues with a typology of animations by purpose of use:

1.  Onboarding animation

2.  Loading animation

3.  Navigation animation

4.  Feedback and reassurance

5.  State changes

Alexei compares prototyping tools and libraries and then summarizes the most common mistakes:

1.  Overloaded effects

2.  Ignoring accessibility

3.  Neglecting testing

The future of animations is heading towards adaptive and AR-enhanced solutions that will offer more context-sensitive experiences
