# Empty state
> An empty state is a situation where the interface has nothing to display – for example, during first use or after an unsuccessful search. A good interface explains why and offers what to do next.

An empty state ([empty state](https://www.pencilandpaper.io/articles/empty-states)) is a situation where an application has nothing to display – for instance, on first launch, after a search with no results, or when a user has deleted all their data. Instead of a blank screen, it's far better to offer a clear message and a call to action. This can be the deciding factor in whether a user stays or leaves the app.

Imagine a new task management app. Instead of a blank page, it displays a message: *"You don't have any tasks yet. Add your first one."* The user then knows everything is fine and what they can do next. When someone searches for something and finds nothing, a simple suggestion helps: *"Try different words or check your spelling."*

The tone of the message is also crucial. For first-time use, it should be welcoming; for searches, more neutral; and for completed tasks, feel free to be joyful. Avoid obscure jokes or empty filler that doesn't help the user.

An empty state is often a person's first contact with an application. It's a chance to build a relationship and show that you understand their needs. If you waste this moment, the user might feel something is broken.

An empty state is the beginning. Design it to make sense.
