# Content priority guide
> The content priority guide is a simple content planning method that ranks information by importance even before starting the rough visual page layout in the wireframe.

The content priority guide ([content priority guide](https://alistapart.com/article/priority-guides-a-content-first-alternative-to-wireframes/)) is a subtle but effective tool for website and application design. It helps ensure people find what they need in the correct order. Unlike wireframes, which deal with appearance and interaction, this guide focuses purely on content. It's about what should be on the page and what weight it should carry.

Main advantage? It considers the user. Instead of internal hierarchy or marketing goals, the output is based on the real needs of people visiting the site. Whether they want to buy something, get contact information, or just quickly solve a problem, the guide helps determine what comes first. On a help page, for example, search and frequently asked questions might take precedence over a newsletter signup.

The format is intentionally simple. It's just plain text – a clearly ordered list of content by importance. No graphics, no placeholder text, no distracting elements. This allows the team to focus on what's essential and avoid unnecessary revisions in later stages.

The content priority guide also facilitates collaboration between designers, developers, and stakeholders. It helps align expectations, refine goals, and create understandable, usable interfaces.

It's a simple step that can significantly improve the overall outcome. And most importantly – it keeps attention where it belongs. On the people who use your site.
