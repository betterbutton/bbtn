# Tell me what problem you're solving, not what solution you need
> 

More text that could easily be a tip in our book, [wrote Marek Lecián](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7230207136612454400/). Marek mentions that clients often come to him with a request that already contains a specific solution method. In his case, it's setting up Google Analytics 4. But according to Marek, this is suboptimal.

> "If we don't know what business questions to ask, any tool setup is a pointless waste of time."\
> **— [Marek Lecián](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7230207136612454400/)**

When implementing analytical tools like Google Analytics or Google Tag Manager, it's important to focus on what you actually need to measure. Pure technical proficiency and the ability to deploy tracking isn't enough – the key is knowing what specific business questions you want to answer.

Tools are a means, not an end. The foundation is establishing clear goals, selecting appropriate tools, and using them effectively. Regular analysis of the collected data and flexibility in approaching new trends will then ensure the success and value of analytical tools.
