# FormSubmit.co will send form data via email
> FormSubmit.co sends forms via email without programming. Simple deployment, no registration, support for post-submission redirects, sender copies, and webhooks.

![Screenshot of the FormSubmit website](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/6f370b70-271c-44a1-bd00-b2c56b9826dd_1897x1067.png "1456x819")

[FormSubmit.co](https://FormSubmit.co/) will send the form content to your email.

[FormSubmit.co](https://FormSubmit.co) allows sending form data directly to your email without needing to program a backend server. No registration, no API keys, no subscription, no complex setup. After the first form submission, you will receive a confirmation message that must be approved. From that point on, all submitted forms will be automatically delivered to your inbox.

FormSubmit supports several advanced features that allow customization of form behavior. For example, you can set up an automatic redirect to a custom page after form submission, use a custom email subject, send message copies to additional recipients, or filter unwanted content using a blacklist. For advanced use and integration with other services, webhooks, reCAPTCHA disabling, or sending automatic replies are available.

FormSubmit is the solution if you want to process form data quickly and easily without complex setup. You can use it for static websites where you don't have server-side script support. Just be [very careful about what you send via the form](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/je-bezpecne-pouzivat-sluzbu-fo-sXU3Lae_R8qQP8wHcSHC3g#0). The company behind the service, Devro Labs, doesn't say much about itself. It states it's from Sri Lanka and declares that it does not store form data. For forms with personal or otherwise sensitive data, it's better to use a more secure solution like [Netlify Forms](https://docs.netlify.com/forms/setup/) or the old reliable [Google Forms](https://forms.new/). You never know.
