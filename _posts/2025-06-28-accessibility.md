# Accessibility
> Accessibility ensures that your digital product can be used by everyone, regardless of abilities or devices.

[Accessibility](https://www.dia.gov.cz/cs/nase-cinnosti/na-cem-pracujeme/pristupnost-internetovych-stranek-a-mobilnich-aplikaci/co-je-pristupnost) ([accessibility](https://www.w3.org/WAI/fundamentals/accessibility-intro/), [a11y](https://www.a11yproject.com/)) is a fundamental element of quality digital design. It is based on the international standard [WCAG](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/) and ensures that websites and applications can be used by people with disabilities – for example, those who rely on screen readers, keyboard navigation, or captions. For creators of digital services, this is not just best practice – from June 28, 2025, it is also a legal obligation under [European legislation](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424).

An accessible interface must be *perceivable* – for example, with alternative text and sufficient contrast. It must also be *operable* – usable without a mouse, understandable, and without unnecessary time limits. It must be *understandable* – with clear instructions and predictable behavior. And finally, it must be *robust* – functioning across various technologies, including future ones.

Accessibility expands the target audience, reduces legal risks, and improves usability for everyone – including people on mobile phones, with poor connections, or in noisy environments.

Automated tools alone are not enough. Thoughtful code, correctly used HTML, meaningful labels, and testing with real users are essential. A proven approach is so-called progressive enhancement – creating a basic functional version first and only then adding advanced features.
