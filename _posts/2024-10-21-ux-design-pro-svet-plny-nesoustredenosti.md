# UX design for a world full of distraction
> 

Today's world is full of distractions, and users are constantly switching between tasks. UX designers thus face the challenge of creating user experiences that can engage even in this multi-tasking era.

> "It's no longer just about capturing attention — you need to hold it."\
> — **[Harsha Royal](https://medium.com/@iharsharoyal/designing-for-distraction-ux-in-a-multi-tasking-world-77e0f4208dd1)**

In addition to clarity and simplicity in design, it is recommended to use microinteractions and snackable content, meaning easily digestible. It is also important to think about easy returns to the app after interruption and customizable notifications.

1.  Prioritize understandability: Don't force users to (over)think.
2.  Embrace microinteractions
3.  Shorten it: Content for busy people that can be consumed quickly.
4.  Design for repeat visits
5.  Notifications are meaningful, not distracting
6.  Plan for distraction: Design with interruptions in mind

The key is design that adapts its functions to the needs of users in their scattered world.
