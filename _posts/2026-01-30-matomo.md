# Matomo – web analytics
> Open-source analytics platform that emphasizes data ownership and privacy protection.

![Matomo statistics overview screenshot](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a3e44756-5de4-4bc1-a41a-5e6a6e6ed8b6_986x558.webp "986x558")

Matomo analytics dashboard.

*[Matomo](https://matomo.org/)* is an analytics platform for websites, built on a simple but fundamental principle: whoever operates a website should also have control over the data generated by its use. The system can be run on your own server or use a managed cloud service. This makes Matomo one of the tools that truly offers a choice between full control and operational convenience.

In terms of features, Matomo covers the common needs of modern web analytics. It tracks visits, page views, traffic sources, devices, campaigns, goals, and e-commerce. Beyond that, it offers tools for deeper understanding of user behavior, such as journey analysis, heatmaps, or session recordings. Data is presented directly and understandably, without trying to hide its meaning behind complex models.

The most significant difference lies in the approach to privacy protection and data management. When self-hosted, all analytics data is stored in the infrastructure controlled by the website operator and is not automatically shared with third parties. Matomo allows detailed configuration of IP anonymization, cookie handling, data retention periods, and tracking opt-out options.

This configurability also has legal significance in connection with GDPR and similar regulations outside the EU. Choosing Matomo also means taking on responsibility. Self-hosting requires maintenance, updates, and performance monitoring. The cloud variant removes part of this burden but at the cost of less control. In both cases, Matomo represents a conscious shift towards analytics as a governed and responsible practice.
