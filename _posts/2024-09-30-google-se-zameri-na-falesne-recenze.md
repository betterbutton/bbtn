# Google will focus on fake reviews in Maps
> 

Google has updated its policies for [Google Business Profile](https://www.google.com/intl/en_us/business/) and is introducing stricter measures against fake and incentivized reviews, [reports SEO Roundtable](https://www.seroundtable.com/google-reviews-restrictions-business-profile-violations-38068.html). Businesses that violate the Fake Engagement policy [may face three types of penalties](https://support.google.com/business/answer/14114287?hl=cs):

1.  a prohibition on accepting new reviews,

2.  temporary hiding of existing ratings, or

3.  displaying a warning about fake reviews on the profile.

This policy, originally applicable only in the United Kingdom, is now being expanded globally. With this move, Google is responding to the growing problem of fake reviews and strengthening the credibility of ratings on its platform.

The system uses machine learning and has already detected over 170 million suspicious ratings. [Live adds](https://www.zive.cz/clanky/konec-lzi-na-google-mapach-podniky-dostanou-pres-prsty-za-falesne-recenze/sc-3-a-230428/default.aspx) that, on the other hand, Google simultaneously supports honest businesses, for example, by awarding the Crystal Pin. The company is responding to the growing efforts to manipulate ratings and strengthen or harm a business's position in the market.
