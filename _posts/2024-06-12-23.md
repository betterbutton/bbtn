# Linkodrome

**Coders wanted them, but are they using them?** We're talking about the CSS property [Container Queries](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/CSS_containment/Container_queries). It's a property similar to [media queries](https://www.vzhurudolu.cz/prirucka/css3-media-queries), most commonly used to make a webpage adapt to the browser window width. Container queries detect the context directly for a specific element and apply other properties accordingly. One component, like a user profile card, can thus look different on a profile page and different in a side dropdown menu or a tooltip that appears on hovering over a username.

According to the website [Can I Use](https://caniuse.com/css-container-queries), the browser supporting this property reaches 92% of internet users. [Martin Michálek wrote about them](https://www.vzhurudolu.cz/prirucka/container-queries) over a year ago: *"It's something I really want in web design. And believe me, you do too."* A year has passed, and Chris Coyier notes that we still don't encounter container queries on the web very often. He offers [several examples from his own work](https://frontendmasters.com/blog/weve-got-container-queries-now-but-are-we-actually-using-them/) where components transform based on the context of use.

**[Collection of Apple templates for Figma.](https://www.figma.com/@apple)** While [Material Design for Figma](https://www.figma.com/@materialdesign) has had official support for a long time, Apple's official templates in Figma are only a few weeks old. Their number is growing quickly, and today they offer designers [thirteen source files](https://www.figma.com/@apple) not only with components for the Human Interface of operating systems for [iPhones, iPads](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1248375255495415511/ios-17-and-ipados-17), [Macs](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1251588934545918753/apple-design-resources-macos), and [glasses](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1253443272911187215/apple-design-resources-visionos), but also for [Apple Pay](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1367915141082663884/apple-pay), [Sign in with Apple](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1367916685468040146/sign-in-with-apple), [iMessage](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1367916269438172112/imessage-apps-and-stickers), and more.

# Living with Technologies

Slash pages – this nerdy thing is like from the late nineties, but it's trending right now. Maybe it's somehow connected [to the return of popularity of newsletters, RSS](https://www.niemanlab.org/2022/12/this-is-the-year-of-the-rss-reader-really/), [static websites](https://www.netguru.com/blog/what-are-static-site-generators), and [a shift away](https://www.mightybytes.com/blog/leave-social-media/) from [social networks](https://www.buzzfeed.com/meganeliscomb/why-people-quit-social-media). Over time, a practice for addresses of certain web pages has become established – they are called [slash pages, pages after the slash](https://slashpages.net/). The [naming](https://manuelmoreale.com/slashes) was most likely coined by Manuel Moreale.

Our website also has a page at the address [/about](https://reknisioweb.cz/about). Often, thanks to convention, you don't even need to use the page navigation to find out that, for example, RSS is at [/feed](https://reknisioweb.cz/feed), the sitemap at [/sitemap](https://reknisioweb.cz/sitemap), and old articles at [/archive](https://reknisioweb.cz/archive). And we didn't do anything to deserve the existence of these pages; Substack generates them directly, from where we send our newsletter.

As people started pampering their personal websites again and the indie web grows, the list of slash pages also grows. Besides the ones already mentioned, new ones appear. For example, /now, /blogroll, /bookmarks, /colophon, /coffee, or /chipotle. A beautiful example of slash pages is, for instance, Michael Sasse's website [ShellSharks.com](https://shellsharks.com/slashes).

# Tip from Our Book

Judge a website by how it works, not by how it looks.

![Illustration from the book 75 Tips on How to Ask About a Website shows an obese figure skater being judged by a jury giving only tens.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/880df1b2-891c-4411-9111-27da74e0061e.avif "480x388")

Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 Tips on How to Ask About a Website](https://reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

You would be surprised how many successful and frequently visited websites look outdated – for example, the classifieds portal [Craigslist](https://craigslist.org/) and locally, for instance, [Bazoš](https://www.bazos.cz/). Don't focus only on appearance. Business performance is what matters most.

You won't avoid sharing work drafts with colleagues. For them, it will be much harder than for you not to focus on appearance, but on logical organization. If even the concept designers deliver you something visually polished, come up together how best to explain that it's not the final design yet. For example, switch to a black-and-white scale.
