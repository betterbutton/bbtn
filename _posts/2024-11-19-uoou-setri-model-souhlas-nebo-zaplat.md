# The Office for Personal Data Protection is investigating the 'consent or pay' model. Seznam.cz received an injunction
> 

[Lupa writes](https://www.lupa.cz/clanky/uoou-setri-ceske-vydavatele-kvuli-pay-or-ok-seznamu-sbirani-souhlasu-predbezne-zakazal/) that the Office for Personal Data Protection is investigating the [consent or pay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consent_or_pay) practice, where website visitors must either pay or give consent for targeted advertising. Seznam.cz has received a preliminary measure and may not obtain this consent. Mafra and CNC are also under investigation, but without the same prohibitions.

The model raises questions about users' freedom of choice and GDPR compliance, with European regulations in this area not being uniform. Publishers defend the model as necessary for content funding.

The outcome of the dispute depends on the further position of the European Data Protection Board. [It already addressed the issue in April](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/news/news/2024/edpb-consent-or-pay-models-should-offer-real-choice_cs), as we wrote in [Better Button #17](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/17#:~:text=Velk%C3%A9%20slu%C5%BEby%20maj%C3%AD%20nab%C3%ADdnout%20bezplatn%C3%BD%20p%C5%99%C3%ADstup%20bez%20c%C3%ADlen%C3%A9%20reklamy.). According to the EDPB's opinion, such a model often violates the requirements for valid consent under the GDPR. Users should be offered a free alternative without targeted advertising.

The model has been abandoned under pressure from regulators in the EU by Meta (Facebook and co.). It is, on the contrary, common among German media.
