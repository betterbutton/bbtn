# Microcopy
> Microcopy – Short texts in digital interfaces that subtly but fundamentally influence the user experience.

[Microcopy](https://www.strafelda.cz/microcopy) is the subtle content in the user interface that can decide whether a user proceeds smoothly or gets stuck. Microcopy helps with orientation, reduces uncertainty, and builds trust. It includes button and control labels, menus and link texts, status and system messages, validation and error messages, form texts, guides, onboarding, confirmation messages and feedback, help, tips and warnings, or default texts for empty states, skeletons, and placeholders.

When microcopy is well-written, it also contributes to better results—for example, a higher number of submitted forms or completed orders. And yet it is often overlooked.

Successful microcopy is clear, well-timed, and written with the user's feelings in mind. Sometimes a single sentence explaining what happens after a click is enough. Other times, a short note reassuring the user that their data is safe helps. The important thing is that microcopy assists without delaying or overwhelming the user.

However, it often happens that microcopy is created at the last minute. The original text from the design is left as is. Or something generic is used. And the result? The user gets stuck. They don't understand what to do. They receive no feedback. The humanity disappears from the interface.

Good microcopy is the result of collaboration between content, design, and development. It is created in advance, tested, and refined. When you get it right, the user might not even notice—but they will move forward without trouble.

It's not about the amount of text. It's about having the right words in the right place. Words that guide, help, and provide certainty. And sometimes they can even delight.
