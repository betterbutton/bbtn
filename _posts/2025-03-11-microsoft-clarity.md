# Microsoft Clarity shows how people navigate your website and how far they scroll down the pages
> Microsoft Clarity – an analytical tool for visualizing user behavior on the web through session recordings, click heatmaps, and page scroll tracking.

![Screenshot from the Microsoft Clarity website](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ed6b04ca-1de4-4484-ac56-d8f074dc166e_1905x1079.png "1905x1079")

Microsoft Clarity is a free tool for visualizing website user behavior

[Microsoft Clarity](https://clarity.microsoft.com/) is a free clone of the popular [HotJar](https://www.hotjar.com/) service. It uploads user website journeys, records them in the form of heatmaps showing how far users scrolled down a web page or which website elements they clicked. By visualizing user behavior, you can understand what people are doing on your website and easily identify bottlenecks in the conversion path.

User journey recordings reveal where people encounter validation of values entered into forms, how they get lost in navigation, or when they misunderstand text and backtrack in their journey. The page scroll heatmap will show where people did not see content [below the fold](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/above-the-fold) or which links did not interest them enough to click.

If you have reservations about Microsoft, a similar service is also available in the free tier of the originally Czech [Smartlook](https://www.smartlook.com/) tool.
