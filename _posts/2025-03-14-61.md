## Linkodrome

**Low-fidelity design allows for quick idea testing** and focusing on the right problems without unnecessary details. Mathias Van Daele presents [three essential tools](https://medium.com/@mathias_van_daele/the-right-tool-for-the-right-problem-low-fidelity-ux-done-right-379495bb370b) – Miro, Figma, and AI solutions like UX Pilot and Uizard – and explains when to use them. Miro proves effective for quick wireframes and team collaboration with stakeholders without prior experience. Thanks to ready-made templates, Miro enables effective creation of user flows and interactions.

**There are various methods of user testing** – from moderated lab tests to informal guerrilla testing. [Properly chosen user testing methods](https://medium.com/@Shakuro/what-is-usability-testing-methods-types-usability-research-explained-bc540393522a) can save costs on design rework and contribute to a product's market success.

Did you name your layers?

**A guide to naming objects in Figma.** Are you among the designers who don't bother with layer naming, or do you meticulously follow rules for naming objects? If you'd like a clear system, try the [Component naming manifesto](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1345404188541317938).

**How do marketing, product, and user research differ?** Marketing research answers questions about demand, competition, and a product's business potential. Product research deals with how to design a product to be usable and aesthetically appealing. User research focuses on user behavior, needs, and frustrations. [Each of these types of research has its purpose](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/market-vs-design-vs-user-research-the-cheat-sheet-every-uxer-needs-845fb658e2d8) and proper timing.
