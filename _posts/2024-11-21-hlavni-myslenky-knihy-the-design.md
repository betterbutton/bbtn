# Key Ideas from the Book The Design of Everyday Things
> 

![Donald Norman introduces the term "affordances" in the book. Objects inherently indicate how they can be used. He demonstrates this using various types of door handles. A vertical tubular handle is pulled toward oneself, a metal plate on doors serves for pushing.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/75ddb689-2103-405d-835e-4929826ff30c_500x500.png "500x500")

The concept of [Norman doors](https://uxdesign.cc/intro-to-ux-the-norman-door-61f8120b6086) on the website [Sketchplanation](https://sketchplanations.com/affordance)

The book [The Design of Everyday Things](https://ia902800.us.archive.org/3/items/thedesignofeverydaythingsbydonnorman/The%20Design%20of%20Everyday%20Things%20by%20Don%20Norman.pdf) by Donald A. Norman is today considered a [foundational text in the field of design](https://medium.com/@anshikkasaini/design-of-everyday-things-by-don-norman-summary-336287b7bd73) and user-friendliness. It emphasizes the [importance of user-centered design](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/9-main-keys-about-the-design-of-everyday-things-4bfce6f68a8c), which focuses on users' needs, abilities, and behaviors.

Norman examines the psychology of everyday actions and introduces key concepts such as the [gulf of execution and evaluation](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/two-ux-gulfs-evaluation-execution/) and the [seven stages of action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_action_cycle). The book highlights how design that doesn't account for users leads to frustration and errors. It is an invaluable guide for designers seeking effective and user-friendly solutions.
