# Usability
> Usability – a property of a system that determines whether people can accomplish something with it without frustration.

Usability ([usability](https://baymard.com/learn/website-usability)) refers to how easily and with how much effort people can control a system – be it a website, a mobile app, or an ATM. If a user doesn't know what to do, what just happened, or how to go back, it's not their fault. It's a design problem.

A usable system is easy to learn, easy to operate, memorable, forgiving of errors, and pleasant to use. Good usability doesn't make a fuss – it just works, doesn't get in the way, and doesn't bother anyone. When something doesn't work, the error is not in the person, but in the system.

Some people think usability is something to tackle only at the end. That's a mistake. Usability is a fundamental property. If a person doesn't know where to click, what is a button and what isn't, or what the system just did, everything else loses its meaning.

How do you know if something is usable? Watch people use it. If they stop somewhere, hesitate, or get confused, something is wrong. You can ask a few simple questions: Does the user know what to do? Do they understand what happened after they did it? Can they make a mistake? And if they can – what happens afterwards?

Usability isn't just a nice detail. It's the difference between what people enjoy using and what they close with a frustrated expression.
