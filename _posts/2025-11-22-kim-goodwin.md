# Kim Goodwin: From Personas to Principles
> A UX pioneer who reshapes design leadership through organizational ethics, human values, and strategic influence.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/bc81d939-aef7-48e9-8178-1cfdb0aabc41_1536x1024.png "1456x971")

[Kim Goodwin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kimgoodwin) (approx. \*1970) is among the most influential figures in UX design. She became famous for her book *[Designing for the Digital Age](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/3880458)* and her work at the Cooper agency, where she co-developed the methodology of goal-directed design and professionalized the creation of personas as a research tool.

Her legacy, however, extends beyond methods. She argues that a great user experience is born where decisions are shaped by culture—not just visual impression. Her concept of decision-making systems shifts the role of design: it's not just about consistency, but about ethical alignment across the organization, based on respect for human dignity and autonomy.

As a design leader at *PatientsLikeMe* or *Woebot Health*—and as a consultant and speaker—she brings principles from medical ethics into technology development. According to her, digital tools are part of the largest unregulated experiment on humans in history.

Her message is clear: if we want to design responsibly, we must build cultures that put people above metrics and values above artifacts. Designers must move from drawing screens to influencing how companies behave.

Whether she trains teams in large corporations, mentors leaders, or speaks at global conferences, Kim Goodwin reminds us that the most important output of design is values—and the most effective tool is conversation.
