# Shape Principles 
> Gestalt explains how users naturally group elements in their minds, thereby facilitating intuitive interface design.

[Shape psychology](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gestaltismus) ([Gestalt psychology](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gestaltpsychologie)) originated in early 20th century Germany. It was shaped by the works of psychologists such as Prague native [Max Wertheimer](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max_Wertheimer), [Kurt Koffka](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurt_Koffka), and [Wolfgang Köhler](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolfgang_K%C3%B6hler). The goal was to understand how the human mind organizes visual stimuli into meaningful wholes. Instead of perceiving individual parts separately, people see the whole – and this principle became the foundation for the modern approach to design.

In interface and digital product design, this is crucial. Users don't evaluate each button separately. They perceive a page or application as one coherent whole. If elements don't appear unified, the experience suffers – even if individual parts are designed correctly.

## Basic Gestalt Principles

- **[Proximity](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/proximity)** (Proximity): Elements that are close to each other are perceived as related.

- **[Common Region](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/common-region)** (Common Region): Enclosed elements or elements on a common background are perceived as related.

- **[Similarity](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/similarity)** (Similarity): Objects that look alike are grouped together.

- **[Closure](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/closure)** (Closure): We tend to complete missing parts of shapes.

- **Continuity** (Continuity): We perceive lines as continuous and smooth.

- **[Figure-Ground](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/figure-ground)** (Figure-Ground): We separate an object (figure) from the background.

- **[Uniform Connectedness](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/uniform-connectedness)** (Uniform Connectedness): Visually connected elements are perceived as one meaningful whole.

- **Common Fate** (Common Fate): Elements moving in the same direction are considered connected.

- **[Prägnanz](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/pragnanz)** (Prägnanz): People tend to perceive the simplest and most stable shape.

- **Symmetry** (Symmetry): Symmetrical shapes appear to belong to one structure.

- **Past Experience** (Past Experience): Perception is influenced by what we know.

When a designer respects shape principles, the user experience appears clear and logical. When they are ignored, the interface may look modern but will be confusing for users.

The power of Gestalt lies not in beauty, but in understanding. It reflects the natural way humans perceive. Whether you're designing an app or a website, think like a user — and see like them.

---

**Translation Note:** The term *gestalt principles* has entered Czech terminology from English, and it is customary to simply adapt the expression to Czech spelling and grammar as *gestaltové principy* or *geštaltové principy*. Even in contemporary psychological literature, shape psychology or holistic therapy is written about as Gestaltism.

When choosing the dictionary entry *tvarové zásady* (shape principles), we adhered to consistency with the approach used for other terms, such as translating *gulf of execution* as *propast realizace*. We are aware that the translation may obscure the connection to the English equivalent, but we perceive a positive impact in the greater comprehensibility of the Czech term, especially for beginning designers and the non-design public.
