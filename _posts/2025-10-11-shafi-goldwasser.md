# Shafi Goldwasser, guardian of internet privacy
> A pioneer whose theory enables digital communication to be private, secure, and trustworthy.

![Shafi Goldwasser in front of a blackboard, generated drawing based on a 2013 photograph by Adrian Bisson](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/894af962-a2a4-41f3-ae81-fb358ef21bf0_1536x1024.webp "1456x971")

Shafi Goldwasser, co-creator of probabilistic encryption and zero-knowledge proofs.

**[Shafrira Goldwasser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shafi_Goldwasser)** (*born 1958). Every secure connection on the web, from online banking to private messages, stands on the work of Shafi Goldwasser. Her mathematical theories defined what it truly means for information to be secure and built the invisible foundations of the modern internet.

Shafi, an Israeli-American computer scientist and recipient of the [Turing Award](https://amturing.acm.org/byyear.cfm#:~:text=Lamport%2C%20Leslie%20Barry-,(2012), together with *[Silvio Micali](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silvio_Micali)*, coined the term probabilistic encryption in the 1980s. She introduced an element of randomness into encryption algorithms, so the same message is never encrypted the same way twice. This step became the foundation for [HTTPS protocols](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Glossary/HTTPS), digital payments, and the entire web security infrastructure.

She is also a co-author of the concept of *[zero-knowledge proofs](https://peach-dev.cz/wiki/zero-knowledge-proof/#:~:text=Zero%20Knowledge%20Proof%2C%20neboli%20d%C5%AFkaz,konkr%C3%A9tn%C3%AD%20informace%20o%20tomto%20tvrzen%C3%AD.).* These allow one to prove the truth of a statement without revealing the data that proves it. What initially sounded like a theoretical curiosity today powers blockchains, passwordless logins, and private transactions on the internet.

Beyond scientific discoveries, Shafi is also bringing cryptography into new areas. Through the company Duality Technologies, she promotes homomorphic encryption, which allows computation on data that remains encrypted. This method is crucial for the secure use of artificial intelligence and cloud services.

> “If you keep your data private, then you are obtaining the power to some extent.”\
> **— [Shafi Goldwasser](https://engineering.ucdavis.edu/news/uc-berkeleys-shafi-goldwasser-discusses-cryptography-and-safe-machine-learning)**

Her influence is both technical and ethical. She argues that privacy is not a convenience but a civil right. Strong cryptography protects human autonomy in a digital society. She showed that trust on the internet can be mathematically proven, not just assumed.

For creators of digital products, her legacy is clear: every secure login, every encrypted message, and every digital signature stands on her principles. In an era where data leaks with every click, Shafi Goldwasser remains a silent guardian of internet privacy—proof that mathematics can defend human dignity in the digital world.
