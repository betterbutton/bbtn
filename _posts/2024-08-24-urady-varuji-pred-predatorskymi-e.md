# Authorities warn against predatory e-shops
> 

The National Office for Cyber and Information Security (NÚKIB) and the Office for Personal Data Protection (ÚOOÚ) issued a joint statement warning about [risks associated with e-shop applications](https://uoou.gov.cz/novinky/vse/spolecne-prohlaseni-uoou-a-nukib), which require non-standard permissions and may collect excessive amounts of personal data.

The statement does not mention specific e-shops, but Jan Sedlák on Lupě [assumes that it concerns Temu and Shein](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/ceske-urady-varuji-pred-e-shopy-sbirajicimi-data-uzivatelu-zrejme-jde-o-cinske-temu-ci-shein/). Specifically regarding Temu, the consumer magazine dTest [complained](https://www.dtest.cz/clanek-10928/dtest-spotrebitelska-organizace-dtest-podala-stiznost-na-temu) to Czech authorities.

According to the authorities, users should be careful when granting permissions to these applications, especially if they require access to sensitive data that is not necessary for their functioning. The risk also includes the possibility of transferring this data to third parties. It is recommended not to install such applications on devices containing sensitive information, and if so, uninstall them after use.
