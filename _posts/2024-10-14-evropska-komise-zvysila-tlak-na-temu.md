# European Commission increased pressure on Temu
> 

[European Union increased pressure on the Chinese e-commerce platform Temu](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/11/temu-gets-more-questions-from-the-eu-about-illegal-product-risks/), to ensure its compliance with the Digital Services Act regulations. The Commission demands detailed information on measures against the sale of illegal goods and consumer protection.

If Temu does not provide the necessary information by October 21, [the EU can initiate a formal investigation.](https://www.euronews.com/next/2024/10/11/temu-asked-by-the-eu-commission-to-clarify-measures-taken-against-illegal-products) Temu has been in the spotlight since May 2024, when it was designated as a *[large online platform](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/temu-vlop?utm_source=publication-search)*. Consumer protection organizations welcome the pressure on the platform, which faces criticism for insufficient transparency and inadequate user protection.
