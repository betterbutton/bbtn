# Confirmshaming is a Bad Idea and How to Achieve Better UX
> 

[Confirmshaming](https://www.cevelova.cz/slovnicek/confirmshaming/) is a practice in UX design that uses emotional pressure to force users into a specific decision. A typical example are phrases like "No, I don't want to save money" on rejection buttons. This strategy increases conversions in the short term, but in the long run it undermines user trust and satisfaction.

[Experts recommend using positive motivation instead of pressure](https://paylode.com/articles/confirmshaming), such as rewards for engagement or neutral language. Ethical design practices, like easy rejection options and personalized offers, lead to better results and brand reputation.
