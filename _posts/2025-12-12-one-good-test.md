# One Good Test Is Worth More Than a Thousand Expert Opinions
> In user experience design, one real test reveals more truth than endless expert debates.

> *You are opening the twelfth window of the BBtn Advent Calendar for 2025. This year's advent series looks at popular UX designer proverbs. Today's is:* *One Good Test Is Worth More Than a Thousand Expert Opinions.*

It takes just one test with real users to reveal what no consultation or analysis can. You watch people try to complete common tasks, and within minutes you see where they get lost, what they don't understand, and what prevents them from completing an action. That's why the old saying attributed to *[Wernher von Braun](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wernher_von_Braun)* reminds us that testing is always better than guessing.

Research confirms this. The so-called *"expert blind spot"* phenomenon shows that even experienced experts evaluating the same website find different problems and often agree on only a small fraction. Comparing expert evaluations with user testing revealed that experts uncover only about forty percent of actual issues. Real users, on the other hand, reveal critical errors that experts miss.

The power of testing isn't just in the data, but in how it changes company culture. A short video where a user fruitlessly searches for the *"Buy"* button silences even the loudest opinions and refocuses attention on facts.

Expert reviews and checklists have their place—they quickly reveal obvious flaws. But until users confirm them, they are just assumptions. Not even a formal accessibility audit compares to seeing a person with a disability struggle on your website.

For designers and web managers, the lesson is clear: test early, test often, even on a small scale. Every observation from real users brings more certainty than a pile of opinions. [Evidence-based design](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/evidence-based-design) isn't a luxury—it's the fastest path to a better website. In UX, the only true expert is the user.
