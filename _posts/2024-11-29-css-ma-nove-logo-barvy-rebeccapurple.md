# CSS has a new logo color rebeccapurple
> 

![Logo of the Cascading Style Sheets standard](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d405bef3-a98d-480c-a581-03ebef5d27a9_1193x895.png "372x279")

CSS has introduced a new logo in the iconic color [rebeccapurple](https://zeldman.com/2014/06/10/the-color-purple/). [The logo was created by Javi Aguilar](https://itsjavi.com/projects/new-css-logo/), who states it is also the result of a community process designed and selected by members of the [CSS-Next group at W3C](https://github.com/CSS-Next/css-next). Javi created several variants, which were then publicly discussed and evaluated by the community.

The final version of the logo symbolizes the evolution of web technologies. It has rounded corners reminiscent of a tooltip, references modern CSS properties, and uses a popular web color. The design is intended to be functional at various sizes, legible in both black-and-white and color versions, and harmonize with logos of other web technologies.

The resulting logo was chosen in a vote with over 460 votes. Beyond its visual aspect, the logo is a symbol of cohesion, empathy, and humanity in the technological world. It was co-created by Adam Argyle from Google and the design author, who iteratively incorporated feedback from various community platforms.

The font used, [DINish Preview Condensed](https://github.com/playbeing/dinish), is a modern interpretation of the classic German typeface DIN 1451, which is another subtle nod to place and culture.

The logo bears the purple color `#663399`. Its name was added to the [CSS standard](https://www.w3.org/TR/css-color-4/#valdef-color-rebeccapurple) in 2014 as a memorial to Rebecca Alison Meyer, the daughter of developer [Eric Meyer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_A._Meyer), who died of a brain tumor on her sixth birthday. The purple `#663399` was her favorite shade.
