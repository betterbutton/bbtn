## Linkodrome

**How to handle payment demands for photos,** [advocate Petra Dolejšová advises](https://www.petradolejsova.cz/blog/fotky-z-fotobank-a-vyzvy-k-ne-zaplaceni). Payment demands for unauthorized use of stock photos from companies like Copytrack or PicRights are multiplying. Some cases are legitimate, others are pure scams. If the demand is legitimate, you have three options: pay, ignore (which carries legal risks), or negotiate. Always consider consulting a lawyer before agreeing to any settlement.

**Why do we still encounter poor design?** No, we won't answer with a counter-question, but we'll send you to Scott Berkun. He summarizes [10 main reasons](https://whydesignishard.substack.com/p/the-10-reasons-bad-design-happens) why this happens: Organizational incompetence, not perceiving design as a key strategy, managers' unwillingness to take risks with design, ignorance of the creative process, or inability of UX leaders to influence decisions, too many decision-makers, unclear idea of the user, focus on features instead of scenarios, insufficient definition of the project goal, and poor interpretation of user data.

**Intuitive variable visualization in Figma.** [Variable Visualizer](https://www.figma.com/community/plugin/1457362132545070106/variable-visualizer) changes the way you work with variables in Figma by displaying them clearly on an infinite canvas. It allows you to easily visualize relationships between variables via drag-and-drop, quickly search for variables, or simulate their behavior in different modes. This makes working with variables more intuitive and clearer. Variable Visualizer also lets you instantly see all variables used in any layer or component, making it easier to manage complex design systems.
