# Linkodrome

**Figma, AI and the commoditization of design: What awaits us?** At the June ConFig, Figma [presented new AI features](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/config-2024) and it sparked mixed reactions in the design community. While some features, like layer renaming or enhanced search, can save time, the *make design* feature is seen as controversial. Critics argue that AI contributes to design homogenization and leads to creating average results.

Let's set aside concerns about copyright and plagiarism generation, which led Figma to [pause AI design generation](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/figma-vypnula-ai). The true value of design lies in the human approach, creativity, and empathy. AI suggestions still feel like outputs from people on Fiverr, which AI most often replaces. Over what constitutes a human touch in design, Scott Riley reflects in the article [On AI and the commoditisation of design](https://www.scott.is/writing/about/ai-and-design) and he's not far off with strong words.

**Heuristic? Audit? Checklist.** When you want to be sure your websites or apps are well designed and implemented, you turn to a UX consultant and order a second pair of eyes from them. To start, it's more effective and faster than launching user research and quality measurement. Consultants sell this service as a heuristic or an audit. It sounds really substantial, but essentially it means they have a [checklist](https://www.checklist.design/) and they evaluate your project against it.

In the Better Button newsletter #21 we focused on [Nielsen's heuristics](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/144652153/zivot-s-technologiemi), but there are [quite a few checklists](https://uxdesign.cc/ux-checklists-bf2dda85787f) [you can use to evaluate your projects](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2022/09/ux-checklists-for-interface-designers/). Checklists help maintain consistency and minimize errors, which is key for quality user experience. They improve work efficiency and predictability because they provide a clear overview of necessary steps. They facilitate communication and teamwork because everyone knows what needs to be completed. A checklist serves as documentation of work progress, which is useful when you want to improve long-term.

Value Proposition Canvas by Alexander Osterwalder. Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://www.katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 tips on how to ask for a website](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

**Want problem analysis from research. Leave solution ideas for the concept phase**

Don't anticipate, don't invent solutions for non-existent problems. Describe what your clients need to solve, explore their motivations, and see what troubles they struggle with. When the time comes to design a concept, this will make it easier to identify ideas that fit your website.

Don't invent arguments for your ideas. Do it the other way around and look for ideas for problems and opportunities that emerge from research.

Try filling out the [Value Proposition Canvas](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/the-value-proposition-canvas). Its right half describes your clients' situation. You'll use the left half later during concept design, but you can already note in it how your competition reacts to your clients' situation.
