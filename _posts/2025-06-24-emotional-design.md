# Emotional Design
> The path to a deeper relationship with the user through feelings and experiences.

[Emotional Design](https://wikisofia.cz/wiki/Vyu%C5%BEit%C3%AD_emoc%C3%AD_v_designu) ([emotional design](https://www.nngroup.com/books/emotional-design/)) determines how people feel when using a product – and it is precisely these feelings that decide whether they will return, whether they will grow to love the product, and whether they will recommend it to others. Emotions come into play at three levels: the first impression, the actual usage, and the memory we take away with us.

On the first, so-called visceral level, appearance plays the main role. Colors, shapes, fonts – all of these determine the impression a product makes even before we do anything with it. A pleasant appearance builds trust and sparks the desire to continue.

On the second, behavioral level, the key is that the product works as the person expects. Simple controls, fast responses, clear feedback – all of this creates a sense of certainty. When we know what is happening and everything proceeds smoothly, we take joy in using it.

And finally, there is the reflective level – what we take away from the usage. If a product becomes part of our identity, if it evokes pride in us or reminds us of values we share, we will remember it. And with it, the brand behind it.

For creators and managers of digital products, emotional design is a powerful tool. It transcends the boundaries of functionality and helps build a relationship. Products that appeal to emotions are not just usable – they are beloved. And that is precisely what determines whether they succeed.
