# Pre-mortem and post-mortem
> Pre-mortem and post-mortem analysis – two methods that help prevent errors and learn from them in software development and operations.

[Pre-mortem](https://medium.com/@talisa/how-to-map-your-fears-1cd115eccd35) and [post-mortem](https://www.linkedin.com/advice/0/heres-how-you-can-conduct-post-mortem-analysis-i9sze) are ways to avoid problems during application development and to learn from them when they occur.

Before launching a new feature, someone in the team can ask: what could go wrong here? That's how [pre-mortem](https://www.atlassian.com/team-playbook/plays/pre-mortem) begins. We imagine that everything failed and look for reasons. This will reveal weaknesses we wouldn't have otherwise discovered. Perhaps the interface is confusing, perhaps the database can't handle the load. The goal is not to scare, but to prevent unnecessary errors.

[Post-mortem](https://www.mural.co/blog/how-to-run-post-mortems) is done after an incident. For example, when the system stopped sending emails or when production went down. It's not about finding the culprit. It's about finding out exactly what happened, how we reacted, and what we'll do differently next time. It's a tool for team learning and improvement.

Both methods help think about system development more consciously. Pre-mortem helps beforehand, post-mortem afterwards. When used regularly, they reduce risks and strengthen confidence in both the team and the product.

In the fast-paced world of web and mobile applications, it is precisely this foresight that can differentiate a stable service from one that regularly burns.
