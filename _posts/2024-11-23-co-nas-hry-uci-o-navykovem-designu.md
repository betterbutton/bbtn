# What games teach us about addictive design

> 
> "Games give us a sense of competence thanks to easy tasks — for example jumping over a mushroom in Super Mario Bros."\
> **— [Vikram Bhat](https://medium.com/@bhat.vikram?source=post_page---byline--eb8e57eb7ce1--------------------------------)**

Addictive games and applications use psychological principles to [create intuitive and engaging user experiences](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/the-psychology-of-ux-what-games-teach-us-about-addictive-design-eb8e57eb7ce1). From easy tasks that boost self-confidence, to freedom of choice and connection with others — everything is designed to activate the brain and keep users engaged.

> "Behind the repeating actions of games is the so-called core loop: action → reward → repetition."\
> **— [Vikram Bhat](https://medium.com/@bhat.vikram?source=post_page---byline--eb8e57eb7ce1--------------------------------)**

It is also key to create repeating 'loops' with rewards and progression that grows with user skills. An example is Apple Fitness+ with visual motivation (closing rings) and social challenges. This approach can be applied to UX design — from gamified onboarding processes to surprise rewards.

> "Apple Fitness+ closes users into habit loops through visual motivation and surprise rewards."\
> **— [Vikram Bhat](https://medium.com/@bhat.vikram?source=post_page---byline--eb8e57eb7ce1--------------------------------)**
