## From The Designer

![Vlwvo (incorrectly) long lines of text. On the right (correctly) moderately long lines of text.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/0b9df52c-7397-446c-bc53-924625a56935_2160x1080.png "1456x728")

**Keep your line length in check.** Reading overly long lines is tiring.\
\
❌ More than 90 characters per line\
✅ Ideally 50–70 characters per line\
\
👉 Easier eye movement while reading.\
👉 People read more.\
\
Short line, longer attention.

Trevor Calabro offers a simplified three-step [process for building a top task overview](https://trevorcalabro.substack.com/p/ux-research-starts-with-tasks), which helps anchor research and design in real user behavior. **Localized URLs? Unnecessary work and extra problems,** says Zdeněk Dvořák, as he [warns against localizing technical parts of URLs](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7335268650578243585/), such as category names or filters. He claims that consistent URLs across markets significantly simplify reporting, browsing management, and internal workflows. Moreover, users barely see URLs, and large companies like Amazon or Heuréka also choose a universal format. Zdeněk recommends using identifiers and numerical IDs, especially for e-commerce sites, where complex URLs can complicate management and analytics.

**Prompt engineering is becoming an important skill for developers.** Addy Osmani offers a [practical guide on how to effectively prompt](https://addyo.substack.com/p/the-prompt-engineering-playbook-for) AI assistants so that results are accurate, creative, and beneficial. Addy shows how important context, specificity, and breaking down tasks step-by-step are. He emphasizes iteration and feedback, which transform AI into a useful colleague. Through examples and anti-patterns, you'll gain proven templates for tuning, refactoring, and creating new features. The result is better code and deeper understanding.

**Will it surpass Stackoverflow in traffic and Wikipedia next?** Marek Rolný comments on the launch of Google's AI Overviews in the Czech Republic. [AI is transforming the search results page](https://optimalne.net/clanek/google-ai-overviews) – artificial intelligence answers informational queries directly in the search engine, significantly reducing the number of clicks through to websites. SEO is thus losing its traditional role and must rely more on strategy, trust, and brand. Perhaps we're writing that [glossary](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/slovnicek) in vain.

**Another blow awaits e-shops.** [Google is introducing an agentic cart.](https://blog.google/products/shopping/google-shopping-ai-mode-virtual-try-on-update/) Google's shopping AI will now be able to track the price development of selected products and automatically purchase goods.

**AI Overviews may instead use translations of English-language sites,** [writes Pavel Ungr](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7335296480708079616/): *“When Google \[…\] doesn't have enough authoritative content \[…\], it translates it from English super-authoritative sites like CNN or Forbes.”* This isn't good news for SEO specialists, but for users it means that, thanks to Google, their world doesn't end at their language's borders, [as Wittgenstein would say](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7333726033575100416/).
