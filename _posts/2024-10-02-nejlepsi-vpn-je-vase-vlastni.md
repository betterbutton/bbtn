# The Best VPN Is Your Own
> 

> "The best VPN is one you’ve set up yourself... using software that has been inspected and audited by security researchers."

[TechCrunch published](https://techcrunch.com/2024/09/30/think-you-need-a-vpn-guide-start-here/) [a series of articles](https://techcrunch.com/2024/09/30/how-to-make-your-own-encrypted-vpn-server-in-15-minutes/) [about VPNs](https://techcrunch.com/2024/09/30/vpn-providers-do-not-protect-your-privacy-online-here-is-what-can/). In the article [We are skeptical of VPN providers, and you should be, too](https://techcrunch.com/2024/09/30/we-are-skeptical-of-vpn-providers-and-you-should-be-too/), it writes that VPN providers often claim to protect your privacy, but in reality, there is no reason to trust them. Regardless of whether the VPN is free or paid, you are transferring your trust from one entity (your internet provider) to another, often unreliable one. Many of them retain data about your behavior, and even claims of "no logs" are not always trustworthy. The best solution is to create your own VPN server, which provides real control over your data. A VPN can be useful, but you need to know its limits and not expect absolute anonymity.
