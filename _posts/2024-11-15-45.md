# Linkodrome

**Will UX designers become curators as a result of democratization?** The growing influence of artificial intelligence and design systems is changing the role of designers, who are moving from the position of creators to that of strategic curators. [This is how Andy Budd sees our future.](https://andybudd.com/archives/2024/11/the-future-of-design-how-ai-is-shifting-designers-from-makers-to-curators)

Thanks to available AI tools and advanced models, even people without deeper design skills can create visually high-quality designs. However, the democratization of design brings the risk of losing the professional value of designers, because *good enough* designs are now accessible to anyone. As a result, designers are taking on the role of curators who ensure quality, authenticity, and ethics in creation.

**Useful NN/g templates for UX research and design.** The Nielsen Norman Group offers [a collection of free templates and guides](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/free-ux-templates/) to facilitate the work of UX designers and researchers. These tools cover all phases of the process, from research and workshop facilitation to user experience mapping and content design. The templates include specific areas such as AI aids for research, evaluation checklists, visual designs, or methodologies for interviews and customer journey mapping.

**Black-and-white filter for Figma.** Sometimes it's not worthwhile to prepare [low-fidelity designs](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/low-fidelity-prototyping/) because you already have a rich component library ready for your product. However, you still want to present early designs so that it's clear to everyone that these are not final designs but concepts. In such a case, it can help to run your designs in Figma through [a filter that converts them to grayscale](https://www.figma.com/community/plugin/1105916617805028875/grayscale). No need to worry, the filter is non-destructive.

Jared cites a car dashboard as an example of a bad dashboard. The temperature gauge shows the coolant temperature and takes up a lot of space. Yet that information is rarely useful. Similarly, the fuel gauge shows the fuel level in the tank, even though what's more important to us is the range information. The speedometer and tachometer are also legacies of the analog era. As drivers, we would need to know more about whether we are driving safely, economically, and not exceeding the speed limit.

It is said that the best dashboards are designed by people for themselves. Libor Kříž, owner of a furniture manufacturing company, took an old display and [shows an up-to-date overview of orders](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7259910631682686976/) on it, which he maintains in Paper on Dropbox. Years ago, when we worked at Energomonitor, our colleague Michal Valoušek showed us how he made a [smart mirror for the bathroom](https://www.raspberrypi.com/tutorials/how-to-build-a-super-slim-smart-mirror/) for his family from an old mobile phone, a glass plate, and [light-transmitting mirror foil](https://www.alza.cz/hobby/samolepici-folie-d-c-fix-zrcadlova-ochranna-folie-3398050-0-675x1-5-m-d6462713.htm#discussionPosts). A few years later, [he experiments with a discarded Kindle](https://x.com/msgre/status/1701135815240241656).

Some dashboards leave us speechless even years later. Once at a barcamp in Vienna, Andreas Koller showed a dashboard designed for Vienna Airport by the studio Strukt. It was 2012, and [Visionair is still stunning to this day](https://vimeo.com/55007729).

**The website design must suit you**

Visual or graphic design represents how the website looks in the final stage. It is the visual embodiment of the product and provides the basis for interface developers.

The visual designer (often referred to as a UI designer on the web) is responsible for its realization, achieving results through thoughtful composition of colors, graphic elements, free space, and the creation of illustrations, photographs, or animations. Visual design can help users overcome uncertainty and influence their emotions in line with the product or brand focus.

Look for designers whose designs aesthetically suit you. Many of them have their own style, which is difficult for them to abandon. Only a few designers can smoothly switch between different styles.
