# Design Critique
> Structured internal feedback that refines designs before validation.

A good [design critique](https://kisk.phil.muni.cz/100metod/designova-kritika) ([design critiques](https://library.gv.com/guide-to-design-critique-86ebf499bed5), [design feedback](https://uxdesign.cc/get-better-at-giving-design-feedback-d8c719319fde)) rests on a few principles. We talk about the design, not the person. We start from user goals and needs. Feedback should be clear and actionable. And designers should receive feedback with openness. Listen, don't defend.

A practical guide to a formal design critique can be found, for example, in [Design Sprint 2.0](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YW2ifUTVd_E). Critique has a clearly defined place in decision-making. It helps select ideas for prototyping. Unlike validation, which tests solutions with real users, critique is an internal process. It serves to improve the design based on shared goals and the needs of the people for whom the product is being created.

Critique in Design Sprint 2.0 begins with a silent review of the designs. Everyone quietly looks at the sketches without names or comments. This is followed by voting with small dots. Everyone votes for what they find interesting or strong. Then comes a short group discussion. The designs are quickly commented on. The author remains anonymous. Subsequently, the decider determines which designs move forward.

Critique, when done correctly, is not a barrier but an accelerator. It helps align the team and move the design forward. It creates space for improvement and fosters team collaboration without unnecessary hierarchy.
