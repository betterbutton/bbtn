# When intuition leads beyond data…
> 

Intuition is not just a vague feeling, but a key ability of researchers, built on experience and empathy. According to [Daniel Kahneman](https://fs.blog/daniel-kahneman-on-intuition/), intuition is part of fast, subconscious thinking that arises from heuristics and pattern recognition.

[Gary Klein](https://www.amazon.com/Power-Intuition-Feelings-Better-Decisions/dp/0385502893) shows how professionals in crisis situations rely on intuition for quick decisions. Research intuition allows finding inconsistencies and signals that hard data does not reveal. However, its validation with systematic methods is important. [Intuition is a skill that can be strengthened](https://medium.com/@plates_insights/when-the-numbers-dont-speak-but-your-intuition-does-e5d3fffcb291) and helps us uncover hidden truths.
