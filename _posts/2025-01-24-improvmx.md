# ImprovMX will create an alias for your mailbox
> ImprovMX is an email redirection service. It comes in handy when you don't want to set up new mailboxes and each of the recipients uses an email on a different domain.

![Screenshot of alias settings for email addresses in the ImprovMX service](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ff89eaaa-a4c1-4595-8a6c-aeae508b9692_2640x1454.jpeg "1456x802")

ImprovMX – an email forwarding service

[ImprovMX](https://improvmx.com/) is a service for email forwarding – it's something like mail redirection for physical mail. The sender sends a message to one address, but it will be delivered to you at another. This is how we handle emails for the reknisioweb.cz domain. Jakub reads incoming mail in his business email, Martin in a different one, and Josef on Gmail. The addresses behave like aliases. This solution is useful for projects where it doesn't pay to set up separate email mailboxes when you can't create aliases above already existing mailboxes in a shared domain.

In the free version, ImprovMX handles up to 25 addresses in one domain and can forward incoming mail from one address to multiple mailboxes. If you want, you can also set up a domain catch-all, which is an address or list of addresses where ImprovMX will deliver messages sent to emails that you haven't explicitly set up in the domain. You just need to put an asterisk instead of the part before the @ in the settings. You'll be pleased that ImprovMX supports the [plus sign trick in the part before the @](https://support.google.com/mail/thread/228350356/plus-sign-in-gmail-address?hl=cs).

In the paid version, ImprovMX also offers the setup of an SMPT server for sending mail from forwarded addresses, but you can do quite well without this service. For sending, you can use any other outgoing mail server. In the [help section you'll also find a guide](https://improvmx.com/guides/send-emails-using-gmail/) on how to reply from your Gmail account under a forwarded address. This way, you can use, for example, the free [Google Workspace Essentials](https://workspace.google.com/essentials/), which doesn't include email accounts, along with your personal Gmail or other service account, and still receive and send emails from your own domain.
