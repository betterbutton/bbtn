**Give the page only one H1 heading.** H1 is the main title of the page, not decoration.\
\
❌ Multiple H1s just for visual effect\
✅ One H1 + other heading levels for structure\
\
👉 Structure is clear for both humans and robots.\
👉 Screen readers navigate better.\
\
[One H1 per page](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Reference/Elements/Heading_Elements#avoid_using_multiple_h1_elements_on_one_page) is the basic building block of hierarchy.

## Direct design and prompt-to-UI approaches devalue design in clients' eyes

> **Direct design** – a method of interface design where a designer describes the intent and AI directly creates and modifies the interface in code or a design tool from it.

AI-powered tools shorten the time from idea to design and finished products. Designing flows and screens into a rough draft using prompts can be done by designers with quality UI component libraries in a matter of hours. And it doesn't matter whether designers now directly prototype with AI in code or use [MCP tools](https://github.com/southleft/figma-console-mcp) for object manipulation in Figma or [Pencil.dev](https://www.pencil.dev/).

However, speed brings a new risk. When designs emerge from prompts, the designer can become detached from the result. They might not be able to explain why the interface works the way it does. Such outputs lose visible value for clients.

If it was previously true that a designer who can't write can't think, today it's multiplied. Added to this is the ability to describe flows and interfaces using formalized models – or at least the ability to understand the static and dynamic models of applications generated from prompts. And of course, the foundation for success is quality design systems or UI kits that serve as building blocks.

## Linkodrome

**The first hundred active users are more valuable than 10,000 downloads.** The first active users who actually use the application reveal weak points, ambiguities, and features that make sense. Their feedback helps improve onboarding, simplify processes, and decide what to develop and what to remove. Instead of chasing marketing growth, [focus on learning and iteration](https://medium.com/@floydjsmith02/your-apps-first-100-users-matter-more-than-your-first-10-000-downloads-f6106a2b769f).

**Figma fundamentally changed the way digital products are designed.** Whereas previously design relied on sending files between team members, [Figma brought real-time collaboration](https://medium.com/@quicktask.net/how-figma-changed-modern-product-design-workflows-73b5e6841304) in a single shared environment. Designers can work on the same file simultaneously, see colleagues' cursors, comment on designs, and iterate quickly. This eliminates version chaos and lengthy feedback handovers. The result is faster development, better communication, and more efficient work for distributed teams.

**How to turn AI into a product team.** *Shashank Kumar* describes why he stopped using AI just as an assistant and started organizing it as [a product team with clear roles](https://theshashankkumar.medium.com/i-turned-claude-into-a-product-organization-and-it-changed-how-i-build-74fcecdf9fe9): UX, UI, research, business analysis, or product management. According to him, such architecture reduces "decision debt," involves monetization already during design, and allows for broader but still coherent exploration.

**Questioning fundamentals helps designers find the real problem.** It's a way of thinking that breaks down a problem to its most basic truths and refuses to blindly follow established practices. It helps designers understand [the real needs of users instead of copying existing solutions](https://medium.com/@writeme.sharif/first-principle-questioning-in-ui-ux-design-a-powerful-method-for-understanding-problems-from-the-055cf51be879). It stands on repeatedly asking "why" until the core of the problem is revealed.

“When you see a project this problematic, the last thing you want to do is automate and streamline its operation.” — [Lucy Suchman](https://wave.rozhlas.cz/nepodrizujeme-se-potrebam-nasich-technologii-prilis-pta-se-antropolozka-lucy-7910316#:~:text=Kdy%C5%BE%20vid%C3%ADte%20takto%20problematick%C3%BD%20projekt%2C%20tak%20posledn%C3%AD%2C%20co%20chcete%2C%20je%20automatizovat%20a%20zefektiv%C5%88ovat%20jeho%20provoz.)

*[Lucy Suchman](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/lucy-suchman)* (*February 12, 1954*) transformed the field of human-computer interaction. In her famous book *Plans and Situated Actions*, she showed that human action doesn't stem from fixed plans but arises in specific social and material situations; plans serve more as orienting resources. At *Xerox PARC*, she established detailed video analysis of technology use and moved HCI from laboratory tests to observation of real work practices. In later texts, she critically examines the notion of autonomous artificial intelligence and emphasizes the human and institutional infrastructures behind it. Her work shifts technology design from technical optimization to sociomaterial accountability.

*[UI kit](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/ui-kit)* is the foundation of a consistent web interface. It consists of a set of reusable interface elements that help maintain visual and interaction consistency of a website. It includes components like buttons, forms, navigation, typography, or spacing and enables faster design and development.

*[Chunking](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/chunking)* is a human-centered design principle that organizes information and tasks into meaningful wholes to reduce cognitive load.

*[AI agent](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/ai-agent)* is software that not only generates answers but can independently plan and perform tasks across digital systems. Good page structure, semantics, and quality APIs therefore gain new meaning – they help orient not only humans but also machines.
