# Vision board
> A vision board is a permanent, visual tool that helps align on user experience, design direction, and shared goals; it bridges strategy and execution.

[Vision board](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jakubkarlec_m%C3%A1te-vision-board-v-2fresh-jsme-to-zkusili-activity-7288176254707507200-1zMl/) ([vision board](https://www.netguru.com/blog/tips-for-writing-ux-product-vision)) offers a clear way to unite different teams—from designers and developers to managers—around a shared vision, development direction, and user experience. Whether you're designing a new feature or redesigning an entire product, a vision board helps keep the team focused on what matters.

## From Inspiration to Strategy

A [vision board](https://www.plotbase.cz/blog/co-je-to-vision-board-navod-jak-na-to) serves to bring scattered ideas together into a single, understandable whole. It can include screenshots, sketches, quotes from user tests, or examples from competitors. For UX designers, this means creating a clear narrative instead of a collection of links and notes—for example, about how a design system is evolving, which navigation variants are being tested, or what users are telling us.

Tools like [Milanote](https://milanote.com/templates/moodboards/vision-board), [FigJam](https://www.figma.com/templates/vision-board-template/), [Miro](https://miro.com/vision-board/), [Canva](https://www.canva.com/create/vision-boards/), or [Notion](https://www.notion.com/blog/product-vision-boards-for-teams) make it easy to combine these elements into meaningful visual structures even before starting the actual interface design.

## A Shared Image for the Whole Team

But a vision board isn't just a tool for designers. It acts as a common language across the team. When developers, product managers, and marketing all see the same principles, user journeys, and conceptual designs, they have a clearer idea of *what* is being built and *why*. Moreover, when you embed the board directly into tools like Jira or Notion, it becomes part of daily work, not just a one-time deliverable.

## Bringing Vision Back into the Roadmap

Roadmaps often lose broader context. Instead of a long-term goal, you're left with a list of features. A vision board helps restore that context. It allows you to show how individual sprints and tasks fit into the bigger picture.

For example, when redesigning a homepage, you can create a board that shows research findings, competitor inspiration, early interaction sketches, and the development plan. As the project progresses, the board evolves with it. It stays current and alive.

## A Tool That Grows with the Project

A vision board is also useful during retrospectives or planning. Instead of relying on memories or lengthy documents, you have a clear overview of where you're headed and what needs adjustment.

A [vision board](https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/the-product-vision-board/) connects intention with actual work. It transforms ideas into understandable, shared, and editable stories. It helps create products that are not only functional but also thoughtful and cohesive.
