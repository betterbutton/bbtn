# Minimum lovable product
> The smallest complete version of a digital product that solves a real problem and gains user trust through clarity, care, and a positive first experience.

[Minimum lovable product](https://www.aha.io/roadmapping/guide/plans/what-is-a-minimum-lovable-product), or MLP for short, is the smallest version of a website or digital service that people not only can use but actually *want* to use. It offers one clear benefit, one meaningful journey, and does so in a way that makes the user feel confident, respected, and eager to return. On the web, where alternatives are just one click away, "it just works" is usually not enough.

This term emerged as a reaction to how [minimum viable product](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mvp) is often interpreted in practice. An MVP was meant to serve for learning from real usage, not as an excuse for unfinished or frustrating interfaces. Yet today, many websites are built with unclear value, broken steps, or rough visual treatment under the guise of "we'll polish it later." For most users, however, there is no later. The first impression forms almost instantly and strongly influences perceptions of both usability and trustworthiness.

With an MLP, the "minimum" refers to the *scope* of features, not the *quality*. The product does fewer things, but it does them whole and properly—from start to finish. Performance, accessibility, clear language, and error handling are not bonuses but fundamental prerequisites. At the same time, the team consciously chooses just a few specific places to show extra care—perhaps a reassuring confirmation, a human-written error message, or a clear explanation of the next step.

An MLP allows you to launch a website early, but not carelessly. When you focus on one lovable experience instead of many unfinished things, you create a website that feels trustworthy from the very first visit and builds a solid foundation for further development.
