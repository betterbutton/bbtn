# Talking to customers isn't for everyone
> 

The universal approach *everyone does everything* often fails, thinks Ryan Singer, when he jumped on the bandwagon with research democratization. Effective collaboration requires respect for individual expertise. [Developers don't have to conduct customer interviews](https://www.feltpresence.com/not-everyone-needs-to-be-talking-to-customers/) to contribute to the development of a great product, just as designers don't have to master everything from research to aesthetics.

"Real collaboration happens when each of us knows something deeply and combines forces."\ **— [Ryan Singer](https://www.feltpresence.com/not-everyone-needs-to-be-talking-to-customers/)**

The key is to connect the deep knowledge of various specialists, create a common language, and appreciate the strengths of each team member. Methods like breadboarding can simplify collaboration and enable groups to respond quickly and find solutions. The true value lies in synergy, where 1+1 is more than 2.
