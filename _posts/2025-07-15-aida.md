# AIDA – Attention Interest Desire Action
> AIDA: a four-phase model – attention, interest, desire, action – helps turn curiosity into a decision.

The [AIDA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AIDA_(marketing)) model – that is, attention, interest, desire, and action – is a proven framework for understanding and influencing user and customer behavior. Originally created in advertising, it remains highly relevant for digital product design. It offers a clear plan for turning a visitor into an active user.

Everything leads to action. Whether it's registration, a purchase, or a newsletter sign-up, every design element should guide toward a clear and easy next step. The user should never feel lost.

The AIDA model is an application of the [stimulus-response](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stimulus%E2%80%93response_model) principle. It uses the hierarchical approach of [CAB](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/341130959_A_Cognitive-Affective-Behavioral_Responses_of_Customer_Experience_CAB-CE_Model_for_Service_Delivery_Improvement_in_the_Healthcare_Industry) (Cognition→Affection→Behaviour) and is known thanks to publications by [Eliase St. Elmo Lewise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E._St._Elmo_Lewis) at the turn of the 19th and 20th centuries. In the 1960s, [Russell Colley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russell_Colley) derived the [DAGMAR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DAGMAR_marketing) model from AIDA, which is de facto the standard for advertising.

Beyond marketing, AIDA finds application in other fields. For example, in education, a similar model called Discover→Learn→Use→Goal ([DLUG](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JTqCR84fzeg)) is used. AJ&Smart borrowed it as a template for the output of the mapping phase in Design Sprint 2.0. In 2013, analyst [Avinash Kaushik](https://www.kaushik.net/avinash/bio/) derived the business framework See Think Do Care ([STDC](https://www.kaushik.net/avinash/see-think-do-content-marketing-measurement-business-framework/)) from the AIDA model.

Before action occurs, desire must come first. The product or service must be perceived as personally useful. This could be a demonstration of benefits, reviews from other users, or personalized content. Informing is not enough – you must persuade.

For web and app creators, AIDA is more than just theory. It's a practical tool to improve copy, structure, and overall experience – and to create an environment where people don't get lost.
