# Substack incentivizes app installation with free subscription
> 

A free subscription is an oxymoron, but how else to describe [the incentive Substack chose](https://writing.exchange/@ryansingel/113426819401882005) to persuade its users to install its app? If you subscribe to any publication with a subscription on Substack, you may have also been offered one month of free subscription if you install the app.

> Recommendations drove 2 million subscriptions last month across the platform, but the mobile app drove 3 million. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/05/substack-is-giving-out-free-subscriptions-to-boost-app-installs/))

The campaign increased daily iOS app installs from 7,752 to 17,382 in just a few days and propelled Substack to fourth place among news apps in the US iOS App Store – [writes TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/05/substack-is-giving-out-free-subscriptions-to-boost-app-installs/). Statistically, mobile users are more active and subscribe to content more often, Substack explains that the mobile app has now become the primary source of new subscribers, even surpassing their proven cross-publication recommendation system. Similarly, other companies, mostly from the e-commerce sector, also motivate app installations.
