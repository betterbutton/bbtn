# Basics of Wireframing
> 

A while ago, Vishal Gautam started writing an [intern's diary](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/denik-ux-stazisty). Now, as an intern, he has started sharing advice. But don't worry, his [guide to wireframing is really good](https://medium.com/@vishalgautam.ux/a-beginners-guide-to-wireframing-e9c2ff0d67d6). After all, the best people to explain a topic are often those who are just learning it themselves. And Vishal will teach you the Urdu word *dhaancha*, which means [skeleton or structure](https://rekhtadictionary.com/meaning-of-dhaancha-1), but also [sketch](https://www.urdupoint.com/dictionary/urdu-to-english/dhancha-meaning-in-english/81205.html).

A wireframe is a basic schematic of an application or website that helps visualize structure and functionality without visual details. This process saves time, money, and energy through early synchronization between the designer and the client. A wireframe example makes it easier for the client to envision the final product and reduces the need for costly revisions. Tools like pencil and paper or Figma can help create a basic design, which is refined based on feedback. For beginner designers, it's important to focus on layout and flow, which contributes to a good user experience.
