**Google updated SEO recommendations for CDN usage.** Pavel Ungr [pointed out on Twitter](https://x.com/PavelUngr/status/1875877943622438961) the new recommendations regarding the use of Content Delivery Networks (CDN) from a search engine bot's perspective. CDNs significantly speed up website loading by serving content to users from geographically closer servers. Additionally, they provide protection against overload or cyberattacks. Crawling by bots like Googlebot is more efficient on a CDN, but brings challenges such as cache initialization or the possibility of crawlers being blocked due to firewall settings. For optimization, it's important to [correctly set access rules](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/12/crawling-december-cdns), use tools like Google Search Console, and regularly check for blocks.

[Typopo](https://typopo.org/) fixes typographic slips in text

**Typopo fixes common typographic errors in manuscripts.** When preparing print, you have an InDesign plugin that solves most typographic slips using regular expressions, but what about the web or when preparing a newsletter? Exactly this is how [Braňo Šandala](https://brano.me/) struggled ten years ago. From his struggle emerged a simple tool [Typopo](https://typopo.org/), which will substitute correct quotation marks and apostrophes for you in the text, fix dashes and spaces, replace three dots with an ellipsis, add non-breaking spaces where they belong, and a few other common manuscript ailments. And what's great about it, it handles Czech, Slovak, English, German, and even Rusyn.

# So We Understand Each Other

![AI-generated drawing: A rocker, a punk girl, and a girl in a dirndl stand by a jukebox.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/5584543c-b7e7-41c5-92f5-8c19bde34bc4_1024x1024.png "504x504")

**Design by Committee.** It commonly happens: A designer brings proposals to a meeting to present them, and before long they're sitting there changing them based on instructions from other meeting participants. That's no longer a designer, that's a jukebox playing songs on request.

The result is, at best, very compromise, bland, and tasteless. The intention was to please everyone, and in the end no one is satisfied. Everyone adds what they like and what pleases them, like when a dog and a cat baked a cake. Along the way, the vision, uniqueness, and innovation get lost. It's then hard to hold such a concept together, and it's very difficult to find its internal structure that would allow it to be developed and scaled.

Design is indeed a team discipline, but this game has quite different rules than collaborative designing. A design team should be composed of diverse personalities with varied experiences and backgrounds. Rather than a group of people creating one output together, designers work side by side, inspiring each other, experimenting, building on each other, learning from one another, and borrowing. They quickly prototype, verify, and develop proposals in iterations. From the geyser of ideas, only the one that survives user testing prevails.

The purpose of designing is to precisely describe the problem and then solve it effectively. In design teams, it's hard to precisely determine authorship. Someone comes up with an idea, someone adds to it, another improves it, refines it. However, designers retain sovereignty and responsibility; they cannot hide behind collective voting. In contentious issues, the one who has the most to lose from a bad decision always decides. But in collective decision-making, no one is ultimately responsible.
