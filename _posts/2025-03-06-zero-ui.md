# Zero UI
> Zero UI – a concept of interacting with technologies without traditional interfaces, using voice, gestures, artificial intelligence, and in the future even thought control.

**Zero UI** – [zero user interface](https://digitalnizahradka.cz/2024/zero-ui/) – aims to eliminate traditional graphical user interfaces with buttons, keyboards, and screens, replacing them with [natural ways of control](https://medium.com/@Alekseidesign/the-future-of-ui-designing-for-voice-and-gesture-84f5f7061c65), such as voice, gestures, or artificial intelligence. Thanks to voice assistants, motion recognition, and automation, people can control technology without direct intervention.

One of the biggest steps forward is the interface for thought control, known as BCI (Brain-Computer Interface – [brain-computer interface](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brain-computer_interface)). This technology enables communication with a computer using only thought. This could fundamentally change how we interact with the digital world. The greatest hope lies with Zero UI for disabled users.

Zero UI brings several advantages. These include instant interaction, the possibility of multitasking, and more intuitive device control. Although Zero UI seems appealing, it is associated with several unresolved issues: technical limitations, security and privacy concerns, or individual neurological differences. From a practical implementation standpoint, reliable feedback remains a challenge.

The future of Zero UI and BCI depends on technological advancement. If brain signals can be reliably read and interpreted, a revolution could occur. Traditional interfaces may disappear, and technology will adapt to our thoughts and needs without the need for physical or voice interaction.
