# Screening questionnaire
> Quickly obtains key information about a potential respondent to decide whether they are suitable for the study or not.

A screening questionnaire ([screener](https://www.lyssna.com/blog/ux-screener-questions/)) is a short and structured set of questions that helps researchers easily identify respondents suitable for a specific study.

For teams building applications or websites, screening questionnaires are an unobtrusive but very useful tool. They obtain important information without overwhelming or deterring respondents.

For this to work, the questions must be short, meaningful, and understandable. The user should understand why you are asking something. A poorly designed questionnaire feels unnecessary and can be off-putting. A well-designed one, on the other hand, feels natural.

In the context of user experience research, it's like a first handshake: concise, friendly, and clear.
