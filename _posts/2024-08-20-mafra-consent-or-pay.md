# Mafra is preparing 'consent or pay'
> 

Mafra will introduce a [subscription with almost no ads](https://www.idnes.cz/zpravy/mediahub/obsah-sluzby-idnes-eu-souhlas-cookies-platba-financovani-premium.A240813_140449_mediahub_maka) for 149 crowns per month on its websites iDnes.cz, Lidovky.cz and Expres.cz. Subscribers will also get [access to the Premium service](https://www.idnes.cz/premium/), which currently (with ads) costs 89 crowns per month. Non-paying users will continue to have access to parts of the websites outside the Premium service in exchange for consent to targeted advertising.

The choice between giving up privacy or paying a subscription is something users commonly encounter with German news websites. It has been widely adopted by Meta services (Facebook, Instagram, Threads, WhatsApp, Messenger…) and in the Czech Republic by [Seznam.cz](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/15?utm_source=publication-search#:~:text=Seznam%20za%C4%8Dal%20prod%C3%A1vat%20digit%C3%A1ln%C3%AD%20v%C3%BDkupn%C3%A9) or [Czech News Center](https://premium.ikiosek.cz/web-bez-reklam) (Blesk.cz, iSport.cz, e15.cz, Auto.cz, Reflex.cz and Živě.cz). The 'consent or pay' approach is a [thorn in the side of the European Commission](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/meta-dma).

Commentators [under the report on Lupa](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/zaplat-nebo-dej-souhlas-mafra-zavadi-na-idnes-cz-platby-za-necileni-reklam/) are debating technical solutions for blocking tracking after venting frustration over the unclear stance of the Office for Personal Data Protection. The Office is likely waiting for the outcome of the [administrative proceedings](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/meta-dma) by the European Commission against Meta for violations of the [DMA](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/). This will probably not conclude until the latest possible deadline, which is March 2025.
