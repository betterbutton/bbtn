# Pain Point
> A pain point is a recurring problem in usage that causes friction, frustration, or user churn from a digital product.

Pain points ([pain points](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pain-points/)) are the hidden causes of user frustration. They are small flaws in design or performance that erode trust, complicate usage, and ultimately cause people to stop using your product. They might not show up in analytics. But they become clear in how people behave—when they don't finish registration, abandon a shopping cart, or repeatedly seek help.

Most often, pain points appear in four areas. In [usability](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/usability), when the user doesn't know what to do next. In [accessibility](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/accessibility), when some people hit a barrier due to a health limitation. In performance, when the product is slow or unreliable. And in support, when a person can't get help when they need it most.

Analytics will tell you where something squeaks. But it won't tell you why. That's why user research is essential. Watch how people interact with your website or app. Ask them what confuses them. Look at recordings of their screens. You'll see exactly the spots where they lose orientation or patience.

Fixing pain points isn't just about tweaking details. It's about closing the gap between you and your users. Every eliminated pain point improves the experience and reduces frustration.

Listen. Observe. Act.

And you'll create a product that people don't just use. They'll understand it—and want to return to it.
