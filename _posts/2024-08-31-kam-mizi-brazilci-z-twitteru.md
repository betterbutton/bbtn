# Where are Brazilians going after Twitter's ban?
> 

A Brazilian court blocked the social network Twitter after it failed to comply with the requirement to appoint a local statutory representative. Judge Moraes also made a rather unusual decision when [he announced that bypassing the ban using a VPN will be fined](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/30/world/americas/brazil-elon-musk-x-blocked.html#:~:text=VPN%2C%20could%20be%20fined%20nearly%20%249%2C000%20a%20day) at a rate of up to $9,000 per day. This somewhat dashes Elon Musk's expectations that [Brazilians will find a way to Twitter on the web](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/1829665753416491026).

In Brazil, there are [184 million internet users](https://www.gov.br/en/government-of-brazil/latest-news/the-number-of-households-with-internet-access-in-brazil-has-increased) and of those, [24 million Twitter users](https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/twitter-users-by-country). When such a large number of users start moving now, it could significantly shift the adoption of other social networks, to which users who disagreed with Elon Musk's actions after taking over Twitter had already begun to turn.

[Bluesky reports its highest activity in history](https://bsky.app/profile/bsky.app/post/3l2xndrs3oh2t) and [over half a million new users](https://bsky.app/profile/bsky.app/post/3l2ye7wjt662c). It can be assumed that the network Threads is also seeing a large influx of users, for which [Brazil, with 36 million app downloads](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2024-brazil#:~:text=Instagram%20users%20in%20Brazil%20in%202024), is the second largest user base after India. How Mastodon is doing is harder to determine, as it is by nature a distributed network with many independent servers. Nevertheless, [statistics from FediDB show an increase of two million users](https://fedidb.org/) in the last month, which is unusual (but there will be more reasons than just the events around Twitter in Brazil).

[Brazil was the second largest country on Twitter](https://semiocast.com/brazil-becomes-2nd-country-on-twitter-superseds-japan/) right after the USA. If the network remains blocked for a longer period and users get accustomed to another network, it could trigger another wave of user departures from the platform in other countries as well. Twitter reported [570 million monthly active users](https://x.com/XData/status/1810701857419100592) in July.
