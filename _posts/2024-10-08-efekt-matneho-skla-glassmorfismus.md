# The Frosted Glass Effect: Glassmorphism. Will it be the trend of 2025?
> 

Glassmorphism is a visual design style that mimics the effect of frosted glass. It uses various levels of transparency and background blur to create a sense of depth and hierarchy in the user interface. It originated in 2020 and its elements are often used in systems like Apple SwiftUI (macOS Big Sur) or Microsoft Fluent Design.

Although glassmorphism can bring aesthetic value, its incorrect use, especially without adhering to accessibility principles, [can lead to problems](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/glassmorphism/) with readability. Therefore, a balance between visual effect and functionality is crucial.

In design, it is important to ensure readability and contrast between text and background. [Glassmorphism works](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/glassmorphism?srsltid=AfmBOooAm2JwnmdMxj0HBSLGV2hQ2kfWYiHrQjmGA8UOJr9u5FlF6KPq) best with muted colors and subtle gradients, but it needs to be used sparingly so as not to impair usability. Accessibility is ensured by sufficient contrast and balanced use of colors and transparency.
