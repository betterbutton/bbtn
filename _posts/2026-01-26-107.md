## Codeless is not no‑code

For a whole decade, we've had the terms *no‑code* and *low‑code* as low-threshold approaches to software creation. Mostly, it's about visualized algorithm creation, used primarily for automating routine tasks. However, the output of no‑code development is mostly not portable code, but just the configuration of some product like [n8n](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/n8n).

With the arrival of programming AI agents and *[vibecoding](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/vibe-coding)*, however, an approach to programming is emerging where the author doesn't need to control a programming language at all and doesn't even need to be able to describe the algorithm at the level required by no‑code tools, yet still produces software with source code.

*Anil Dash* comes up with a new name for such an approach: *[codeless](https://www.anildash.com/2026/01/22/codeless/)*. The difference between codeless and no‑code lies in who has control over the result and how sustainable such software is. No‑code emphasizes accessibility and speed within the boundaries of a given platform.

Codeless in the agent concept emphasizes the lever: a person formulates the intent, and artificial intelligence creates common code. The main output is a repository, not configurations in a tool. This enables versioning, reviews, independent deployment, and long-term ownership.

The difference is also in the requirements. No‑code lowers the entry barrier but limits possibilities. Codeless reduces manual labor but requires clear thinking, a sense for architecture, and consistency in working with quality.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/8bc1f62a-e706-4388-8f42-b89fca9a7d7b_800x533.webp "800x533")

A codeless programmer is an orchestrator of AI agents.

Do we need the term codeless when we already have vibecoding? Vibecoding and codeless work with AI in software creation, but differ in the degree of control and responsibility. Vibecoding is an intuitive, exploratory approach: a person describes the intent freely, tries what the model offers, and lets themselves be guided by feeling, similar to sketching ideas. The result is often a quick prototype, often without a solid structure and without emphasis on long-term sustainability.

Codeless in the newer, agent concept is, in contrast, a disciplined process. A person doesn't write code manually but systematically formulates requirements, constraints, architecture, and control mechanisms, while AI agents generate and modify actual code. The difference, therefore, is not whether code is written, but whether it's improvised experimentation or a controlled development method that accounts for quality, responsibility, and long-term ownership of the result.

## Linkodrome

**The return of mythical unicorns.** *Jakob Nielsen* was once skeptical of UX unicorns. Universal designers who master the entire design process from research to content strategy are, however, according to Jakob, becoming a reality thanks to artificial intelligence. AI significantly [narrows the gaps between experts and beginners](https://www.uxtigers.com/post/ux-unicorn), thereby increasing the efficiency and quality of generalists' work. Whereas previously a UX unicorn meant more of a compromise, today with AI one can deliver "decent design" on most projects.

**SEO isn't dying.** SEO is rumored to be dying due to AI. A study by *Graphite.io* and *Similarweb*, however, shows that this is not true. Organic search traffic in the USA decreased year-on-year by only 2.5%, not dramatically by a quarter as some sources claim. The decline mainly concerns medium-sized websites, while the largest and smallest grew. ChatGPT is changing user behavior, but [search engines remain the dominant source of traffic](https://graphite.io/five-percent/debunking-the-myth-that-seo-traffic-has-dramatically-declined).

**Effective SEO data processing without cloud.** *Marek Prokop* shared an [elegant solution for processing millions of URLs](https://www.prokopsw.cz/cs/clanky/screaming-frog-duckdb) from Screaming Frog in DuckDb. It replaces cumbersome tools like BigQuery. DuckDb is a local, fast, and easily manageable database designed for analytical queries. Marek step-by-step shows how to export CSV from a crawl, import them into DuckDb, and analyze using SQL or even via AI tools like Claude Desktop with an MCP server.

**10 rules for better design critique.** *Geoff Hemeon* summarizes ten principles for [conducting meaningful and safe design critique](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7417611399792771073/). Critique should not be a trial but a joint effort to improve the output. The foundation is trust, separating work from identity, and the ability to formulate specific feedback considering the goal, context, and quality of craftsmanship.

“Reality has always been too small for the human imagination. We’re always trying to transcend.” — [Brenda Laurel](https://web.archive.org/web/20161222051200/www.wired.com/1993/02/brenda/#:~:text=Reality%20has%20always%20been%20too%20small%20for%20the%20human%20imagination.%20We%27re%20always%20trying%20to%20transcend)

*[Brenda Laurel](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/brenda-laurel)* (November 20, 1950). She shifted the perception of interaction from technical efficiency to human experience. In the book *Computers as Theatre*, she proposed understanding interfaces as theatrical stages, where the user becomes an actor, not just an operator. Thus, she perceives the interface not as an object but as an environment.

She introduced the concept of a flying wedge of possibilities—a metaphor showing how design shapes the space for user action. Brenda advocates an ethical, research-based approach to design, for example in games for girls. Her legacy lies in the question of what kind of person the user can become during interaction.

[Dev stack](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/dev-stack) is the set of technologies and tools that determine not only how a website or application is developed but also its future sustainability. The path to long-term success isn't the latest tools but resilience: maintainable planning thanks to long-term version support, performance, accessibility, and security integrated into the stack itself.

[No‑code and low‑code platforms](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/no-code) are changing how websites and applications are developed. They offer rapid prototyping and reduce entry costs, which is beneficial for marketing websites and internal tools. Risks, however, appear over time: visual logic is harder to maintain, vendor lock-in occurs, and security issues arise from insufficient configuration.

[N8n](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/n8n) is an open‑source process automation platform that combines a no‑code approach with the capabilities of a full-fledged developer tool. Its visual editor allows easy connection of services and logic, while the platform is built for operation in a production environment and supports running on your own server.
