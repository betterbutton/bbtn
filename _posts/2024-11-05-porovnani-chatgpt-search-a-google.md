# Comparison of ChatGPT Search and Google
> 

OpenAI has launched the ChatGPT Search engine, which competes with Google and Perplexity. This tool is available to users with a subscription and allows them to get answers directly within the ChatGPT interface. Its advantage is the integration of citations and sources, which simplifies fact-checking. [Pavel Ungr summarized what we know about ChatGPT Search](https://www.pavelungr.cz/novy-ai-vyhledavac-search-chat-gpt/) in a concise article.

ChatGPT Search runs on Bing technology, so it is recommended to optimize pages for Bing SEO standards. Bing has a more stable algorithm, but differences from Google include preferences for longer content or different handling of social signals. However, ChatGPT Search still lags behind Google, especially in simple navigational queries.

Maxwell Zeff looked at [ChatGPT Search from a user's perspective](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/04/chatgpt-search-is-not-openais-google-killer-yet/) rather than an SEO specialist's. According to him, it aspires to become a competitor to Google, but it does not yet offer sufficiently reliable performance for everyday use.

It does provide clear answers to long questions and can uncover information hidden behind SEO articles, but it often makes mistakes in short queries, such as sports match results or navigational queries ("library opening hours").

Unlike Google Search, which is more precise and faster for simple searches, ChatGPT Search remains an unfinished tool, especially for short queries that people rely on in daily life.
