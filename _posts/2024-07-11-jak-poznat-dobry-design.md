# Is it good design, or just a pretty picture?
> 

Design should be primarily functional, aesthetics is secondary. James Harrison from UX Collective reflects on [distinguishing quality design from attractive designs](https://uxdesign.cc/is-it-good-design-or-does-it-just-look-good-e6c5d175627f). He recalls how two years ago he bought a coffee maker based solely on its appearance. It was a mistake. Although it looked amazing, it was impractical and has complicated controls. Aesthetics overrides functionality.

> „When aesthetics becomes the main priority of design, users suffer.“
> **— James Harrison, [Is it good design, or does it just look good?](https://uxdesign.cc/is-it-good-design-or-does-it-just-look-good-e6c5d175627f)**

Designers often prioritize appearance over functionality, which leads to user disappointment. Historical examples confirm that aesthetics can ruin even quality products. Exceptions are areas where functionality is key. Ideal design combines aesthetics and functionality into a harmonious whole.
