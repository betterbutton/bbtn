# Feature Creep
> A slowly growing number of features that obscures digital products and weakens their purpose.

Feature creep ([feature creep](https://dovetail.com/product-development/what-is-feature-creep/)) is the gradual and usually well-intentioned addition of new features to a digital product. And yet, this is one of the most common reasons why products lose their direction and usability.

It starts innocently. Someone suggests adding a filter. Another would welcome the option to switch the interface. Someone else would want integration with a calendar. Each suggestion seems like a small thing. But when features are added without a clear boundary, they start to accumulate, overlap, and complicate the overall experience.

Originally a simple tool transforms into a complex system. The user interface is cluttered. It's not clear what's important. New users get lost. Development slows down. Documentation grows, but doesn't help.

It's not that new features are bad. It's that they rob the product of clarity and strength. And if there's no clear vision or ability to say "no," feature creep continues.

Avoiding it requires discipline. Teams must distinguish what is truly important. A good product isn't the one that does everything. It's the one that does something important well.

The question isn't "can we add it?" but "does it help fulfill why the product exists in the first place?". And if the answer isn't clear, it's better to leave it be.
