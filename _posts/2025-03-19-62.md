## Linkodrome

**Linked projects in Figma allow collaboration on a single project** without having to pay for additional user seats. Only administrators from both sides, who must also approve the link, [can set up the connection](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/30124855491863-Guide-to-connected-projects). The project's functionality is determined by the host team's plan – if the host has an Enterprise plan, external users will also benefit from it. Users remain on their own plans and only pay for their own accounts. The number of linked projects is limited according to the plan. By unlinking a project, external users lose access to files.

**101 days.** That's exactly how many days separate us today from the effective date of the [European Accessibility Act](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424). Lawyer [Petra Dolejšová reminds us](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7307655531454685187/) that it's high time to start dealing with the law. We add that [it might not apply to you](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424#p2) or at least [grants you a deferral](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424#p28). Anyway, take a look at [which authority supervises you](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2023-424#p19) and find out how it will proceed. Good luck! … No, we can't leave it at that. :) First, there is no official certification that gives you a stamp confirming you meet the law's requirements. So you'll need access to the standard [EN 301 549 on accessibility in IT](https://accessible.canada.ca/en-301-549-accessibility-requirements-ict-products-and-services-1) (don't be confused that the website is Canadian). Check out [the clear overview of areas](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/wcag-planek-metra) covered by [WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/) and download [the e-book on accessibility](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/pristupnost-ebook-zdarma) or at least take a look at [the recommendations from the UK government agency](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/designeri-britske-vlady-radi-s-pristupnosti). Stay tuned – we will continue to cover this topic.
