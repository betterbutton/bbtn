# Design sprint
> A fast method for validating ideas, solving problems, and testing design – now even faster than before.

[Design sprint](https://www.thesprintbook.com/the-design-sprint) is a structured way to quickly solve problems and test ideas with real users. It originally started as a five-day process at Google Ventures, but in newer versions it has been shortened – the most well-known is [Design Sprint 2.0](https://medium.com/svejda-goldmann-webdesign/vyu%C4%8Dujeme-design-sprint-2-0-a-co-%C5%BEe-to-vlastn%C4%9B-je-533e9a4de5e6), which condenses the entire process into four days. Some teams shorten it even further and manage it in two days, or even in a single day.

The main principle remains: align the team, create a solution, build a prototype, and test it with real people. Version 2.0 refines the timeframe, moves some preparation outside the sprint itself, and focuses on what's most important. The shortest variants often skip the prototype and work only with sketches or simple designs – all to get feedback as quickly as possible.

This drive to shorten sprints matches the pace of today's digital product development. Teams building apps or websites can thus validate ideas faster, align opinions, and avoid unnecessary work.

But the sprint isn't just a method – it's a mindset. It emphasizes empathy with the user, the courage to experiment, and speed. Whether it lasts five days or one, it remains an effective tool for teams that want to build smarter and more flexible products.
