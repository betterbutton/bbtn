# Moderated vs. Unmoderated Testing: When to Use Which?
> 

User testing helps uncover how well users interact with a product. There are two main types: moderated and unmoderated.

Moderated testing involves a facilitator who observes participants' behavior and provides immediate feedback, making it ideal for complex tasks.

On the other hand, unmoderated tests are faster and cheaper, as participants work independently in a natural setting. While moderated tests yield deeper insights, unmoderated tests offer greater scalability and flexibility.
