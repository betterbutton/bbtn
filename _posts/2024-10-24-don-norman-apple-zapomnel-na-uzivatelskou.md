# Don Norman: Apple forgot about user-friendliness
> 

[Don Norman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Norman) (*1935), design veteran and former Apple vice president, criticizes the modern design trend that prioritizes aesthetics over functionality. Although he himself contributed to the development of the iPhone, he considers today's Apple products less intuitive and difficult to operate.

Norman [in an interview for El País](https://english.elpais.com/culture/2024-10-20/don-norman-apple-has-fallen-prey-to-the-most-disastrous-part-of-design-which-thinks-its-about-making-something-beautiful-and-elegant.html) points out that design should be primarily easy to understand and usable, not just beautiful. Simplicity and practicality are part of his lifelong philosophy, which is also the main message of his book [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-everyday-things-revised/). He sees Apple's current approach to design as a result of an aesthetic cult that neglects user-friendliness.

This year, Norman's book [Design for a Better World](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262548304/design-for-a-better-world/) was published, which focuses on the broader role of design in the world and critically examines how design can influence society and the environment. Norman argues in it that designers should go beyond mere aesthetics and address complex problems such as social inequalities, sustainability, and environmental impacts. Norman calls on designers to better understand the context of their work, including economics, culture, and government mechanisms, and to create sustainable and ethically responsible designs that help build a better world.
