# Běloučký kůň úpěl ďábelské ódy
> A pangram is a sentence that contains every letter of a given alphabet at least once. It is used in typography or calligraphy for testing and practice.

[Pangram](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangram) is a sentence in which every letter of a given alphabet appears at least once. The most famous example in English is *"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."* Pangrams are often used to test fonts, keyboards, or writing skills because they contain all the letters in one short text.

Pangrams can be of various lengths. Some are concise, others more comprehensible. For example, the English *"Pack my box with five dozen liquor jugs."* is shorter but less natural. In Czech, sentences like *"Příliš žluťoučký kůň úpěl ďábelské ódy."* or the longer variant *"Nechť již hříšné saxofony ďáblů rozezvučí síň úděsnými tóny waltzu, tanga a quickstepu,"* are used, which, unlike the horse version, also include the letters *g*, *q*, *x*, and *w*, which do not typically appear in Czech words.

Pangrams are encountered in phonetic alphabets, but for a script like Chinese, such a sentence would have to cover thousands of characters. And that wouldn't be very practical. Typographers have taken a liking to pangrams because they allow for a quick check of whether you are working with a font that contains characters from a given linguistic script. They easily distinguish a font that, in the [Latin 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO/IEC_8859-1) encoding, contains the characters *á*, *é*, *í*, *ý*, *ó*, *ú*, but not *ě*, *ž*, *š*, *č*, *ř*, *ď*, *ť*, or *ň*, which belong to [Latin 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO/IEC_8859-2). Distinguishing linguistic scripts was mainly necessary before the spread of the [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode) and [UTF-8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UTF-8) encodings, but even today you may come across fonts that do not support certain languages.

Pangrams combine language, design, and technology, offering not only fun but also practical application.
