# American court orders Google to open Play Store to competition
> 

American courts are dealing with Google. Federal judge James Donato ordered Google to open its app store Google Play to competition. The decision is meant to bring more freedom to Android users and allow them to download apps from alternative sources, similar to what we know from the DSA regulation in the EU.

After its defeat in the dispute with Epic Games, Google must allow Android users to download apps from competing sources and permit alternative payment systems, [writes the Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2024/oct/07/google-play-store-epic-games-antitrust-lawsuit). The decision also prohibits Google from paying device manufacturers for pre-installing the Play Store and from sharing revenues with other distributors. A three-member technical committee will oversee the implementation of these changes.

Google plans to appeal, arguing that the changes will weaken its competitiveness against iOS.
