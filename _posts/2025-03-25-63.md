## From The Designer

![Text is written once in uppercase and once in lowercase. Where is it better to read: 1. FROM IDEA THROUGH PROTOTYPE TO DIGITAL PRODUCT. 2. From idea through prototype to digital products.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/07db3309-f736-4c2a-a3d8-d65d29118fda_1080x540.png "520x260")

Text written in uppercase is harder to read than text written in lowercase.

**Don't write long passages of text IN ALL CAPS.** Uppercase letters in the Latin script all have the same height. Text written in uppercase appears uniform, and distinguishing individual characters is more strenuous for the reader than with lowercase letters.

The ascenders and descenders of lowercase letters extend above and below the letters—they are called [ascenders and descenders](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%ADsmov%C3%A1_osnova). Together with diacritical marks, they create a varied line appearance that helps the eye better distinguish different characters, thereby reducing reading fatigue.

Text written in lowercase is read faster and is less tiring to read. Text written in uppercase appears aggressive and gives the impression that the author is shouting at the reader.

💡A small change, a big impact. Try it.

## Linkodrome

**Don't point subdomains to the cloud with a wildcard.** Patrick Zandl on Threads [writes about a new trick](https://www.threads.net/@tangerocz/post/DHb43vEo6EI?xmt=AQGzyzcirEQrNKN6Za5NT9Dyv9eczNYx0e84m0cQt22LWw) that scammers use to increase the credibility of their scams. If you redirect subdomains to platforms like Vercel, Netlify, and similar using [a domain record with an asterisk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildcard_DNS_record), they can hijack any of your subdomains to their instance and impersonate you without users having an easy way to detect the trick.

**A plan for leaders from Peter Merholz.** Design leaders need more than just a vision—they need a concrete plan. Peter Merholz offers [a framework built on five key areas](https://www.petermerholz.com/blog/craft-your-leadership-plan-with-these-5-ps/) (People, Programs, Process, Performance, Positioning) that helps structure thinking and guide the team toward long-term goals. Without a plan, the team often just reacts to external demands and has no space to implement their own initiatives. Creating a leadership plan allows teams to control their agenda and gradually achieve real change.

**How apps, instead of service, ruin the café experience.** Maria Panagiotidi describes [a frustrating experience in an Athens café](https://uxpsychology.substack.com/p/when-technology-becomes-the-problem) where customers could only order through an app. Technology didn't improve the user experience but created unnecessary barriers—especially for older visitors and tourists. Maria highlights the need for research, accessibility, and flexibility in UX design. The point? Innovation must not be self-serving—good design starts with people, not technology.

**The Lorem ipsum creative cards help with idea generation.** This is a tip from our own garden. The student agency GEN, which operates at the Department of Multimedia at VŠE, created [a set of creative Lorem ipsum cards](https://www.loremips.cz/karty) inspired by [the SIT methodology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systematic_inventive_thinking). They help systematically generate new ideas. They are intended for anyone struggling with creative block—from marketers to HR. The accompanying workshop shows how to work with the cards effectively and speed up the creative process. The cards are designed to be immediately usable in practice.

**A list of resources for secondary research** was compiled by Nikki Anderson. Don't waste time on research whose results already exist. Today, you can also [use AI](https://www.perplexity.ai/hub/blog/introducing-perplexity-deep-research) to quite easily determine which research reports are relevant and which are not. You can then [add relevant reports to your research repository](https://uxinsight.org/managing-what-we-know-lessons-from-the-atlassian-research-library-think-tank/) and work with them in future projects. You can start, for example, with [Nikki's list of resources for secondary research](https://www.canva.com/design/DAGiQhJeS30/BbgZiVHkX_XtOSsjP4V_oA/edit).
