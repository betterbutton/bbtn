# Design Treasures in the Internet Archive
> 

![Screenshot of the archives.design page featuring design print materials.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a5cc307d-8909-44f7-b4b4-d67c4eb2f410_1900x1080.png "1900x1080")

[archives.design](https://archives.design/)

When we encounter a non-functional link, the [Way Back Machine](https://archive.org/) of the Internet Archive comes to the rescue. However, the Internet Archive functions as a modern-day Library of Alexandria, striving to preserve all the knowledge of a new civilization. Within it, you will also find print materials, videos, or audio recordings.

Designer Valery Marier delved into the print department of the Internet Archive and compiled a [collection focused on graphic design](https://archives.design/) from his discoveries. It collects and presents various design artifacts, such as books, posters, branding materials, typographic specimens, and more.

In Valery's selection, you can find famous works like the [Vignelli Canon](https://archives.design/post/641810480937172992/the-vignelli-canon-2009-massimo-vignelli-a) or Apple's [Human Interface Guidelines](https://archives.design/post/645150403066003456/apple-human-interface-guidelines-1987) from 1987, a time before the web existed. The collection is useful for typophiles and design enthusiasts, who will certainly find their own favorites in it.
