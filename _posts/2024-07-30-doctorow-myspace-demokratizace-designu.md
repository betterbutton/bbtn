# Doctorow: Myspace Shaped Authentic Communities
> 

In a Twitter thread, Cory Doctorow [celebrates the ugliness of Myspace](https://x.com/doctorow/status/1814643988600590638) as a significant element that allowed users without design experience to create pages full of personality. This aesthetic served as an *“anti-co-opting force field”*, protecting authentic communities from commercialization.

Doctorow sees [Myspace](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myspace) as the heir to previous generations of the [democratization of design](https://dl.designresearchsociety.org/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1006&context=pluriversaldesign#:~:text=It%20is%20a%20paradigm%20shift,designer%2C%20and%20design%20become%20democratized.), which enabled amateur and countercultural groups to express themselves. This environment provided a clear visual distinction from professionally designed corporate sites and played a key role in shaping the society's creative ferment.

The democratization of design is once again a topic after [Web 2.0](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_2.0), with the rise of AI tools that [are opening the door to the general public](https://mialoira.medium.com/the-democratization-of-design-99c3f16ffc79) for capabilities previously reserved for professionals. And with democratization comes folk aesthetics once again.
