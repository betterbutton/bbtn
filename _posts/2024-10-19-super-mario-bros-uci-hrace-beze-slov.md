# Super Mario Bros. Teaches Players Without Words
> 

Intuitive controls, a [steep learning curve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_curve), [learnability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learnability), [recognition vs. recall](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/recognition-vs-recall?srsltid=AfmBOorDbRJn-yQa403RCZH90PmcdVNHHwZz-9raLa-GNbtZTx8JAd83) – these are terms UX designers discuss regarding complex interfaces, often seeking inspiration in game design. Super Mario Bros., released in 1985, is considered the [textbook of game design](https://medium.com/@lorenzoardeni/super-mario-bros-as-a-masterclass-in-teaching-how-to-play-36a5ffc75dcd).

> “The game rewards curiosity and discovery. Every block, enemy, and object offers a lesson without the player even noticing.”

The opening level 1–1 offers a literally invisible tutorial, guiding the player naturally without a single word. The game instinctively directs the player to the right, where they encounter a Goomba – the first challenge teaching basic mechanics like avoiding and jumping.

Other elements, such as question blocks and mushrooms, motivate the player to discover and learn through the game itself. This approach inspired subsequent games, including The Legend of Zelda and Dark Souls, and remains a model for innovative design. Lorenzo Ardeni spent a lot of time studying Super Mario's game principle ;) and compiled the [most interesting insights](https://medium.com/@lorenzoardeni/super-mario-bros-as-a-masterclass-in-teaching-how-to-play-36a5ffc75dcd) for us.
