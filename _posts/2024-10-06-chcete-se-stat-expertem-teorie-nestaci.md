# Do you want to become an expert? Theory is not enough.
> 
Vaidehi Raipat reflects on Harry Collins' theory of [interactional expertise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interactional_expertise), which claims that a person can be an expert without practical experience if they understand the concepts and language of the field. The author rejects this view and emphasizes that true [expertise in design requires not only theoretical knowledge but also practical experience](https://medium.com/@vr2240/design-expertise-a-reflective-discussion-59dbacc84c09), reflection, and intuition. The text also cites the opinions of Stelly Tan and Marty Sinclair, which emphasize collaboration, flexible thinking, and the role of intuition as a form of expertise gained through practice.
