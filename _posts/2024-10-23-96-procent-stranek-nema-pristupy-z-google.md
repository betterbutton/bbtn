# On 96.55 percent of pages, no one comes from Google
> 

![96.55% of pages get zero traffic from Google Based on a study of over 14 billion pages in Ahrefs' Content Explorer index 0 visits (96.55%) -13.6B 1-10 visits (1.94%) - 270.4M 11-100 visits (1.08%) 150.6M 101-1,000 visits (0.36%)-50.3M 1,001+ visits (0.07%) 9.8M](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/4864c1b8-8d95-42a6-8dd2-74180b75b63f_1846x1334.png "1456x1052")

Monthly Google search visits according to Ahrefs

According to [Ahrefs' research](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-traffic-study/), 96.55% of web pages have no organic visits from Google. The study from last December identified three main reasons for this issue:

1.  zero interest in the topic,

2.  absence of backlinks, and

3.  misalignment with search intent.

For pages to belong to the minority that gains traffic, it is crucial to choose topics with demand, build quality links, and tailor content to user expectations. SEO is thus tied not only to content but also to the right keyword selection and understanding audience needs.
