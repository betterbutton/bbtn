# Tesler's Rule and the Waterbed Theory
> Tesler's Law states that the complexity of a system cannot be eliminated—it can only be transferred to the user. The Waterbed Theory suggests that suppressed complexity in one area will unexpectedly bulge out in another.

**Larry Tesler's Law of Conservation of Complexity** ([Tesler‘s Law](https://lawsofux.com/teslers-law/)) is a principle in human-centered design that assumes every system has a certain fundamental level of complexity that cannot be entirely removed—only relocated. This principle determines whether the system itself deals with that complexity or if the user must manage it.

A similar concept is the **Waterbed Theory** ([Waterbed Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterbed_theory)). It states that if complexity or costs are reduced in one area, they will increase elsewhere. The waterbed principle is used in economics, regulations, and technology. Just as pressing down on a waterbed causes bulging in another section, simplifying the user experience often requires more complex development and infrastructure.

Designers must grapple with the question of where to relocate complexity. The ideal system absorbs it without restricting user control. Tesler's Law reminds us that great design doesn't lie in trying to eliminate complexity, but in properly managing its distribution.

## Larry Tesler and His Contribution to the World of Computing

[Larry Tesler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larry_Tesler) (1945—2020) was a pioneer in the field of human-computer interaction (HCI). Among his most significant contributions are:

- [Tesler's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_conservation_of_complexity) – a system's complexity below a certain threshold cannot be removed, only transferred to the user.

- The [copy, cut, paste](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dhmz68CII9Y) functions, which he co-created at Xerox PARC and which were popularized by Apple's Macintosh computers.

- [Modeless computing](https://worrydream.com/refs/Tesler_2012_-_A_Personal_History_of_Modeless_Text_Editing_and_Cut-Copy-Paste.pdf) – he advocated for intuitive software without unnecessary modes, improving user-friendliness.

- The development of [graphical user interfaces (GUI)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_graphical_user_interface), which changed the way people interact with computers.

- Leadership roles at Apple, Amazon, and Yahoo, where he influenced the development of user experience and e-commerce.

Tesler's work continues to influence the design of digital products, from smartphones to AI-powered applications.
