# Ecosia and Qwant Search Engines Challenge Google
> 

European search engines [Ecosia](https://www.ecosia.org/) and [Qwant](https://www.qwant.com/?l=en) have joined forces to reduce dependence on Google and Bing and create their own web index focused on European needs and sustainability. The new project, called [European Search Perspective](https://www.eu-searchperspective.com/), will be based in Paris and aims to promote environmentally friendly search results.

Ecosia, which finances tree planting with its profits, hopes to gain greater control over result content through this route. Technical backing will be provided by OVHcloud, which has a stake in the Qwant search engine. The project aims to reach a wider audience and provide an alternative to the big players in the market.

*“We could disadvantage results from unethical or unsustainable companies and conversely advantage those that are responsible,”* says Christian Kroll, CEO of Ecosia. *“Is this a service that users are demanding from search engines?”* Martin wonders.
