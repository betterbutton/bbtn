# Why You (Don't) Need a Sitemap on Your Website?
> 

Dušan Janovský is a search architect at Seznam, but in his free time he writes a knowledge base for web creators [How to Write a Website](https://www.jakpsatweb.cz). In the latest addition, he deals with sitemap (in Czech, sitemapa).

A sitemap is a file that provides search engines with an overview of addresses on the website. Although robots can find pages on their own, a sitemap is especially appreciated for large websites or when debugging errors.

[Dušan shows three formats of sitemap](https://www.jakpsatweb.cz/seo/sitemap.html): XML, RSS, and a simple text file. You might be surprised, just like us, that he himself uses a sitemap in the form of a simple list of addresses.

The importance of a sitemap lies mainly in facilitating the indexing of important pages, although errors in the sitemap are not catastrophic. However, it is good to minimize them. For testing, you can use tools like Google Search Console.
