# Mobile friendly web, but how?
> 

It's still recommended to design for [mobile first](https://www.vzhurudolu.cz/prirucka/mobile-first), [Googlebot loves mobiles](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/google-smartphone-bot) and [users mainly come from mobile](https://gs.statcounter.com/platform-market-share/desktop-mobile-tablet). But what does it mean to design mobile-friendly? According to the Fenix creative blog, [a mobile friendly web has these ingredients](https://medium.com/@fenixcreative.web/mobile-friendly-web-design-why-your-business-cant-afford-to-ignore-it-58329e889321):

**Responsive design:** Web pages adapt to any device they are displayed on.

**Touch navigation:** Make sure your buttons are large enough even for the most clumsy fingers, that navigation links have sufficient spacing so people don't accidentally tap the wrong one, and finally, that navigation works without a mouse cursor and :hover effects. There are still plenty of websites that assume navigation expands on hover.

**Readable text:** If your visitors need a magnifying glass to read your content, you're doing it wrong.

**Optimized images:** Of course we always have fast connections under all circumstances. No one wants to wait for images to load. Don't send them to mobile in unnecessarily high resolution.
