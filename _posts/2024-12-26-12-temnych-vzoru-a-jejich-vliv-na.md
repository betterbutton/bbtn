# 12 Dark Patterns and Their Impact on Users
> 

Dark patterns ([dark patterns](https://www.deceptive.design/)) are tactics in UX design that manipulate users into actions that are often not in their best interest. These practices, defined by [Harry Brignull](https://www.linkedin.com/in/harrybrignull/) in 2010, include tricks like *roach motel* (easy sign-up, hard cancellation), *hidden costs* (fees added at the last minute), or *confirmshaming* (inducing guilt upon refusal).

- Tricky questions

- Sneak into basket

- Roach motel

- Privacy zuckering

- Price comparison prevention

- Misdirection

- Hidden costs

- Bait and switch

- Confirmshaming

- Disguised ads

- Forced continuity

- Friend spam

[These patterns often serve corporate gain but undermine user trust.](https://medium.com/@xd_community_eng/dark-patterns-2dcd0ca3d3f9) Design experts call for more ethical approaches, transparency, and respect for users to minimize manipulation and increase customer satisfaction.
