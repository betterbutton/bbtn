**Animations in user interfaces** add emotion. It's an effective way to provide feedback on a user action, buy time for loading the next state, present even complex procedures or navigation patterns. But if you thought you'd be preparing animations in Figma, you'll soon run into very limited capabilities.

So even for UI animations, preparation still happens in specialized tools like [Adobe After Effects](https://www.adobe.com/cz/products/aftereffects.html), [Principle](https://principleformac.com/), or [Haiku Animator](https://www.haikuanimator.com/). The first one comes with the entire Adobe ecosystem, which disqualifies it in the eyes of some designers, and the other two are only for macOS.

Fortunately, there is still the open-source [Lottie](https://dotlottie.io/) – a format, library, player, and editor for animations. The cloud-based extension [Lottie Files](https://lottiefiles.com/) comes with new integrations, so you'll find a plugin for it in Figma and Canva, and you can even export animations from After Effects into them.

Lottie was created as a way to get animations from After Effects to the web, and [its popularity is driven by AirBnB](https://airbnb.io/lottie/), and over the past five years this format has become the de facto standard for web animation. This year, a [Lottie community](https://dotlottie.io/) formed around it, which will continue to care for the standardization and development of the format.

But enough. Let's [admire for a moment](https://iconscout.com/lottie-animations) [examples of animations](https://www.sliderrevolution.com/design/lottie-animation-examples/) that [Lottie has brought](https://lottiefiles.com/blog/design-inspiration/animated-portfolio-websites-using-lottie-animations-examples) [to the web](https://www.cubstudio.com/lottie-animations).

Instead, we got teleconferences, remote work, and the boss doesn't hover over our shoulder or tap our shoulder anymore, but now he checks how long we've been sitting at Teams and calls us ad hoc to ask about a document that we see in a cloud folder on the disk, but he doesn't have in Sharepoint. Don't take this as a complaint. It's definitely a fine shift. But on the other hand, for our generation, which has seen all three parts of Back to the Future (with a few years delay) in the cinema, it's a bit of unfulfilled longing.

When the coffee ran out in the department's printer room this morning and the new pack wasn't anywhere within reach, we actually agreed that we were disappointed that the printer, which is capable of assigning anyone to empty the waste bin or refill water, isn't capable of ordering a new batch of roasted beans itself. We didn't jingle keys on Wenceslas Square over this (we were small then).

**Break your goals down into smaller steps to make them more achievable**

When you set goals for a new website, they are often large and complex, which is completely normal. However, to successfully achieve them, break them down into smaller parts. If your goal is to increase revenue, you will need to increase conversions. For that to happen, you need to get more hot leads. Greater website traffic will get you there.

This way, you can better plan your budget and track progress. Don't overthink individual methods to achieve this. Focus on results, not on the steps leading to them.
