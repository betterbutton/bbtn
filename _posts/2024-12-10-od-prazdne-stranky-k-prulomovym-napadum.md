# From a blank page to breakthrough ideas
> 

Michael Hay, in a two-part series of articles [on Design Thinking 1](https://medium.com/@michaelhay_90395/applied-design-thinking-part-1-9ae9476fbfbc) and [2](https://medium.com/@michaelhay_90395/applied-design-thinking-part-2-7648dc79baaf), presents the crucial role of team empathy and diversity for successful innovations. Stories from his career illustrate how trust and shared experiences in the team can support open discussion and bring breakthrough results.

> “Without team empathy and a safe space for dissent, the team may invent, but innovations will be lacking.”\
> **— [Michael Hay](https://medium.com/@michaelhay_90395/applied-design-thinking-part-1-9ae9476fbfbc)**

Michael emphasizes methods such as building hypotheses inspired by the scientific method or using affinity diagrams for structuring ideas. These practices lead to better product designs and a deeper understanding of user needs. The key to success is leveraging team potential through empathy, transparency, and focus on real user problems.
