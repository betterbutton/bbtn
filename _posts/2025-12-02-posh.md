# POSH (Plain Old Semantic HTML)
> Developers in practice prefer the semantic layer over the presentational one—it ensures clarity, accessibility, and long-term longevity using standard HTML.

[POSH (Plain Old Semantic HTML)](https://microformats.org/wiki/posh), meaning ordinary semantic HTML, is the foundation of an accessible, resilient, and future-proof web. It means writing HTML to express the meaning of the content. Use `` for an article, `` for navigation, or `` for a button – not `` with a `role` attribute or visual classes like `box` or `red­‑text`.

Thanks to POSH, websites work better with assistive technologies. Screen readers recognize important page sections and allow users to move quickly between content. Semantic HTML also helps search engines and artificial intelligence correctly understand the structure and meaning of text – and generate accurate answers or enhanced search results from it.

For developers, POSH brings more readable code, meaningful class names, and simpler maintenance. Technologies like JSON-LD or [microformats](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/microformats) work best when they have a solid semantic foundation. Modern frameworks like Astro, Next.js, or SvelteKit don’t require POSH – but they benefit from it.

The term POSH originated in the microformats community back in 2007 as a reaction to complex and inaccessible coding. And even today the rule holds: let’s use HTML with understanding. What we have at our disposal is often enough – we just need to know why and how.

POSH won’t necessarily make your website prettier, but it will make it understandable, friendly, and ready for the future.
