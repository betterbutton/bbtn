# Long-Term Support
> **Long-Term Support** (LTS) denotes long-term product support. It provides a stable foundation for the development, maintenance, and use of applications and websites. Developers, administrators, and ultimately users gain the assurance that the product will be regularly updated, patched, and secured against security risks for a certain period.

When the lighting in our wardrobe broke after almost ten years, we discovered that IKEA had meanwhile changed the power supply voltage from 12 Volts to 24 and no longer connects lights with round connectors. It would have been enough to replace the power supply, but because IKEA chose a round connector with a different inner diameter than the usual standard for the original light, it's not entirely easy to find a replacement. If we didn't want to get into soldering work, instead of buying a new adapter for a hundred crowns, we would have to replace the entire lighting for almost two thousand. IKEA makes similar product innovations over two thousand times a year. You won't find products with an LTS label there.

You'll understand the importance of the LTS product strategy when you find yourself in the middle of chaos because your system no longer has support. Suddenly you have to hastily look for an alternative, rewrite parts of the code, or deal with incompatibility with other tools. But if you use products with long-term support, you know exactly how long you can rely on that technology.

LTS stability also impacts the user experience. Users appreciate when an application or website works predictably, which in most cases means consistently. People appreciate not having to adapt to frequent changes and unexpected outages. For developers and designers, this means more room for testing new features and fine-tuning details that contribute to more comfortable use.

Long-term support is particularly important for projects with a long lifespan or that require a high degree of reliability. For example, in public administration, healthcare, or banking, there is no room for constant changes that could disrupt operations. With an LTS version, you have the guarantee that you can plan long-term without unnecessary risks.

Technologies like Angular, Ubuntu, or PostgreSQL provide exactly this kind of support for specific versions. When you use their LTS version, you get a stable foundation to build on without fear of unexpected changes or outdated components.

To find out how long your tools will be supported, check out websites like [endoflife.date](https://endoflife.date) or the documentation, where you'll find an overview of support for individual technologies. Long-Term Support is not just a matter of comfort, but also of stability, reliability, and security for your project.
