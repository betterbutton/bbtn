## Linkodrome

**How to become a better designer?** *Muskaan Arai* points out that improvement in design is not about new tools, but deeper thinking. She recommends starting with the questions *"what problem are we solving"* and focusing on user journey flows and product thinking. She emphasizes [quality UX copy, the ability to defend decisions, accepting feedback](https://medium.com/@muskaanaraidesigns/how-to-improve-as-a-ux-ui-designer-dd973b0d03eb), and typographic fundamentals.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/b8831e50-c55f-46c1-818c-46aa6f86bd95_830x405.webp "427x208")

If God allowed designers to redesign the Earth… ([@raminnasibov](https://www.threads.com/@raminnasibov/post/DUfc0uHiJcw?xmt=AQF0sJC7HU4Kw9hU9__JGgnqIDAUWvBn6V-U7gmGkYaswA))

**How to properly handle icon sizes in a design system.** *Alice Packard* breaks down three approaches to managing icon sizes in design systems: separate components for each size, using mode/variables, and a wrapper component. She recommends [adding a `size` property](https://www.alicepackarddesign.com/blog/managing-icon-component-sizes) [directly to each icon component](https://www.alicepackarddesign.com/blog/managing-icon-component-sizes).

**Font user preferences:** *Fontstand* summarizes [the results of a font selection survey](https://fontstand.com/news/design-news/survey/): important are license clarity, the ability to easily test before purchase, and offer clarity. Respondents prefer platforms that facilitate testing fonts in real context.

**How to price design:** *Carlos Fraccalvieri* offers practical advice on how to set a price for design work: define value, separate price from time, use clear offers and contracts, negotiate based on results, not just hourly rates. He recommends having [standardized packages, calculating risks and reserves, and clearly communicating the scope](https://medium.com/@sicarlos/how-to-price-your-design-work-without-underselling-yourself-7193a01bcc42) of delivery.

**Practical AI use in design.** *Tom Smykowski* tests ten AI tools for UI design and summarizes that the best results come when tools serve as assistants, not as a replacement for creativity. The recommended approach combines human judgment with AI for generating ideas, variants, and quick prototypes. [AI speeds up work but does not eliminate the need for conceptual thinking.](https://tomaszs2.medium.com/i-reviewed-10-ai-skills-for-ui-design-and-they-all-ask-ai-to-not-be-an-ai-3c152b32840a)

**The end of passwords?** The text discusses the decline of traditional passwords and the shift to more modern forms of authentication: passkeys, biometrics, and multi-level verification. [Passwords are vulnerable and inconvenient](https://nma-itservice.medium.com/passwords-in-2026-were-done-with-that-def1f6d5bda5). The devil is in migrating users to new security standards, supporting infrastructure, and a clear communication strategy to make the transition widely acceptable.

## Published on BBtn

![Larry Tesler](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/19ad31e4-9e6e-41cc-bba6-41252042b517_1536x1024.png "502x334")

“Intelligence is whatever machines haven’t done yet.” — Larry Tesler, [AI theorem](https://www.nomodes.com/larry-tesler-consulting/adages-and-coinages?rq=browser#:~:text=Intelligence%20is%20whatever%20machines%20haven%27t%20done%20yet)

*[Larry Tesler](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/larry-tesler)* (April 24, 1945 — February 16, 2020). He left a strong legacy: copy, paste, WYSIWYG, and the end of modes. He insisted on moving complexity from the user side to the system. At *Xerox PARC* he helped introduce concepts like copy/paste and the *“select, then act”* model, laying the foundations for the GUI.

He rejected modes that confuse users and advocated for consistent, modeless interaction. At *Apple* he transformed these principles into the [WIMP](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/wimp) standard (*Lisa*, *Macintosh*) and later transferred them to large internet services. His legacy today seems self-evident: technology is user-friendly precisely because complexity remains hidden inside the system.

*[WIMP](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/wimp)* (windows, icons, menus, pointer) is a usability principle that allowed intuitive computer control through visible choices, spatial orientation, and immediate feedback. At the same time, its limits are shown in the era of touch devices and accessibility. The true legacy of WIMP thus lies in the emphasis on recognition over memory and in designing interfaces that make actions accessible and reduce uncertainty.

*[User-friendly:](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/user-friendly)* software should adapt to people. *[The law of conservation of complexity](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/teslers-law)* says that complication cannot be eliminated, only moved. Good design takes complexity upon itself, not the user.

*[Is It Agent Ready](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/isitagentready)* by Cloudflare is a tool that translates the abstract trend of AI agents into a practical website test. It evaluates five areas from discoverability to business logic and combines established standards with new protocols. Most websites don't need futuristic features, but honestly mastered technical fundamentals. The test result is indicative, yet already helps today to determine if a website holds up even in the eyes of software acting on behalf of a user.
