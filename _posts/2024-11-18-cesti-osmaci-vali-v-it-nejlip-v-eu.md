# Czech 8th Graders Lead in IT Skills in the EU
> 

Czech students excel in computer and information literacy, where they achieve the best result among EU countries (525 points). They also perform above average in computational thinking (527 points), especially due to their ability to work with information and communicate digitally.

However, they spend an above-average amount of time with ICT outside of school (61% more than two hours per day), which is more than the European average (55%). For parents, this means the need to ensure a balanced approach – supporting technological equipment is key, as is setting limits on screen use.

Blanket bans on mobile phones in schools are not advisable, as they would limit the opportunity to educate children in the effective and safe use of digital technologies, which now takes place more outside of school than in school. School should be a place where children learn to develop their digital literacy.
