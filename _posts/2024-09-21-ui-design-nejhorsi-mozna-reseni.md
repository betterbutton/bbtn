# UI Design: The Worst Possible Solution
> 

Sometimes it's hard to capture what we actually want in a brief. It's much easier to express what we *don't* want. In such cases, it helps to start by designing the worst possible solutions, and only then uncover how the interface should *actually* be designed.

![A design for Google Maps navigation that alerts the nearest highway patrol when exceeding the speed limit.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/58ccdd13-8233-49e2-a361-6e476bc2ad8f_483x516.png "401x428")

A design for [Google Maps by Soren Iverson](https://www.instagram.com/p/C_A_Io3OGTq/)

A master of the discipline of [crappy interfaces](https://www.google.com/search?q=cursed+ui) is Soren Iverson, who enjoys [posting satirically conceived designs](https://www.threads.net/@soren.iverson) of modifications to well-known services on social media. Some of his designs so perfectly capture the problem an application suffers from that people can't distinguish his joke from a seriously intended design. And that's precisely what the crappy UI serves best. Thanks to him, we can give a good name to the problem that needs solving.

A similar principle underlies the [SCAMPER brainstorming technique](https://www.post-it.com/3M/en_US/post-it/ideas/articles/scamper-method/), which works with existing design and simply runs it through several creative filters:

> S — Substitute (Replace)\
> C — Combine (Combine)\
> A — Adapt (Adapt)\
> M — Modify (Modify)\
> P — Put to another use (Put to another use)\
> E — Eliminate (Eliminate)\
> R — Reverse (Reverse)

SCAMPER was described in the early 1970s by [Bob Eberle](https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/1422380.Bob_Eberle), author of books on creativity for children and teachers. It is actually a modified version of a tool used by the founder of the advertising agency BBDO, Alex Faickney Osborn, which became known as [Osborn's checklist](https://medium.com/@jgoud/ideation-block-try-osborns-checklist-technique-8a9021081d2a).
