## From The Designer

![On the left (incorrect) a long number without spaces. On the right (correct) a long number split into digit groups.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d134b56e-b97a-41d3-9f27-ca9ea426d0d1_2160x1080.png "1456x728")

**Format numbers for easy reading.** Numbers must be read easily and quickly.
\
❌ 123456789\
✅ 123 456 789
\
👉 Better readability, faster comprehension.
👉 Fewer errors when transcribing or paying.
👉 Follow established digit grouping – you can already tell what kind of number it is by the format.
\
Grouping digits saves time and reduces errors.

## Linkodrome

**Design workshops are not rocket science.** They consist of several basic activities that can be combined as needed, and Slava Shestopalov will show you how, and add a [practical checklist](https://medium.com/design-bridges/workshop-checklist-a4895fe7add0). Examples include writing ideas on sticky notes, sorting them (affinity diagram), visualizing scenarios (storyboarding), or voting (dot voting). Each activity has a clear goal and inputs, often in the form of outputs from the previous phase. These [workshop atoms](https://medium.com/design-bridges/comprehensive-anatomy-of-all-ux-design-thinking-workshops-c416706cd3e2) can be adapted according to the environment, goal, or group dynamics. Success is not led by a rigid methodology but by the facilitator's ability to respond to the context and participants.

[Even Notepad in Windows now has AI.](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/enhance-your-writing-with-ai-in-notepad-4088b954-c97b-46dc-813f-959be01746d5)

**Priority guides offer a content-oriented alternative to wireframes.** They are based on a content hierarchy without visual constraints. Traditional wireframes are a common tool in digital design, but they often distract attention from the actual needs of users. [A priority guide fosters creativity](https://alistapart.com/article/priority-guides-a-content-first-alternative-to-wireframes/), streamlines development and testing, and facilitates responsive interface design. Heleen van Nues and Lennart Overkamp describe a six-step process for creating these guides, which starts with research and ends with iteration based on user testing. The result is a design focused on functionality and value for the user.

## So That We Understand Each Other

In English, they are called [desire paths](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/desire-path); here, we call them well-trodden shortcuts or footpaths. When observing users during their interaction with products, we pay close attention to the exact moments when they choose a method of use that does not correspond to the original designer's intention. These user shortcuts are often a valuable source of inspiration.

In user interface development, the drag-and-drop principle – [drag and drop](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/pretahovani) – represented a revolutionary shift. It presented an easily understandable metaphor that the Macintosh operating system brought almost to perfection when it allowed users to drag files into the trash or an application from a floppy disk to a computer disk.

Every design process begins with a [discovery](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/discovery) phase. During this, designers strive to understand the real needs of users and connect them with business goals. This significantly reduces the risk of creating a product that no one wants or knows how to use.

[Breadcrumb navigation](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/breadcrumbs), like a ball of red thread that Ariadne gave Theseus for his journey through the Labyrinth, guides us through the maze of complex information architectures. It helps us understand the position of the current page in the hierarchical structure of the website and is so important that Google even displays it directly in search results.

[What's in it for me?](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/wiifm) A question that every website should be able to answer for the user on the very first page they open during their visit. The right presentation of the value proposition is the magic that turns visits into conversions.

You will probably never hear the term "software feature." It's always functionality or a [gimmick](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/feature). It represents specific value offered to the user. In UX, we try not only to check off items on the user's task list but also to use software features to deliver a strong experience.
