# Enshittification
> The gradual deterioration of a service, where value systematically shifts from users to the platform operator, typically through small, incremental changes in design and rules.

[Enshittification](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enshittifikace) ([enshittification](https://www.newyorker.com/culture/infinite-scroll/the-age-of-enshittification)) names the playbook that webmasters, designers, and operators of digital services know all too well from practice today. Digital products don't degrade because teams forget how to design well. They degrade because motivations and goals change. What starts as a service designed for users gradually morphs into a tool optimized primarily for revenue.

The progression is similar. First, the service focuses on users. The interface is understandable, barriers are minimal, and [human‑centred design](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/human-cetered-design) principles support people's real needs. Once growth and user lock-in via network effects, data, and habits are achieved, attention shifts to business customers. Reach starts to be sold, visibility becomes conditional.

In the final phase, the platform begins to extract from both sides. Users face more ads and more attention interventions. Partners pay more for the same effect as before. Exit options shrink.

This decline usually doesn't come in one visible step. It happens bit by bit. One more interruption. One more forced setting. One more friction when trying to cancel the service. Each change is small and defensible. Together, however, they erode trust.

For people responsible for digital services, this isn't just a business problem. It's a failure of human‑centred design throughout the entire lifecycle. When optimization narrows solely to revenue, degradation becomes a rational outcome.

The warning is simple. Quality can be cut away easily, but it is very difficult to restore. Once users stop believing the service acts in their interest, redesign and reassurance come too late.

The term was [popularized](https://x.com/doctorow/status/1550457808222552065) by *[Cory Doctorow](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cory_Doctorow)*, who named his book after it in autumn 2025. In 2023, enshittification became the [Word of the Year](https://americandialect.org/2023-word-of-the-year-is-enshittification/) of the American Dialect Society. In Czech, it translates as [zesračkovatění](https://www.seznamzpravy.cz/clanek/kultura-recenze-usa-pred-kolapsem-netflix-doji-realitu-ale-nejde-mu-to-270681#:~:text=tedy%20n%C4%9Bco%20jako%20%E2%80%9E-,zesra%C4%8Dkovat%C4%9Bn%C3%AD,-%E2%80%9C.%20Streamovac%C3%AD%20platformy%20u%C5%BE), although authors usually prefer the English term.
