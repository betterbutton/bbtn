**Google is radically changing search results.** [Last time](https://reknisioweb.substack.com/p/06) we dealt a lot with the European Digital Markets Act. Google has now announced that in response to the DMA, it will [modify the search results page](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/02/search-experiences-in-eea) for countries in the European Economic Area. And it's doing so significantly. The Czech Republic was selected for testing. The changes concern searches for places 📍, products 📦, flights 🛫, and jobs 🧑‍🔬. Maybe it's time to reopen the [SEO guide](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/02/ssg-gets-a-makeover) again.

**Design system for the rest of us.** Governments and corporations build their websites with building blocks, thereby significantly streamlining design and development, improving consistency of the user experience, and thus usability. Building your own design system is laborious, so designer Brad Frost had the idea that a [universal design system for everyone](https://bigmedium.com/ideas/a-global-design-system.html) could be created and immediately built into browsers.

Perhaps you've noticed that we sometimes cobble things together in code. For example, like Jakub [transcribes audio recordings](https://medium.com/svejda-goldmann-webdesign/jak-na-kvalitn%C3%AD-strojov%C3%BD-p%C5%99epis-%C4%8Desk%C3%BDch-nahr%C3%A1vek-ef566ac3095b) using Whisper. We use programmable notebooks for this. In our case, it's [Google Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/). It's actually a text file where you write formatted text using [markdown](https://www.markdownguide.org/getting-started/) like on Wikipedia. [You can intersperse the text with commands for the command line or pieces of scripts.](https://www.tutorialspoint.com/google_colab/your_first_colab_notebook.htm) Google Colab is basically free, and if you wanted to have a programmable notebook on your computer, [install Jupyter notebook](https://jupyter.org/).

In Google Colab and Jupyter, you program in Python, but there are also kernels supporting other languages. Consult with ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, or your other favorite AI assistant on how to work with notebooks, and then don't forget to show off what you've cobbled together!

# What We're Currently Working On

Martin is working on a corporate website. The client has a very well-developed design system. In such a case, does it make sense to skip the lo-fi design and work directly with the design system components? Or should a designer create a lo-fi representation of the existing components?

Martin proposes the first path. The purpose of rough designs is not only efficiency in design but also removing the presentation layer so that stakeholders in feedback and users in testing can focus primarily on the information architecture of the design and the alignment of the concept with the mental model. Hi-fi components of the design system draw attention away from these aspects of the design and pull it toward the presentation layer, interaction, and content.

The second path is not efficient. It's hard to justify creating a design at a lower fidelity at a moment when the company has already invested effort and costs into creating a component building block at high fidelity. With the additional creation of lo-fi components, inconsistency may creep in or superfluous new UI components may be created.

In such a situation, it is advisable to use a [content priority guide](https://alistapart.com/article/priority-guides-a-content-first-alternative-to-wireframes/) as a substitute for wireframes. It is abstract enough that stakeholders and users do not fixate on the visual side of the design. It is effective [in communicating about information architecture](https://zaengle.com/blog/planning-content-for-a-website). It allows [involving stakeholders](https://www.slideshare.net/lennartoverkamp/workshop-priority-guides-the-alternative-to-wireframes) or even users in the design. It [supports design decisions](https://medium.com/workday-design/priority-guide-with-intent-analyze-with-content-early-88110ccf8856) in later phases and is not such a dead end in the process. It is very flexible with changes but also allows for user testing.
