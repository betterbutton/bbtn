# Cherry picking
> We refer to cherry picking as the selection of individual elements from different designs instead of choosing one design variant. This approach leads to inconsistent, non-conceptual design and higher costs.

You're selecting a design for a new website or application. You like each design for a different reason and feel like taking something small from each. This is called **cherry picking**. At first glance, it may seem like a path to a perfect result. In reality, however, it leads to inconsistent design.

Cherry picking is selecting individual elements from different designs instead of choosing one design as a whole. You try to get the best from each design. The result, however, is a chaotic and incongruous design without a unifying concept.

[Consistency](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/unity-v-uniformity) is the foundation of a professional and user-friendly product. Combining parts from different designs causes the design to lose unifying elements. It makes orientation harder for users and reduces your credibility. Combining different elements requires more time and higher development costs, as each element may need a different approach to implementation.

To avoid cherry picking, it's important to have clearly defined project goals and priorities. Clarify what is most important for your website or application. Focus on core functions and aesthetics that best represent your brand. Communicate openly with designers and share your ideas and expectations.

Use reference materials and mood boards. Follow an iterative design process, starting from a rough draft. Communicate and adjust the design regularly to ensure it is coherent and functional.
