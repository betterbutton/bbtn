# Business associations demand changes to the draft cyber security law
> 

Business associations criticize the [draft law on cyber security](https://odok.cz/portal/veklep/material/ALBSCSSFKU7S/) due to excessive bureaucracy, high costs, and concentration of powers in [NÚKIB](https://nukib.gov.cz/), writes [Jan Sedlák on Lupa](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/firmy-poslaly-nastvany-dopis-premierovi-chteji-nove-zadani-pro-nukib-ohledne-kyberzakona/). In a letter to the prime minister, they demand that the obligations of [NIS2](https://osveta.nukib.gov.cz/course/view.php?id=145) be implemented in the same way as in other EU states, that the law be based on quality impact analyses, and that supply chain control be excluded from the law and approved separately. They propose coordination at the government level and ask the prime minister to re-engage in the process and provide new clear instructions for NÚKIB.

The letter was signed by the Economic Chamber of the Czech Republic, ICT Union, Association of Mobile Network Operators, Czech Association of Electronic Communications, and Committee of Independent ICT Industry.

On Friday, NÚKIB informed that the [government's legislative council recommended the draft law for approval](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/lrv-nis2).
