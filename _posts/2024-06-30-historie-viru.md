# Altair: Fascinating Similarities Between Digital and Biological Threats
> 

Michal Altair Valášek elaborated on his blog [about the history of computer viruses](https://www.altair.blog/2024/06/michelangelo). [John von Neumann](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_von_Neumann), the theoretical father of modern computers and one of the key figures of the Manhattan Project, lectured in 1949 about self-replicating programs and thus laid the foundations for the concept of a computer virus. Real viruses work similarly; they are harmless until they find a suitable host where they begin to multiply.

> "A virus in itself is essentially harmless. It is not alive, does not reproduce, eats or drinks nothing."
> **— Michal Altair Valášek, [Covid and Michelangelo](https://www.altair.blog/2024/06/michelangelo)**

Viruses, whether computer or biological, can mutate, which makes their detection and elimination difficult. Vaccines and antivirus programs are therefore constantly updated to cope with new variants.

The similarity between these two types of viruses is fascinating and shows how information can be dangerous if it spreads uncontrolled.
