# Consistency is not Uniformity
> Consistency improves user orientation and trust. Uniformity tires and leads to design blindness. Design should be diverse, tailored to user needs.

[Consistency and Uniformity](https://designx.community/talks/hayley-hughes-ux-manager-shopify-unity-not-uniformity-with-design-systems/) are often confused.[1](#footnote-1) Consistency is the foundation of effective design. However, uniformity can hinder innovation and worsen the user experience. What is the difference between them and why is it important to find a balance?

### Consistency as the Foundation of Trust

Consistent design creates an environment that is understandable and predictable. Users find it easier to navigate and trust it more. The same visual elements, behaviors, and structures across the product facilitate use. This is particularly important in areas like banking or healthcare, where ambiguity can be fatal.

[Jakob's Law](https://lawsofux.com/jakobs-law/) states that users expect your product to work similarly to other products they know. Adhering to established patterns and practices facilitates understanding and increases satisfaction.

### Uniformity Dulls Attention

Excessive uniformity can be detrimental. Repeating the same patterns and functions regardless of context can seem sterile and leads users to overlook important elements. Every element should be designed with its purpose and user needs in mind.

When seeking solutions to specific design problems, a [catalog of design patterns](https://ui-patterns.com/patterns) can help. It offers proven practices for various situations, allowing for diversity without sacrificing functionality.

### Human Perception and Cognitive Load

Consistency facilitates product use by reducing cognitive load. Users do not have to repeatedly figure out how the system works. However, uniformity can lead to overlooking important information. If everything looks the same, hierarchy is lost and orientation becomes more difficult. Design should be clear but also diverse. Visual distinctions and dynamic elements can maintain attention without compromising understandability.

### The Emotional Aspect of Design

Sterile uniformity can lead to uninteresting and alienating design. This is particularly problematic for products meant to engage, such as games or educational apps. Subtle details and surprising elements can enliven the design. These small touches contribute to a more pleasant and memorable experience.

Strive for consistency, but avoid uniformity. Consistency ensures understandability and reduces [cognitive load](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/minimize-cognitive-load/). Uniformity limits creative solutions to problems and complicates user orientation within the product.

[1](#footnote-anchor-1)

In English, the term *Unity vs. Uniformity* is used. In Czech, we would translate it most accurately as *Jednotnost vs. Stejnost*. For this article, we chose *Consistency and Uniformity* to avoid sounding too pedantic.
