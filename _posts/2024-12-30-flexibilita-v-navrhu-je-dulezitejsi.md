# Flexibility in design is more important than uniformity
> Consistency is often highlighted as a key principle in UX design, but it is not always decisive. The main goal of design should be usability, not uncompromising adherence to the design system. The author emphasizes that excessive rigidity can harm the user experience, as demonstrated by examples such as early versions of Facebook or Amazon, which thrived due to intuitive control despite inconsistency. [A practical approach is contextual consistency](https://shanepwilliams.medium.com/consistency-in-ux-design-nice-to-have-not-a-must-have-5a0a52bf31f9), meaning adapting the design to user needs. Consistency should serve as a guide, not a dogma, with regular testing and flexibility being key to successful design.
