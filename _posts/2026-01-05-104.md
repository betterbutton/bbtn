## From The Designer

![Left (incorrect) placeholder in a form field instead of a label. Right (correct) label next to the form field.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/69b678a6-f7f2-4eac-8dc3-ea2ee8ddb9b2_2160x1080.png "1456x728")

**Don't hide labels in placeholders.** A placeholder is not a replacement for a label.\
\
❌ The label disappears when I start typing\
✅ A visible label above the field + a supplementary placeholder\
\
👉 The user knows what belongs in the field even while typing.\
👉 The number of erroneous inputs is reduced.\
\
A label next to a form field saves memory and errors.

## What won't miss us this year

We tried to peer into the crystal ball and predict trends for the upcoming year that will affect internet business, e-commerce, and SEO:

- Mass expansion of zero-click purchases – via search, AI assistants, without needing to visit a website.

- Normalization of social commerce – TikTok Shop, Instagram Checkout, YouTube Storefronts in the EU.

- Voice shopping and interaction as the main UX in mobile apps and smart assistants.

- Full dominance of AI answers in search (especially Google and Bing). Decreasing relevance of metadata, growing importance of brand mentions, authority, and citations.

- Expansion of structured data for content: entities, topical clusters, schemas (schema.org), LLM optimization.

- Emergence of autonomous agents in online services (orders, account management, troubleshooting).

- Greater emphasis on accessibility-by-default due to legal risk (especially in the EU and USA).

- Increased interest in headless CMS and composable architecture for performance and agility.

- Development of legal frameworks around digital faces, deepfakes, and AI avatars.

- Bans on real-time facial recognition in the Czech Republic, France, and other EU countries.

- Strengthening of user rights: digital rights, data access, right to explanation of AI decisions.

Let's see if we're right when we look back at the end of year 2026. Besides trends, a legislative whirlwind is bearing down on us, mostly originating from the European Commission and Parliament. We looked at the legislative process for this year:

- Full effectiveness of the [Cybersecurity Act](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2025-264)

- Amendment to the Competition Act ([introduction of personal liability of managing employees for cartels](https://odok.gov.cz/portal/veklep/material/ALBSDNCA86EG/))

- Mandatory ["Withdraw from contract" button](https://retailnews.cz/2025/11/19/tlacitko-odstoupit-od-smlouvy-revoluce-v-online-obchodovani/) for online transactions from June 19.

- Adoption of European rules for [employee cards and work permits](https://www.mpsv.cz/pro-cizince) (important for startups, hiring digital nomads, and foreigners)

<!-- -->

- Draft law [Cloud and AI Development Act](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/thinktank/en/document/EPRS_BRI(2025)779251) (aimed at supporting European infrastructure and setting standards for cloud services)

- [Revision of the Digital Markets Act](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/consultation-first-review-digital-markets-act-2025-07-03_en#:~:text=Commission%20is%20required%20to%C2%A0conduct%20a%20review%20of%20the%20DMA%20by%203%20May%202026) (DMA) (possible expansion of scope to more entities)

- Full application of the [AI Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj/eng) (August 2, obligation of documentation for personalization, AI scoring in e-commerce, etc.)

- [Digital Fairness Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Fairness_Act) (limitations on manipulative interfaces: [dark patterns](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/dark-patterns), transparency of advertising and recommendation systems)

## Linkodrome

**Evo Design System from eBay won the Design System Awards 2025.** The [eBay Playbook design system](https://playbook.ebay.com/) received recognition from the professional community for its clarity and inspirational approach. Other [award-winning design systems](https://zeroheight.com/blog/celebrating-the-2025-design-system-awards-winners/) were Dice (Entain & BetMGM), DDS (Tesco), PIE (Just Eat Takeaway), pAIella (Thomson Reuters), and Rise Design System (Nutrien Ag Solutions).

**The research phase is a myth.** Instead of asking how many interviews or A/B tests are enough, we should ask what value they bring and when it's time to amplify or dampen them. *Adrian Howard* criticizes the approach based on research and design phases. He proposes an approach based on perceiving signals from the environment: [research and testing are processes that need constant tuning](https://adrianhoward.com/posts/turn-it-up-turn-it-down/), not one-off activities.

**Without user involvement, there is no human-centered design.** *Jared M. Spool* criticizes the practice where designers and developers create products without a deeper understanding of the people they are designing for. Instead of real users, we rely on mediated opinions from experts or AI-generated synthetic personas. We cannot describe even one specific day of a specific user. Without this knowledge, [our products are just assumptions](https://articles.centercentre.com/its-time-we-seriously-talk-about-users-and-experiences/).

**How to create an effective UX research repository in Notion:** *Odette Jansen* details how [she created her own UX research repository in Notion](https://odettejansen.medium.com/utilising-notion-as-your-ux-research-repository-005c7dc36726). She highlights benefits like accessibility, efficiency, or knowledge retention and advises on setting up taxonomy using filters, tags, or structure.
