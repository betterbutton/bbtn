## What All Did Time Take?

One of the defenses against bloat and feature creep is to end services that no longer fulfill their function. Even this year, several services we will miss came to an end.

- [Rise Calendar](https://risecalendar.com/) (March 31, 2025) – was a calendar that could block time in all your calendars when an event appears in one of them. A very useful thing if you separate work and private calendars.

- [Skype](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/blog/2025/02/28/the-next-chapter-moving-from-skype-to-microsoft-teams/) (May 5, 2025) – Skype was finally replaced by Teams. We shed a tear of nostalgia for the time when Skype development was at the corner of Radlická and Plzeňská streets in Prague and we went there for ideas and parties.

- [Pocket](https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/future-of-pocket) (July 8, 2025) – many services can save articles for later reading, but Pocket also looked good and had useful connectors to other services.

- [Glitch](https://blog.glitch.com/post/changes-are-coming-to-glitch/) (July 8, 2025) – this service allowed many people to get their creations on the web at a time when programming required setting up a complex dev stack. It was replaced by coding in the cloud.

- [Google URL Shortener](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/google-url-shortener-links-will-no-longer-be-available/) (August 25, 2025) – you know it, you're looking for a reliable service, so you put your trust in a big corporation's product, and they shut it down. Goo.gl is a good reminder that important things on the internet should be operated.

- [Meta Workplace Platform](https://www.workplace.com/help/work/1167689491269151) (August 31, 2025) – we probably never used this service, but for many companies it was an alternative to corporate groupware.

- [Apple Clips](https://support.apple.com/en-us/123359) (October 10, 2025) – it's always nice to have a native app directly in the suite, but honestly we admit that we more often reached for CutCap.

- [Windows 10](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/lifecycle/end-of-support/end-of-support-2025) (October 14, 2025) – well, we have this here more for completeness; as it gradually shows, that end won't be so dramatic.

- [DNS0.eu](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/evropska-alternativa-dns-bez-varovani-skoncila-dns0-eu-nenasla-penize-na-provoz/) (October 20, 2025) – if you also like to use DNS at addresses like 1.1.1.1 or 8.8.8.8 and similar, you probably also noticed DNS0, which offered similar services in Europe.

- [Dropbox Passwords](https://help.dropbox.com/en-us/installs/dropbox-passwords-discontinuation) (October 28, 2025) – we slowly abandoned Dropbox and for password management we use other alternatives and probably many of us did.

- [Google Play Instant](https://developer.android.com/topic/google-play-instant) (December 2025) – you might not even have known about this service, but we're sorry for it, although we don't use Android phones. It was enough to bring the phone close to an NFC chip or a QR code with a link to a lightweight app, it downloaded and launched immediately and then disappeared – very useful for one-time use apps, for example for paying for lunch.

- [Microsoft Lens](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/retirement-of-microsoft-lens-fc965de7-499d-4d38-aeae-f6e48271652d) (December 15, 2025) – everything that Lens could do is now handled directly by the camera app on mobile and the browser on desktop. Scan and straighten an image, convert an image to editable text – it was easy to get used to.

## Linkodrome

**What did Jakob Nielsen have to (un)learn in 2025?** People are ceasing to be an asset – interventions often worsen AI outputs. User interfaces are disappearing because AI responds directly to intent. Visualizations are experiencing a revolution thanks to the *Nano Banana Pro* tool. The pace is slowing down – quality AI conclusions need time. The value of humans lies more in decision-making than in skills. And instead of 'prompting', navigation through latent space is coming, where searching for answers resembles discovering an unknown city. These are in a nutshell the biggest changes in thinking that Jakob Nielsen [acknowledges](https://www.uxtigers.com/post/2025-review) this year.

**Have you wondered what makes design good?** *Sarah Gibbons* and *Kelley Gordon* embarked on a series of articles in which, according to the rule that most good design can be learned, they analyze examples of good practice. In the first part, they looked at [why some designs look good](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/why-does-design-look-good/). The second part, almost two years later, shows [the anatomy of good design](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/why-does-a-design-look-good-part2/) on four exemplary websites. And with another two years' distance, Kelley focuses on [the principles of visual design](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/good-visual-design/) and their impact on user perception.

**What happens when you draw every day for a month?** *Sarani Mendis* sketched with a pen on paper every day for a month. This simple habit [changed her way of thinking about UX design](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/i-sketched-every-day-for-a-month-and-it-changed-how-i-think-about-ux-fa6137c42ecb). Drawing without the possibility of correction taught her to trust intuition, perceive structure before perfection, and accept errors as part of the process. Everyday, inconspicuous topics like breakfast, clothing, or quiet moments turned into exercises in visual hierarchy, abstraction, and systemic thinking.

**Why do experienced designers move into business?** As designers grow into roles with greater responsibility, their attention shifts from pixels to the product's functioning within corporate strategy. Design becomes for them a tool for decision-making and business optimization, not just aesthetics. Instead of *how it looks*, *why we do it* and *how it affects the business* are more important. The shift requires knowledge of business metrics, empathy, and the ability to systemic thinking. As designers mature, they become strategic partners in company leadership, not just interface creators.

[William E. Hick](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/william-edmund-hick) laid the foundations of HCD

*[William Edmund Hick](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/william-edmund-hick)* was a British experimental psychologist who in 1952 formulated a rule showing that the time needed for decision-making grows logarithmically with the number of choices.

Although his law is often cited in UX as a reason for reducing the number of choices, Hick himself gave no design advice. His real contribution lies in showing how human decision-making can be quantified and modeled based on cognitive limits.

This laid the foundations for design that takes into account human mental capacity – a principle that still shapes technology design today.

## Toolbox

[Unused CSS](https://unused-css.com/) is a tool that automatically identifies and removes unused cascade styles on your website. The service analyzes actually used styles directly on the live page and creates a cleaned file. The result is a faster website, smaller data volume, and clearer code, leading to better user experience and higher conversion.

## To Understand Each Other

[Usability debt](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/usability-debt) is an inconspicuous but fundamental problem of websites: it occurs when the interface formally works but prevents users from smoothly completing tasks. It accumulates if development speed or internal logic is prioritized over people's real needs. It often hides in non-intuitive navigation, illogical forms, or redundant explanations.

The term [enshittification](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/enshittification) describes a pattern where a digital service gradually deteriorates because it deviates from user needs and focuses on profit maximization. Initially, the service respects human-centered design principles, but once it gains a user base, it starts favoring advertisers and business partners. The process of enshittification is gradual, subtle, but relentless.

[Storyboard](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/storyboarding) in UX design shows how a user goes through a specific situation over time. It doesn't address screens but context, motivation, and risks. It helps align the team, reveal design weaknesses, and translate research data into real scenarios. Its strength lies in simplicity – quick sketches, minimal text, one scenario. It is most effective between research and prototyping.

[Task analysis](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/task-analysis) is a method that focuses on users' actual work, not just how they interact with the interface. It helps reveal where digital systems support or complicate work. A task is understood as a meaningful goal (e.g., paying an invoice), which includes decisions, errors, and context. If designers confuse tasks with individual steps, they improve the interface but don't solve fundamental problems.
