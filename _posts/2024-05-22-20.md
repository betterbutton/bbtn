**Googlebot has new IP addresses.** [It's good to know](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-crawler-documentation-has-a-new-ip-list/514861/) if you block indexing of your site by bots that merely pretend to be Google. This link was also [shared by Pavel Ungr](https://x.com/PavelUngr/status/1791013731758927877).

Above them shines the hexadecimal color value in RGB, and if you hit which of the three has that color, you deflect the attack. With each level, the invaders' colors become more confusing.

**On Saturday, people sort emails.** According to Mailkit, up to 23% of newsletter unsubscribes happen on Saturday and [29% of messages are marked as spam](https://www.mediaguru.cz/clanky/2024/05/cesi-se-e-maily-probiraji-nejcasteji-v-sobotu/). Unsubscribing is a much better response for metrics and newsletter deliverability than marking as spam. Work on easy unsubscribing. Since February, Google requires [one-click unsubscribe](https://support.google.com/a/answer/81126?sjid=14507333960250331454-EU&visit_id=638518432822128577-4260473972&rd=1#requirements-5k&zippy=%2Crequirements-for-sending-or-more-messages-per-day) for newsletters with over five thousand subscribers.

**You slowed down your own website.** This is indicated by a study that measures [the impact of Chrome extensions on website loading speed](https://www.debugbear.com/blog/chrome-extensions-website-performance). # Life with Technologies

We are the limit. No matter how advanced technology we invent as humanity, we always encounter its miserable application because we are limited in imagination during design. On one hand, the rise of automation and integration today is overwhelming. On the other hand, integrators often ignore user scenarios. The examples are endless. A chip-operated door doesn't deactivate the alarm because it has to be set manually; a university teacher logs into the computer in every classroom again and it doesn't remember the state in which he left it in the previous classroom; you can't buy the best tablet on the market because you already have a stylus for a lower model. Let's not underestimate in practice the analysis and user research on the technology design side, but especially for the purpose of integration.

# Tip from our book

![A stylized illustration from the book shows a scene from the movie Back to the Future. In the front, a time machine in the form of a sports car and behind it enormous clock hands.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/6ba3ce7e-c417-4dbb-b760-ce83e090be76.avif "480x480")

Illustration © [Kateřina Čápová](https://katerinacapova.cz/75-tipu/), 75 tips on how to ask about a website

We humans are generally very bad at estimating what is common in our lives. There's no point asking how people usually solve a situation. We're not the best at estimating the frequency of occurrence of a situation either. However, we are very good at recognizing deviations from established patterns.

> Don't ask how people usually solve situations. Ask how they solved it last time.

In conversations with customers, ask when the situation you're interested in last occurred. If you ask enough people, you can infer the usual frequency from that. Ask how they last solved it. We're good at reconstruction. Then ask what was different from usual during that last occurrence.
