## Web Summit 2025: Technology Returns to People

*Web Summit 2025* in Lisbon showed a shift from theory to implementation. Main topics this year were: the end of Western technological dominance, the rise of agent AI, investment orientation on the use of technology in civil and military spheres, and the growing value of human authenticity.

This is **5 trends set by Web Summit 2025**:

1.  [End of the game: Western technological dominance ends](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/179331936/konec-hry-technologicka-nadvlada-zapadu-konci)

2.  [The payment revolution wasn't launched by a bank. Brazil launched it.](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/179331936/platebni-revoluci-nespustila-banka-spustila-ji-brazilie)

3.  [Artificial intelligence is moving from answering to acting](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/179331936/umela-inteligence-prechazi-od-odpovidani-k-cinum)

4.  [Authenticity is the new currency](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/179331936/autenticnost-je-novou-menou)

5.  [The future focuses on people](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/179331936/budoucnost-se-soustredi-na-cloveka)

Examples like the Brazilian payment system *[Pix](https://www.bcb.gov.br/en/financialstability/pix_en)* or the Portuguese startup for grant applications *[Granter](https://granter.ai/)* show that innovation comes from unexpected places. Artificial intelligence is changing from a tool of answers to a tool of action. Web Summit confirmed that technology no longer amazes by itself, but must prove its service to people – the future is shaped by those who understand human needs as the core of innovation.

## Linkodrome

**In companies, strategy is missing due to a system that doesn't favor strategic thinking.** Emphasis on performance and productivity leaves little room for discussion, reflection, and deep thinking. Strategy often ends with visual presentations of pillars and goals that look actionable but lack diagnosis and thoughtful steps. *John Cutler* warns of the risk where [only a narrow circle of people understand the strategy, and others just react](https://cutlefish.substack.com/p/tbm-3052-why-do-we-have-no-strategy).

**Guide to UX strategy** by *Sarah Gibbons* and *Anna Kaley* summarizes [the principles of creating a UX strategy](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/strategy-study-guide/). It stands on three pillars: vision (where we're heading), goals (how we measure progress), and plan (how we get there). The vision must be realistic, research-backed, and reflect value for users. Goals should be measurable and connected to business outcomes. The plan then details specific steps, roadmaps, and prioritization. The guide offers dozens of articles and videos that explain individual areas in detail.

**How to decide between ideas.** Product managers often end the discovery phase with a plethora of ideas and no clear direction. *Ryan Singer* suggests not evaluating proposals based on scores or hopeful stories, but focusing on [what happens when the problem isn't solved](https://www.ryansinger.co/discovery-how-to-decide/). Two questions help: *What does the customer do instead?* and *What's wrong with it?*

**8 little things that increase sales.** *Ryan Almeida* points out [eight subtle but highly effective UX decisions](https://medium.com/@ryan.almeida86/8-ux-decisions-that-silently-increase-revenue-e61c012d1081) that boost revenue: fewer choices, clear button labels, faster loading, contextual help, or shorter forms. Users don't consciously perceive these adjustments, but they lead to higher engagement and purchases. These changes won't bring fame in the team, but quietly and permanently improve results.

**5 fundamentals of UI design everyone should know.** *Jeferson Brito* concisely summarizes [five basic principles of UI design](https://blog.codeminer42.com/the-5-ui-fundamentals-a-developer-must-know/): color, typography, visual hierarchy, contrast, and alignment. These elements fundamentally influence the impression of the app and its usability. Practical tips include using typographic scales, color rules like 60‑30‑10, or the importance of contrast for accessibility. Jeferson emphasizes that even without talent, these principles can significantly improve product UI.

**Modern CSS is becoming difficult to master purely manually.** *Andreas Møller* points out that limitations no longer lie in the language's capabilities itself, but in its writing. Visual editors – from tools for working with OKLCH colors to animations and complex curves – remove barriers to understanding and allow not only more precise control but also discovery of new features. According to Andreas, perhaps [the future of CSS lies more in visual design](https://blog.nordcraft.com/css-has-become-too-powerful) than in traditional coding.

**How the section for the deaf was created in the [72 Hours](https://www.72h.gov.cz/) project.** *Kateřina Pešková* and *Petr Vysuček* from Deaf Friendly describe [how a separate section in videos in Czech Sign Language was created](https://www.deaffriendly.cz/blog/o-projektu-72-hodin-a-proc-nebylo-cilem-prelozit-vsechno) for the crisis communication project of the Ministry of the Interior. They didn't translate the entire website – the goal was to convey key information understandably and effectively. They were inspired by approaches from Finland and Sweden.

## Kim Goodwin: Design as Culture, Not Decoration

![Kim Goodwin – drawing generated from a photograph](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/bc81d939-aef7-48e9-8178-1cfdb0aabc41_1536x1024.png "492x328")

„Numbers are often too simplifying and teams tend to over-optimize them.“ — [Kim Goodwin](https://miro.com/blog/kim-goodwin-user-centered-design/#:~:text=numbers%20tend%20to%20be%20overly%20simplistic%2C%20and%20teams%20tend%20to%20over%2Doptimize%20for%20them)

[Kim Goodwin](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kim-goodwin) is known as the author of *Designing for the Digital Age*. She fundamentally influenced UX design not only with persona creation methodology but also with emphasis on ethics and culture in decision-making. She argues that responsible design doesn't arise just from drawing interfaces, but in organizations where human values determine direction. She transfers principles of medical ethics to technology and points out that digital products are part of a global experiment without rules.

## Toolbox

[User Research Incentive Calculator](https://www.userinterviews.com/lp/ux-research-incentive-calculator) is a tool that helps UX researchers set fair and market-appropriate rewards for research participants. It's based on data from real studies and considers participant type, duration, format, and data collection method. It allows defending the budget and preventing disproportionate amounts that could skew results.

## So We Understand Each Other

[Model context protocol](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mcp) (MCP) is an open standard from *Anthropic* that connects artificial intelligence with tools like Figma, Notion, or GitHub. It allows AI access to contextual data and tasks through a unified interface. It brings faster prototyping, documentation automation, and better collaboration thanks to AI that understands context.

[Cascading Style Sheets](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/css) (CSS) are a language for defining the appearance of web pages. They separate the visual layer from the structure and content of HTML. They were created by *Håkon Wium Lie* in 1994 together with *[Bert Bos](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/bert-bos)*. CSS evolved from simple color and font adjustments into a powerful tool for interface design, which today includes grids, variables, and responsive designs.

[Persona](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/persona) is a research-backed user archetype that helps designers consider real needs and behaviors of people, not assumptions. It was created thanks to *[Alan Cooper](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/alan-cooper)* and developed by *[Kim Goodwin](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kim-goodwin)*. Persona is a tool for empathy, unifies the team, and helps avoid designing for the so-called elastic user.
