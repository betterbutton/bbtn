# Alternative text: Practical Guide for Accessible Content
> 

The British civil service has a huge lead over the Czech one in digital transformation, so it's worth taking inspiration from them, even when it comes to good accessibility practices. Katherine Moonan and Samantha Merrett on the Accessibility in Government blog published the article [Alternative text in action](https://accessibility.blog.gov.uk/2024/07/17/alternative-text-in-action/), where they offer practical tips for writing alternative text for images.

Alternative text (alt text) is essential for ensuring image accessibility for users with visual impairments. Chris Jenkins from Defra compares omitting alt text to throwing away trash. Marian Avery emphasizes that image descriptions on a page can help not only assistive technology users but also people with low vision and those reading content in sunlight.

[Using AI to generate alt text](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/alt-text-generator?utm_source=publication-search) can be useful, but human review is essential for accuracy and context. Adding alt text to social media increases accessibility and audience engagement.
