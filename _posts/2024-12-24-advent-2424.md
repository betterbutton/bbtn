# Keep a Project Diary
> 

A project diary is a useful tool for anyone who wants to keep an overview of the development of their website or mobile application. It helps maintain control over the progress of work, prevents forgetting important tasks, and serves as a source of experience for future decision-making. Additionally, it allows you to capture events that impact the project, whether it's deploying new features, launching a marketing campaign, or significant changes in the market. Writing down everything essential aids in better team communication and makes it easier to explain the steps you've taken.

## How to Keep a Digital Project Diary

The digital format ensures easy searching of older records, allows comfortable sharing with other team members, and permits adding attachments or visual material. Useful online tools include [Trello](https://trello.com/), [Asana](https://asana.com/), [Notion](https://www.notion.com/), [Google Docs](https://docs.google.com/), and [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/features/issues).

It is advisable to keep records in a format that can be automatically processed and displayed in a calendar or timeline. When using data formats for content sharing, it is possible to automatically link project records with other sources. Such formats include [RSS feed](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/11#%C2%A7zivot-s-technologiemi), [iCalendar](https://icalendar.org/), or [Event according to Schema.org](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/event).

Regularity plays a very important role. It's good to write down every day or week what was addressed, who was involved, and what the reason for the steps taken was. Looking back, this makes it easy to understand the circumstances that led to a particular decision. For better clarity, you can supplement text posts with visual material, such as design examples, screenshots, or sketches of planned modifications.

## What a Project Diary Should Contain

A project diary should serve as a comprehensive record of everything that can affect the project's course. You can write about the routine tasks you are currently tackling. You can also record difficulties encountered during testing and describe the user experience with deployed features. Writing down ideas as well as suggestions for further development helps find paths for continuous improvement.

The diary should also include mentions of important decisions, such as whether you chose a new content management system or why you switched to a different hosting provider. It should also include documentation of major changes, like launching an advertising campaign or modifying the page structure.

Equally important is recording external events. These include significant sporting events, periods with movement restrictions, various holidays, algorithm updates in search engines, or complications on social networks. Looking back, you can easily determine from the diary why there was a drop or rise in user interest and take appropriate measures.

## How a Project Diary Helps with Improvement

At the end of the week or month, it's advisable to review the latest entries. This allows you to better assess what went well and where complications arose. This gives you a basis for further planning and setting priorities. When you also look into past records, you'll see why you resorted to certain approaches earlier. You can then better evaluate whether your current development concept is still suitable or if an adjustment in approach is necessary.

A project diary also facilitates teamwork. Everyone can verify at any time what happened and why. Team members are not reliant on verbal communication and don't waste time searching for who handled a specific task. In case of a client presentation, you can easily document the progress of work and inspire confidence in your ability to respond to changes or unexpected issues.

If you display the diary as annotations over traffic graphs or revenue records, you'll gain a comprehensive overview of when and why fluctuations in project metrics occurred. The project diary thus becomes a layer that gives the data broader meaning. Thanks to this connection, you can more quickly find the cause of unexpected trends and learn for future steps.

A project diary is a useful support for anyone developing or managing a website or application. It allows you to capture steps and events that have a direct or indirect impact on your project's success. The digital format brings the possibility of unlimited sharing and storing attachments as well as quick searching in past records.

When you regularly write in the diary, you also gain valuable support for long-term and strategic decisions. All steps are thus easier to evaluate, coordinate, and prevent the confusion that can occur when major changes happen. A project diary will help you keep development on the right track, even when various obstacles and influences appear that could otherwise cause undesirable chaos.🎄24
