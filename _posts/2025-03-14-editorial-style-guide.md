# Editorial style guide
> Editorial style guide is a set of rules that helps maintain a consistent, understandable, and professional character of written content. It is used to ensure a unified communication style across different channels.

**Editorial style guide**, often referred to as [editorial style guide](https://neilpatel.com/blog/editorial-style-guide/), is a guide for creating clear, consistent, and professional content. It serves to maintain a uniform tone, structure, and visual style across all types of texts – from web articles and applications to marketing materials and corporate documents.

Without clearly defined rules, content can appear confusing and unprofessional. Different authors may use varying writing styles, different terminology, or inconsistent formatting. This can lead to unclear messaging and damage brand credibility. A style guide helps maintain consistency, facilitates collaboration between authors, and improves content readability.

It is used in various fields, for example in digital content and UX writing, where it ensures consistent terminology, or in marketing and on social media, where it helps maintain a unified brand voice. It is also important in printed materials and corporate documentation, where it promotes a systematic approach to communication.

A well-crafted guide includes rules for grammar, communication tone, formatting, SEO, accessibility, and visual style. It can take the form of a simple document or an extensive digital manual with specific examples of correct usage.

Regardless of its format, an editorial style guide is an important tool for anyone working with written content. It helps ensure that all communication is clear, understandable, and professional, thereby contributing to a better user experience and strengthening brand identity.
