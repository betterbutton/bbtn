## From The Designer

**Don't rely on color alone.** Color by itself is not readable for everyone.\
\
❌ The difference between states is only a different color\
✅ Color + icon, shape, or text label\
\
👉 You help color-blind users.\
👉 The state is clear even on poorer displays.\
\
🛑 The combination of color and shape makes the interface more understandable.

## Linkodrome

**Design without process is tempting** in its speed, but unsustainable in the long run. *Pavel Samsonov* advocates for [friction as a constructive force](https://productpicnic.beehiiv.com/p/the-myth-of-no-design-process) that gives design meaning, depth, and influence. He criticizes efforts to optimize at all costs, including blind trust in AI tools that promote superficial solutions. According to him, designers have lost power because they gave up the process in the name of efficiency. Returning to a thoughtful process is a way to regain trust and role in product development—and to turn friction into an advantage.

**UX fails because it still behaves like an agency.** UX is not failing because of Agile, but because [it still operates as a traditional agency](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ux-isnt-failing-because-agile-its-still-works-like-robert-duebelbeis-khodc/)—with pre-planned hours, locked decisions, and fixed deliverables. *Robert Duebelbeis* argues that UX operates in the old advertising paradigm of *"surprise and delight"*, instead of focusing on learning and value.

![A 1999 print ad for Google promises clean search without weather, news, ads, and other portal ballast.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ce084942-afc9-4e28-8d32-2e674c765a0b_718x718.jpeg "354x354")

Search without unnecessary dogs. ([@historyinmemes](https://x.com/historyinmemes/status/1940320538574168196))

**AI killed the homepage.** *Michal Půr* looks back on the end of the era of classic media, which in 2025 lost its primary source of traffic—search engines and social networks. General-interest media are disappearing; chances are for strong brands, narrow specialized niches, or creators with their own audience. According to Michal, [survival depends on trust, context, and direct monetization](https://www.nwslttr.cz/p/michal-pur-nekrolog-za-homepage-proc?hide_intro_popup=true)—subscriptions, sponsorships, or video content on global platforms.

**Marketplaces are displacing classic e-shops.** Traditional multi-brand e-shops without their own brand or specialization are becoming meaningless—the market is dominated by marketplace platforms like *Temu*, *Amazon*, or *Allegro*, which have advantages in pricing, logistics, and marketing. Small Czech shops are becoming unsustainable if they don't find a narrow market segment or build their own brand, thinks *Jan Trval*. China's regulations don't solve the situation—they only level the playing field. [Only those with clear added value for the customer will survive.](https://www.seznamzpravy.cz/clanek/ekonomika-ocima-byznysu-komentar-e-shopy-jak-je-zname-konci-a-neni-to-jen-straseni-293055)

> “Low-maturity product orgs fetishize ideas.”\
> **— [Pavel Samsonov](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7405365843045552128/)**

**UX research has a greater impact when you experience it.** *Kate Kaplan* presents [three types of workshops](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-research-workshops/): workshops for alignment with research, workshops for building empathy, and workshops focused on applying findings. She offers specific activities like a gallery walk, empathy mapping, or *“[How might we](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/how-might-we)”* ideation. Emphasis is placed on connecting insights, emotions, and action—and on a flexible combination of approaches based on the team's context.

**Dashboards fail because they are not actionable enough.** They overwhelm users with data without clear distinction of what requires attention. *Vitaly Friedman* presents [the concept of action dots](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/designing-effective-dashboards-ux-action-dots-vitaly-friedman-9jzee/) by *[Nick Desbarats](https://www.practicalreporting.com/)*, which solves this problem. Instead of traditional indicators, it uses action dots to distinguish between critical, neutral, and exceptionally good states of metrics. These visual signals are intuitive, easily readable, and accessible even for color-blind users. The result is clearer dashboards that highlight what truly requires intervention and facilitate daily decision-making.

> “The best tech innovation in the last couple of years is not AI, it’s the ‘undo’ button when you just sent an email and noticed you forgot the link/attachment.”\
> **— Stéphanie Walter**

**100% accessibility does not exist.** *Adrian Roselli* points out that even consistent [compliance with the WCAG 2.2 standard does not guarantee that a product will be accessible to everyone](https://adrianroselli.com/2025/12/you-cant-make-something-accessible-to-everyone.html), because accessibility in reality varies across diverse human needs, technologies, and contexts. Adrian emphasizes that accessibility is not a purely technical problem and that instead of generalized statements, we should precisely describe which features make something more accessible.

**More accessible fonts.** Some fonts contain character variants that support easier distinction of letters `Il1` or `0O`. In CSS, you can set a preference for more readable variants using the low-level property [font-feature-settings](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/font-feature-settings).

    font-feature-settings: "cv08", "cv05", "zero";

Alternatively, you can use properties [font-variant-alternates](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/font-variant-alternates) and [font-variant-numeric](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/font-variant-numeric).

*[Elizabeth Feinler](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/elizabeth-feinler)*, as head of the *Network Information Center* for *ARPANET*, introduced the WHOIS directory information system, which clarified who stood behind internet services. Her team designed the top-level domains. [The .com domain was originally supposed to be named .bus](https://www.bortzmeyer.org/files/HistoryoftheTLDs.pdf), but it was changed during the implementation of the name server.

> “It will be interesting to see whether the internet and computers augment our intelligence and lives, or whether they replace them.”\
> **— [Elizabeth Feinler](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2019/10/28/2-internet-pioneers-imagine-the-next-50-years/#:~:text=interesting%20to%20see%20whether%20the%20internet%20and%20computers%20augment%20our%20intelligence%20and%20lives%2C%20or%20whether%20they%20replace%20them.)**

In the early days of the Internet, Elizabeth managed the domain list as a single [hosts file](https://365tipu.cz/2016/04/08/tip455-k-cemu-je-nejen-ve-windows-soubor-hosts-a-kde-ho-najdu-a-jak-ho-mohu-editovat/)—yes, the same one you still have on your computer today. Others, if they wanted to use the internet domain system, downloaded her file from FTP. That's how it worked until the first half of the 1980s, when her file was replaced by DNS.

Elizabeth understands the internet as a social space, not just a technical network. After her career, she devoted herself to preserving its history. Her legacy? The internet needs not only technology but also order, responsibility, and memory.

## Toolbox

[URL Pattern Analyzer](https://www.prokopsw.cz/app/url-patterns/) is a free tool from *Mark Prokop* that allows you to quickly analyze the structure of URL addresses on a website. It works directly in the browser and does not send any data. It emphasizes privacy. It's useful for SEO audits, redirects, or content migration. By inserting a domain or a list of URLs, you get clear patterns and a breakdown of addresses by part of the site. The [source code](https://github.com/MarekProkop/url-pattern-analyzer) is open and available on GitHub.

## So We Understand Each Other

[Backlinks](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/backlink) once determined a website's position in search results, but their importance has been gradually declining since 2012. Instead of link weight, search engines now evaluate the relevance, quality, and trustworthiness of content. Today they serve more for page discovery and as a secondary trust signal.

[Availability heuristic](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/availability-heuristic) is a [cognitive bias](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/cognitive-bias) where we consider easily recallable experiences to be common or important, even when they are not. In UX and team decisions, one strong incident—like a website failure—then outweighs stable data. This phenomenon is related to cognitive fluency: we believe more in what is familiar. The solution is to document decisions, show trends, and balance strong stories with data.

[Design challenge](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/design-challenge) is a simple but strategic question that turns a vague brief into a specific creative direction. It helps the team focus on what's essential: not how to design, but why. A question like *[How might we...](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/how-might-we)* connects a business goal with a user need and serves as a compass throughout the design. Unlike functional lists or user stories, it determines both direction and success criteria.

[Edge case](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/edge-case) is a rare but valid user situation that tests the robustness of a design. It is precisely in these extremes—long names, weak connections, stressful moments—that system failures often occur. They most frequently affect the most vulnerable users. Experts recommend testing outside ideal conditions. Edge cases are not exceptions but a common part of the real world.
