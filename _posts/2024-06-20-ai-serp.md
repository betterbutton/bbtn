# AI in search results is hiring SEO specialists
> 

It was only a matter of time before AI made its way into search results. Yet when Google introduced AI-generated summaries ([AI Overviews](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-overviews)), it really messed with SEO specialists' heads. It is expected that websites can slowly say goodbye to approaches for informational queries, as the AI in the search engine will be able to provide a quality answer directly.

Digital marketer Neil Patel refers to the anticipated drop in search traffic already [in response to Google's March algorithm update](https://neilpatel.com/blog/google-march-2024-algorithm-update/):

> “Google is raising the bar for what it considers quality content and is trying to improve user comfort and the overall integrity of search results.”
> **― Neil Patel, [March 2024 Google Algorithm Impact](https://neilpatel.com/blog/google-march-2024-algorithm-update/)**

Becky Lawlor claims that [up to 40% of low-quality and unoriginal content disappeared from Google search](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7183833622951464960/) due to the algorithm changes.

The Czech search engine Seznam is also developing products using artificial intelligence. Since March, it has been [testing article summaries](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/12#:~:text=Seznam%20testuje%20AI%20pro%20shrnut%C3%AD%20%C4%8Dl%C3%A1nk%C5%AF) on the homepage and fine-tuning its own version of the [ŠeLLMa language model](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/22#:~:text=Share-,Pokusy%20se%20%C5%A0elmou,-.%20Jazykov%C3%BD%20model).

So, if you have been building content for informational queries for years and your website didn't drop out of the index after the March algorithm update, once AI summaries also appear in Czech search, you can say goodbye to organic search traffic.

Marketing consultant Michal Krutiš polished his crystal ball on his blog and [predicts the future for marketing agencies](https://www.krutis.com/ai-seo-ecommerce/):

> “With a declining number of e-commerce clients and the growing use of AI-based automation tools, demand for traditional 'marketing worker' services is decreasing. Many agencies are thus fighting for survival, being squeezed out by senior specialists and large network agencies with sufficient background.”
> **― Michal Krutiš, [The world of search has changed](http://Svět%20vyhledávání%20se%20změnil.%20Vyhledávače%20pomocí%20AI%20upevňují%20kontrolu%20nad%20informacemi%20a%20ziskovostí%20ecommerce%20firem)**

[Jan Štráfelda assumes](https://www.strafelda.cz/seo-marketing-ve-svete-ai) that the advent of AI in search will have a significant impact on website content strategies; companies will have to involve user research much more in them. The lack of organic search traffic will increase demand for advertising, and cost-per-click prices will rise. There will be far greater pressure on email marketing.

Jakub and Martin looked at the future of search engine optimization from a UX designer's perspective at the SEO Restart conference:

> “User experience is becoming a signal for search engines. Metrics like Core Web Vitals, bounce rate, and mobile-first are trying to quantify UX quality so that search engines can use it as one of the factors for ranking pages in search results.”
> **― Jakub Goldmann & Martin Kopta, [Better Button #12](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/142565604/zivot-s-technologiemi)**

The aforementioned Neil Patel [offers as a solution](https://neilpatel.com/blog/content-marketing-and-beyond/) a greater focus on branding, use of structured data, long-tail optimization, and EEAT: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness.

There is no time to wait. The future with artificial intelligence is already here.
