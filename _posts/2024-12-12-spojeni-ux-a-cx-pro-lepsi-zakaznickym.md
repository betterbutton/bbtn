# Combining UX and CX for Better Customer Journeys
> 

Combining UX and CX brings a shift in approach to customer experience, [writes Kim Flaherty](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ux-and-cx-merge/). Instead of product focus, organizations orient towards the design of customer journeys that combine marketing, support, technology, and other areas.

While UX historically addresses digital products at the interaction level, CX focuses on the lifelong relationship with the customer. Through the joint effort of both approaches, so-called journey teams emerge that solve problems across channels and functions.

This model not only eliminates friction points but also improves business results and opens space for innovation. Journey-centric design thus becomes key to modern customer strategy.
