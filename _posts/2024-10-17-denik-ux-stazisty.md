# UX Intern Diary
> 

Vishal Gautam started writing an [UX intern diary](https://medium.com/@vishalgautam.ux). So far, he has published the first two installments. [In the first installment](https://medium.com/@vishalgautam.ux/diary-of-an-ux-intern-chapter-1-b635cef71ebb?source=user_profile_page---------1-------------3e6a399b3e81---------------), he shares the beginnings of his career and insights he gathered during the first two months of his internship.

> “Aesthetics does not equal sales.”

Although he has eight months of theoretical training behind him, he found that practical experience is invaluable. He focuses on the differences between the aesthetic side of design and what actually works in marketing and user experience. For beginners, he emphasizes the importance of [Call to Action](https://www.uxdictionary.io/call-to-action) and the personal growth that comes with learning from practice.

[In the second chapter of the diary](https://medium.com/@vishalgautam.ux/diary-of-a-ux-intern-chapter-2-af076f58f9cc?source=user_profile_page---------0-------------3e6a399b3e81---------------), Vishal writes about his experience with the phenomenon known as [designer bias](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/bias?srsltid=AfmBOoqNc0QfoS7U8rRPrN7E4rYWkuZcPrzIjCUWdKcK6lkieEHbzf8H) — the natural tendency to be uncritical of one's own work immediately after creating it.

He describes how his perception of design changes over time and how important it is not to wait for distance but rather to involve feedback from people from different fields. He points out the usefulness of user testing and the need for a broader perspective when evaluating designs.

We are rooting for Vishal to succeed in UX and will continue to follow his diary. [Will you join?](https://medium.com/@vishalgautam.ux)
