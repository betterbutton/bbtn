# Linkodrome

**A verdict has been passed on AI-generated works and copyright.** The Municipal Court in Prague dealt with a case where the plaintiff sought to establish authorship of an image created by the DALL-E artificial intelligence. As [writes the ePrávo server](https://www.epravo.cz/top/clanky/mestsky-soud-v-praze-o-umele-inteligenci-a-autorskem-pravu-117318.html), the court ruled that the plaintiff did not prove authorship and that the image created by artificial intelligence does not meet the criteria for a copyrighted work under Czech copyright law, as it is not a unique result of the creative activity of a natural person.

Martin's lovely 🥰 works in a multinational corporation and takes care of education. Localizing video courses is always a production-intensive project. **But with machine translation, voice cloning, and virtual avatars, video course localization is becoming something that one person in a company can quite possibly handle even without an agency.**

[Spotify announced](https://newsroom.spotify.com/2023-09-25/ai-voice-translation-pilot-lex-fridman-dax-shepard-steven-bartlett/) back in September that it would clone podcasters' voices and translate their episodes. [YouTube has been dubbing videos](https://blog.youtube/inside-youtube/meet-aloud-ai-co-creators/) since last summer. There are even [plugins for video conferencing](https://www.vidvoice.ai/) that interpret and synchronize lip movements in real time.

At the end of the year, a major event occurred in voice cloning. [MyShell.AI](http://myshell.ai/) released open-source software for voice cloning. It's a similar breakthrough to when OpenAI published open-source code for [Whisper](https://medium.com/svejda-goldmann-webdesign/ef566ac3095b) last year. Now you can incorporate voice cloning into your projects, and it will likely become ubiquitous just like text dictation soon.

The results aren't completely perfect yet, but that's rarely the case with first versions of software. [You can try cloning](https://huggingface.co/spaces/myshell-ai/OpenVoice) on the MyShell.AI profile on Hugging Face.

If Hugging Face is still too much of a geek thing for you, know that Facebook, or rather Meta, isn't sitting idle either. In the [Audiobox project](https://audiobox.metademolab.com/maker), you can clone your voice, add other virtual avatars and gestures, and create, for example, a conversation or a scene just using text prompts.

# What we're currently working on

**Design Sprint 2.0.** January is traditionally dedicated to teaching students [multimedia at VŠE](https://kme.vse.cz/). For our course, which aims to show students website and application design, we chose a four-day [Design Sprint 2.0](https://medium.com/svejda-goldmann-webdesign/533e9a4de5e6) from the Berlin agency AJ&Smart.

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/ce57242c-6f57-4055-b716-f8f8fa2c1682_960x799.jpeg "960x799")

In the design sprint, we take students through the entire design process in just a few days. We try to show them a design approach to solving projects. We chose the [version from AJ&Smart](https://www.invisionapp.com/inside-design/design-sprint-2/), mainly because they have a very [detailedly prepared workshop minute-by-minute schedule](https://app.sessionlab.com/sessions/hdqhfj) and [excellent](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Uu8jmVhwj_o&list=PLxk9zj3EDi0VGRRgG6gtLt_Mo_Ox2ErlQ) [videos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZtYp7XzmXr8&list=PLxk9zj3EDi0X5CgoFckoheIFAx-uT2i7j) [on](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZgMG_OflDq0&list=PLxk9zj3EDi0V8W4wbyezKHgCRCVvDryCY) [Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IFHfsRNTGCM&list=PLxk9zj3EDi0VzC4BmYsOpxPBPeJh7ujEq). Students can study the theory at their own pace at home, and we use the joint time at school for facilitation, design leadership, and consultations.
