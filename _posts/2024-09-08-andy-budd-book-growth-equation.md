# Andy Budd knows the growth equation for startups
> 

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/84edcdc4-4743-473b-9bfd-ccdaa7dc9a04_1600x2560.jpeg "174x278")

Andy Budd moved from web design and development to the role of an investor, and so his new book [The Growth Equation](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/217830934-the-growth-equation) is not about CSS or blog design, but deals with a guide to startup growth. The book hasn't been released yet, but [Andy has already published sample chapters](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7236711803798396930/), from which we draw.

The Growth Equation brings a pragmatic view on startup growth that both budding entrepreneurs and experienced company founders will appreciate. The book focuses on balancing rapid growth with sustainability and long-term stability – topics that are crucial for today's dynamic startup ecosystem.

Andy emphasizes understanding both internal and external factors that influence growth and offers useful advice to minimize risks associated with rapid expansion. His approach stems from real-world experience with consulting and advisory in the field of design and digital products, which adds credibility to the book.

Unlike many authors focused on business growth, Andy Budd avoids radical shortcuts and focuses on strategic planning, team dynamics, and emphasis on product quality. For readers seeking a chase for immediate success, the book may be less attractive because it emphasizes patience and systematic approach.

And what is that growth equation? Maybe it reveals a little from [Andy's presentation](https://speakerdeck.com/turingfest/andy-budd-the-growth-equation-7-essential-steps-to-finding-product-market-fit) from July's [Turing Fest](https://turingfest.com/).
