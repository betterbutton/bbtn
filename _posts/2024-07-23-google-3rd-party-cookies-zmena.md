# Google and 3rd-party cookies: course correction or new trick?
> 

Google is surprisingly not ending support for third-party cookies, but instead [is introducing a new approach to consent](https://digiday.com/marketing/after-years-of-uncertainty-google-says-it-wont-be-deprecating-third-party-cookies-in-chrome/). This change in strategy [raises questions about Google's consistency and reliability](https://privacysandbox.com/intl/en_us/news/privacy-sandbox-update/), especially after years of preparing for a future without third-party cookies.

This [move could allow Google to bypass restrictions](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/16#:~:text=p%C3%AD%C5%A1e%20Lupa.-,Aktualizov%C3%A1no,-2024%2D04%2D24) imposed by the UK's Competition and Markets Authority, while still maintaining control over cookies through other mechanisms. A strict consent collection system could lead to a significant drop in addressability, without the need for regulatory approval. The question remains how regulators will deal with this new dynamic.

[Ivo Lukačovič](https://www.patreon.com/posts/google-slibil-ze-108650869) on his Patreon also comments on Google's step. He considers it a transparent and cunning tactic. Marek Lecián on Linkedin openly states that it is merely [a way to cover their backs](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7221238915611930625/) against lawsuits for abusing a dominant market position.
