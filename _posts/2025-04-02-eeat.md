# E‑E‑A‑T
> E‑E‑A‑T represents Google's framework for evaluating content quality: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness as the foundation of a trustworthy website and better search engine rankings.

[E‑E‑A‑T](https://www.mariehaynes.com/resources/eat/) is an acronym for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. It is believed that this concept plays a significant role in how [Google assesses content quality](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2022/12/google-raters-guidelines-e-e-a-t), even though it is not a direct ranking factor. Nevertheless, its principles are reflected in many algorithmic signals and have a real impact on how well your content ranks in search results.

Google uses E‑E‑A‑T as a framework in the *Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines* document ([PDF, 8.5 MB](https://static.googleusercontent.com/media/guidelines.raterhub.com/en//searchqualityevaluatorguidelines.pdf)), which serves human evaluators in assessing whether content appears trustworthy and useful. This approach is particularly important for topics classified as YMYL – those affecting people's health, finances, or safety.

*Experience* means personal, first-hand involvement with the topic – reviews, practical guides, real-life stories. *Expertise* refers to professional qualifications or knowledge. *Authoritativeness* indicates whether the author or website is recognized as a respected source. And *trustworthiness* ensures everything appears honest, transparent, and secure.

SEO experts agree: To improve your website's E‑E‑A‑T, showcase authors and their profiles, work with credible sources, gather reviews, publish case studies, and maintain accuracy and transparency. E‑E‑A‑T is more than theory – it's a guide to creating trustworthy and sustainable content.
