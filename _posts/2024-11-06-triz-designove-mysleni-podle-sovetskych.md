# TRIZ: Design Thinking According to Soviet Engineers
> 

Alexej Blagich analyzes the differences between the [TRIZ](https://www.designorate.com/practice-guide-to-solve-problems-with-triz/#:~:text=The%20TRIZ%20method%20is%20a,Inventive%20Problem%20Solving%E2%80%9D%20in%20English.) (Teoriya Resheniya Izobretatelskikh Zadach – Theory of Inventive Problem Solving) methodology and [design thinking](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/5-stages-in-the-design-thinking-process#:~:text=Design%20thinking%20is%20an%20iterative,%2C%20Ideate%2C%20Prototype%20and%20Test.) and explores how these tools complement each other.

TRIZ is a methodology that Soviet scientists presented to the world after World War II as an effective way to solve technical problems. Western technology companies have incorporated it into their processes for its practical results.

While TRIZ focuses on rapid problem-solving through the analysis of contradictions, design thinking emphasizes empathy and iterative prototyping, allowing for a thorough understanding of user needs.

Alexej emphasizes that the [proper use of both methods](https://medium.com/@blagih/%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B7-%D0%B8-%D0%B4%D0%B8%D0%B7%D0%B0%D0%B9%D0%BD-%D0%BC%D1%8B%D1%88%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B8%D0%B5-%D1%8D%D1%82%D0%B0%D0%BF%D1%8B-%D0%B1%D0%B8%D0%B7%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%81-%D0%BC%D0%B5%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%B4%D0%BE%D0%B2-design-thinking-%D0%B8-triz-%D0%B8%D0%BD%D1%81%D1%82%D1%80%D1%83%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%82%D1%8B-%D0%BC%D0%B5%D1%82%D0%BE%D0%B4%D0%BE%D0%BB%D0%BE%D0%B3%D0%B8%D0%B9-b8bf5e598f20) can streamline business processes: TRIZ helps find solutions quickly, while design thinking offers a deep analysis of the problem. Combining these approaches can save time and product development costs.
