# You can guess 10% of PINs on the first try
> 

Analysis of nearly three and a half million security pins shows that [people often enter PINs in the form of dates](https://informationisbeautiful.net/visualizations/most-common-pin-codes/) (DDMM or MMDD), years (19XY, 20XY) or double numbers (XXYY). A case in point are numerical sequences. Not the Fibonacci sequence, but simple 1234 and 4321.

> You may be able to crack over 10% of all pins in one try! If you expand it, you can get 20% with just five numbers!
> **— Nick Berry, [PIN analysis](http://www.datagenetics.com/blog/september32012/index.html)**

Nick Berry from Data Genetics, who [published the analysis](http://www.datagenetics.com/blog/september32012/index.html), also shows that the 200 most common combinations will cover over 40% of cases.
