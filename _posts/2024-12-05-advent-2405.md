# Local SEO: How to Get Your Business on the Map and Improve Search Visibility
> 

Local SEO in a small market like the Czech Republic and the Czech language doesn't have as much weight as in world languages and large countries. However, if your business operates primarily in a specific area, it helps to target search engine optimization locally as well. Whether you run a restaurant, a shop, or a service, proper optimization will help you not only increase website traffic but also attract more customers.

# Google My Business and Firmy.cz

The first step to success is creating and optimizing your profile on [Google My Business](https://www.google.com/business/) and on Seznam's [Firmy.cz](https://www.firmy.cz/). When setting up (or updating) your business profile, remember the acronym NAP. It stands for name, address, and phone – remember to keep the same name, the same address (both physical and electronic), and the same phone number across all business profiles. Don't forget to choose the correct category, and if it makes sense, don't overlook other profile details such as opening hours, interior photos, or featured products.

Use the same NAP information across all platforms (website, social networks, directories). Consistency builds credibility with search engines.

# Structured Data Understandable to Search Engines

Structured data from schema.org is a way to provide accurate information about your business to search engines. Use markup types like [LocalBusiness](https://schema.org/LocalBusiness) or [Organization](https://schema.org/Organization) and include basic details such as address, opening hours, and phone.

Example of JSON-LD usage:

    {
      "@context": "https://schema.org",
      "@type": "LocalBusiness",
      "name": "Your Company",
      "address": {
        "@type": "PostalAddress",
        "streetAddress": "Street 123",
        "addressLocality": "City",
        "postalCode": "12345",
        "addressCountry": "CZ"
      },
      "telephone": "+420123456789"
    }

The [Google Rich Results Test](https://search.google.com/test/rich-results) tool can help with validation.

# Local Links Have Power

Acquiring quality links from local websites can significantly improve your position in search engines. Collaborate with local media, blogs, or community organizations. Create content that will engage the local audience – for example, city guides, lists of the best places to visit, or articles about local events.

Social networks are great for linking. When businesses in the area link to each other and interact, search engines can better confirm that you operate in close proximity. Partnerships with other local businesses bring mutual benefits. For example, you can exchange website links or organize joint events.

# Organize Events and Engage Your Community

Local events such as Experience the City Differently, Open Courtyards, or Open House are great ways to attract attention while providing search engines with additional signals about which locality you belong to. Whether it's a workshop, a lecture, or a community support event, don't forget to promote it on your website, social networks, and through Google My Business.

Use schema.org [Event](https://schema.org/Event) structured data to increase your event's visibility:

    {
      "@context": "https://schema.org",
      "@type": "Event",
      "name": "SEO Workshop",
      "startDate": "2024-12-10T10:00",
      "location": {
        "@type": "Place",
        "name": "Your Company",
        "address": "Street 123, City, 12345"
      },
      "description": "Learn how to optimize your website for search engines!"
    }

# Locality in URL and On-Page Factors

Use keywords in your URLs (e.g., `/pizzeria-brno-koliste` instead of non-descriptive `/branch-602`). Also optimize page headings and texts for local keywords. Remember that people often search for unofficial place names like Anděl or Na Knížecí instead of district and street names. Create content focused on a specific locality, such as articles about local specialties or neighborhood guides.

# Get Reviews

Reviews on Google or other platforms play a significant role in customer decisions directly on the search results page. If there's a business review website in your industry, list your business there too. For travel, it might be Tripadvisor; for cafes, Yelp. Encourage customers to write reviews. Search engines take data from review sites, and increasing recent reviews signal to them that your business is still active. Ask your customers for ratings and respond to them professionally.
