# UX researchers should be curious like journalists
> UX research can be fundamentally improved if we [adopt a journalistic approach](https://benlarsenwrites.medium.com/unveiling-the-power-of-journalistic-curiosity-in-ux-research-a-guide-to-customer-insights-3ac76b9d8e0b) – curiosity and thorough exploration of hidden user needs. Using techniques such as in-depth interviews, contextual observation, and storytelling leads to discovering deeper and emotional aspects of user behavior that would otherwise go unnoticed.

> „By adopting the mindset and techniques of seasoned journalists, we can unlock a treasure trove of insights that might have otherwise remained hidden.“\
> — **[Ben Larsen](https://benlarsenwrites.medium.com/unveiling-the-power-of-journalistic-curiosity-in-ux-research-a-guide-to-customer-insights-3ac76b9d8e0b)**

The insights gained this way help create designs that truly resonate with user needs. Journalistic skills, such as the ability to ask questions and tell stories, help researchers gain a more comprehensive view of their target audience.
