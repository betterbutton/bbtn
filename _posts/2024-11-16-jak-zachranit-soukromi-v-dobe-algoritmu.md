# How to Save Privacy in the Age of Algorithms
> 

Lowry Pressly, in his book [The Right to Oblivion](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/209349509-the-right-to-oblivion?from_search=true&from_srp=true&qid=F26LhfQSFs&rank=1), offers a new perspective on privacy as protection against the very creation of data, not just its misuse. He traces the historical roots of this concept, from Victorian critiques of photography to today's digital age, where our information is mass-produced and used against us.

> “Privacy has value not because it allows us to control information, but because it prevents its creation.”\
> **— Lowry Pressly**

Lowry emphasizes that privacy enables entry into *oblivion*—a space of the unknown and unarticulable, crucial for renewal, dignity, and personal development. This approach transcends legal frameworks and positions [privacy as a question of power and the value of life in an algorithm-driven society](https://www.newyorker.com/culture/the-weekend-essay/what-is-privacy-for).
