# 6 most common mistakes in qualitative interviews and how to avoid them
> 

Qualitative interviews can be complex, even for experienced interviewers. Common mistakes include interrupting, asking leading questions, and excessive positive reinforcement. It's also important to pay attention to building rapport with the participant and maintaining a neutral tone throughout the interview.

> „The participant should always be talking more than the moderator. My rule of thumb is that the transcript should be 80% participant and 20% moderator…“\
> **— Nikki Anderson-Stanier, [6 Common Mistakes to Avoid During Qualitative Interviews](https://dscout.com/people-nerds/qualitative-interview-mistakes)**

[6 most common mistakes when conducting an interview:](https://dscout.com/people-nerds/qualitative-interview-mistakes)

- You ask yes/no discovery questions.
- You lead the participant with suggestive queries.
- You create the impression that a certain answer is socially desirable.
- You talk over the participant.
- It's easy to understand what the question is driving at.
- You use evaluative expressions.
- You lose focus.
- You forget important questions.

Nikki Anderson-Stanier recommends slowing down, letting participants finish their thoughts, and avoiding attempts to compare their experiences with ours. Through self-evaluation and feedback from colleagues, interviews can be gradually improved to yield higher-quality data.
