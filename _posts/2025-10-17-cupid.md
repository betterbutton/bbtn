# CUPID: Clear, Unobstructed, Predictable, Indulgent, Digestible 
> A designer system that helps reduce long-term ergonomic load – not just to facilitate first use.

[CUPID](https://hansv.com/book/CUPID-Cheat-Sheet.pdf) is a framework for designing user interfaces created by *[Hans van de Bruggen](https://hansv.com/)*. It helps designers uncover and eliminate persistent sources of friction that users experience when using applications daily. Unlike common heuristics that focus mainly on learnability, CUPID addresses what remains difficult even after users have mastered the system.

The acronym CUPID stands for five principles:

- **Clear** – Clarity of state and feedback,

- **Unobstructed** – Elimination of unnecessary delays,

- **Predictable** – Consistency and predictability,

- **Indulgent** – Forgiveness for imprecise input,

- **Digestible** – Reduction of cognitive load.

CUPID works as a pre-launch checklist. It allows designers to find places where the interface burdens users even after they have learned to use it. It is best suited for tools, applications, and systems that people use repeatedly – such as office software, design tools, or professional platforms.

The framework does not replace user testing or learnability metrics. It complements them with an important aspect: persistent load. It highlights subtle but long-term barriers that remain hidden during first impressions.
