# From Intuitiveness to Dictatorship: Critique of Modern UIs
> 

Oliver Meredith Cox is a user, not a designer. He warns of the dangers brought by today's pursuit of overly intuitive and simple user interfaces. He claims that these UIs can function as soft tyranny, where we are deprived of control and freedom in handling technology.

> "We used to have choices. Now we are steered."\
> **— Oliver Meredith Cox, [The accidental tyranny of user interfaces](https://uxdesign.cc/the-accidental-tyranny-of-user-interfaces-eef15dc555a0)**

Specifically, he criticizes YouTube on Android: Every tab on a video acts as a link across the entire surface. You cannot navigate to the channel or select and copy text.

As another example, Oliver mentions WhatsApp. Someone might send you payment details, but you don't have the option to select the account number separately and copy it. You have to copy the entire message as a whole.

Maybe there's something to it. Maybe by tailoring interfaces to the least capable users, we are shortchanging the advanced ones.
