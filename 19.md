**Tomorrow is [Global Accessibility Awareness Day](https://accessibility.day/).** Up to a billion people worldwide have some form of disability. The most common impairments are in the areas of vision, hearing, motor skills, or cognition. Besides people with permanent disabilities, [each of us](https://uxdesign.cc/understanding-accessibility-makes-you-a-better-insert-your-name-here-1f7a15c50e7f) occasionally encounters [situational accessibility problems](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/333448990_Understanding_Situational_Disabilities_and_Situational_Awareness_in_Disasters). Don't forget that starting next year, the [EU Accessibility Act](https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=1202&langId=en) will be effective in the EU, which no longer applies only to public administration and even extends beyond the internet and software. The topic of accessibility will be covered by the [Agora conference](https://agora.muni.cz/). If you want to understand accessibility better, try the [course by Sara Soueidan](https://practical-accessibility.today/). Until May 20th, you can get it with a 25% discount, and Sara will give you another quarter off because you're from the Czech Republic.

**CZPodcast #319 about browser news.** Roman Pichlík and Jiří Fabián [talked with an internet security expert](https://soundcloud.com/czpodcast-1/cz-podcast-319-michal-spacek-o-novych-vlastnostech-prohlizecu) Michal Špaček. Michal mainly talks about changes in third-party cookies, the introduction of new security headers, browser access to Bluetooth or USB, and technologies like WebAssembly. Michal explains how these changes affect user tracking, increase security, and change how browsers work.

**A UX researcher is like detective Colombo,** [writes researcher Michala Pítrová](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7195656527909715968/). In her new article [Four Mistakes When Asking Questions in Research](https://www.uxvyzkumnice.cz/4-chyby-pri-pokladani-otazek-ve-vyzkumu/), she focuses on the way questions are asked in interviews with respondents and offers better practices.

**The term UX appeared 29 years ago**, [reminds Petr Kovář](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7195733590364573697/). User experience was written about by Don Norman, Jim Miller, and Austin Henderson in the conference paper [What You See, Some of What's in the Future, And How We Go About Doing It: HI at Apple Computer](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/202165701_What_You_See_Some_of_What's_in_the_Future_And_How_We_Go_About_Doing_It_HI_at_Apple_Computer) at the CHI ’95 conference. So it wasn't exactly the first time user experience was written about, but from that moment, UX began to be an industry.
