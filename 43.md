# Linkodrome

**Mirek Uďan has written a great article on [startup success metrics](https://www.miroslavudan.cz/blog/jake-jsou-nejdulezitejsi-metriky-ve-startupu/)**. It's as if the subject [financial analysis of a company](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finan%C4%8Dn%C3%AD_anal%C3%BDza#Nejd%C5%AFle%C5%BEit%C4%9Bj%C5%A1%C3%AD_pom%C4%9Brov%C3%A9_ukazatele) at the University of Economics got a continuation for the summer semester. A dozen metrics help keep an eye on the company's condition. Mirek adds tactics for each metric that can influence it. However, the article lacks a substantial thing: optimal value ranges for the metrics are not provided.

For example, the customer lifetime value ([CLV](https://www.strafelda.cz/clv)) should ensure sufficient profit after deducting acquisition and operating costs. The customer churn rate ([Churn rate](https://www.shean.cz/churn-rate/)) can also significantly affect the customer value. Optimal values always depend on your costs and margins.

An interesting [perspective on financial analysis with specific magic indices](https://www.threads.net/@accuinsights/post/DBtKCalSnmo?xmt=AQGzbPpFSmSM1CiJznag63I9gPIsTzInZk_eBAbw8qqSZw) is offered by Muzamil Ahmad using Netflix, Spotify, and Adobe as examples. According to Muzamil, Spotify has double the customer churn rate of Netflix and Adobe. It must therefore significantly improve the ratio of customer value to acquisition costs.

Awareness of financial indicators of a company's health is still low here, and it would be great if, for example, banks offered operational dashboards to newly established companies that open accounts with them. Or the Ministry of Industry and Trade could equip business offices with an educational e-book and an Excel sheet. This would significantly boost the economy of small businesses and micro-enterprises, which often operate without any financial management.

# Life with Technology

Do you read manuals? When we bring new power tools home, we often misplace the manual and sometimes even look for a video on YouTube to see how to set up and use the new toy.

With software, and especially with web services, such a thing would never occur to us. We skip guides, skip onboarding, and dive straight into working with the interface. Maybe only during regular updates do we glance at a tutorial to learn about new features, but otherwise, help is a forbidden word for websites and apps.

![Four ways of arranging the knobs on a four-burner gas stove. The most usable variant is where the knob arrangement duplicates the layout of the burners in the surface.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/2b01803e-d8e0-4624-b669-75ffb621b536_1005x881.png "468x410")

Image of control mapping on a gas stove from Donald A. Norman's book [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.databazeknih.cz/knihy/design-pro-kazdy-den-46545)

It is said that an interface is like a joke: if it needs explaining, it's not very good. How did we get to such a state? At least since the 1980s, interaction design has been a subject of study for cognitive psychologists. Where designers of physical tools work with anatomy, efficiency, ergonomics, and safety, we add cognitive principles to software interfaces. With various variations by author, it's still the same seven that Donald Arthur Norman outlined in the book [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.knihovny.cz/Record/nkp.NKC01-002127896?sid=23149994): discoverability, feedback, conceptual model, affordances, signifiers, mapping, and constraints.

Importing a theme template in Google Slides

Yesterday we met at Cafe Terapie to plan further activities. Jakub was sitting in the back room at a computer, trying to create his own presentation template in Google Slides. We won't keep you in suspense: it can't be done. You can only edit an existing theme (Slide → Edit theme / Slide → Edit theme). When you then want to use it in another presentation, you must display the theme menu (Theme / Theme) and load your previously edited file using the button at the end of the available themes list. It's a peculiar way to (not) implement something.

**Define the structure of offers in advance**

If you give potential suppliers freedom in presentation, they might impress you with an attractive offer, but not necessarily the best one for your website. If you're not sure how to tender correctly, consult someone experienced.

Publish a working version of the assignment and invite selected suppliers to propose additions and modifications to it. For larger projects, it's good to organize public consultations before the tender process.

A quality supplier will ask questions about the assignment, which will help you eliminate any ambiguities. Offers in a standardized format are then much easier to compare and evaluate.
