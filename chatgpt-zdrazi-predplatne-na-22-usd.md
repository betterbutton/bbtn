# TechCrunch: ChatGPT Plus to raise prices to cover losses
> 

According to the magazine TechCrunch, the price of ChatGPT Plus will soon [increase](https://techcrunch.com/2024/09/27/openai-might-raise-the-price-of-chatgpt-to-22-by-2025-44-by-2029/) from $20 to $22 per month and could reach up to $44 by 2029. According to The New York Times, this increase reflects [pressure from investors to reduce losses](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/09/27/technology/openai-chatgpt-investors-funding.html#:~:text=Roughly%2010%20million%20ChatGPT%20users,five%20years%2C%20the%20documents%20said.) at OpenAI, which expects a loss of $5 billion this year, although it achieved a monthly revenue of $300 million in August.

The costs of ChatGPT were at a peak of around $700,000 per day. However, the higher price could deter users, many of whom already consider the current amount high.

While OpenAI is attracting investors with billion-dollar profits from ChatGPT, it is also battling enormous costs. The company expects to reach revenues of $3.7 billion this year, but also losses of around $5 billion. The main reason is rising costs for cloud services, especially from Microsoft, which is also a key investor.

OpenAI is now negotiating a new funding round of $7 billion, which would increase its valuation to $150 billion. At the same time, it must change its structure to a for-profit company within two years, otherwise the investments will turn into debt.
