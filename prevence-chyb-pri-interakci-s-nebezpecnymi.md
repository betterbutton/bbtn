# Preventing Errors During Interaction with Dangerous Actions in UI
> 

Victor Ponamariov explains various [strategies to protect users from unintended or irreversible actions](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2024/09/how-manage-dangerous-actions-user-interfaces/) in user interfaces. Beyond general principles like requiring confirmation for dangerous steps, he also covers specific techniques: from modal dialogs and inline confirmations to two-factor authorization.

> "Good error messages are important, but the best error design carefully prevents them before they happen."\
> — **[Victor Ponamariov](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2024/09/how-manage-dangerous-actions-user-interfaces/)**

Victor emphasizes the need for clear communication, a requirement for further action (e.g., text input), and visual warnings. He also warns against the inappropriate overuse of confirmation dialogs, which can desensitize users. A key method is providing the ability to undo an action.
