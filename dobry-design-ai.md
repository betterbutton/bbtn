# What is good design in the era of artificial intelligence
> 

In light of recent events where Figma temporarily removed the Make Design AI-powered feature, it's interesting to see how Figma's own product designers perceive design in relation to artificial intelligence.

Last week in Figma's magazine, an article was published where Figma product designers Noah Levin, Oscar Dumlao, Jordan Singer, Marco Cornacchia, and Natasha Tenggoro successively reflect on [design in the AI era](https://www.figma.com/blog/what-is-good-design-in-the-age-of-ai).

> “As AI democratizes product development, design will be the differentiator.”
> **— Noah Levin, [What is good design in the age of AI?](https://www.figma.com/blog/what-is-good-design-in-the-age-of-ai/)**

According to Figma's designers, even in the era of artificial intelligence, design remains a key factor for product differentiation. Just as the mobile era required new design patterns, AI demands experimentation and time to find its full potential. Good design should be based on principles like empathy, creativity, and focus on real user needs.

AI should not merely automate processes but should collaborate with designers and enhance creative processes. A pragmatic approach is important, where AI-assisted tools can increase efficiency and creativity. Instead of creating content without context, AI should intelligently anticipate needs and adapt to user goals, becoming an active collaborator in the creative process.
