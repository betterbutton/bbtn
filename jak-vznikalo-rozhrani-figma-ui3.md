# How the New UI3 Interface for Figma Was Created
> 

Perhaps you also noticed a small change in Figma's appearance at the beginning of the week when [floating palettes changed back to docked](https://www.threads.net/@figma/post/DAlnAW0vciU?xmt=AQGzYkoZnLpI5lwSVH6QWP4MC_kbTV64PgXhYuhTnHF3og). Figma is still working on the new UI3 and offers [a look behind the scenes of the redesign](https://www.figma.com/blog/our-approach-to-designing-ui3/).

> "Redesigns are fun for designers, but they can be truly daunting for users."\
> — **Marcin Wichary**, Design Director, Figma

The redesign of the UI3 interface in Figma was created with the goal of minimizing distraction and focusing designers on their work. The Figma team spent over two years on iterations and reviewing their decisions, for example, with floating panels, which eventually returned to fixed placement based on user feedback.

New features like Minimize UI offer greater flexibility and simpler navigation. The key was the balance between designers' intuition and users' needs, which helped improve the product for millions of people.
