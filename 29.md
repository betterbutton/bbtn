# Linkodrome

**UI Density: How to Define and Design It.** The user interface has loosened up over the last decade. This is clearly visible when comparing contemporary websites and applications with those from the turn of the century. [UI density](https://matthewstrom.com/writing/ui-density/?ref=sidebar) encompasses not only the visual aspect but also the amount of information and the effectiveness with which it is provided to the user for absorption.

Classic information density like on the Bloomberg terminal is visually cluttered but rich in information. Edward Tufte coined the term [data-to-ink ratio](https://www.codeconquest.com/blog/data-ink-ratio-explained-with-example/#:~:text=The%20data%2Dink%20ratio%20is,required%20to%20print%20the%20graphic.) in his book [The Visual Display of Quantitative Data](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/17744.The_Visual_Display_of_Quantitative_Information_2nd_Ed_), meaning the ratio of useful information to total visual elements. This concept can also be applied to UI, where design density contributes to overall usability.

It's not that the user interface shouldn't be airy, but compact. Above all, it should not increase the [signal-to-noise ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signal-to-noise_ratio). [Ornament is crime.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ornament_and_Crime)

An important aspect is also data density over time, which depends on the UI's response speed. The ultimate goal is to maximize the information transfer the user gains for the least effort expended.

**Practical Tips for Choosing Keyboards in Mobile Design.** Ksenia Toloknova reminds us in the article [Are you designing with the right keyboard in mind?](https://uxdesign.cc/are-you-designing-with-the-right-keyboard-ab2f02df42b6) that in iOS you can find up to fifty types of user input and on Android around thirty. Choosing the correct input field type influences the keyboard appearance, for example for passwords or emails.

Action buttons on keyboards can be customized for various actions, improving the user experience. There are as many as twelve types on iOS and seven on Android. Proper use of keyboards improves navigation and overall interaction with the app.

**Atomic UX research transforms researchers' work.** When you get a research repository today, it's likely to be [opinionated software](https://basecamp.com/gettingreal/04.6-make-opinionated-software) and will guide you toward one specific research methodology – [atomic user research](https://medium.com/design-kisk/atomic-ux-research-trochu-jin%C3%BD-jadern%C3%BD-v%C3%BDzkum-298f8551134e).

![A diagram of atomic research. It starts with an experiment, from which we distill facts. We then group the facts, which represent insights. And based on the insight, we determine the next steps.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/cc5251f1-b32c-4ffc-9ef4-ab483b5227e4_875x558.png "875x558")

The atomic research diagram from the article [What is Atomic UX Research?](https://blog.prototypr.io/what-is-atomic-research-e5d9fbc1285c)

Atomic UX research presents a concept of breaking down findings into small, basic parts – nuggets. Nuggets allow for fine sorting, organization, and reuse of findings. Tomer Sharon and Daniel Pidcock [took inspiration for this model](https://blog.prototypr.io/what-is-atomic-research-e5d9fbc1285c) from the [Atomic Design](https://atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/chapter-2/) methodology and the scientific model [DIKW](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/DIKW_pyramida) (Data, Information, Knowledge, Wisdom).

Atomic user research builds on previously used methods of processing qualitative data in sociology, anthropology, and other social sciences. For example, [thematic analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thematic_analysis), [grounded theory](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zakotven%C3%A1_teorie#:~:text=Zakotven%C3%A1%20teorie%20vych%C3%A1z%C3%AD%20stejn%C4%9B%20jako,volnosti%20k%20pe%C4%8Dliv%C3%A9mu%20prozkoum%C3%A1ni%20jevu.), or [qualitative content analysis](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsahov%C3%A1_anal%C3%BDza). In the UX environment, atomic research was preceded by the use of [affinity diagrams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affinity_diagram), [framing theory](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teorie_r%C3%A1mov%C3%A1n%C3%AD), or [narrative analysis](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anal%C3%BDza_narativ%C5%AF).

The advantage of atomic UX research is modularity and scalability. Nuggets can be easily combined across studies and research projects. Nuggets can be collected and supplemented continuously, so insights don't become obsolete. Stakeholders don't need to familiarize themselves with a complex research report; they can simply perceive formulated insights in the form of, say, just one group of nuggets.

Each insight is tied to nuggets, and nuggets can be sourced all the way back to specific experiments, which supports evidence-based decision-making. Nuggets are easily stored in information systems that can be keyword-sorted and searched, so multiple people can access research results without research training.

# Tip from our book

**Important information must be visible immediately**

![An illustration from the book shows a newspaper stand with displayed newspapers.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/9ea0e6f1-49c7-4709-8080-fd6a5794536d_5638x3800.jpeg "1456x981")

Illustration: [Kateřina Čápová](https://www.katerinacapova.cz/) ([75 tips on how to talk about the web](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha))

Go to a newsstand and see what's visible from newspapers stacked on the counter. In most cases, you only see the header with the newspaper's name. At best, the top half of the front page is visible. In English, this is called "above the fold." On the web, "above the fold" is what you see of a webpage immediately after loading on the first screen.

The content a website visitor sees on the first screen must motivate them to scroll down the page. Newspaper editors use the composition principle of the inverted pyramid. They tell you the essential information first, and only below the fold and on subsequent pages do you learn the details. Follow the same approach when creating a website.
