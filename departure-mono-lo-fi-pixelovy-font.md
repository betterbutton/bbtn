# Departure Mono: Lo-Fi pixel font
> 

![Example of using the Departure Mono font, which is made of a grid of dots.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/739ff0b2-dddb-4024-a13c-fb53eceda780_1894x1073.png "1456x825")

Example of using the [Departure Mono](https://departuremono.com/) font

When Martin was little, he drew on tractor-feed paper, and on the telephone table in the hallway lay a neatly stacked pile of perforated labels. Tractor-feed paper was actually an endless roll of paper, zigzag-folded into sheets separated by perforations. On the sides, the roll had narrow strips with round holes. These were used to feed the paper from the tray into the printer—hence the name "tractor."

Sometimes the paper was pre-printed on one side. Back then, printing was mainly done with [character printers](https://www.cnews.cz/clanky/od-bubnovych-a-retezovych-tiskaren-az-po-led/#:~:text=Bubnov%C3%A9%2C%20%C5%99et%C4%9Bzov%C3%A9%20a%20znakov%C3%A9%20tisk%C3%A1rny). These worked on a principle similar to typewriters. The image of a character was stamped onto the paper through an inked ribbon. However, some papers were printed with (then) modern [dot matrix printers](https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jehli%C4%8Dkov%C3%A1_tisk%C3%A1rna), which built the character images from dots in a matrix.

The dots in a dot matrix printer's print are essentially pixels. The dot matrix printers of that time had very low resolution—typically a little over 60 dots per inch. Today's office LED printers print ten, twenty, or forty times more finely. The dots in a dot matrix print were clearly visible to the naked eye.

The font [Departure Mono](https://departuremono.com/), designed by designer [Helena Zhang](https://www.helenazhang.com/), who until recently led designers at Medium.com, references those times. Departure Mono is a monospace pixel font inspired by the limitations of early command-line and graphical user interfaces, small pixel fonts from the turn of the 90s and the new century. Its use is ideal for retro interfaces, but it will also find its place in sci-fi environments.
