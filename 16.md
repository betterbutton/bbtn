# Linkodrome

**Auditing traffic on the Czech web and the end of third-party cookies,** that's going to be exciting. [SPIR is looking for a new supplier](https://www.spir.cz/spir-vyhlasuje-tendr-na-realizaci-projektu-netmonitor/) as a replacement for NetMonitor, [writes Lupa](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/spir-hleda-provozovatele-netmonitoru-ktery-zvladne-merit-navstevnost-bez-3rd-party-cookies/).

> **Updated 2024-04-24 at 09:00.** That third-party cookie thing won't be as quick as it seemed. Jan Tichý just [shared a link on Linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jantichy_update-on-the-plan-for-phase-out-of-third-party-activity-7188789386287697921-wSz2) to [a message from Google](https://privacysandbox.com/intl/en_us/news/update-on-the-plan-for-phase-out-of-third-party-cookies-on-chrome/), where on Friday they announced an update to the plan to end support for third-party cookies in Chrome. Apparently, it's not just service providers who are caught off guard by the speed and consequences (Google has been announcing this for years), but regulators are monitoring that the push to cancel this technology has exactly the opposite effect on the protection of privacy and personal data of users than originally intended (Google will [track you directly in the browser](https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/personal/2023/09/chromes-enhanced-ad-privacy-what-you-need-to-know), [Meta has unified accounts](https://www.meta.com/help/quest/articles/accounts/privacy-information-and-settings/account-information-shared-with-family-of-apps-and-third-parties/) between FB, IG, WA, and Threads, and the Czech Republic is creating [Czech Ad ID](https://www.mediaguru.cz/clanky/2022/05/cesky-reklamni-identifikator-czech-ad-id-ma-velkou-sanci-uspet/)).

**The Citizen Portal has a mobile app.** [DIA launched another mobile app.](https://www.dia.gov.cz/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/TZ_aplikace_Portal_obcana.pdf) Among other things, you can manage your data mailboxes in the app.

**Illegal content on the internet is assessed directly by service providers.** *„Indeed, ČTÚ, as the national coordinator of digital services, will not evaluate/assess the illegality of content.“* writes on Twitter the chairman of the ČTÚ board [Marek Ebert](https://twitter.com/MarekEbert/status/1782324976458088768). ČTÚ has a [nice summary](https://ctu.gov.cz/evropske-narizeni-o-digitalnich-sluzbach-dsa) on its website regarding the European Digital Services Regulation.

**When something looks, swims, and quacks like a duck…** Jakub Linowski [on recent A/B tests at Etsy and Makro](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7186734020519440385/) shows that it's much better when buttons look like buttons. Petr Kosnar [adds in the comments](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7186734020519440385?commentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Acomment%3A%28activity%3A7186734020519440385%2C7186818937966206977%29&dashCommentUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afsd_comment%3A%287186818937966206977%2Curn%3Ali%3Aactivity%3A7186734020519440385%29) the technical term [perceived affordance](https://www.interval.cz/clanky/digitalni-afordance/). Simply, if someone is supposed to click on it, it mustn't be a greyed-out icon, but a button.

**Creating on a foreign platform is a dead end.** Joan Westenberg describes [the pitfalls awaiting creators](https://joanwestenberg.com/blog/the-creator-economy-trap-why-building-on-someone-elses-platform-is-a-dead-end) who use platforms like Tiktok, Instagram, or Substack for distribution, like we do. As an alternative, she offers the indie scene.

**[Forendors published statistics](https://blog.forendors.cz/tipy-triky/platit-za-obsah-je-normalni/),** from which it follows that the majority of paying podcast listeners have exactly one subscription. Subscribers usually last ten months, but now they more often pay for podcasts for just one month. The average subscription price is 148 crowns per month.

**Can you mix a color in RGB?** Play this [game with three sliders](https://susam.net/myrgb.html) for red, green, and blue and see how accurately you can hit the color.

Jakub often regrets while driving that Siri isn't a bit more agile. It can tell a driver stuck in traffic that they have a meeting somewhere else soon and offer to send a message or call a contact, but it could just call itself and move the appointment in the calendar. When we see what Wondering can do, maybe we're not that far off. Martin can't praise Shortcuts in iOS enough. When we're driving, one of Martin's shortcuts automatically opens Waze when the phone connects to CarPlay.

For integration, utilities with clearly defined input and output are suitable. Integration is essentially like a script in a shell. We already use [AI instead of searching the manual](https://twitter.com/tomaskafka/status/1782799213887201390) when we want to use a program with a bunch of switches, like ffmpeg for video manipulation or wget for browsing links on the web. So why couldn't AI be the shell itself with a natural language interface?

# What we're currently working on

After years, Martin is doing guerrilla testing again. He chose the proven environment of the Prague intersection at Anděl, where there are plenty of cafes: *„I only now realized that cafes here open significantly later than before and that not so many people sit there working with laptops anymore.“* Even so, he manages to collect feedback from respondents. He just needs to bypass more locations. The reward in the form of a chocolate bar still works well too.
