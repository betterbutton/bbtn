# The government has announced a competition for a unified visual style for the state administration
> 

> **Updated July 21, 2025:** The competition results are already known. The [winning design](https://www.najbrt.cz/aktuality/vizualni-identita-statni-spravy-ceske-republiky) comes from Studio Najbrt. [In second place](https://klarakvizova.graphics/projekt/jednotna-prezentace-statni-spravy-ceske-republiky/) is the joint proposal by Klára Kvízová and František Štorm. [Third place](https://x.com/PragerGroup/status/1946041289557873026/) was taken by Superior Type. Further places go to (4.) Dynamo design, (5.) Heyduk, Musil, Strnad, (6.) Joint proposal by Superlative.works, Chci.xyz. and zetzetzet, and (7.) Times New Realism. We devote more attention to the competition results [in the BBtn#80 newsletter](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/80?open=false#%C2%A7co-me-prastilo-do-oci-a-bolelo-to).

The Czech Republic has announced [a competition for a unified visual style for the state administration](https://nen.nipez.cz/verejne-zakazky/detail-zakazky/N006-24-S00000012), which is to unify the appearance of government institutions, strengthen public trust, and bring savings. In addition to unifying the visual style of institutions, the state's visual identity is also intended to reduce costs for graphics. The submission deadline is the morning of January 28, 2025, and it will be evaluated by an expert committee.

Inspiration comes from countries like Finland or Denmark, where a unified style has streamlined administration and simplified communication with citizens.

The state's visual identity should also be reflected in the [design system](https://designsystem.gov.cz/), thereby facilitating orientation on websites and in applications. We wish for the state to demonstrate maturity and actually use its identity, so that it does not end up like the transition to the `gov.cz` domain and the use of the design system.
