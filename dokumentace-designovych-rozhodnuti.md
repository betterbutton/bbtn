# Documentation of design decisions
> 

We are used to documenting styleguides for text or design systems, but how well do we document design decisions? Kai Wong offers a simple [framework for documenting design changes](https://dataanddesign.substack.com/p/how-to-archive-your-design-system):

- **Use case:** The scenario we are designing for, including context. What are the best practices in this area?

- **Alternative options:** What variants are available to solve the use case.

- **Design decision:** How and why we chose the selected alternative.

- **Quick recommendations and tips:** Examples of how to use the design and how not to.

- **Links to documentation:** Hyperlinks to the design system component page or styleguide.

Sometimes the same discussions about a design keep coming up in circles. Every new stakeholder tends to reopen already closed questions. Documentation allows us to efficiently go through feedback, helps developers better understand how to implement the design, and in future follow-up projects we will know what led us to the situation we are solving. Documentation also provides room for change, as through it we know the original conditions of the decision-making and can better judge whether it is time to change the decision.

Kai Wong writes that the most important documentation is at the moment we decide to abandon the existing design system and implement a new one instead. Then the documentation guides us through the transition between the original and new components.
