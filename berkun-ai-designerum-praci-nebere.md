# Scott Berkun: Not Yet. AI Still Won't Take Designers' Jobs
> 

We recently mentioned Scott Berkun's unfinished book on design [here](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/instalateri-designerum). This time, Scott ponders whether there is a real threat that AI could take away designers' jobs.

**TL;DR — Not yet. Not for a "long" time either.**

Berkun concludes that while AI can automate the production part of design, human creativity and the ability to plan remain irreplaceable. Therefore, designers must focus on the planning phase, asking the right questions, and deciding what and how to make. New technologies often don't eliminate old ones but bring new challenges and opportunities. The key to success is the ability to explain and advocate for one's ideas, a skill that AI cannot yet replace.

> “Computers are useless: they can only give you answers.”\
> **— [Pablo Picasso](https://quoteinvestigator.com/2011/11/05/computers-useless/)**

Martin, in the context of this debate, often reminds us that UX design has evolved toward values of participation and evidence-based practice. If we don't compromise these values just to reduce design costs, AI tools will also have to consider the creative input of users and stakeholders during design, and their outputs will need to be defensible by referencing sources that include not just best practices but also user research.
