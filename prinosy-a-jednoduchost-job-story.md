# Benefits and Simplicity of Job Story Mapping
> 

![When (situation) I want to (Motivation)](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/f46ee1e1-daa5-4878-b009-971aa72fd02f_875x350.png "875x350")

[Job Story schema according to Alan Klement](https://jtbd.info/replacing-the-user-story-with-the-job-story-af7cdee10c27)

Thomas Ian Wilson criticizes complex and incomprehensible approaches that often appear in discussions, and emphasizes the need to [keep the human element at the center of every map](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7239943013395759105/). [Job Story](https://jtbdtoolkit.medium.com/job-stories-revisited-13ad0b54eb3c) Mapping is an effective method for creating storyboards, customer journeys, and blueprints based on needs.

> “Story mapping helps break complex jobs into smaller, actionable tasks,
> which makes prioritization clearer.“
> **— [Thomas Ian Wilson](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7239943013395759105/)**

Quality customer journeys should be data-driven and include contextual user stories. By breaking down large tasks into smaller steps in mapping, teams can focus on key areas. The benefits of JTBD ([Jobs To Be Done](https://jtbd.info/replacing-the-user-story-with-the-job-story-af7cdee10c27)) include a unified view of user needs and the ability to identify and eliminate inefficiencies.
