# Animated Favicon
> 

The [favicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Favicon) first appeared with Microsoft Internet Explorer 5 in 1999. Originally, the icon was displayed for pages saved in bookmarks. Today, it finds much broader use. The icon is especially useful in tab ears.

If your website is also an application and you occasionally need to indicate that something in the app requires the user's attention, then the favicon is a subtle place to display a notification flag.

Another option is to animate the icon at such a moment. Here we leave it to your discretion how long and how wildly the icon should move, as it's not hard to overdo the effects, and people with concentration disorders or epilepsy probably won't appreciate automatically triggered animations in their field of vision.

Favicon itself does not support animation. But it's easy to change the icon with JavaScript. So just prepare the individual frames of the animation and change the icon at the right moment. The [Animated Favicon Generator](https://www.faviconvideo.com/) tool will help you prepare the script. Just upload an animated GIF to it, and it will take care of the rest.
