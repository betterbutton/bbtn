# Cyberattack Paralyzed the Internet Archive
> 

The Internet Archive's [Wayback Machine](https://www.archive.org/), which preserves historical versions of websites, [faced a DDoS attack on October 9](https://www.lupa.cz/aktuality/archiv-wayback-machine-po-utoku-docasne-nepridava-nove-zaznamy/), writes Lupa. It caused an outage and subsequent service limitation to read-only mode. Before the attack, over 31 million records from the user database were leaked.

Founder Brewster Kahle warns that further intervention may be necessary, which would mean another temporary service interruption. Archiving new pages is currently [suspended due to a security review](https://www.theverge.com/2024/10/14/24269741/internet-archive-online-read-only-data-breach-outage), shortly after [Google began linking to the Wayback Machine](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-integrates-internet-archive-links-into-search-results/526804/) instead of its own archiving service.
