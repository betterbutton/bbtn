# Linkodrome

**A Canadian interactive guide for submitting public procurement.** One of the fairly common wishes of people in public administration is for templates to exist for ICT public procurement. The Canadian government is approaching this in a quite interesting way. It is creating [interactive online guides](https://2021-prod.ict-cio.ssc-spc.cloud-nuage.canada.ca/) for these purposes. It has also prepared one for submitting accessibility requirements according to the [EN 301 549](https://accessible.canada.ca/en-301-549-accessibility-requirements-ict-products-and-services-1) standard, which is also required here. We have somewhere to get inspired!

![Sign: Drugs and nuclear weapons are not allowed in toilets](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/13ee6003-c637-45a2-809f-4a84292ae355_480x388.webp "480x388")

**The Belgian government has a security problem.** In Belgium, they let domains expire that were previously used by the government. Security expert Inti De Ceukelaire immediately [registered them](https://inti.io/p/when-privacy-expires-how-i-got-access). On the domains, he then collected emails from incoming mail and let users reset passwords. This gave him access to a lot of personal data. [Jakub Onderka writes about it on LinkedIn.](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7199756421570117633/) We can keep our fingers crossed for the Czech government to keep the original domains unused for some time after switching to gov.cz. Experts recommend that we keep the domain for at least ten years after we cease operating services on it.

# Living with Technologies

When computing power was limited and expensive, it made sense to create single-purpose hardware that was tuned for one specific thing and nothing else could be done with it. As power grows and price drops, generally usable hardware without a specific focus is increasingly prevailing.

![Old Russian digital game Wait, little hare. A wolf stands in a henhouse and catches eggs that chickens lay at increasing speed.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/58d59845-c431-40cd-b8a1-28407361ed0b.avif "480x288")

Display devices are moving from segment displays of digital watches to the raster displays of modern smartwatches. The interface is no longer formed by a few buttons and rotary knobs, but via the keyboard and mouse we have progressed to touchscreens.

Can a similar development be expected for software as well? From single-purpose command-line programs, software has moved on to [superapps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super-app) and [suites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_suite). And now artificial intelligence is entering the scene, promising to make a similar revolution that the arrival of [point and click](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_and_click) made in the user interface.

# Tip from Our Book

Expect analysis of problems from research. Leave solution proposals for the concept phase.

![Value proposition canvas. On the left are products and services, pain relievers, and gain creators. On the right are customer jobs, pains, and gains. In the middle between them is the product-market fit.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/4d8b866a-f326-4df2-8c28-98e719292d62.avif "480x480")

Value Proposition Canvas from the book [75 tips on how to Better Button](https://reknisioweb.cz/p/kniha) ([Kateřina Čápová](https://katerinacapova.cz/))

Don't anticipate, don't invent solutions for non-existent problems. Describe what your clients need to solve, explore their motivations, and see what troubles they are dealing with. When the time comes to design a concept, it will make it easier for you to identify ideas that fit your website.

Don't invent arguments for your ideas. Do it the other way around and look for ideas for problems and opportunities that emerge from the research.
