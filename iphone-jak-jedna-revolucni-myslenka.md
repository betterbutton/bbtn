# iPhone: How One Revolutionary Idea Reversed Steve Jobs' Decision
> 

In 2004, Steve Jobs nearly halted the development of the iPhone, which was originally an experimental project. Jobs was concerned about the complications of partnering with mobile carriers and didn't believe that smartphones would appeal to a broad market. Initially, [Jobs had no idea the iPhone was being developed](https://www.fastcompany.com/91209261/the-surprising-story-of-how-the-iphone-was-invented-illustrates-how-to-change-your-boss-mind), says Brian Solis.

However, the Apple team eventually convinced him through a combination of creativity and determination. Despite early setbacks and Jobs' preference for an "iPod-like" design, the team returned to its roots and created a revolutionary product, launched in 2007. The iPhone became an icon thanks to innovations in both design and user interface, defining a new era of smartphones.
